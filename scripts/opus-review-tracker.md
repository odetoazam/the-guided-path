## Rework Instructions: 009-at-tawbah/ayahs-029.md (RESOLVED — rework approved 2026-04-09 at 7.5/10)

This is the jizya verse and the first file to fail Opus review. The content is not bad — the din al-haqq analysis is genuinely insightful and the prose quality is high. But for a verse of this sensitivity and public significance, the file has critical gaps that prevent approval.

**Required fixes (all must be addressed):**

1. **Add counterbalancing Quranic verses**: 2:256 (la ikraha fi al-din), 60:8-9 (just treatment of non-combatants), 22:40 (defense of churches/synagogues), 29:46 (argue with Ahl al-Kitab in the best way). These are not contradictions — they are the Quran's own internal system. Their absence makes the reflection one-sided.

2. **Add classical juristic texture on jizyah**: At minimum one paragraph covering: (a) jizyah as tax in exchange for protection and military exemption, (b) Prophetic/Rashidun-era implementation and its constraints, (c) relationship between jizyah and zakat as parallel fiscal obligations. Source: al-Qurtubi, al-Jassas, or Ibn al-Qayyim.

3. **Add asbab al-nuzul**: The Tabuk expedition and Byzantine frontier context. This verse was revealed in a specific political-military situation that constrains its application.

4. **Linguistically analyze قتل (qtl) and جزي (jzy) roots in Part 1**: These are the verse's two most contested terms. qatilu — what form, what does Form III imply vs Form I? jizyah — what is the root's semantic field? 'an yadin — what does this prepositional phrase mean in classical Arabic?

5. **Add a third theme**: Either (a) the Prophetic covenants with Ahl al-Kitab and how the classical tradition regulated this verse's application, or (b) the theological specificity of "People of the Book" as a category distinct from mushrikin in Quranic law.

6. **Diversify mufassirun**: Add al-Qurtubi (jizya fiqh), al-Jassas (ahkam al-Quran), or Ibn al-Qayyim (ahkam ahl al-dhimmah). Tabari + Ibn Kathir alone is not enough for this verse.

7. **Reconsider title**: "Until the People of the Book Submit to the New Order" reads as triumphalist in English. Suggest something like "The Verse That Defined a Fiscal Covenant" or "When Revelation Legislated Coexistence."

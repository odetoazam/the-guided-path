<article class="prose-blog">
  <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed">The Quran does something wonderfully direct with divine love. It names its objects. Allah loves the <strong>muḥsinīn</strong>. Allah loves the <strong>muttaqīn</strong>. Allah loves those who trust Him, those who repent often, those who purify themselves, those who fight in ordered rows, those who act with justice. The repeated formula forms an enumeration of the beloved. Taken together, these verses yield a portrait rather than a list. They show what a human being looks like when divine love rests upon him.</p>

  <p>The root <strong>h-b-b</strong> carries the sense of concentrated attachment and chosen nearness. When the Quran says Allah loves certain kinds of people, it is describing moral proximity under divine favor. The beloved servant is not defined by lineage, class, or spectacle. He is defined by traits that gather around disciplined inwardness and beautiful outward action.</p>

  <blockquote class="ayah-quote">
  <p class="arabic" dir="rtl" style="font-family: var(--font-amiri,'Amiri'),serif; font-size: 1.5rem; line-height: 2.2; color: rgba(201,168,76,0.85);">
    وَأَنفِقُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ وَلَا تُلْقُوا۟ بِأَيْدِيكُمْ إِلَى ٱلتَّهْلُكَةِ وَأَحْسِنُوٓا۟ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يُحِبُّ ٱلْمُحْسِنِينَ
  </p>
  <p class="translation">Do ihsan. Surely Allah loves the muhsineen.</p>
  <cite>Al-Baqarah (2:195)</cite>
</blockquote>

  <p>The first beloved type is the <strong>muḥsin</strong>, from the root <strong>h-s-n</strong>, the field of beauty, excellence, and fine form. Allah loves those whose actions are brought to a beautiful finish. This means the beloved servant does what is right with grace, proportion, generosity, and refinement. Love begins here with beautified obedience.</p>

  <blockquote class="ayah-quote">
  <p class="arabic" dir="rtl" style="font-family: var(--font-amiri,'Amiri'),serif; font-size: 1.5rem; line-height: 2.2; color: rgba(201,168,76,0.85);">
    بَلَىٰ مَنْ أَوْفَىٰ بِعَهْدِهِۦ وَٱتَّقَىٰ فَإِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يُحِبُّ ٱلْمُتَّقِينَ
  </p>
  <p class="translation">Fulfill the covenant. Surely Allah loves the muttaqeen.</p>
  <cite>Aal-e-Imran (3:76)</cite>
</blockquote>

  <p>The <strong>muttaqī</strong> comes from the root <strong>w-q-y</strong>, to guard, shield, protect. <strong>Taqwā</strong> is guarded God-consciousness, the soul keeping itself under watch. Allah loves those who live with inward vigilance, who guard covenants and keep the heart from breach. The beloved servant is therefore not merely spontaneous. He is watchful.</p>

  <blockquote class="ayah-quote">
  <p class="arabic" dir="rtl" style="font-family: var(--font-amiri,'Amiri'),serif; font-size: 1.5rem; line-height: 2.2; color: rgba(201,168,76,0.85);">
    فَبِمَا رَحْمَةٍ مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ لِنتَ لَهُمْ وَلَوْ كُنتَ فَظًّا غَلِيظَ ٱلْقَلْبِ لَٱنفَضُّوا۟ مِنْ حَوْلِكَ فَٱعْفُ عَنْهُمْ وَٱسْتَغْفِرْ لَهُمْ وَشَاوِرْهُمْ فِى ٱلْأَمْرِ فَإِذَا عَزَمْتَ فَتَوَكَّلْ عَلَى ٱللَّهِ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يُحِبُّ ٱلْمُتَوَكِّلِينَ
  </p>
  <p class="translation">When you have resolved, place your trust in Allah. Surely Allah loves those who rely upon Him.</p>
  <cite>Aal-e-Imran (3:159)</cite>
</blockquote>

  <p>The <strong>mutawakkil</strong> belongs to the root <strong>w-k-l</strong>, which conveys entrusting a matter to another, appointing one as guardian over it. Allah loves those who rely upon Him after taking counsel and resolving. The beloved servant acts, decides, and then leans his unfinished matter into divine care. Trust here is not softness of will. It is strength released from self-sufficiency.</p>

  <blockquote class="ayah-quote">
  <p class="arabic" dir="rtl" style="font-family: var(--font-amiri,'Amiri'),serif; font-size: 1.5rem; line-height: 2.2; color: rgba(201,168,76,0.85);">
    وَيَسْـَٔلُونَكَ عَنِ ٱلْمَحِيضِ قُلْ هُوَ أَذًى فَٱعْتَزِلُوا۟ ٱلنِّسَآءَ فِى ٱلْمَحِيضِ وَلَا تَقْرَبُوهُنَّ حَتَّىٰ يَطْهُرْنَ فَإِذَا تَطَهَّرْنَ فَأْتُوهُنَّ مِنْ حَيْثُ أَمَرَكُمُ ٱللَّهُ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يُحِبُّ ٱلتَّوَّٰبِينَ وَيُحِبُّ ٱلْمُتَطَهِّرِينَ
  </p>
  <p class="translation">Surely Allah loves those who repent often and loves those who purify themselves.</p>
  <cite>Al-Baqarah (2:222)</cite>
</blockquote>

  <p>This verse introduces two more beloved types. The <strong>tawwāb</strong>, from <strong>t-w-b</strong>, is one who returns repeatedly. The root conveys turning back, circling toward the point of origin after deviation. The <strong>mutaṭahhir</strong>, from <strong>ṭ-h-r</strong>, belongs to purification, cleanliness, the removal of stain. The beloved servant is therefore marked by return and cleansing. Divine love meets the person who refuses to settle inside impurity or estrangement.</p>

  <blockquote class="ayah-quote">
  <p class="arabic" dir="rtl" style="font-family: var(--font-amiri,'Amiri'),serif; font-size: 1.5rem; line-height: 2.2; color: rgba(201,168,76,0.85);">
    لَا تَقُمْ فِيهِ أَبَدًا لَّمَسْجِدٌ أُسِّسَ عَلَى ٱلتَّقْوَىٰ مِنْ أَوَّلِ يَوْمٍ أَحَقُّ أَن تَقُومَ فِيهِ فِيهِ رِجَالٌ يُحِبُّونَ أَن يَتَطَهَّرُوا۟ وَٱللَّهُ يُحِبُّ ٱلْمُطَّهِّرِينَ
  </p>
  <p class="translation">Allah loves those who purify themselves.</p>
  <cite>At-Tawbah (9:108)</cite>
</blockquote>

  <p><strong>At-Tawbah (9:108)</strong> reiterates purity in a communal setting. Purification in the Quran is bodily, ritual, and moral. The beloved community cares about cleanliness because purity prepares a place for worship and truthful nearness. The repeated emphasis suggests that divine love is attached to receptivity. The heart and body are to be made ready.</p>

  <blockquote class="ayah-quote">
  <p class="arabic" dir="rtl" style="font-family: var(--font-amiri,'Amiri'),serif; font-size: 1.5rem; line-height: 2.2; color: rgba(201,168,76,0.85);">
    إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يُحِبُّ ٱلَّذِينَ يُقَٰتِلُونَ فِى سَبِيلِهِۦ صَفًّا كَأَنَّهُم بُنْيَٰنٌ مَّرْصُوصٌ
  </p>
  <p class="translation">Surely Allah loves those who fight in His cause in ranks, as though they were a firmly joined structure.</p>
  <cite>As-Saff (61:4)</cite>
</blockquote>

  <p>Here the beloved servant appears within ordered solidarity. The row is beloved because it exhibits disciplined cohesion. The image of a firmly joined structure suggests moral architecture: separate persons aligned into one steadfast body. Love, in this verse, rests upon discipline, courage, and collective order.</p>

  <blockquote class="ayah-quote">
  <p class="arabic" dir="rtl" style="font-family: var(--font-amiri,'Amiri'),serif; font-size: 1.5rem; line-height: 2.2; color: rgba(201,168,76,0.85);">
    وَإِن طَآئِفَتَانِ مِنَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ ٱقْتَتَلُوا۟ فَأَصْلِحُوا۟ بَيْنَهُمَا فَإِنۢ بَغَتْ إِحْدَىٰهُمَا عَلَى ٱلْأُخْرَىٰ فَقَٰتِلُوا۟ ٱلَّتِى تَبْغِى حَتَّىٰ تَفِىٓءَ إِلَىٰٓ أَمْرِ ٱللَّهِ فَإِن فَآءَتْ فَأَصْلِحُوا۟ بَيْنَهُمَا بِٱلْعَدْلِ وَأَقْسِطُوٓا۟ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يُحِبُّ ٱلْمُقْسِطِينَ
  </p>
  <p class="translation">Act with justice. Surely Allah loves those who are just.</p>
  <cite>Al-Hujurat (49:9)</cite>
</blockquote>

  <p>The just, from the root <strong>q-s-ṭ</strong>, belong to fairness, equitable distribution, proper balance in judgment. With this final trait, the portrait becomes complete. The beloved servant is beautiful in action, guarded in heart, trusting in dependence, repentant in failure, pure in body and soul, disciplined in community, and just in judgment.</p>

  <p>Read together, these verses present divine love as ethically textured. Allah loves traits that make a human being stable, generous, clean, balanced, and turned toward Him. Love is attached neither to raw intensity nor to inherited status. It rests upon a formed character.</p>

  <p>This portrait also reveals the deep kinship between <a href="/hub/mahabbah">mahabbah</a> and all the neighboring virtues of the Quran. The beloved human being is a person in whom <strong>iḥsān</strong>, <strong>taqwā</strong>, <strong>tawakkul</strong>, <strong>tawbah</strong>, purity, courage, and justice have learned to live together. Love becomes a theological summary of moral beauty. To ask what Allah loves is to ask what sort of human being the Quran is trying to shape.</p>

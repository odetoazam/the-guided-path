# If They Truly Believed

## Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:81)

[ayah:5:81] وَلَوْ كَانُوا۟ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلنَّبِىِّ وَمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مَا ٱتَّخَذُوهُمْ أَوْلِيَآءَ وَلَـٰكِنَّ كَثِيرًا مِّنْهُمْ فَـٰسِقُونَ

*Wa law kanu yu'minuna bi-Allahi wa al-nabiyyi wa ma unzila ilayhi ma ittakhadhuhum awliya'a wa lakinna kathiran minhum fasiqun.*

**And if they had truly believed in Allah and the Prophet and what was revealed to him, they would not have taken them as allies; but many of them are defiantly disobedient.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

Sometimes the Quran does not merely condemn a bad outcome. It goes looking for the broken root that produced it.

People can talk for a long time about poor alliances, compromised loyalties, misread strategy, social pressure, and the desire to survive in a hostile world. All of that may describe the surface. But this ayah does not stop at the surface. It asks a more frightening question: what would have been different if iman itself had been alive?

That is what makes the verse so sharp.

It does not say simply that these people made an unfortunate relational choice. It says: if they had really believed in Allah, the Prophet, and what was sent down to him, they would not have taken those people as awliya'. In other words, the alliance is treated not merely as a tactical mistake but as evidence of a deeper rupture in faith.

[PAUSE]

This should make us pause over our own attachments. Sometimes what we call "complexity," "survival," or "pragmatism" is not only that. Sometimes it is the place where weak iman has already begun outsourcing safety, honor, or nearness to what revelation did not authorize.

The hook of the ayah is this: **false alliances are often the outward shape of an inward deficit in faith.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Wa law kanu yu'minun**: The Ayah Speaks in the Language of an Unmet Condition

The verse begins conditionally:

**wa law kanu yu'minun** - and if they had been believing.

This matters because the ayah is not simply narrating what they did. It is naming what would have prevented it. The condition implies an absence. Something that should have been operative was not fully operative.

[PAUSE]

And notice that the verb is not merely a static label. It is living belief: **yu'minun**. A faith that is active enough to shape loyalties, not merely claimed enough to decorate identity.

The verse is therefore teaching us that real iman is not proven only in worship's private interior. It becomes visible in whom the heart entrusts itself to, whom it leans on, and what kinds of allegiances it finds morally acceptable.

### 2. **Bi-Allahi wa al-nabiyyi wa ma unzila ilayhi**: The Ayah Binds Faith to a Full Chain of Authority

Then the condition is unpacked:

**bi-Allahi** - in Allah.

**wa al-nabiyyi** - and the Prophet.

**wa ma unzila ilayhi** - and what was sent down to him.

[PAUSE]

This is a complete chain.

Faith in Allah without loyal reception of the Prophet is incomplete.
Faith in the Prophet without what was revealed to him is sentiment without submission.

The verse binds all three together because real belief is not vague God-talk. It is Allah, His messenger, and the revelation carried by that messenger. Once those three stand together in the heart, they create a moral order that regulates who can be taken as wali and who cannot.

That means the problem in the ayah is not only emotional weakness. It is dislocation from the full structure of revealed authority.

### 3. **Ma ittakhadhuhum awliya'**: True Faith Would Have Prevented This Closeness from Ever Settling

Then comes the verdict:

**ma ittakhadhuhum awliya'** - they would not have taken them as allies.

The language is decisive. Not "they might have been more careful." Not "they would probably have reconsidered." The verse states a direct incompatibility between living faith and this chosen protective closeness.

[PAUSE]

That is because **ittikhadh** is not casual contact. It is adopting, taking into a role. And **awliya'** in this section has consistently carried intimate allegiance, protective nearness, camp-level loyalty.

So the ayah is teaching that some forms of attachment are not neutral. They reveal what the soul thinks will actually keep it safe, honored, or established.

When iman is healthy, those attachments are disciplined. When iman is weak, the soul starts granting sacred weight to relationships and alignments that truth should have prevented.

### 4. **Wa lakinna kathiran minhum fasiqun**: The Real Diagnosis Is Not Merely Error, But Exit from Boundary

Then the verse concludes:

**wa lakinna kathiran minhum fasiqun** - but many of them are defiantly disobedient.

[PAUSE]

The word **fisq** is important here. It carries the sense of stepping out, breaking out from the boundary, exiting the obedient frame. So the issue is not just intellectual confusion. It is moral خروج - a departure from the boundary that should have contained them.

That clarifies the whole verse. The false alliance is not random. It is one symptom of a wider condition: they have already stepped outside the disciplined enclosure of faith.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Our Alliances Reveal What We Think Will Save Us

We tend to imagine faith as what we say under ideal conditions.

But the Quran repeatedly forces faith to reveal itself under pressure:

Whom do you trust?
Whom do you admire?
Whom do you fear losing?
Whom do you align with when obedience becomes costly?

[PAUSE]

This is where alliances become spiritually diagnostic. The people we seek for deepest protection often reveal what we think really governs outcomes. If Allah and revelation no longer feel sufficient, the soul starts searching elsewhere for the emotional shelter that iman should have provided.

That does not mean every human relationship is suspect. It means the moral shape of our loyalties matters. The heart must keep asking: is this closeness ordered beneath Allah's truth, or is it slowly competing with it?

### Theme 2: Weak Iman Often Dresses Itself as Pragmatism

One reason this verse is so piercing is that the behavior it condemns can easily be narrated in respectable language.

We call it realism.
We call it strategic wisdom.
We call it complex circumstances.

[PAUSE]

Sometimes some of that is true. Life is complicated. But the ayah warns us that underneath the respectable explanation there may be a deeper truth: if faith had been standing where it should, this alliance would never have settled into place.

That is not meant to flatten every hard judgment into a slogan. It is meant to keep us honest. We should not let sophisticated language hide from us what revelation has already named as an iman problem.

## Closing Synthesis

This ayah begins with an unmet condition and ends with a diagnosis.

If they had truly believed in Allah, the Prophet, and what was sent down to him,
they would not have taken them as awliya'.

But many of them are fasiqun.

[PAUSE]

That is the structure.

Living faith would have restrained the heart.
Instead, a boundary was crossed.
And that crossing became visible in the form of chosen loyalty.

So the verse teaches us that one of the most honest tests of iman is not only what we confess with our tongues, but what kinds of closeness our hearts allow themselves to formalize. Some bonds are not just social. They are theological disclosures.

### Questions to Carry

1. What alliances in my life reveal where I really think safety and honor come from?
2. Have I ever used pragmatic language to hide an inward weakness in faith?
3. Do I receive Allah, the Messenger, and revelation as one binding authority structure, or do I separate them when inconvenient?
4. Where might fisq - exit from boundary - already be showing itself in the shape of my attachments?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**This ayah teaches that when real faith in Allah, the Prophet, and revelation is alive, it disciplines allegiance; when that faith is weak, forbidden loyalties become possible because the soul has already begun stepping outside the boundary.**

### Du'a

Allahumma, make faith in You, Your Messenger, and Your revelation alive enough in us to govern our loyalties. Do not let our hearts seek protection, status, or nearness in ways that compete with Your truth. Save us from calling weak iman "pragmatism," and keep us inside the boundaries of obedience when pressure rises. Amin.

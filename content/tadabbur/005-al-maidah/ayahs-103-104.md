# The Names Allah Never Gave

## Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:103-104)

[ayah:5:103] مَا جَعَلَ ٱللَّهُ مِنۢ بَحِيرَةٍ وَلَا سَآئِبَةٍ وَلَا وَصِيلَةٍ وَلَا حَامٍ ۙ وَلَـٰكِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ يَفْتَرُونَ عَلَى ٱللَّهِ ٱلْكَذِبَ ۖ وَأَكْثَرُهُمْ لَا يَعْقِلُونَ

[ayah:5:104] وَإِذَا قِيلَ لَهُمْ تَعَالَوْا۟ إِلَىٰ مَآ أَنزَلَ ٱللَّهُ وَإِلَى ٱلرَّسُولِ قَالُوا۟ حَسْبُنَا مَا وَجَدْنَا عَلَيْهِ ءَابَآءَنَآ ۚ أَوَلَوْ كَانَ ءَابَآؤُهُمْ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ شَيْـًٔا وَلَا يَهْتَدُونَ

*Ma ja'ala Allahu min bahiratin wa la sa'ibatin wa la wasilatin wa la hamin. Wa lakinna alladhina kafaru yaftaruna 'ala Allahi al-kadhiba wa aktharuhum la ya'qilun.*

*Wa idha qila lahum ta'alaw ila ma anzala Allahu wa ila al-rasuli qalu hasbuna ma wajadna 'alayhi aba'ana. Awa law kana aba'uhum la ya'lamuna shay'an wa la yahtadun.*

**Allah has not ordained any bahirah, sa'ibah, wasilah, or ham. But those who disbelieve fabricate lies against Allah, and most of them do not use reason. And when it is said to them, "Come to what Allah has sent down and to the Messenger," they say, "Enough for us is what we found our fathers upon." Even if their fathers knew nothing and were not guided?**

## Introduction

Some people do not abandon religion by becoming irreligious.

They abandon it by becoming inventive.

They keep the language of reverence, the gestures of sanctity, the social seriousness of inherited custom, but the thing that made religion religion in the first place has quietly been removed: permission from Allah. Revelation is replaced by naming. Authority is replaced by familiarity. Sacredness is no longer received from above. It is manufactured within the tribe, then defended as though heaven itself had spoken.

That is the world these ayahs enter.

Al-Ma'idah has already been teaching us, again and again, that sacred life must be governed by what Allah actually made lawful, forbidden, consecrated, and binding. But here the surah exposes a different disease. It is not the open rejection of holiness. It is the creation of false holiness. Certain animals are given inherited cultic labels. Social custom surrounds them. People begin to treat those labels as though they carry divine force. Then, when they are called back to what Allah sent down and to the Messenger, they answer with one of the most revealing sentences in the Quran: **hasbuna ma wajadna 'alayhi aba'ana**. Enough for us is what we found our fathers upon.

The hook of the passage is this: **the Quran teaches that one of the most dangerous ways to leave Allah's religion is not by mocking the sacred, but by inventing sacred names He never gave and then defending them with the prestige of ancestry.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Ma ja'ala Allahu min ...**: The Ayah Begins by Erasing Divine Authorization Completely

The first words matter immensely:

**ma ja'ala Allahu**.

Allah has not made. Allah has not appointed. Allah has not instituted.

The ayah does not begin by saying these customs are socially inconvenient, historically outdated, or symbolically excessive. It goes straight to the real question: did Allah make this so?

Then comes the little word that widens the negation:

**min**.

**Ma ja'ala Allahu min bahirah ...**

In Qur'anic Arabic, **min** after a negation often intensifies totality. Not one instance. Not a single member of this category. The ayah is not trimming excess from an otherwise valid sacred structure. It is denying divine authorization at the root.

[PAUSE]

Then the four labels come one after another: **bahirah, sa'ibah, wasilah, ham**. The exact pre-Islamic technical differences between them are discussed in tafsir, but the verse does not dwell there. That is instructive. The point is not antiquarian detail for its own sake. The point is that people had built a vocabulary of sanctified exceptions around animals, and Allah sweeps the whole system away with one denial of authorship.

That matters spiritually because false religion often hides in terminology. Once a community names something enough times, repeats it enough times, and inherits it for long enough, the label starts to feel inevitable. Eventually no one asks the first question anymore: **Who gave this name its sacred force?**

So the ayah does that work for us. It takes a hammer to inherited inevitability. Allah did not make these categories. Which means their emotional weight, their social prestige, and their ritual seriousness cannot rescue them. If Allah did not institute them, then no amount of ancestral repetition can turn them into worship.

### 2. **Yaftaruna 'ala Allahi al-kadhiba**: The Problem Is Not Mere Error but Fabrication Against Allah

Then the verse moves from negation to indictment:

**wa lakinna alladhina kafaru yaftaruna 'ala Allahi al-kadhiba**.

But those who disbelieve fabricate lies against Allah.

The verb **yaftaruna** comes from the root **f-r-y**, the language of inventing, cutting out, producing something false and attributing it as though it were real. This is stronger than simple mistake. The ayah is not describing a harmless folk confusion. It is describing the act of assigning religious authority to Allah without warrant from Him.

[PAUSE]

This should sober us. There is a great difference between saying, "I do not know," and saying, "Allah wants this," when He did not say it. The first is ignorance. The second is trespass.

And notice where the lie is aimed:

**'ala Allahi**.

Against Allah. Upon Allah. In His name.

That is what makes fabricated religion so serious. It does not remain a private eccentricity. It borrows divine legitimacy. It recruits the Name of Allah into protecting something Allah never authored.

Then comes the closing diagnosis:

**wa aktharuhum la ya'qilun**.

Most of them do not reason.

The ayah does not say only that they lack transmitted knowledge. It says they are not using **'aql**. The root carries the sense of binding, restraining, tying the mind to what is real. Reason here is not cold abstraction. It is the inward discipline that refuses to let inherited pressure run loose without accountability.

So the verse names two linked failures: they fabricate against Allah, and they do not reason. False sacrality survives where attribution is careless and the mind has stopped asking whether the claim is actually tethered to revelation.

### 3. **Ta'alaw ... ila ma anzala Allahu**: Revelation Is Described as an Upward Return

The next ayah shows how the cure is offered:

**ta'alaw ila ma anzala Allahu wa ila al-rasul**.

Come up to what Allah has sent down and to the Messenger.

The command **ta'alaw** is striking. It is not merely "come." It carries the sense of rise, come upward, move to a higher place. So the verse does not portray revelation as one more opinion inside the marketplace of inherited customs. It portrays revelation as elevation out of a lower settledness.

[PAUSE]

And what are they being called to?

**ma anzala Allahu**.

What Allah sent down.

There is an exquisite contrast here. Their customs are built horizontally through inheritance, tribe, social memory, and repetition. Revelation comes vertically, sent down by Allah. Then the command addressing them is itself upward: come up. The whole ayah becomes a map of true religion. It descends from Allah, then lifts the human being above the gravity of inherited illusion.

And it does not stop with the Book in abstraction.

**wa ila al-rasul**.

And to the Messenger.

Because revelation is not only text to be admired. It is revelation received, explained, embodied, judged, and lived through the Messenger. The ayah refuses the common human escape of wanting "God" at a distance while resisting the concrete form His guidance takes when it enters history.

### 4. **Hasbuna ma wajadna**: The Psychology of Ancestral Sufficiency

Their answer is devastating in its familiarity:

**hasbuna ma wajadna 'alayhi aba'ana**.

Enough for us is what we found our fathers upon.

The key word is **hasbuna**. It means sufficient for us. Enough. We need no more. The door closes there.

They are not asking whether their fathers were right. They are not even defending the custom through evidence. They are defending it through sufficiency. Familiarity itself has become proof.

[PAUSE]

Then the verb:

**wajadna**.

What we found.

That is also revealing. They did not say, "what our fathers proved," or "what our fathers knew from Allah." They said, "what we found." Inherited religion is often protected by the passive authority of arrival. It was already there when we got here. It came furnished. It felt old. It bore the weight of generations. So the soul mistakes prior existence for truth.

The ayah then tears that veil with a question:

**awa law kana aba'uhum la ya'lamuna shay'an wa la yahtadun**.

Even if their fathers knew nothing and were not guided?

Not a little ignorance. **Shay'an**. Anything. And not only absence of information, but absence of direction: **wa la yahtadun**. They were not guided.

That distinction matters. A person may know many inherited details and still not be guided. Another may be sincere in transmission and still lack actual divine orientation. The Quran is saying that ancestry, by itself, cannot guarantee either knowledge or guidance. So why should ancestry alone be enough?

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The Nafs Loves Invented Religion Because It Feels Costless

There is a reason false sacredness keeps reappearing in religious communities.

Invented religion often feels easier than revealed religion.

Why? Because once we make the sacred ourselves, we can shape it to fit our fears, loyalties, class habits, family temperaments, and inherited aesthetics. We can preserve identity without undergoing surrender. We can feel serious without being corrected. We can become fiercely protective of forms that were never hard precisely because they were never sent down to confront us.

[PAUSE]

This happens in every age, even when the surface details change. A community begins treating a cultural instinct as though it were divine law. A family canonizes its habits. A movement wraps its anxieties in moral language. A people inherit a tone, a boundary, a symbolic gesture, or a taboo, and over time the emotional reaction to violating it becomes stronger than the reaction to disobeying Allah plainly. Then revelation arrives and exposes the mismatch.

That exposure feels humiliating, because false sacredness borrows the prestige of piety. It lets us feel devout while staying largely self-authored.

So when the ayah says they fabricated lies against Allah, it is not only condemning an ancient pagan taxonomy. It is unveiling a permanent spiritual temptation: **the temptation to prefer a religion we can inherit, manage, and defend over a revelation that descends with the right to rename our world.**

### Theme 2: Ancestry Is Honored in Islam, but It Is Never Allowed to Replace Guidance

The second disease in the passage is subtler because it feels respectable.

They do not say, "We reject Allah."

They say, "What we found our fathers upon is enough for us."

That is how inherited reverence can become a veil. Love of fathers, loyalty to family, gratitude for transmission, and belonging to a people are all real human goods. Islam does not train us to despise ancestry. But it absolutely refuses to let ancestry become an independent source of sacred truth.

[PAUSE]

That is why the Quran's question is so sharp. Even if their fathers knew nothing and were not guided?

The issue is not whether something is old. The issue is whether it is guided.
The issue is not whether it came from those before us. The issue is whether it came down from Allah.

This is an especially important medicine for the religious soul, because we often fear that examining inherited practice is an act of betrayal. But the ayah teaches the opposite. The real betrayal is to protect our fathers with a claim they themselves could not bear before Allah. The most honorable thing we can do for those who came before us is not to make them infallible. It is to submit with them, and above them, to what Allah sent down.

And this works inwardly too. Every one of us has "fathers" in a broader sense: the inherited scripts of our upbringing, our community reflexes, our default emotional theology, the things we found already waiting for us. Some of those are beautiful. Some are distortions. Revelation does not ask you to hate where you came from. It asks you to stop calling it enough before you have measured it against Allah's word.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs move with devastating clarity.

First Allah denies authorship of the invented sacred categories completely.
Then He names them not as harmless custom but as fabrication against Him.
Then He diagnoses the deeper inner failure: the collapse of disciplined reason.
Then the cure is offered: come upward to what Allah sent down and to the Messenger.
Then the refusal is exposed: inherited sufficiency.
Then the final question breaks the spell: what if those fathers possessed neither knowledge nor guidance?

[PAUSE]

That is the architecture of the passage: false naming, false attribution, failed reason, revealed summons, ancestral defense, exposing question.

And the whole thing leaves us with a difficult mercy. We are not allowed to call something sacred because it is familiar. We are not allowed to protect a practice with inherited warmth if Allah did not authorize it. We are not allowed to hide from revelation inside loyalty to those before us. But precisely in that discomfort there is liberation. The burden of inventing religion is lifted. We do not have to guess what holiness is. We do not have to inherit our way toward Allah blindly. We are called upward to what He actually sent down.

### Questions to Carry

1. What in my religious life feels sacred mainly because I inherited it, not because I know Allah authorized it?
2. Have I ever confused familiarity with proof and ancestry with guidance?
3. When revelation corrects something beloved to my family or community, do I rise toward it or hide behind "this is what we found"?
4. Do I still have enough inner 'aql to ask whether the sacred names governing my life were really given by Allah?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs teach that false religion begins when people give sacred authority to names Allah never gave, then defend those inventions by calling ancestral inheritance sufficient in place of revelation and guidance.**

### Du'a

Allahumma, free us from attributing to You what You did not say, and do not let inherited comfort become a veil between us and what You sent down. Give us the humility to rise when revelation calls, the honesty to examine what we found before us, and the intelligence of heart to distinguish between what is old and what is truly guided. Amin.

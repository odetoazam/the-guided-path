# Will They Not Turn Back?

## Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:72-74)

[ayah:5:72] لَقَدْ كَفَرَ ٱلَّذِينَ قَالُوٓا۟ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ هُوَ ٱلْمَسِيحُ ٱبْنُ مَرْيَمَ ۖ وَقَالَ ٱلْمَسِيحُ يَـٰبَنِىٓ إِسْرَٰٓءِيلَ ٱعْبُدُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ رَبِّى وَرَبَّكُمْ ۖ إِنَّهُۥ مَن يُشْرِكْ بِٱللَّهِ فَقَدْ حَرَّمَ ٱللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَمَأْوَىٰهُ ٱلنَّارُ ۖ وَمَا لِلظَّـٰلِمِينَ مِنْ أَنصَارٍ

[ayah:5:73] لَّقَدْ كَفَرَ ٱلَّذِينَ قَالُوٓا۟ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ ثَالِثُ ثَلَـٰثَةٍ ۘ وَمَا مِنْ إِلَـٰهٍ إِلَّآ إِلَـٰهٌ وَٰحِدٌ ۚ وَإِن لَّمْ يَنتَهُوا۟ عَمَّا يَقُولُونَ لَيَمَسَّنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ مِنْهُمْ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ

[ayah:5:74] أَفَلَا يَتُوبُونَ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَيَسْتَغْفِرُونَهُۥ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ غَفُورٌ رَّحِيمٌ

*Laqad kafara alladhina qalu inna Allaha huwa al-masihu ibnu Maryam. Wa qala al-masihu ya bani Isra'ila u'budu Allaha rabbi wa rabbakum. Innahu man yushrik bi-Allahi faqad harrama Allahu 'alayhi al-jannata wa ma'wahu al-nar. Wa ma lil-zalimina min ansar.*

*Laqad kafara alladhina qalu inna Allaha thalithu thalathah. Wa ma min ilahin illa ilahun wahid. Wa in lam yantahu 'amma yaquluna la-yamassanna alladhina kafaru minhum 'adhabun alim.*

*Afala yatubuna ila Allahi wa yastaghfirunahu. Wa Allahu ghafurun rahim.*

**They have certainly disbelieved who say, "Allah is the Messiah, the son of Mary." While the Messiah said, "O Children of Israel, worship Allah, my Lord and your Lord." Indeed, whoever associates others with Allah - Allah has forbidden Paradise for him, and his refuge is the Fire. And there are not for the wrongdoers any helpers. They have certainly disbelieved who say, "Allah is the third of three." And there is no god except one God. And if they do not desist from what they are saying, there will surely touch those who disbelieve among them a painful punishment. So will they not turn to Allah and seek His forgiveness? And Allah is Forgiving and Merciful.**

## Introduction

There are moments when mercy speaks by removing all fog.

People often imagine mercy only as softness, accommodation, or the refusal to name error too directly. But the Quran teaches a deeper mercy than that. Sometimes mercy is clarity so sharp that it rescues people from the lies they were calling holiness. Sometimes compassion comes in the form of a sentence that refuses to let a ruinous belief keep wearing sacred language.

That is the atmosphere of these ayahs.

The claims are named plainly. Saying "Allah is the Messiah, son of Mary" is kufr. Saying "Allah is the third of three" is kufr. The Quran does not negotiate the center of tawhid. It does not treat theological confusion at this level as a harmless difference in spiritual expression.

[PAUSE]

But then something astonishing happens. Right after naming the disbelief, right after warning of Paradise being forbidden for shirk and punishment touching those who do not desist, the passage asks: **Will they not turn back to Allah and seek His forgiveness?**

That is the brilliance of the sequence.

Clarity without vagueness.
Warning without cruelty.
Boundaries without despair.

The passage does not spare the truth in order to sound compassionate, and it does not close the door after telling the truth. It brings both together. Isa himself is quoted calling to worship Allah alone. Shirk is given its real weight. Then tawbah is offered before the soul can conclude that naming the gravity of its error means it has been abandoned.

The hook of the passage is this: **the Quran's mercy is not that it blurs shirk; it is that after naming shirk fully, it still opens the road back to Allah.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Laqad kafara alladhina qalu**: The Passage Begins with Verdict, Not Ambiguity

Both ayahs 72 and 73 begin almost identically:

**laqad kafara alladhina qalu** - they have certainly disbelieved, those who said...

The structure matters. The verse does not say merely that the claim is mistaken, imprecise, or spiritually unhelpful. It names the claim as kufr.

[PAUSE]

That opening decisiveness tells us something about tawhid: there are truths at the center of the deen that cannot be blurred without tearing the whole structure. If Allah is no longer one in the uncompromising sense the Quran insists on, then the foundation has been breached.

This is why the ayah's clarity is itself mercy. False sacred claims can feel emotionally warm, culturally refined, or devotionally poetic. But if they relocate divinity into what Allah did not authorize, the Quran must break the spell. Mercy does not leave people inside beautiful theological falsehood.

### 2. **Wa qala al-masihu ... u'budu Allaha rabbi wa rabbakum**: Isa Is Brought Forward as the Witness Against the Claim Made in His Name

Then verse 72 does something powerful:

It does not only reject the claim about the Messiah. It brings the Messiah's own speech.

**Wa qala al-masihu** - and the Messiah said:

**u'budu Allaha rabbi wa rabbakum** - worship Allah, my Lord and your Lord.

[PAUSE]

This is beautiful and decisive at once. The very one around whom the excess claim was built is quoted denying the basis of that excess. He is not presented as competitor to Allah, nor as an ambiguous bridge to a softened divinity. He is presented as servant-caller, directing Bani Isra'il upward beyond himself.

And the phrase **rabbi wa rabbakum** is especially humbling.

My Lord and your Lord.

One shared Lord.
One shared servanthood.
No divine exception built into the person of Isa.

So the Quran is not attacking Isa. It is rescuing him from being placed where he himself never placed himself.

### 3. **Man yushrik bi-Allahi faqad harrama Allahu 'alayhi al-jannah**: Shirk Is Named as Catastrophic Loss, Not Minor Error

Then the ayah broadens from the specific claim to the universal principle:

**man yushrik bi-Allahi** - whoever associates partners with Allah.

Not only this group, not only this historical statement, but the whole theological crime of shirk.

[PAUSE]

And then the consequence:

**faqad harrama Allahu 'alayhi al-jannah** - Allah has forbidden Paradise to him.

This is one of the most severe formulations in the Quran because it reveals what shirk really is. It is not merely an intellectual slip. It is the corruption of the highest relationship in existence. If Jannah is the home of rightly ordered worship, then shirk stands in direct contradiction to the very thing for which Jannah is prepared.

Then:

**wa ma'wahu al-nar** - and his refuge is the Fire.

Again the irony is sharp. What was taken as a spiritually elevating claim turns out to be a road into ruin.

And then:

**wa ma lil-zalimina min ansar** - the wrongdoers have no helpers.

Shirk is called **zulm**, putting what belongs to Allah elsewhere. And no helper can save the soul from the consequence of that displacement once Allah's judgment arrives.

### 4. **Wa ma min ilahin illa ilahun wahid ... afala yatubun**: The Ayah Moves from Absolute Oneness to Opened Mercy

Verse 73 names another claim:

**inna Allaha thalithu thalathah** - Allah is the third of three.

And the reply is breathtaking in its simplicity:

**wa ma min ilahin illa ilahun wahid** - there is no god except one God.

[PAUSE]

The sentence is clean, unornamented, impossible to misunderstand. This is how the Quran often answers complexity at the center: by returning to utter clarity.

Then comes the warning:

**wa in lam yantahu 'amma yaquluna la-yamassanna ... 'adhabun alim** - if they do not desist from what they are saying, a painful punishment will surely touch those who disbelieve among them.

The verb **yantahu** matters. Stop. Refrain. Cease the utterance. The passage is not only exposing error; it is demanding that it be abandoned.

And then, after all this:

**afala yatubuna ila Allahi wa yastaghfirunahu** - will they not turn back to Allah and seek His forgiveness?

This is astonishing mercy. The ayah has not softened the gravity of the claim. It has named it as kufr. It has warned of punishment. Yet it still asks, almost with tenderness and astonishment, why they do not come back.

Then the final names:

**wa Allahu ghafurun rahim** - and Allah is Forgiving, Merciful.

The names do not erase the warning. They make return possible after it.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: We Often Confuse Theological Softness with Compassion

Modern people are often tempted to think that strong theological clarity is somehow incompatible with mercy. If you really care, we imagine, you will blur differences, avoid naming falsehood too directly, and preserve emotional gentleness even if doctrinal truth becomes indistinct.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs refuse that assumption.

They are compassionate precisely because they are clear.

If a belief destroys tawhid, then failing to name that destruction is not mercy. It is abandonment. Real mercy tells the truth about what ruins the soul. It does not become polite at the expense of salvation.

So these ayahs teach us to rethink gentleness. Gentleness is not the refusal to speak clearly about Allah. Gentleness is speaking clearly about Allah while still leaving the sinner or mistaken person a real road back.

### Theme 2: Isa Is Honored Most by Being Placed Where He Placed Himself

There is also a profound lesson here about love and excess.

Human beings often imagine that honoring the beloved means raising him beyond his true station. But the Quran's logic is the opposite. Real honor is right placement.

[PAUSE]

Isa is not diminished by being called servant of Allah. He is protected by it.
He is not belittled by saying "my Lord and your Lord." He is ennobled by truthful servanthood.

This matters beyond theology. We often distort what we love by refusing to let it remain where Allah placed it. Reverence without discipline becomes excess. Love without truth becomes corruption.

The Quran saves Isa from the theology built in his name by returning us to his own words.

### Theme 3: The Door of Tawbah Opens Even After Grave Error - But Only Through Honest Renunciation

Verse 74 is one of the most hopeful lines in the whole section, but its hope is not cheap.

The call to tawbah comes after the error has been fully named.
The offer of forgiveness comes after the warning has been stated.

[PAUSE]

This means tawbah is not available through redefining the sin into harmlessness. It is available through turning away from it honestly.

That is an enormous mercy for every sinner.

You do not need Allah to lower the standard before you can return.
You need to stop pretending the standard was not real.

This is true far beyond the specific theological claims here. Every time we soften, rename, or aesthetically dress up what Allah has called ruinous, we move away from tawbah. And every time we let His naming stand, even if it wounds our pride, the road back begins opening.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs move in a stunning order.

First: false claims are named as kufr.
Then: Isa himself is quoted calling to worship Allah alone.
Then: shirk is shown in its final consequence - Paradise barred, Fire as refuge, no helpers.
Then: the core is restated with luminous simplicity - there is no god except one God.
And then, after all of that:

**Will they not turn back?**

[PAUSE]

That order is the mercy.

The Quran does not save by confusion.
It saves by clarity followed by an open door.

So this passage teaches us how Allah deals with grave error: He names it, warns of it, dismantles its false sacredness, and still calls the servant home before the end arrives.

### Questions to Carry

1. Have I ever mistaken theological vagueness for compassion?
2. What loves in my life am I tempted to honor by exaggerating beyond the place Allah gave them?
3. Do I really believe tawbah is possible only after I let Allah's naming of sin stand over me?
4. Where do I need to stop defending an error and simply turn back?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs teach that tawhid must be guarded with full clarity, that Isa himself calls to the worship of Allah alone, and that even after grave theological error the door of tawbah remains open for whoever stops and turns back to Allah.**

### Du'a

Allahumma, keep tawhid clear and beloved in our hearts. Do not let us honor anyone or anything by placing it where only You belong. Make us truthful enough to accept Your naming of error and humble enough to turn back before warning becomes judgment. And let Your names, Al-Ghafur and Al-Rahim, pull us toward sincere tawbah whenever we stray. Amin.

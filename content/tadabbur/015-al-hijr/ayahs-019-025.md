# We Spread the Earth, Brought Forth Everything in Measure, Sent the Winds, and Your Lord Will Gather Them All

## Surah Al-Hijr (15:19-25)

[ayah:15:19] وَٱلْأَرْضَ مَدَدْنَـٰهَا وَأَلْقَيْنَا فِيهَا رَوَٰسِىَ وَأَنۢبَتْنَا فِيهَا مِن كُلِّ شَىْءٍ مَّوْزُونٍ

## Introduction

This passage moves like a widening circle.

It begins with the earth under your feet. Then mountains. Then growth. Then livelihoods. Then hidden treasuries. Then wind and water. Then life and death. Then the knowledge of where people advance and where they fall behind. Then, finally, gathering before Allah.

Nothing here is random background. The surah is not simply listing blessings. It is teaching a worldview: provision is measured, descent is measured, life is measured, death is measured, human movement is known, and return is inevitable. The world is not self-running abundance. It is governed distribution.

The hook of the passage is this: **Al-Hijr teaches that all provision, growth, descent, life, death, timing, and final return move under Allah's measured and wise command, so the visible world is not loose abundance but disciplined mercy.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Min kulli shay'in mawzun ... bi-qadarin ma'lum**: The Passage Repeats the Language of Measure

The earth section says:

**wa anbatna fiha min kulli shay'in mawzun**

We caused to grow in it from every thing that is weighed, measured, proportioned.

[PAUSE]

Then the passage later says:

**wa ma nunazziluhu illa bi-qadarin ma'lum**

We do not send it down except in a known measure.

These two phrases belong together.

**Mawzun** comes from the root **w-z-n**, weight, balance, measure. **Qadar** comes from the root **q-d-r**, measuring out, assigning, proportioning precisely. The surah is not using one casual word for sufficiency and then moving on. It is deliberately returning to the grammar of measuredness.

Growth is measured. Descent is measured.

That means abundance in the Quran is not chaos. Allah's generosity is not disorder. Provision comes in forms, timings, and amounts that are not outside knowledge or wisdom, even when the creature cannot read the pattern from inside the moment.

### 2. **'Indana khaza'inuhu**: What Reaches the World Is Only a Sent Portion of a Hidden Treasury

Then comes one of the most expansive statements in the surah:

**wa in min shay'in illa 'indana khaza'inuhu**

There is not a thing except that with Us are its treasuries.

[PAUSE]

Not some things. Not only food. Not only rain. Not only wealth. **Min shay'**. Anything whatsoever.

Then immediately:

**wa ma nunazziluhu illa bi-qadarin ma'lum**

We do not send it down except in a known measure.

This creates a decisive distinction between **source** and **portion**. What appears in the world is not the treasury itself. It is something sent down from the treasury, in measure, by decree.

That is spiritually important because the soul often mistakes the portion in hand for the whole reality. We see what reached us and assume that is all there was to give, all there was to receive, all there was to be written. The ayah says no. The خزائن are with Allah. What reached you is a measured descent from a source you do not control and cannot exhaust.

### 3. **Wa ma antum lahu bi-khazinina ... wa nahnu al-warithun**: You Benefit From What You Do Not Own

The water verse ends:

**fa-asqaynakumuhu wa ma antum lahu bi-khazinina**

We gave it to you to drink, and you are not its keepers.

[PAUSE]

You consume it. Depend on it. Live by it. But you do not store its real source, own its final treasury, or guarantee its return.

Then the passage widens further:

**wa inna la-nahnu nuhyi wa numitu wa nahnu al-warithun**

Surely We alone give life and cause death, and We are the Inheritors.

This is the same correction in a deeper register. Not only water, but life itself is received without ownership. We live inside gifts we cannot secure permanently. Allah remains after creatures, after holdings, after claims, after generations. That is what **al-warithun** does here. It strips false permanence from the human hand.

Then:

**wa la-qad 'alimna al-mustaqdimina minkum wa la-qad 'alimna al-musta'khirin**

We certainly know those who go ahead among you and those who stay behind.

The passage has now moved from measured provision to measured human placement. Movement itself is known to Allah.

And it lands with:

**wa inna rabbaka huwa yahshuruhum**

Your Lord is surely the One who will gather them.

The measured world ends in gathered accountability.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: One of the Soul's Great Illusions Is Confusing Use With Ownership

We drink water and begin to feel, subtly, that the world is ours.

We live off provision and begin to think of ourselves as stable possessors rather than temporary recipients. We plan careers, stock kitchens, fill accounts, build systems, and before long the heart begins to speak as though access means control.

[PAUSE]

But this passage keeps dismantling that illusion.

You live in the earth Allah spread.

You rely on mountains Allah fixed.

You eat from growth Allah brought out in measure.

You survive on livelihoods Allah made.

You drink water Allah sent and you are not its keeper.

You live by a life Allah gives and lose it by a death Allah sends.

The problem is not using created things. The problem is forgetting their grammar. We are users of gifts, not owners of origins.

### Theme 2: Measured Provision Is Hard for the Anxious Heart, but It Is Also Mercy

The phrase **bi-qadarin ma'lum** can unsettle the anxious person at first.

Because we often want open-ended control. We want infinite access, immediate arrival, no delay, no thinning, no uncertainty. But the surah keeps saying: measured, known, assigned, sent down.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this is to notice that measure is not merely restriction. It is also what makes a world livable. Too much rain destroys. Too little destroys. Too much wind destroys. Too little stagnates. Too much exposure crushes. Too little leaves the heart asleep.

Measuredness is not the opposite of mercy. Often it is mercy given form.

That does not make deprivation easy. But it does mean the believer tries to suffer scarcity without imagining that scarcity proves absence of خزائن with Allah. The treasury is not empty because the present portion is painful. The خزائن are still with Him. What reaches us is what He sends, in what He knows, under what He knows.

[PAUSE]

## Closing Synthesis

This passage begins with the earth and ends with gathering.

In between, it teaches a single governing truth from many directions: Allah is the One who measures what appears, what grows, what descends, what is drunk, who lives, who dies, who advances, who lags, and who will finally be gathered.

So the world is not a field of self-generated possession. It is a field of assigned portion.

The earth under us, the water in us, the life animating us, and the place we occupy among others all belong to a wisdom deeper than our immediate reading of events.

### Questions to Carry

- Where have you been speaking like an owner when you are really a recipient?
- What present portion in your life has made you forget the خزائن are still with Allah?
- Can you receive measuredness as mercy without denying the ache of limitation?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs teach that the visible world is a measured descent from Allah's hidden treasuries, so provision, life, death, and return all belong to His wise and knowing command rather than to our illusion of ownership.**

### Du'a

*Allahumma, teach us the grammar of Your gifts. Do not let us mistake use for ownership, portion for treasury, or temporary access for control. Give us trust in what You measure out, gratitude for what You send, and readiness for the day You gather us back to You.*

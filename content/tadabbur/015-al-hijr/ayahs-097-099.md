# We Know Your Chest Tightens, So Glorify, Prostrate, and Worship Until the Certainty Comes

## Surah Al-Hijr (15:97-99)

[ayah:15:97] وَلَقَدْ نَعْلَمُ أَنَّكَ يَضِيقُ صَدْرُكَ بِمَا يَقُولُونَ

[ayah:15:98] فَسَبِّحْ بِحَمْدِ رَبِّكَ وَكُن مِّنَ ٱلسَّـٰجِدِينَ

[ayah:15:99] وَٱعْبُدْ رَبَّكَ حَتَّىٰ يَأْتِيَكَ ٱلْيَقِينُ

*Wa la-qad na'lamu annaka yadiqu sadruka bima yaqulun.*

*Fa-sabbih bi-hamdi rabbika wa kun mina al-sajidin.*

*Wa'bud rabbaka hatta ya'tiyaka al-yaqin.*

**And We certainly know that your chest tightens because of what they say. So glorify with praise of your Lord and be among those who prostrate. And worship your Lord until the certainty comes to you.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

The surah does not end by telling the Prophet to become less human.

It does not say: their words should not touch you.

It does not say: if your tawakkul were stronger, you would feel nothing.

[PAUSE]

Instead Allah names the pain with exquisite mercy. We know your chest tightens because of what they say.

That recognition alone is tenderness. The hurt is neither denied nor mocked. But the passage does not leave the Messenger inside constriction. It gives him a way through it: tasbih, praise, sujud, and continuous worship until certainty arrives.

The hook of the passage is this: **Al-Hijr teaches that constriction of the chest is not healed by arguing endlessly with the voices that caused it, but by turning the burden into worship until the heart is reopened before Allah.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Yadiqu Sadruka**: The Quran Names Spiritual Pain as Constriction

Allah says:

**wa la-qad na'lamu annaka yadiqu sadruka bima yaqulun**

We certainly know that your chest tightens because of what they say.

[PAUSE]

The expression is embodied. **Yadiqu** from the root of narrowness and constriction. **Sadr**: the chest, the inner front of the self, the place where breath, courage, patience, and emotional bearing are felt.

This is not abstract sadness. It is compression. Reduced space. Difficulty carrying what one is carrying.

And notice the tenderness before the diagnosis: **na'lamu**. We know. Allah begins by informing the Messenger that his interior state is fully seen. The one carrying revelation is not unseen by the Lord who sent it.

### 2. **Fa-Sabbih bi-Hamdi Rabbika**: Praise Is Given as an Exit From Pressure

Then the ayah turns immediately:

**fa-sabbih bi-hamdi rabbika**

So glorify with praise of your Lord.

[PAUSE]

The command is not random consolation. **Tasbih** clears Allah of every deficiency. **Hamd** fills the tongue and heart with grateful praise. Together they reposition the servant inside a truer reality.

The constricted self becomes trapped inside what people said. Tasbih breaks that enclosure. It returns the heart to the Lord who is untouched by their distortions, who remains perfect, praiseworthy, and sovereign even while the servant feels pressed by human speech.

### 3. **Kun mina al-Sajidin ... Hatta Ya'tiyaka al-Yaqin**: Bowing Low Is the Long Road to Stability

Then:

**wa kun mina al-sajidin**

Be among those who prostrate.

And finally:

**wa'bud rabbaka hatta ya'tiyaka al-yaqin**

Worship your Lord until the certainty comes to you.

[PAUSE]

Sujud is the body's deepest enacted humility. The face goes down, the ego lowers, the servant comes nearest in helplessness and devotion.

Then the horizon widens all the way to **al-yaqin**. In the classical tafsir of this verse, it means death, the certainty that unquestionably comes. The command is not temporary stress management. It is a life-program: worship until the end.

The surah closes not with a promised date of relief, but with a mode of fidelity. Keep worshipping until certainty arrives.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The Heart Often Tries to Heal Constriction by Staying Inside the Wound

When people say painful things, the soul often circles the injury.

Replay the sentence.

Rehearse the insult.

Imagine the rebuttal.

Return to the scene.

Search for some wording that would finally make the pain close.

[PAUSE]

But that keeps the chest inside the very speech that constricted it. The ayah offers another movement. Not suppression, but redirection. Allah does not say the pain is fake. He says: I know. Then He commands worship.

This means one of the deepest treatments for inner tightness is not endless inward rumination, but a return to the Lord through praise, prayer, and lowered nearness. The wound is carried upward rather than merely turned over inwardly.

### Theme 2: The Quran Does Not Promise the Worker a Finish Line in Dunya, Only Faithfulness Until the End

The final command of the surah is astonishingly spare:

**wa'bud rabbaka hatta ya'tiyaka al-yaqin**

[PAUSE]

Not: worship until they stop mocking.

Not: worship until the mission becomes easy.

Not: worship until you see full outward victory.

Worship until certainty comes.

This is one of the most stabilizing commands in the Quran. It teaches the servant that success is not surviving every emotional difficulty without feeling it. Success is remaining in worship until life ends.

That rescues us from a thousand false expectations. We stop bargaining with obedience. We stop telling ourselves, "I will stay faithful until this person changes, until this environment softens, until this burden passes." The surah closes that door. Worship is not tied to improved conditions. It is tied to the Lord.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask what words have been tightening your chest lately, and whether you have been trying to heal that pressure by thinking harder inside it rather than by returning more fully to tasbih, sujud, and steady worship.

## Closing Synthesis

Allah tells His Messenger that He fully knows the constriction caused by what the people say. Then He gives no argument back to the mockers, but a path for the heart: glorify with praise, be among those who prostrate, and worship your Lord until certainty comes.

[PAUSE]

The surah ends where the whole life must end: not with the disappearance of opposition, but with uninterrupted servanthood. Constriction is acknowledged, worship is prescribed, and the road stretches all the way to death.

### Questions to Carry

- What words or environments have been narrowing your chest lately?
- When you feel inwardly compressed, do you mostly replay the wound or return to worship?
- Have you been waiting for easier conditions before giving Allah a steadier form of devotion?
- What would change if you accepted that the assignment is not temporary emotional relief, but worship until certainty comes?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**This closing passage teaches that when the chest tightens under the speech of people, the believer is invited not into retreat from worship but deeper into praise, prostration, and lifelong servanthood until death.**

### Du'a

*Allahumma, You know every constriction inside our chests before we can explain it. Open us through Your remembrance, anchor us in sujud, and do not let the speech of people pull us away from steady worship. Let us meet the certainty while still bowed to You.*

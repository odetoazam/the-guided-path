# Even If a Gate Were Opened in the Sky, They Would Still Say: Our Eyes Have Been Dazzled

## Surah Al-Hijr (15:10-15)

## Introduction

The Prophet is not being told here merely that opposition exists. He is being told that ridicule has a history, and that history has a pattern.

The opening of Al-Hijr already named the Quran clear and named long hope as one of the great sedatives of denial. Now the surah adds another consolation and another warning. Consolation for the Messenger: no messenger came to earlier peoples except that they mocked him too. Warning for the listener: once rejection hardens into a criminal interior pattern, even overwhelming signs do not necessarily heal it.

That is why this passage ends with one of the most dramatic hypotheticals in the Quran. If a gate were opened in the sky and they were allowed to keep ascending through it, they still would not surrender. They would reinterpret the experience rather than let the experience reinterpret them.

The hook of the passage is this: **Al-Hijr teaches that some hearts do not fail because the signs were weak, but because ridicule, criminal habit, and refusal have become so settled that even a heavenly opening would be explained away rather than obeyed.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Fi shiya'i al-awwalin**: Rejection Travels in Camps, Not Just Individuals

The passage opens:

**wa la-qad arsalna min qablika fi shiya'i al-awwalin**

We certainly sent before you among the factions of the earlier peoples.

[PAUSE]

The word **shiya'** comes from the root **sh-y-ʿ**, which points to groups, parties, camps, aligned followings. The Messenger is being told that revelation did not only meet isolated skeptics. It entered worlds already socially arranged into loyalties, inherited solidarities, and shared resistance.

That matters because mockery often feels personal to the one receiving it. But the ayah widens the frame. This is not simply about one person disliking one messenger. It is about entrenched camps receiving a truth that threatens the emotional order of the camp.

Then the next ayah makes the pattern explicit:

**wa ma ya'tihim min rasulin illa kanu bihi yastahzi'un**

No messenger came to them except that they used to mock him.

The verb **yastahzi'un** is Form X, the form of active taking-up of ridicule. They are not accidentally amused. Mockery becomes a chosen posture, a repeated style of response, a way of reducing the messenger so the self does not have to be reduced in humility before the message.

### 2. **Naslukuhu fi qulub al-mujrimin**: Some Rejection Becomes Threaded Into the Interior

Then the surah says:

**kadhalika naslukuhu fi qulub al-mujrimin**

Thus We make it pass into the hearts of the criminals.

[PAUSE]

The key verb is **naslukuhu** from the root **s-l-k**: to make something enter, travel through, pass along a path, be threaded into a course.

The ayah is describing more than momentary contact. Something is being run into the interior world of the **mujrimin**. Into hearts already marked by criminality, not innocent confusion. The next ayah clarifies the outcome:

**la yu'minuna bihi**

they do not believe in it.

So whatever passes into them does not soften them into receptivity. It meets an already corrupted moral chamber. The point is not that Allah wrongs them. The point is that revelation enters a heart whose prior chosen state changes what the encounter becomes.

Then:

**wa qad khalat sunnatu al-awwalin**

the pattern of the earlier peoples has already passed.

**Sunnah** here is not casual repetition. It is an established way, a recurring law, a discernible pattern in history. The surah is saying: this has happened before, enough times that it is now readable.

### 3. **Fa-zallu fihi ya'rujun**: Even a Heavenly Opening Would Be Explained Away

Then the ayah gives one of the most astonishing hypotheticals in revelation:

**wa law fatahna 'alayhim baban min al-sama'i fa-zallu fihi ya'rujun**

even if We opened for them a gate from the sky and they kept ascending through it.

[PAUSE]

This is not a fleeting flash. Not a rumor. Not a secondhand report. A **bab** from the sky opened. A sustained ascent. The verb **ya'rujun** suggests rising upward, ascending step after step.

And what would they say?

**innama sukkirat absaruna bal nahnu qawmun mashurun**

Our eyes have only been dazzled, blocked over, confounded. Rather, we are a people under sorcery.

The first explanation is internal malfunction: our perception has been tampered with. The second is external manipulation: we have been bewitched.

In both cases, the sign is not allowed to stand as sign. They preserve the self's prior refusal by redesigning the explanation.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: People Usually Do Not See Only With Their Eyes; They See Through a Moral Posture

This passage is deeply unsettling because it tells us that sight itself is not enough.

We often fantasize that if only the proof were stronger, if only the sign were bigger, if only Allah made the truth impossible to miss, then surrender would become easy and universal.

[PAUSE]

But the ayahs say otherwise. The problem may not be access to evidence. The problem may be the moral and emotional posture through which evidence is received. A heart trained in ridicule can turn even amazement into dismissal. A self invested in not yielding can reinterpret the most disruptive event so that the ego stays in charge.

This should make us gentler in how we talk about guidance and far more serious about our own inward honesty. The crucial question is not only: have I seen enough? It is also: what kind of self is doing the seeing?

### Theme 2: Mockery Is Often the Last Defense Before Obedience Would Cost Too Much

No messenger came except they mocked him.

That sentence tells us something about the psychology of ridicule. Mockery is not just entertainment. It is often a defensive technology. It shrinks what threatens you so that you do not have to reckon with its claim.

[PAUSE]

When the truth would cost pride, inheritance, social belonging, or the internal story you tell about yourself, mockery becomes cheaper than repentance. It lets a person feel superior while standing in front of a truth they are actually afraid might be real.

That is why the surah joins mockery to history, to criminal hearts, and to impossible signs explained away. Ridicule is not always light. Sometimes it is the final mask worn by a frightened ego before the point at which surrender would reorder everything.

[PAUSE]

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs comfort the Prophet by telling him that he stands in the long company of mocked messengers.

But they also expose something severe about the human soul. Once rejection is socially normalized, inwardly threaded, and morally chosen, the issue is no longer merely the absence of proof.

The passage moves from camps of earlier peoples, to repeated ridicule of every messenger, to a criminal inward state, to a settled historical pattern, and finally to a hypothetical sign so overwhelming that it should end all argument. Yet even there, the deniers still protect themselves by reinterpreting the experience.

The message is not that signs do not matter. It is that signs meet souls, and the condition of the soul matters enormously.

### Questions to Carry

- Where are you tempted to use irony or dismissal to avoid submitting to something you suspect is true?
- Have you been asking Allah for clearer signs while quietly protecting the habits that would reinterpret those signs anyway?
- What kind of heart is currently doing your seeing?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs teach that when ridicule and criminal refusal settle deeply enough in the heart, even an opened gate in the sky will be explained away rather than obeyed.**

### Du'a

*Allahumma, do not let mockery become our shield against truths we fear will humble us. Purify the heart that sees, so that when Your signs come, we receive them as guidance and not as material for one more excuse.*

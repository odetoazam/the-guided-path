# Proclaim What You Are Commanded; We Are Sufficient for You Against the Mockers

## Surah Al-Hijr (15:94-96)

[ayah:15:94] فَٱصْدَعْ بِمَا تُؤْمَرُ وَأَعْرِضْ عَنِ ٱلْمُشْرِكِينَ

[ayah:15:95] إِنَّا كَفَيْنَـٰكَ ٱلْمُسْتَهْزِءِينَ

[ayah:15:96] ٱلَّذِينَ يَجْعَلُونَ مَعَ ٱللَّهِ إِلَـٰهًا ءَاخَرَ ۚ فَسَوْفَ يَعْلَمُونَ

*Fa-sda' bima tu'maru wa a'rid 'ani al-mushrikin.*

*Inna kafaynaka al-mustahzi'in.*

*Alladhina yaj'aluna ma'a Allahi ilahan akhar fa-sawfa ya'lamun.*

**So proclaim what you are commanded and turn away from the polytheists. Indeed, We are sufficient for you against the mockers, those who set up another god with Allah. They will soon know.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

There is a point at which carrying the truth quietly is no longer what obedience requires.

The heart would prefer to remain careful, measured, strategic, less exposed. But revelation reaches a moment when caution itself can become disobedience, and the messenger must step into the public weight of saying what Allah commanded him to say.

[PAUSE]

That is the threshold of this passage. The surah has named the Quran, exposed those who fracture it, and sworn that they will be questioned. Now the Prophet is not left in suspense. He is told exactly what to do: break open with what you have been commanded. Turn away from the polytheists. We are sufficient for you against the mockers.

The hook of the passage is this: **Al-Hijr teaches that open proclamation of truth becomes possible when the servant stops making the mockers the axis of his emotional world and learns to move under Allah's sufficiency instead.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Fa-Sda'**: Truth Sometimes Must Be Brought Into the Open With Forceful Clarity

The command begins:

**fa-sda' bima tu'mar**

So proclaim what you are commanded.

[PAUSE]

The root **s-d-'** carries the sense of splitting, breaking open, cleaving apart. The command is vivid. It is not merely "mention it" or "suggest it." There is an outwardness, a bursting into openness, a refusal to keep the truth sealed inside private caution.

That does not mean recklessness or lack of wisdom. It means that revelation is not indefinitely negotiable around people's sensitivities. There comes a stage in obedience where clarity itself is worship.

And the phrase **bima tu'mar** matters too. He is not told to proclaim himself, his preferences, or a self-authored message. He is told to proclaim what he has been commanded. The courage of da'wah is grounded in the fact that the truth is not ours to invent or soften beyond recognition.

### 2. **Wa A'rid 'Ani al-Mushrikin**: Do Not Let Opposition Set Your Rhythm

Then:

**wa a'rid 'ani al-mushrikin**

And turn away from the polytheists.

[PAUSE]

This does not cancel proclamation. It protects proclamation from being swallowed by reactive entanglement.

The root of **a'rid** signals turning aside, not giving oneself over to fruitless orbit. The idolaters are not to become the center of the Messenger's emotional management. He is to deliver what he must deliver, but not let their resistance dictate the internal posture of his mission.

Many sincere people lose strength not because they lack truth, but because they become over-occupied with the people resisting truth. The ayah breaks that orbit.

### 3. **Inna Kafaynaka al-Mustahzi'in**: Allah Does Not Always Remove Mockery, but He Does Take Responsibility for It

Then comes one of the surah's most intimate assurances:

**inna kafaynaka al-mustahzi'in**

Indeed, We are sufficient for you against the mockers.

[PAUSE]

The verb **kafayna** from **k-f-y** is enoughness, sufficiency, adequacy. Allah does not first say, "They are harmless," or "You will not feel this." He says: We are enough for you regarding them.

And who are these mockers? The next ayah names them as people of shirk:

**alladhina yaj'aluna ma'a Allahi ilahan akhar**

those who set up another god with Allah.

This is crucial. Their mockery is not just social rudeness. It emerges from a theological corruption. Once the heart is comfortable assigning rivals beside Allah, it becomes unsurprising that it also treats Allah's messenger with ridicule.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Mockery Gains Power When We Start Living Before It

One reason ridicule wounds so deeply is that people begin imagining their whole obedience in front of hostile eyes.

How will this sound?

How will this be received?

What will people say?

How much reputation will this cost?

[PAUSE]

Once that gaze settles over the heart, proclamation shrinks. A person starts editing truth, delaying truth, softening truth into something that no longer breaks open anything.

But this passage relocates the believer's field of vision. Proclaim what you are commanded. Turn away from the polytheists. We are sufficient for you against the mockers. In other words: do not stand before them while obeying Me. Stand before Me while they speak.

That is a whole liberation. The mockers are real, but they are demoted. They are no longer the court before which faithfulness is performed.

### Theme 2: Tawhid Produces Courage Because It Ends the Illusion of Rival Powers

The mockers are described as those who make with Allah another god.

That is not incidental description. It reveals the deep structure of fear. People lose courage when the world fills up with rival powers whose reactions feel decisive: audience, boss, crowd, elite, state, family, platform, market, reputation.

[PAUSE]

But tawhid keeps collapsing these false ultimates. If Allah is enough, then no created reaction can finally decide the value of obedience. This does not remove pain, exposure, or slander. It removes their god-status.

That is why the ayah can combine public proclamation with interior steadiness. The servant does not become brave because mockery disappears. He becomes brave because shirk has been unmasked and sufficiency has been reassigned to Allah.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask whose reaction has become large enough in your life to make you hesitant about an obedience that is already clear. What truth are you still carrying privately because you are living too much before the mockers and too little before the One who said, "We are sufficient for you"?

## Closing Synthesis

Allah commands His Messenger to break open with what he has been commanded, to turn aside from the polytheists, assures him that He is sufficient against the mockers, then identifies those mockers by the deeper crime from which their ridicule grows: setting up another god with Allah.

[PAUSE]

The sequence is exact and healing: proclaim, do not orbit, trust sufficiency, remember tawhid. The heart becomes able to speak openly when it stops treating human reactions as rival lords.

### Questions to Carry

- What obedience have you delayed because you are imagining it too intensely before other people's reactions?
- Where has mockery, dismissal, or anticipated embarrassment made you carry truth too privately?
- Do you really believe Allah can be sufficient for you against the people whose opinions most unsettle you?
- Which created powers have quietly taken on god-size weight in your emotional world?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**This passage teaches that truth is proclaimed with courage when the servant stops revolving around the mockers and starts moving under Allah's enoughness.**

### Du'a

*Allahumma, make us people who proclaim what You command without turning Your truth into something timid and hidden. Free our hearts from captivity to mockery, make tawhid larger than every audience, and let Your sufficiency steady us whenever obedience becomes exposed.*

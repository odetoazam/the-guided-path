# We Placed Constellations in the Sky, Beautified It, and Guarded It From Every Banished Shaytan

## Surah Al-Hijr (15:16-18)

[ayah:15:16] وَلَقَدْ جَعَلْنَا فِى ٱلسَّمَآءِ بُرُوجًا وَزَيَّنَّـٰهَا لِلنَّـٰظِرِينَ

[ayah:15:17] وَحَفِظْنَـٰهَا مِن كُلِّ شَيْطَـٰنٍ رَّجِيمٍ

[ayah:15:18] إِلَّا مَنِ ٱسْتَرَقَ ٱلسَّمْعَ فَأَتْبَعَهُۥ شِهَابٌ مُّبِينٌ

*Wa la-qad ja'alna fi al-sama'i burujan wa zayyannaha li al-nazirin.*

*Wa hafiznaha min kulli shaytanin rajim.*

*Illa mani istaraqa al-sam'a fa-atba'ahu shihabun mubin.*

**And We have certainly placed constellations in the sky and beautified it for those who look, and We have guarded it from every banished devil, except one who steals a hearing, and then a clear flaming trail follows him.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

The surah has just shown us a people who would explain away even an opened gate in the sky.

Now Allah directs the gaze upward anyway.

But the sky here is not given merely as scenery. It is architecture, beauty, and protection all at once. It is adorned for human looking, yet also guarded against rebellious access. What looks open from below is not ownerless. What looks beautiful is also governed. What appears silent is not unsecured.

The hook of the passage is this: **Al-Hijr teaches that the sky is both a sign for contemplation and a guarded domain under Allah's rule, so that even beauty above us is never independent of order, protection, and limit.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Burujan ... wa zayyannaha li al-nazirin**: The Sky Is Made to Be Looked At, Not Merely Passed Under

The first ayah says:

**wa la-qad ja'alna fi al-sama'i burujan wa zayyannaha li al-nazirin**

We have certainly placed in the sky constellations and beautified it for those who look.

[PAUSE]

The word **buruj** carries the sense of elevated structures, great visible stations, towering placements. In the sky it points to the great marked formations above, the ordered visible architecture that the eye can trace.

Then comes:

**wa zayyannaha**

and We beautified it.

The sky is not described as accidentally pleasing. Its beauty is assigned. Intended. Given.

And crucially:

**li al-nazirin**

for those who look.

The ayah does not say merely "for all people" in the abstract. It names the ones who actually direct the gaze. The sky is decorated for the looking human being. That means the problem is often not that the signs are absent, but that the gaze is inattentive. There is beauty above us that only becomes spiritually active when someone actually looks as a looker.

### 2. **Wa hafiznaha**: Beauty Above Does Not Mean Openness Without Guard

Then the passage pivots:

**wa hafiznaha min kulli shaytanin rajim**

and We guarded it from every banished devil.

[PAUSE]

The same sky that was just beautified is now described through **hifz**: guarding, preserving, protecting from intrusion.

And the intruder named is:

**shaytanin rajim**

a shaytan who is **rajim**. Stoned away, driven off, banished, repelled.

This matters because the ayah refuses a sentimental reading of the heavens. The sky is beautiful, yes, but it is not unruled. Not casually penetrable. It belongs to an order under Allah's command. The one who tries to enter unlawfully is not a curious observer. He is an expelled rebel.

### 3. **Istaraqa al-sam'a ... fa-atba'ahu shihabun mubin**: Illicit Access Is Brief and Immediately Pursued

Then comes the exception:

**illa mani istaraqa al-sam'a**

except one who steals a hearing.

[PAUSE]

The verb **istaraqa** is Form VIII from the root **s-r-q**, to steal. That matters. This is not legitimate access. Not granted hearing. Not invited reception. It is furtive theft.

And what is stolen is not the whole unseen. It is:

**al-sam'**

the hearing. A snatched fragment, not a rightful possession.

Then immediately:

**fa-atba'ahu shihabun mubin**

then a clear flaming trail follows him.

The response is immediate pursuit. The stolen hearing does not become stable ownership. The flame follows. The sky remains a guarded domain even in the moment of attempted theft.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The World Above Us Is Meant to Awaken Attention, Not Casual Familiarity

Modern people live under the sky constantly and often almost never really see it.

We move from roof to roof, screen to screen, forecast to forecast. The heavens become weather data, background color, astronomical content, or nothing at all. But this ayah gives us back the sky as deliberate beauty for the one who looks.

[PAUSE]

That means one of the losses of heedlessness is not only moral dullness. It is perceptual dullness. You can lose access to wonder without losing access to eyesight. You can retain vision while no longer being among **al-nazirin** in the Quranic sense.

And when that happens, beauty stops functioning as sign. Not because Allah withheld it, but because we ceased to meet it with attention.

### Theme 2: Not Every Access to Hidden Things Is Noble

This passage is also a correction for another human temptation: the desire to access what is hidden without discipline, permission, or humility.

The devil here does not patiently receive. He steals hearing.

[PAUSE]

That matters beyond the unseen world itself. Spiritually, we are often tempted by shortcut-forms of knowing. The urge to bypass submission and still gain access. To seize insight without purification. To want secrets more than obedience. To be fascinated by hidden things while neglecting what Allah has already made plain.

The ayah warns that illicit access is not honor. It is theft. And theft in a guarded world is followed by pursuit.

[PAUSE]

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs give the sky two meanings at once.

It is beautified for those who look.

And it is guarded from rebellious intrusion.

So the heavens are neither mute decoration nor spiritually neutral space. They are a field of signs for attentive human beings and, at the same time, a protected domain whose boundaries are enforced by Allah.

The one who looks properly is invited into wonder. The one who tries to steal what was not given is driven back.

### Questions to Carry

- When was the last time you looked upward as one of **al-nazirin**, not just as someone passing under the sky?
- Where in your life are you trying to seize insight instead of receiving what Allah has already made clear?
- Has beauty in creation become spiritually active for you, or merely familiar?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs teach that the sky is a beautified sign for attentive hearts and a guarded realm under Allah's rule, so wonder and boundary meet above us in the same creation.**

### Du'a

*Allahumma, make us people who really look at Your signs and do not pass beneath them asleep. Give us reverence before what You have guarded, gratitude for what You have beautified, and hearts content to receive what You open rather than to grasp for what You did not give.*

# I Will Beautify It for Them in the Earth, Except Your Purified Servants

## Surah Al-Hijr (15:39-42)

[ayah:15:39] قَالَ رَبِّ بِمَآ أَغْوَيْتَنِى لَأُزَيِّنَنَّ لَهُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَلَأُغْوِيَنَّهُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ

[ayah:15:40] إِلَّا عِبَادَكَ مِنْهُمُ ٱلْمُخْلَصِينَ

[ayah:15:41] قَالَ هَـٰذَا صِرَٰطٌ عَلَىَّ مُسْتَقِيمٌ

[ayah:15:42] إِنَّ عِبَادِى لَيْسَ لَكَ عَلَيْهِمْ سُلْطَـٰنٌ إِلَّا مَنِ ٱتَّبَعَكَ مِنَ ٱلْغَاوِينَ

*Qala rabbi bima aghwaytani la-uzayyinnanna lahum fi al-ardi wa la-ughwiyannahum ajma'in.*

*Illa 'ibadaka minhum al-mukhlasin.*

*Qala hadha siratun 'alayya mustaqim.*

*Inna 'ibadi laysa laka 'alayhim sultanun illa man ittaba'aka min al-ghawin.*

**He said: My Lord, because You let me fall into error, I will surely make things appear beautiful to them in the earth, and I will surely mislead them all together, except Your servants among them, the purified ones. He said: This is a straight path leading to Me. Surely My servants, you have no authority over them, except those who follow you from among the deviating.**

## Introduction

Some forms of evil are loud.

They announce themselves with threat, mockery, or direct pressure.

But some of the most devastating forms of evil do something quieter first. They decorate. They reframe. They make a wrong thing feel fitting, tasteful, necessary, overdue, or even beautiful.

[PAUSE]

That is where this passage goes after the curse of Iblis. He has been expelled and reprieved, and now he tells us exactly what he plans to do with that borrowed time. He will not usually drag people by force. He will work on perception. He will alter the felt appearance of things in the earth until misguidance becomes attractive from the inside.

And then Allah places the boundary. The threat is real, but it is not absolute. The whisper is serious, but it is not sovereignty. There remains a class of servants he cannot own.

The hook of the passage is this: **Al-Hijr teaches that Shaytan's deepest strategy is not raw compulsion but beautification of falsehood, yet even that strategy stops at the edge of Allah's purified servants and never becomes true authority unless a person follows him.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. He Turns His Own Story Into a Program for Others

Iblis begins:

**bima aghwaytani ... wa la-ughwiyannahum**

Because You let me fall into error, I will surely mislead them.

[PAUSE]

The same root returns on both sides of the sentence: **g-w-y**. First in his description of himself, then in his vow toward humanity.

This is psychologically devastating. He takes the story he tells about his own downfall and turns it into a mission. What he claims about himself becomes what he wants for everyone else.

This is often how corruption spreads among people as well. A person refuses to repent of a distortion and eventually begins recruiting others into it, because shared darkness feels like relief. If others descend too, then maybe my own descent will feel less like ruin.

The ayah lets us overhear that logic at its origin. Iblis does not simply say he will harm them. He says, in effect: what I am, I will now work to reproduce.

### 2. **La-Uzayyinnanna Lahum**: Misguidance Begins by Becoming Attractive

Then comes the key verb:

**la-uzayyinnanna lahum fi al-ard**

I will surely beautify for them in the earth.

[PAUSE]

The root **z-y-n** is about adornment, beauty, embellishment, making something appear attractive. In this intensified form, the promise is not casual suggestion. It is active beautification.

This is one of the Quran's most important insights into temptation. Falsehood often does not first arrive looking ugly. If it looked ugly from the start, many fewer people would touch it. It arrives with styling. It borrows emotional lighting. It puts on the clothes of relief, freedom, sophistication, realism, self-protection, or justice.

And notice the setting: **fi al-ard**. In the earth. In lived human life. In the place of appetite, trade, status, visibility, rivalry, loneliness, family pressure, bodily need, and public image. Temptation is not described here as a purely abstract whisper. It works inside earthly conditions.

That is why so many sins feel reasonable before they feel sinful. The beauty-work came first.

### 3. **Al-Mukhlasin** and **Laysa Laka 'Alayhim Sultan**: The Threat Is Real, but It Has Limits

Iblis says he will mislead them all,

**illa 'ibadaka minhum al-mukhlasin**

except Your servants among them, the purified ones.

[PAUSE]

The wording is precious. It does not say merely "the sincere" in a purely self-generated sense. It says **al-mukhlasin**: those whom Allah has purified, selected, singled out, made clean for Himself. Their safety is not self-authored heroism. It is divine protection meeting real servanthood.

Then Allah answers:

**inna 'ibadi laysa laka 'alayhim sultan**

My servants, you have no authority over them.

This is one of the most liberating lines in the Quran's theology of Shaytan. The ayah does not deny that he whispers. It does not deny that he beautifies. It does not deny that he stalks earthly life. But it denies him **sultan** over Allah's true servants.

**Sultan** is not a passing suggestion. It is authority, command, compelling leverage, an enforceable mastery. The passage refuses to grant him that.

And then comes the exception:

**illa man ittaba'aka min al-ghawin**

except the one who follows you from among the deviating.

That is the crucial moral line. He does not possess autonomous sovereignty over human beings. His avenue opens where following opens.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The First Battle Is Often Over Appearance, Not Decision

Most people do not wake up wanting ruin.

They wake up wanting relief. Vindication. Attention. Escape. Tenderness. Control. A little beauty in a hard day. A justification for the resentment they are tired of carrying. A story that makes the self look less guilty.

[PAUSE]

That is why beautification is such an effective strategy. Before the hand moves, the eye has often already been trained to see wrongly. Before the decision is made, the soul has often already been taught to admire what should have repelled it.

Something once obviously indecent becomes expressive.

Something once clearly dishonest becomes strategic.

Something once plainly arrogant becomes confidence.

Something once morally compromising becomes just "how the world works."

[PAUSE]

This is not only about spectacular sins. It reaches into speech, ambition, relationships, online life, self-presentation, religious performance, and private fantasy. The heart is constantly receiving proposals about what should look appealing. Some come from truth. Some are beautified poison.

So a major part of taqwa is not only asking, "Is this allowed?" but also asking, "Who taught my heart to find this beautiful?" That question exposes a deeper layer of the struggle.

### Theme 2: Shaytan's Power Grows Where Followership Begins

These ayahs are simultaneously sobering and hopeful.

They are sobering because the threat is real. Iblis has a strategy. He understands the earth. He works through appearance. He is committed.

But they are hopeful because Allah names a hard limit. There is no independent **sultan** over Allah's servants.

[PAUSE]

That means the believer should neither trivialize Shaytan nor mythologize him. He is not nothing, and he is not a rival god. He is an enemy who whispers, decorates, pressures, and exploits openings. But he does not create the servant's final allegiance out of thin air.

The opening appears when a person begins to follow. That followership can be dramatic, but often it starts in small permissions: let me enjoy the thought a little longer; let me keep the resentment warm; let me narrate the wrong as right; let me call this compromise maturity; let me stay near the edge because I like how it feels.

And the other side of that truth is deeply consoling. If his access widens through following, then it narrows through refusal, repentance, dhikr, humility, and seeking Allah's protection. We are not trapped in a universe where the whisper automatically becomes the deed. The ayah restores moral seriousness without surrendering hope.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this is to realize that many of our spiritual battles are not lost when temptation appears. They are lost when we begin cooperating with its narration. The suggestion arrives. Then we polish it. Defend it. Personalize it. Build a case for it. That is where followership begins to take shape.

## Closing Synthesis

In these ayahs, Iblis responds to his curse by blaming Allah for his own fall, vowing to beautify misguidance within earthly life and to mislead all he can. He himself concedes an exception: Allah's purified servants. And Allah answers by setting the boundary clearly. There is a straight path under His authority, and Shaytan has no **sultan** over Allah's servants except those who choose to follow him among the deviating.

[PAUSE]

The architecture is both severe and merciful. Severe, because the strategy of beautification is real and we are naive if we ignore it. Merciful, because the ayah refuses to let us imagine that temptation is destiny. The servant remains answerable, but the servant is not abandoned.

### Questions to Carry

- What wrong in your life first became attractive before it became active?
- Which stories have recently made compromise look beautiful to you?
- Where have you been cooperating with a whisper by polishing its excuses?
- What would it mean, today, to live more consciously as one of Allah's servants rather than as someone available for followership of the ghawin?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**Shaytan's great strategy is to beautify falsehood inside earthly life, but the Quran cuts off despair by teaching that he never gains true authority over Allah's servants unless they begin to follow him.**

### Du'a

*Allahumma, show us falsehood before it has time to dress itself as beauty. Purify our hearts so that what displeases You does not become attractive to us, and what pleases You does not become heavy to us. Keep us among Your servants over whom Shaytan has no authority, and whenever we begin to follow a whisper, pull us back before it becomes a path.*

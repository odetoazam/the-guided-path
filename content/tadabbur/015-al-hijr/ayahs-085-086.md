# We Did Not Create the Heavens and the Earth Except in Truth, So Pardon With Beautiful Pardon

## Surah Al-Hijr (15:85-86)

[ayah:15:85] وَمَا خَلَقْنَا ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ وَمَا بَيْنَهُمَآ إِلَّا بِٱلْحَقِّ ۗ وَإِنَّ ٱلسَّاعَةَ لَـَٔاتِيَةٌ ۖ فَٱصْفَحِ ٱلصَّفْحَ ٱلْجَمِيلَ

[ayah:15:86] إِنَّ رَبَّكَ هُوَ ٱلْخَلَّـٰقُ ٱلْعَلِيمُ

*Wa ma khalaqna al-samawati wa al-arda wa ma baynahuma illa bi-al-haqqi wa inna al-sa'ata la-atiyatun fa-sfahi al-safha al-jamil.*

*Inna rabbaka huwa al-khallaqu al-'alim.*

**And We did not create the heavens and the earth and whatever is between them except in truth, and surely the Hour is indeed coming, so pardon with beautiful pardon. Surely your Lord, He is the All-Creating, the All-Knowing.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

The surah has just shown us overturned cities, mountain houses that failed, and histories that became warnings.

Now it suddenly opens the frame all the way out.

Heavens. Earth. Everything in between. The Hour. Then, astonishingly, a command to pardon beautifully.

[PAUSE]

That movement is the whole point. The Quran is not changing the subject. It is teaching the Prophet, and us with him, how to bear the weight of human rejection inside a universe that is not meaningless, not unobserved, and not without a final hour of unveiling.

The hook of the passage is this: **Al-Hijr grounds beautiful pardon not in denial of wrongdoing but in cosmic truth: creation is purposeful, the Hour is coming, and the One who asks for forbearance is the Lord who creates abundantly and knows completely.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Illa bi-al-Haqq**: Creation Is Not Decorative or Empty

The verse says:

**wa ma khalaqna al-samawati wa al-arda wa ma baynahuma illa bi-al-haqq**

We did not create the heavens and the earth and what is between them except in truth.

[PAUSE]

The phrase **bi-al-haqq** is one of the Quran's great world-defining phrases. It means with truth, with right purpose, with non-futility, with moral and ontological seriousness.

This cuts directly against the feeling that history is random or that oppression and mockery are just loose events floating in a morally indifferent cosmos. No. The whole created order stands inside haqq. That means our lives are not moving through a meaningless container. They are moving through a truth-bearing world under a truthful Lord.

### 2. **Wa Inna al-Sa'ata La-Atiyah**: The Hour Is the Unavoidable Completion of Meaning

Then:

**wa inna al-sa'ata la-atiyah**

Surely the Hour is indeed coming.

[PAUSE]

The emphatic structure matters. This is not possibility language. It is certainty language.

And in the flow of the verse, the Hour is not an added doctrine hanging loosely beside creation. It is the completion of creation's truth. If the world was made **bi-al-haqq**, then the Hour must come, because moral reality must finally be disclosed, sorted, and answered.

That means the believer's patience is never asked to rest on vagueness. It rests on the certainty that the unfinished moral tensions of the present are moving toward an appointed unveiling.

### 3. **Fa-Sfah al-Safh al-Jamil**: Beautiful Pardon Is an Act of Theological Confidence

Then comes the surprise:

**fa-sfah al-safh al-jamil**

So pardon with beautiful pardon.

[PAUSE]

The root **s-f-h** carries the sense of turning aside, overlooking, not compulsively extracting response in the present moment. But the phrase adds **al-jamil**: beautiful.

That matters. This is not merely gritted-teeth tolerance. Not weak surrender. Not inner collapse disguised as politeness. Beautiful pardon suggests a forbearance with dignity, a relinquishing that is not ugly with spite, pettiness, or frantic self-defense.

And the only way such pardon is possible is if the servant really believes the earlier part of the verse. If the cosmos is true, if the Hour is coming, then I do not need to settle every account now out of panic. Beautiful pardon becomes possible where trust in Allah's final justice becomes real.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: People Often Become Harsh When They Forget the Hour

One reason human beings cling so tightly to immediate vindication is that they feel, at some level, that if they do not force resolution now, it may never come.

So they become sharp, frantic, punitive, constantly rehearsing grievances and trying to extract from the present world a complete justice the present world was never built to finish.

[PAUSE]

But the verse reconnects the heart to scale. The One who made the heavens, the earth, and everything between them in truth has not abandoned the story. The Hour is coming. This does not make wrongdoing light. It makes immediate revenge less absolute.

The believer can pardon beautifully not because wrong did not matter, but because he knows it mattered too much to be lost in the hands of anyone other than Allah.

### Theme 2: Beautiful Pardon Is Stronger Than Performative Toughness

Many people imagine restraint as weakness and harsh reaction as strength.

But this verse quietly inverts that assumption. The command comes after cosmic truth and the certainty of the Hour, then concludes with Allah's names:

**al-Khallaq, al-'Alim**.

[PAUSE]

**Al-Khallaq** is not merely Creator in a minimal sense. It carries abundance, repeated creating, unfailing creative power. **Al-'Alim** is perfect knowledge. Together the names say: your Lord fully brings realities into being and fully knows what each reality contains.

That means the one who pardons beautifully in obedience to Allah is not acting from fragility. He is leaning on the strongest possible ground. He is refusing to let the ugliness of other people dictate the state of his own soul because he trusts the Lord who knows all and creates all outcomes.

This kind of pardon is not always the same as legal concession, social passivity, or refusal to name wrong. It is first a state of the heart that does not let resentment become its governing style because it has already placed the unfinished account with Allah.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask whether our inability to pardon well comes from a hidden cosmology problem. Have we forgotten the Hour? Have we shrunk the world until the present moment feels like the only court there is? Have we lost touch with **bi-al-haqq** and therefore become desperate to squeeze total justice out of partial conditions?

## Closing Synthesis

Allah declares that the heavens, the earth, and all between them were created only in truth, that the Hour is surely coming, and from within that certainty commands the Prophet to pardon with beautiful pardon. Then He seals the command by naming Himself as the All-Creating, the All-Knowing.

[PAUSE]

So the verse offers a profound sequence: cosmic purpose, final accountability, present forbearance, and trust in the Lord whose power and knowledge leave nothing unmeasured. Beautiful pardon is not emotional softness floating without structure. It is a moral discipline rooted in the architecture of reality.

### Questions to Carry

- Where have you been trying to force total moral resolution out of the present world because you have lost sight of the Hour?
- What would beautiful pardon look like in your life that is neither weak nor bitter?
- Are there grievances you keep clutching because you fear that if you release them, justice will disappear?
- How would your patience change if you truly lived inside **bi-al-haqq** and **la-atiyah**?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**This passage teaches that beautiful pardon becomes possible when the heart remembers that creation is not futile, the Hour is certainly coming, and the One who knows every wrong has not relinquished the final account.**

### Du'a

*Allahumma, root our hearts in the truth of Your creation and the certainty of the Hour. Teach us the beautiful pardon that comes from trust in You, not from weakness before people. Do not let grievance deform us, and let Your names al-Khallaq and al-'Alim steady us whenever the unfinishedness of this world becomes hard to bear.*

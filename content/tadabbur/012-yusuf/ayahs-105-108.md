# They Pass by the Signs, Mix Faith with Association, and Are Called to a Path of Clear Sight

## Surah Yusuf (12:105-108)

[ayah:12:105] وَكَأَيِّن مِّنْ ءَايَةٍ فِى ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ يَمُرُّونَ عَلَيْهَا وَهُمْ عَنْهَا مُعْرِضُونَ

[ayah:12:106] وَمَا يُؤْمِنُ أَكْثَرُهُم بِٱللَّهِ إِلَّا وَهُم مُّشْرِكُونَ

[ayah:12:108] قُلْ هَـٰذِهِۦ سَبِيلِىٓ أَدْعُوٓا۟ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ ۚ عَلَىٰ بَصِيرَةٍ أَنَا۠ وَمَنِ ٱتَّبَعَنِى ۖ وَسُبْحَـٰنَ ٱللَّهِ وَمَآ أَنَا۠ مِنَ ٱلْمُشْرِكِينَ

Do they then feel secure that an overwhelming covering of Allah's punishment will not come to them, or that the Hour will not come upon them suddenly while they are unaware? Say, This is my path: I call to Allah upon clear sight, I and whoever follows me. And glory be to Allah, and I am not of those who associate others with Him.**

## Introduction

The surah has just told us that most people will not believe. Now it explains part of why.

It is not because signs are scarce. It is because people can move through a world saturated with ayat while remaining inwardly turned away. The heavens are above them, the earth is beneath them, revelation is recited to them, history is opened before them, and still the human being can pass by without truly reading what is all around them.

[PAUSE]

Then the surah gets even sharper. Some forms of belief are themselves compromised. It is possible to say "Allah" and still not be free of shirk. It is possible to feel safe while standing beneath suddenness. And against all of that, the Prophet is commanded to declare a different way entirely: a path of da'wah to Allah on **basirah**, purified by tasbih and disavowal of shirk.

The hook of the passage is this: **the deepest blindness is not lack of signs but passing over signs without surrender, and the cure is a path to Allah built on clear sight rather than inherited fog.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Yamurruna 'alayha wa hum 'anha mu'ridun**: They Pass Directly Over the Sign While Turning Away From It

Allah says:

**"How many a sign in the heavens and the earth..."**

The opening **wa ka-ayyin min ayah** gives abundance. The signs are not rare exceptions. They are many, beyond easy counting.

[PAUSE]

Then comes the devastating pair:

**"They pass over it while they are turned away from it."**

The verb **yamurrun** is important. They are not distant from the sign. They move right across it, alongside it, over it. The tragedy is not absence of contact.

Then **mu'ridun** names the inward posture: turned away, averted, uninterested in letting the sign do its work.

That combination is one of the Quran's most accurate diagnoses of heedlessness. Most people are not living in a signless universe. They are living in an over-exposed universe. The problem is not access. It is attention surrendered to avoidance.

### 2. **Yu'minu ... illa wa hum mushrikun**: There Is a Kind of Belief That Is Still Contaminated

Then the surah says:

**"Most of them do not believe in Allah except while they are associating others with Him."**

This is an unsettling sentence because it refuses a simplistic division between total belief and total disbelief. It tells us that there is a kind of professed iman that still carries compromise inside it.

[PAUSE]

The grammar is sharp: **illa wa hum mushrikun**. The belief is not described as pure and then occasionally interrupted by shirk. It is described as arriving already entangled with it.

That should make us sober. Shirk is not only a matter of idols in the obvious sense. At the level of spiritual posture, the human being is always tempted to let something else share the space that belongs to Allah alone: fear, status, tribe, desire, image, intermediaries of control, inner authorities we obey more deeply than revelation.

The verse forces the listener to stop congratulating themselves too quickly for merely saying "I believe in Allah." The real question is whether Allah has been left unrivaled in the heart's surrender.

### 3. **'Ala basirah**: The Prophetic Path Is Not Guesswork, Haze, or Inherited Momentum

Against heedless passing, mixed belief, and false safety, the Prophet is told to say:

**"This is my path."**

Not one possible route among many. Not one devotional option. **Hadhihi sabili.**

And what defines the path?

**"I call to Allah upon basirah."**

**Basirah** is more than information. It carries the sense of inner seeing, clear discernment, sight that understands what it is looking at. The call is not built on fog, mimicry, or inherited inertia.

[PAUSE]

Then the Prophet adds:

**"I and whoever follows me."**

This matters because it means basirah is not a private prophetic luxury. It is the shared condition of the real following community.

And the verse closes with purification:

**"Subhan Allah, and I am not of the mushrikin."**

Tasbih and disavowal of shirk belong together. Once Allah is exalted beyond all rivals, the path becomes clean.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Familiarity With Signs Can Become a Form of Blindness

There is a kind of blindness that comes not from distance but from repetition.

You see the sky every day, so you stop seeing it. You live on the earth, so you stop reading it. You hear Quran often, so the sound becomes familiar enough to pass through you without rearranging you. You experience mercy, risk, reversals, answered du'a, warnings, and narrowly averted falls, and still the soul can slide back into passing over all of it.

[PAUSE]

This is one of the hidden diseases of modern life as well as ancient life. Constant exposure creates emotional numbness. The sign is still there, but our response has been blunted by routine, distraction, and overconsumption. We move over reality like skimming water, never entering it deeply enough to be altered.

The Quran's solution is not to create more signs. Allah already created abundance. The solution is **basirah**. We need our way of seeing healed. A world full of ayat is wasted on a heart trained only to glance.

That is why spiritual life involves deliberate interruption: pausing, recollecting, re-reading, making dhikr, looking upward again, walking the same road as if it might still be charged with meaning because Allah has not run out of speech through creation.

### Theme 2: The Prophetic Path Is Defined as Pure Da'wah to Allah

Verse 108 is one of the most concise mission statements in the Quran.

It tells us what the path is, what the call is toward, how it is made, who shares in it, and what it refuses.

[PAUSE]

The path is **to Allah**, not to personality, brand, tribe, faction, inherited mood, or self-display. The method is **basirah**, not manipulation, haze, or crowd management. The community of the path includes all who truly follow. And the purification of the path is explicit: **I am not of the mushrikin**.

That should unsettle every diluted form of religious work. Da'wah can be outwardly active and inwardly compromised. It can speak the language of Allah while subtly calling to self, group ego, or symbolic power. The verse returns us to prophetic austerity. If the path does not reduce rivals and increase Allah's sole claim on the heart, it has drifted.

So these ayahs are not only diagnosing the disbeliever. They are disciplining the caller.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs move from abundance of signs to human inattentiveness, from compromised belief to false safety, and then from all of that confusion to a direct prophetic declaration: this is my path, I call to Allah on basirah, and I am not of the mushrikin.

[PAUSE]

The logic is profound. If people can pass by signs while turned away, then what they need is not merely more data but sight. If belief itself can be mixed with shirk, then what they need is not mere religious language but purified tawhid. And if punishment or the Hour may come suddenly, then the path cannot be delayed into indefinite later.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: what signs am I passing over because familiarity has made me inattentive? Where is my claimed iman still entangled with rival loves, fears, or dependencies? And if I say I am following the Prophet, is my path actually a path to Allah on basirah, or have I been walking in inherited fog?

[PAUSE]

Allah teaches us here: **do not confuse exposure to signs with surrender to signs, do not confuse saying "Allah" with giving Him the heart unshared, and do not call anything the Prophetic path unless it is truly a path of clear sight to Allah alone.**

# Unseen Reports Were Given by Revelation, and the Messenger Asked No Wage

## Surah Yusuf (12:102-104)

[ayah:12:102] ذَٰلِكَ مِنْ أَنۢبَآءِ ٱلْغَيْبِ نُوحِيهِ إِلَيْكَ ۖ وَمَا كُنتَ لَدَيْهِمْ إِذْ أَجْمَعُوٓا۟ أَمْرَهُمْ وَهُمْ يَمْكُرُونَ

[ayah:12:103] وَمَآ أَكْثَرُ ٱلنَّاسِ وَلَوْ حَرَصْتَ بِمُؤْمِنِينَ

[ayah:12:104] وَمَا تَسْـَٔلُهُمْ عَلَيْهِ مِنْ أَجْرٍ ۚ إِنْ هُوَ إِلَّا ذِكْرٌ لِّلْعَـٰلَمِينَ

*Dhalika min anba'i al-ghaybi nuhihi ilayk, wa ma kunta ladayhim idh ajma'u amrahum wa hum yamkurun.*

**That is from the reports of the unseen which We reveal to you. And you were not with them when they resolved their affair while they were plotting. And most people, even if you are eager, will not be believers. And you do not ask them for any payment for it. It is nothing but a reminder for all worlds.**

## Introduction

The human story of Yusuf has reached its resolution. But the surah is not done.

Instead of simply ending on reunion, Allah turns the camera. Suddenly the listener is addressed. The Prophet is addressed. The story is taken out of the register of beautiful narrative and placed back into the register of revelation, ghaib, prophetic pain, and purity of mission.

[PAUSE]

That turn matters because the Quran does not tell stories for atmosphere. It tells them as truth with consequence. So after letting us dwell in pit, palace, prison, famine, scent, shirt, throne, and du'a, Allah says in effect: you need to understand what this actually is, where it came from, why many will still refuse it, and what the Messenger is and is not asking from you.

The hook of the passage is this: **the story becomes light only when we stop treating it as literature we admire from a distance and receive it as revealed ghaib that asks something of us while asking nothing from us.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Min anba'i al-ghayb nuhihi ilayk**: The Story Is Reclassified as Revealed Unseen

Allah says:

**"That is from the reports of the unseen."**

The phrase **anba' al-ghayb** gives the story a particular weight. These are not just details from the past. They are reports belonging to the unseen realm from the perspective of the hearers. This is not something the Prophet could have acquired by ordinary witnessing.

[PAUSE]

Then:

**"We reveal it to you."**

The verb **nuhihi** puts the entire story under wahy. The point is not simply that the content is true. The point is that its arrival to the Messenger is itself revelatory. The surah is reminding us that what sounded like a narrated drama is in fact disclosure from Allah.

Then the verse intensifies the point:

**"You were not with them when they resolved their affair while they were plotting."**

The phrase **ajma'u amrahum** captures the brothers at the earlier point of gathered decision. The plan was collectively fixed. And the verse mentions that scene while adding **wa hum yamkurun**. Their unity is not neutral. It is unity inside plotting.

The Messenger was not there. He did not stand by the well. He did not overhear the brothers. He did not watch the private convergence of their plan. The only way he knows is because Allah told him.

### 2. **Wa ma aktharu an-nasi wa law harasta bi-mu'minin**: Truth Is Not Measured by Majority Reception

Then comes a sentence of prophetic realism:

**"And most people, even if you are eager, will not be believers."**

The word **harasta** matters. It is not a casual wish. It names earnest concern, intense desire, real effort. The verse acknowledges the Messenger's longing for people to believe.

[PAUSE]

This is a mercy. Allah does not describe the Prophet as indifferent. He is eager. He wants people saved. He wants hearts opened. But the verse also protects him from measuring the truth of the message by the quantity of response.

That correction is permanently relevant. Human beings are always tempted to believe that if something is clear enough, beautiful enough, sincere enough, then surely most people will accept it. The Quran removes that illusion. Reality includes refusal. Clarity does not force surrender.

So the believer must learn to love truth without enslaving truth to metrics.

### 3. **Wa ma tas'aluhum 'alayhi min ajr**: The Message Is Pure Gift, Not Transaction

The next verse says:

**"And you do not ask them for any payment for it."**

This line is morally decisive. The Messenger is not extracting wealth, status, or worldly leverage from the very people he is warning and teaching. There is no hidden invoice beneath revelation.

[PAUSE]

Then the closing:

**"It is nothing but a reminder for all worlds."**

**Dhikr** is a powerful word to end on. The Quran is not selling novelty. It is not offering elite secret knowledge for a fee. It is remembrance. It recalls human beings to what they were made to know and live by.

And it is not tribalized. It is **lil-'alamin**. For all worlds, all peoples, all those addressed by this universal reminder.

That universality, joined to the absence of wage, reveals the extraordinary purity of the prophetic call.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Revelation Is Meant to Relocate You, Not Merely Impress You

Many people love the story of Yusuf.

They love its beauty, reversals, psychological depth, and literary elegance. All of that is real. But these verses warn us against stopping there. If the surah remains only a story we admire, then we have not yet received it the way Allah frames it here.

[PAUSE]

Allah says this is from **anba' al-ghayb** revealed to the Messenger. That means the proper posture before it is not detached appreciation but receptivity. The story is not standing below us as content. We are standing below it as recipients of revelation.

That shift changes everything. Once the story is revelation, it no longer exists mainly to entertain our minds or refine our literary taste. It exists to expose us, guide us, warn us, and remember us back to Allah. The well, the shirt, the prison, the throne, the dream, the du'a all become mirrors and summonses.

The heart matures when it stops asking only, "Wasn't that beautiful?" and begins asking, "What is Allah showing me about myself, my family, my sins, my hopes, my way of reading His decree?"

### Theme 2: Sincerity Does Not Guarantee Majority Response

One of the most painful temptations in da'wah, teaching, parenting, and counsel is the belief that enough sincerity should guarantee visible response.

If I really care, if I really explain, if I really serve without selfish motive, then surely people will come. Surely most will soften. Surely clarity plus love plus sacrifice should add up to mass acceptance.

[PAUSE]

But Allah tells His Messenger something harder and more liberating: most people will not believe, even if you are deeply eager for them.

That frees the servant from a hidden form of despair. It teaches us that refusal is not always proof of failure. Sometimes it is simply part of the human condition under truth. The task is not to manipulate outcomes into majority validation. The task is to remain sincere, wage-free, and anchored in dhikr.

This is not permission for coldness or laziness. The verse explicitly preserves **hirs**. The Prophet cares. The believer should care. But we care without imagining ourselves sovereign over hearts.

The purity of the call is protected by two things together: you cannot force belief, and you cannot charge for the reminder.

## Closing Synthesis

These verses step out of the story in order to tell us what the story really is. It is from the reports of the unseen, revealed by Allah. The Prophet was not there when the brothers gathered their plot. Most people will still not believe, even if he longs for that belief. And yet he asks for no wage at all, because the Quran is only reminder for all peoples.

[PAUSE]

The movement is bracing: revelation, non-presence, majority refusal, zero transaction, universal remembrance. Allah strips away every false frame by which we might domesticate the surah.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: have I been consuming revelation more as moving content than as disclosed ghaib addressed to me? Do I secretly measure truth by majority response? And when I serve Allah's deen in any way, am I still free of the subtle wages of image, control, praise, and return?

[PAUSE]

Allah teaches us here: **receive the story as revelation, not spectacle; let sincerity survive the pain of small response; and keep the reminder pure enough that it can still belong to all worlds because it was never built as a transaction.**

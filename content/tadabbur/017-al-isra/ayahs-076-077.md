# When Expelling the Messenger Shortens the Stay of the Expellers

## Surah Al-Isra (17:76-77)

[ayah:17:76] وَإِن كَادُوا۟ لَيَسْتَفِزُّونَكَ مِنَ ٱلْأَرْضِ لِيُخْرِجُوكَ مِنْهَا ۖ وَإِذًا لَّا يَلْبَثُونَ خِلَـٰفَكَ إِلَّا قَلِيلًا

[ayah:17:77] سُنَّةَ مَن قَدْ أَرْسَلْنَا قَبْلَكَ مِن رُّسُلِنَا ۖ وَلَا تَجِدُ لِسُنَّتِنَا تَحْوِيلًا

*Wa-in kadu la-yastafizzunaka mina al-ardi li-yukhrijuka minha wa-idhan la yalbathuna khilafaka illa qalila. Sunnata man qad arsalna qablaka min rusulina wa-la tajidu li-sunnatina tahwila.*

**And indeed, they almost provoked you from the land so that they might expel you from it. And then they would not remain after you except a little. This is the way with those We sent before you among Our messengers, and you will not find any change in Our way.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

First they try to change the message.

Then they try to remove the messenger.

That is the movement from the previous passage into this one. In 17:73-75, the pressure is theological and social: alter what was revealed, and they will take you as an intimate friend. Here the pressure becomes geographic and political: they almost provoke you from the land in order to expel you from it.

The Qur'an names both moves because they are related. A society that cannot absorb revelation by softening it may try to survive revelation by pushing it out. If the message cannot be edited, perhaps the messenger can be displaced.

But Allah answers with a reversal.

They think expelling the Messenger will preserve their place. Allah says that if they do so, they will not remain after him except a little. The act by which they try to secure the land becomes the act that shortens their own stay in it.

Then verse 77 lifts this from one moment into a law: this is the sunnah with the messengers sent before you, and Allah's sunnah is not converted into something else.

That means the passage is not only history. It is a pattern. When a people treats revelation as a threat to its control, when it tries first to dilute and then to expel, it is no longer simply managing a public problem. It is stepping into an old divine law.

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. `Wa-in kadu`: The Nearness of Their Plot

The passage begins with the same tense structure we heard in the previous unit:

`wa-in kadu`

They almost.

This repetition matters. In 17:73, they almost tempted the Prophet away from revelation. In 17:76, they almost provoke him from the land. The two threats run side by side: distortion and displacement.

The word `kadu` creates nearness without completion. Their plot approaches the threshold, but Allah narrates it from above the threshold. They almost. They do not own the final outcome. Even their plotting is reported inside Allah's speech, which means it is already contained by His knowledge and decree.

This gives the believer a way to read pressure. Nearness is not sovereignty. A plot can come close without becoming lord. Harm can approach without escaping Allah. A door can nearly close, a city can nearly turn, a crowd can nearly overpower, and still the matter belongs to the One narrating it.

Al-Tabari and Ibn Kathir both treat the verse as part of Allah's protection of His Messenger from the plots of the rejecters. The pressure was real. The preservation was more real.

### 2. `La-yastafizzunaka`: They Try to Unsettle Before They Expel

The key verb is vivid:

`la-yastafizzunaka`

They almost provoke you, unsettle you, drive you out by agitation.

This is the same root field that appeared earlier in 17:64, when Allah says to Iblis: `wa-stafziz man istata'ta minhum bi-sawtika` - incite or unsettle whomever you can with your voice. That echo is striking. Satanic pressure works by disturbing a person from settled footing. Here human opposition takes on a similar pattern: unsettle the Messenger from the land.

Expulsion is not only physical removal. It begins with agitation. Make the environment hostile. Make staying feel impossible. Keep pressure constant. Threaten, mock, isolate, bargain, exhaust. The body may leave only after the social world has been made unlivable.

[PAUSE]

This is how many forms of injustice work. They do not always begin by formally expelling someone. They first make the place unable to hold truth. The truthful voice is framed as disruptive. The principled person is made to feel like the problem. The community reshapes the air until leaving looks like the only way to breathe.

The Qur'an sees that before naming the actual removal.

### 3. `Mina al-ard ... minha`: The Land They Think They Own

The phrase continues:

`mina al-ardi li-yukhrijuka minha`

From the land, so they might expel you from it.

The repetition of land is heavy. From the land. From it.

Which land? Classical tafsir discusses the concrete setting around the Prophet, with reports connecting the verse to Makkah and the pressure that culminated in hijrah, while some reports mention other hostile pressure. The essential Qur'anic meaning is broader and clear: those opposing the Messenger seek to remove him from the place where Allah placed him to convey.

The irony is that they speak and act as though the land belongs to them.

But Al-Isra has already been teaching that land and sea are carried spaces, not independent shelters. The same surah just said that Allah carries human beings on land and sea. It warned that land can swallow and sea can drown by Allah's command. So when people try to use land as a weapon against Allah's Messenger, they have misunderstood ownership.

They can control houses, roads, assemblies, borders, tribal agreements, and visible power. They cannot control the sunnah of Allah inside history.

This is why the Qur'an often joins expulsion threats to divine reversal. In 14:13, disbelievers tell their messengers they will expel them from the land or force them back into their religion, and Allah reveals that He will surely destroy the wrongdoers and settle the messengers after them. In 8:30, the plotting against the Prophet includes restraining, killing, or expelling him, while Allah is the best of planners.

Human beings may weaponize place. Allah owns place.

### 4. `Wa-idhan la yalbathuna khilafaka illa qalila`: Expulsion Becomes Their Countdown

Then Allah gives the reversal:

`wa-idhan la yalbathuna khilafaka illa qalila`

And then they would not remain after you except a little.

`Yalbathuna` means remain, stay, linger. The people who wanted to remain secure by removing the Messenger are told that their own remaining would become brief. `Khilafaka` means after you, behind you, following your departure. `Illa qalila` compresses their imagined security into a small remainder.

This is one of the Qur'an's great reversals of power.

They assume the Messenger's departure makes the land safer for them. Allah says it makes their stay shorter.

Ibn Kathir reads this within the pattern of Allah protecting the Prophet and dealing with those who sought to expel him. Al-Tabari preserves the meaning that had they driven him out, they would not have remained after him but little. The Qur'anic law is not that every tyrant falls instantly. It is that expelling a messenger places a people under a shortened moral horizon, whether the exact timing appears immediate to them or not.

[PAUSE]

This is a warning against confusing delay with safety. A society may succeed in removing the troublesome voice, silencing the reminder, exiling the reformer, or pushing out the person who would not flatter falsehood. For a moment, the room feels calmer. But the calm may be the silence before consequence.

### 5. `Sunnata man qad arsalna qablaka`: The Prophet Is Placed in the Long Prophetic Pattern

Verse 77 begins:

`sunnata man qad arsalna qablaka min rusulina`

The way of those We sent before you among Our messengers.

The word `sunnah` here means a settled way, a pattern, a divine law in how Allah deals with messengers and the peoples who reject them. It is not an isolated incident. The Prophet is being consoled by being placed into continuity.

This is deeply important. When a believer suffers pressure, one of the hardest parts is the illusion of abnormality. Why is this happening? Why is truth so unwelcome? Why does obedience produce hostility? The Qur'an answers by connecting the present struggle to the old road.

You are not outside the pattern. You are walking with the messengers.

The verse says `qad arsalna`: We certainly sent. The sending is Allah's act. The messengers before you were not self-appointed disruptors. They were sent, and opposition to them triggered a known divine pattern.

This strengthens the Prophet and teaches the ummah. If the best of creation was placed inside a history of pressure, then hardship is not evidence that truth has failed. Sometimes hostility is evidence that revelation is touching the places people most want to protect.

### 6. `Wa-la tajidu li-sunnatina tahwila`: Allah's Pattern Is Not Converted

The passage closes:

`wa-la tajidu li-sunnatina tahwila`

You will not find any conversion, alteration, or turning away in Our sunnah.

`Tahwil` comes from turning something from one state to another. The verse denies that Allah's sunnah can be converted into a different moral law because people are powerful, modern, numerous, wealthy, strategic, or historically exceptional.

This is one of the great cures for being overawed by human systems.

Every age thinks its arrangements are too solid for the old truths. The Qur'an says no. The divine pattern does not become something else because a new empire, market, media order, or social consensus says so. The forms change. The sunnah remains.

That does not mean we can predict exact timelines. The Qur'an does not give us permission to pretend we know when Allah will act. But it does give certainty about moral structure. Expelling truth does not make falsehood safe. Driving away messengers does not secure a land against Allah. Removing reminders does not remove accountability.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: A Society First Tries to Absorb Revelation, Then to Expel It

The sequence across these verses is psychologically precise.

First, the opponents try to tempt the Messenger away from revelation. They want another version, a softened version, a message that can be welcomed without surrender. If revelation can be edited, the society does not need to change.

But if revelation remains intact, the next move is removal.

This pattern repeats at many scales. A family may first try to pressure a newly practicing member into keeping religion private and convenient. If that person remains firm, the family may begin excluding them from emotional belonging. A workplace may first ask a believer to treat principles as flexible. If the believer refuses, the believer becomes difficult. A public culture may first celebrate religious identity as heritage. If revelation speaks with command, it is recast as a threat to the social order.

Al-Isra names both forms of pressure because sincere believers need to recognize them. Absorption says: stay, but change. Expulsion says: if you will not change, you cannot stay.

The Prophet is protected by Allah, and his hijrah becomes not defeat but the opening of another chapter. That is important. Being pushed out is not always failure. Sometimes leaving is the form Allah gives to preservation. Sometimes the land that rejects truth loses the blessing it did not know it was being offered.

[PAUSE]

The practical lesson is not to seek conflict or dramatize rejection. It is to understand that acceptance is not always proof of wisdom, and expulsion is not always proof of failure. The measure is whether revelation remained intact and whether the servant stayed with Allah.

### Theme 2: Places Do Not Belong to Those Who Can Force Others Out

Human power often expresses itself through place.

Who gets to stay? Who is welcome? Who is treated as native? Who is made to feel like a guest? Who controls the doors, the language, the law, the meeting, the microphone, the neighborhood, the institution?

The opponents in this passage try to use land against the Messenger. But Allah turns the land back into a sign of His sovereignty. If they expel him, they will not remain after him except a little. Their control over place is not ultimate. In fact, their use of place against revelation becomes evidence against them.

This teaches a deep theology of belonging.

The earth belongs to Allah. Communities are entrusted with places; they do not own them against Him. When a people use land, power, or institutional control to drive away what Allah sent for their guidance, they are not proving ownership. They are misusing trust.

The Qur'an repeatedly shows this. Nuh is mocked by his people until the very ground and sky obey Allah against them. Ibrahim is threatened by fire, but the fire becomes cool and safe. Musa is chased to the sea, but the sea opens for him and closes over Fir'awn. The Prophet is driven from Makkah, but the city does not escape Allah's plan.

The believer therefore should not worship access to place. A masjid, organization, country, platform, family home, or institution can be a blessing. But Allah is not contained by any of them. If obedience costs a place, Allah remains Lord of all places.

That is not romantic. Exile, exclusion, and displacement hurt. The Prophet's hijrah was not emotionally trivial. But the Qur'an teaches us that being forced out by falsehood is not the same as being abandoned by Allah.

### Theme 3: Allah's Sunnah Makes History Morally Legible

Without divine sunnah, history looks like noise.

Strong people rise. Weak people suffer. Truth is mocked. Liars gain time. Reformers are expelled. Cities continue. Markets open in the morning. People assume the delay means nothing is wrong.

The Qur'an gives another lens.

There are patterns in how Allah deals with arrogance, rejection, expulsion, and messengers. The exact timing belongs to Him. The pattern belongs to revelation. A people can reach a point where the removal of a messenger or truth-bearing warning becomes not a victory but the beginning of their own undoing.

Verse 77 gives the Prophet consolation through that pattern. This has happened before. Messengers before you were sent. Peoples before this one resisted, threatened, and expelled. Allah's sunnah did not turn into something else for them, and it will not turn into something else now.

This gives the believer both patience and sobriety.

Patience, because we do not need to panic when falsehood appears strong. The sunnah of Allah is not broken by headlines. Sobriety, because we do not get to weaponize the sunnah by making reckless predictions. We can say Allah's moral law is true. We cannot claim ownership over His timetable.

[PAUSE]

The practical fruit is steadiness. Keep telling the truth with wisdom. Do not sell revelation to remain accepted. Do not despair if a place closes. Do not envy people who secured land by expelling guidance. And do not forget that Allah's patterns are more durable than human arrangements.

## Closing Synthesis

### The Architecture of Expulsion and Divine Pattern

The two verses move from attempted displacement to divine law.

First, the opponents nearly provoke and unsettle the Messenger from the land. Their goal is explicit: to expel him from it. This is the geographic form of the previous theological pressure.

Second, Allah reverses their calculation. If they remove the Messenger, they will not remain after him except a little. The act meant to secure their place becomes the act that shortens their stay.

Third, Allah identifies this as sunnah: the settled way with messengers before Muhammad. The Prophet's experience is not isolated but part of the old prophetic road.

Fourth, the passage seals the law: no tahwil in Allah's sunnah. Human power can rearrange appearances, but it cannot convert the moral structure of Allah's dealing with rejection.

### Questions to Carry

Where do I mistake control of a place for real security?

A position, home, platform, or institution can feel permanent. Ask whether your security comes from Allah or from the ability to keep uncomfortable truth outside.

Have I ever tried to make a truthful voice leave rather than listen to what it exposed?

This can happen in families and communities, not only nations. Sometimes we call someone disruptive because their presence reveals what we do not want to repent from.

What pressure do I use first: absorption or expulsion?

Do you try to soften people into silence, and then distance them when they stay clear? The sequence in these verses can appear in miniature inside ordinary relationships.

Can I trust Allah if obedience costs me a place?

This is not an easy question. Sit with it gently. Ask Allah to make you loyal to Him without becoming reckless or hard-hearted.

Do I believe Allah's sunnah is more durable than the current arrangement?

The age always feels exceptional to the people inside it. Revelation trains us to see older, deeper, and truer patterns.

### One-Sentence Distillation

**When a people try to secure their land by expelling the messenger, Allah turns that expulsion into their countdown, because His sunnah with truth and rejection does not change.**

### The Closing Invitation

Let these verses loosen your fear of being displaced for truth. Do not seek rejection, but do not worship acceptance. If Allah keeps you somewhere, be truthful with wisdom. If obedience eventually costs you a room, a circle, or a place, remember that the Lord of the land is not confined to the land. His sunnah is steadier than every door people can close.

### Du'a

Allahumma, do not make us among those who try to absorb truth by changing it or expel truth when it will not change. Keep us loyal to Your messengers and to the revelation they brought. Give us patience if obedience costs us place, belonging, or ease. Make us trust Your sunnah without pretending to control Your timing, and keep our hearts firm wherever You place us.

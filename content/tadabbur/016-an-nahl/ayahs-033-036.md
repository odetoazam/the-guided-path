# They Wait for the Angels, Then Blame Allah's Will for Their Shirk

## Surah An-Nahl (16:33-36)

[ayah:16:33] هَلْ يَنظُرُونَ إِلَّآ أَن تَأْتِيَهُمُ ٱلْمَلَـٰٓئِكَةُ أَوْ يَأْتِىَ أَمْرُ رَبِّكَ ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ فَعَلَ ٱلَّذِينَ مِن قَبْلِهِمْ ۚ وَمَا ظَلَمَهُمُ ٱللَّهُ وَلَـٰكِن كَانُوٓا۟ أَنفُسَهُمْ يَظْلِمُونَ

[ayah:16:34] فَأَصَابَهُمْ سَيِّـَٔاتُ مَا عَمِلُوا۟ وَحَاقَ بِهِم مَّا كَانُوا۟ بِهِۦ يَسْتَهْزِءُونَ

[ayah:16:35] وَقَالَ ٱلَّذِينَ أَشْرَكُوا۟ لَوْ شَآءَ ٱللَّهُ مَا عَبَدْنَا مِن دُونِهِۦ مِن شَىْءٍ نَّحْنُ وَلَآ ءَابَآؤُنَا وَلَا حَرَّمْنَا مِن دُونِهِۦ مِن شَىْءٍ ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ فَعَلَ ٱلَّذِينَ مِن قَبْلِهِمْ ۚ فَهَلْ عَلَى ٱلرُّسُلِ إِلَّا ٱلْبَلَـٰغُ ٱلْمُبِينُ

[ayah:16:36] وَلَقَدْ بَعَثْنَا فِى كُلِّ أُمَّةٍ رَّسُولًا أَنِ ٱعْبُدُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَٱجْتَنِبُوا۟ ٱلطَّـٰغُوتَ ۖ فَمِنْهُم مَّنْ هَدَى ٱللَّهُ وَمِنْهُم مَّنْ حَقَّتْ عَلَيْهِ ٱلضَّلَـٰلَةُ ۚ فَسِيرُوا۟ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ فَٱنظُرُوا۟ كَيْفَ كَانَ عَـٰقِبَةُ ٱلْمُكَذِّبِينَ

*Hal yanzuruna illa an ta'tiyahumu al-mala'ikatu aw ya'tiya amru rabbik. Kadhalika fa'ala alladhina min qablihim. Wa ma zalamahumu Allahu wa lakin kanu anfusahum yazlimun.*

*Fa-asabahum sayyi'atu ma 'amilu wa haqa bihim ma kanu bihi yastahzi'un.*

*Wa qala alladhina ashraku law sha'a Allahu ma 'abadna min dunihi min shay'in nahnu wa la aba'una wa la harramna min dunihi min shay'. Kadhalika fa'ala alladhina min qablihim. Fa-hal 'ala al-rusuli illa al-balaghu al-mubin.*

*Wa la-qad ba'athna fi kulli ummatin rasulan ani u'budu Allaha wa ijtanibu al-taghut. Fa-minhum man hada Allahu wa minhum man haqqat 'alayhi al-dalalah. Fa-siru fi al-ardi fa-unzuru kayfa kana 'aqibatu al-mukadhdhibin.*

**Do they await except that the angels come to them or the command of your Lord comes? Thus did those before them. Allah did not wrong them, but they used to wrong themselves. ## Introduction

There is a way the soul delays obedience that sounds almost religious.

It does not always say, "I do not care what Allah wants."

Sometimes it says, "If Allah really wanted otherwise, He would have stopped me."

Sometimes it says, "When the truth becomes overwhelming, then I will respond."

Sometimes it hides rebellion inside a vocabulary of destiny.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs tear that cover away.

They show people waiting, but only for the sort of evidence that ends the test rather than begins faith. Then they quote a fatalistic excuse: had Allah willed, we would not have worshipped these things, nor kept these prohibitions. And the Quran answers with devastating simplicity: this is an old excuse, messengers were sent to every nation with a clear command, and those who fell were not wronged by Allah - they kept wronging themselves until the consequences of their own mockery closed around them.

The hook of the passage is this: **An-Nahl teaches that using Allah's will to excuse shirk and disobedience is not submission but evasion, because Allah already sent the clear command through every messenger to worship Him alone and avoid Taghut.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Hal Yanzuruna Illa**: They Are Waiting, but Only for the End of Waiting

The passage opens with a searching question:

**hal yanzuruna illa**

Are they waiting for anything except...

[PAUSE]

The verb `yanzuruna` gives the scene a posture of suspended expectation. But what are they waiting for?

Not more reminders.

Not deeper explanation.

Not another prophetly clarification.

They are waiting for:

**an ta'tiyahumu al-mala'ikah aw ya'tiya amru rabbik**

that the angels come to them, or the command of your Lord comes.

In other words, they are waiting for decisive unveiling, the kind of arrival after which the ambiguity they pretended was blocking them no longer exists.

This is one of the Quran's recurring diagnoses of the rebellious heart: it keeps demanding a kind of proof that would end the moral space of choosing. It does not want signs that call it to trust. It wants compulsion dressed as certainty.

Then the surah immediately places them in history:

**kadhalika fa'ala alladhina min qablihim**

Thus did those before them.

So this is not a fresh philosophical difficulty. It is an old human pattern.

### 2. **Wa Ma Zalamahumu Allahu Wa Lakin Kanu Anfusahum Yazlimun**: Divine Justice Is Cleared Before Consequences Arrive

The surah says:

**wa ma zalamahumu Allahu wa lakin kanu anfusahum yazlimun**

Allah did not wrong them, but they used to wrong themselves.

[PAUSE]

This line matters enormously because it appears before the consequences are described. The Quran clears Allah of injustice before naming the blow that fell.

That is theological precision.

The punishment is not arbitrary.

The loss is not because Allah treated them unfairly.

The history of ruin is not evidence of divine harshness detached from human reality.

Rather, the people kept committing `zulm` against themselves. As elsewhere in the Quran, self-wronging means the self is not being guarded in truth. It is being handed over to shirk, mockery, defiance, and corruption.

Then the next ayah gives the consequence in two lines:

**fa-asabahum sayyi'atu ma 'amilu**

So the evils of what they did struck them.

**wa haqa bihim ma kanu bihi yastahzi'un**

And that which they used to mock encompassed them.

[PAUSE]

The wording is striking. Their own evil deeds come back upon them, and the very reality they mocked closes around them. The joke becomes the trap. The thing treated as laughable becomes unavoidable atmosphere.

This is more than punishment. It is moral exposure. The world they kept narrating falsely finally narrates them truthfully.

### 3. **Law Sha'a Allah ... Ma 'Abadna**: Fatalism Used as an Excuse for Shirk

Then the ayah quotes one of the oldest evasions:

**law sha'a Allah ma 'abadna min dunihi min shay'**

Had Allah willed, we would not have worshipped anything besides Him.

[PAUSE]

This is not humble submission to qadar. It is rebellion trying to hide inside qadar.

They are saying, in effect: if our worship and our invented prohibitions were really wrong, Allah would have prevented them, so the fact that they occurred proves their legitimacy.

The Quran does not treat this as profound theology. It treats it as repetition:

**kadhalika fa'ala alladhina min qablihim**

Thus did those before them.

This excuse too is old.

And the answer is as clear as the excuse is slippery:

**fa-hal 'ala al-rusuli illa al-balaghu al-mubin**

Is there upon the messengers anything except clear conveyance?

The prophets were not sent to force hearts. They were sent to clarify, warn, call, and establish the proof. Once the message is made plain, blaming Allah's universal will for our chosen rebellion becomes a refusal to distinguish between Allah's allowing a test to exist and Allah's loving what we choose inside that test.

Then the surah universalizes the answer:

**wa la-qad ba'athna fi kulli ummatin rasulan**

We certainly sent into every nation a messenger.

And what was the shared message?

**ani u'budu Allaha wa ijtanibu al-taghut**

Worship Allah and avoid Taghut.

[PAUSE]

The whole matter is reduced to its cleanest form. Not endless metaphysical fog. Not unanswerable complexity. Worship Allah. Avoid every false authority, false object of devotion, false structure of rebellion that competes with Him.

That is what humanity was told.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Some People Use Qadar to Avoid Repentance Rather Than to Deepen Surrender

There is a healthy way of speaking about Allah's will, and there is a diseased way.

The healthy way produces humility, trust, fear, and dependence.

The diseased way turns qadar into a shield against moral responsibility.

[PAUSE]

We know the difference by the effect it produces.

If speaking about Allah's will makes me quicker to repent, smaller before Him, and more careful with my choices, then I am probably speaking from servanthood.

If speaking about Allah's will makes me looser with sin, more argumentative, and more insulated from blame, then I am probably using theology to protect my ego.

That is exactly what these ayahs expose. The mushrik does not mention Allah's will because he is overwhelmed by divine majesty. He mentions it because he wants to excuse his worship of other than Allah and the religious distortions he and his fathers normalized.

This remains painfully current. People still say versions of it:

If Allah wanted me different, I would already be different.

If this path were truly bad for me, why was it even possible?

If Allah allowed it, who am I to resist it?

[PAUSE]

But the Quran's answer remains simple: Allah already sent the clear message. You are not being asked to decode the hidden decree. You are being asked to obey the revealed command.

### Theme 2: Waiting for Decisive Proof Is Often a Strategy for Avoiding Present Obedience

The ayah begins by asking whether they are waiting for the angels or for your Lord's command.

That is not innocent waiting.

It is delay weaponized.

It is the soul bargaining for the kind of certainty that removes the dignity and risk of faith.

[PAUSE]

Many of us do a softer version of this.

We are not waiting for angels in the literal sense, but we wait for the perfect inner state, the undeniable sign, the crisis that forces seriousness, the future version of ourselves that will supposedly obey more cleanly than the one standing before Allah now.

The problem is that waiting can become a liturgy of refusal.

Every delay trains the heart.

Every postponed surrender strengthens another rival loyalty.

And eventually the person is not merely waiting for proof. He is being shaped by the waiting itself.

That is why the Quran answers delay with history: **thus did those before them**. The old nations also waited until the truth reached the point where waiting was over. By then the test had already been failed.

### Theme 3: The Message to Humanity Was Never Obscure

There is mercy in how the ayah closes.

After quoting the excuse and exposing the old pattern, Allah does not leave the matter abstract. He tells us what every messenger came to say:

**worship Allah and avoid Taghut.**

[PAUSE]

`Taghut` gathers everything that crosses the limit and asks for a loyalty, obedience, reverence, or fear that belongs only to Allah. An idol can be Taghut. A system can be Taghut. A tyrant can be Taghut. An ego can function like Taghut. A culture's sacred rebellion can become Taghut.

The command is therefore both outward and inward.

Worship Allah.

And take distance from every rival center that wants the heart's bowing.

Then the ayah states the human outcome plainly:

some were guided by Allah,

and upon some misguidance became due.

This does not erase responsibility. It seals it. Guidance was offered through messengers. The path was clarified. People responded differently. So the end of nations becomes a readable lesson, which is why the surah sends us back into the earth to look at what happened to the deniers.

[PAUSE]

Travel, in the Quran, is not tourism alone. It is a discipline of moral sight. Walk through the earth, study the wreckage of false certainty, and do not let yourself imagine that theological excuses have ever saved a civilization from the truth.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs move with severe clarity.

They begin by exposing people who keep waiting for the sort of divine intervention that ends the trial rather than entering it. They place that delay inside an old historical pattern. They clear Allah of injustice and place the real wrong in the self-wronging of the people themselves. Then they show consequences landing as the return of deeds and the enclosure of mocked realities.

After that, the surah quotes the fatalistic excuse of shirk - had Allah willed, we would not have done this - only to answer it by reducing the prophetic mission to one unambiguous task: clear conveyance. And then it widens still further: every nation received a messenger with the same essential summons, worship Allah and avoid Taghut.

[PAUSE]

So the architecture is exact: delay, self-wronging, consequences, excuse, prophetic clarification, universal history. The passage leaves us with no permission to hide chosen rebellion inside Allah's decree. What Allah kept hidden is His unseen will. What He made plain is our path: worship Him alone and stay clear of every Taghut that competes for the heart.

### Questions to Carry

- Where am I using theological language to soften my sense of responsibility instead of deepening it?
- Am I obeying the clear command Allah revealed, or hiding in speculation about what He could have prevented?
- What form of Taghut most easily competes for my fear, loyalty, or obedience?
- What would change if I stopped waiting for overwhelming proof and responded to the proof already made clear?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs show that blaming Allah's will for shirk and disobedience is an old evasive pattern, because the messengers were sent to every nation with a clear command: worship Allah alone and avoid Taghut.**

### Du'a

*Allahumma, protect us from using Your decree to excuse our rebellion. Make us people who obey what You made clear, who worship You alone, who avoid every Taghut, and who take warning from the ends of those who denied Your messengers. Guide us with the guidance You love and do not leave us to the crooked arguments of our own souls.*

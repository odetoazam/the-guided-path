# Your God Is One, but the Arrogant Call Revelation Tales and Carry Full Burdens

## Surah An-Nahl (16:19-25)

[ayah:16:19] وَٱللَّهُ يَعْلَمُ مَا تُسِرُّونَ وَمَا تُعْلِنُونَ

## Introduction

After piling up signs in the world outside us, the surah now presses into the world inside us.

Why does a human being resist what is so plain?

Why, after so many signs, does the heart still refuse the conclusion?

Why can someone look at creation, hear revelation, and still answer with contempt?

[PAUSE]

These ayahs name the disease with unusual directness. Allah knows what is hidden and what is displayed. The objects called upon besides Him are lifeless and created. Your God is one. Yet the problem is not lack of evidence. It is inward rejection joined to arrogance. Then the passage shows the behavioral expression of that inward sickness: when asked what Allah has sent down, they sneer, "Stories of the ancients." And that sneer does not disappear into the air. It becomes weight to be carried.

The hook of the passage is this: **An-Nahl teaches that denial of tawhid is not only an intellectual error but a moral condition of arrogant hearts, and that belittling revelation becomes a burden that harms both the self and those one misleads.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Ya'lamu Ma Tusirruna wa Ma Tu'linun**: The Argument Begins by Breaking the Privacy Illusion

The passage opens and then repeats:

**wa Allahu ya'lamu ma tusirruna wa ma tu'linun**

and later:

**anna Allaha ya'lamu ma yusirruna wa ma yu'linun**

[PAUSE]

Allah knows what you conceal and what you declare.

This does two things at once. First, it strips away the illusion that the real issue is hidden from Allah. Second, it tells us that shirk and rejection are not merely public positions. They have an inward chamber.

There is what people say aloud, and there is what the heart protects, resents, refuses, and hides. The surah begins there because the visible argument about idols is finally tied to an invisible moral state.

### 2. **Amwatun Ghayru Ahya'**: False Gods Are Not Only Weak; They Are Dead

Then the objects of worship besides Allah are described:

**la yakhluquna shay'an wa hum yukhlaqun**

They create nothing while they themselves are created.

Then more sharply:

**amwatun ghayru ahya'**

Dead, not alive.

[PAUSE]

The wording is severe. Not merely lesser. Not merely limited. Dead.

The surah wants the absurdity of shirk to become emotionally unmistakable. People are assigning reverence, fear, hope, and orientation to what cannot create, cannot initiate life, cannot even hold life in itself.

And then:

**wa ma yash'uruna ayyana yub'athun**

They do not perceive when they will be resurrected.

Even if read with the broader scene in mind, the point is humiliation: these supposed objects of devotion have no awareness of the very decisive future their worshippers are heading toward.

### 3. **Qulubuhum Munkirah wa Hum Mustakbirun**: The Problem Is Not Lack of Clarity but the Heart's Refusal of It

The surah states:

**ilahukum ilahun wahid**

Your God is one God.

[PAUSE]

The sentence is astonishingly simple. After all the prior signs and arguments, the truth itself is given without complication.

Then comes the diagnosis of those who resist it:

**fa-alladhina la yu'minuna bi-al-akhirati qulubuhum munkirah wa hum mustakbirun**

Their hearts are denying, and they are arrogant.

This is the key. The denial is placed in the hearts, and the arrogance in the whole posture of the person. The surah ties doctrinal refusal to eschatological refusal: not believing in the Hereafter loosens the soul from accountability, and then pride can harden into resistance against the plain truth of tawhid.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Many Rejections of Truth Are Driven Less by Confusion Than by Pride

People often imagine disbelief as though it were always a purely informational problem.

If only the argument were clearer.

If only the evidence were stronger.

If only the idea were explained one more time.

[PAUSE]

But the Quran repeatedly shows that the deeper barrier is often not obscurity. It is arrogance. A heart may understand enough to be morally confronted while still refusing to yield. It may hear the truth but experience surrender as humiliation rather than liberation.

That is why the ayah does not say merely that they are mistaken. It says their hearts are denying and they are arrogant. The resistance is ethical before it is intellectual. They do not want a world in which Allah is one and they are answerable.

This is sobering for us too. We may not stand before carved idols, but every time the ego resists a truth because yielding would lower us, expose us, or oblige us, we taste a branch of the same disease.

### Theme 2: Mocking Revelation Is Not a Neutral Opinion; It Creates Weight

When asked what Allah has sent down, they say:

**asatiru al-awwalin**

Stories of the former peoples.

[PAUSE]

This is more than dismissal. It is a strategy of reduction. If revelation can be relabeled as myth, then it no longer has to be obeyed, feared, or loved. Labeling becomes evasion.

But the next ayah makes the moral consequence explicit. They will carry their burdens in full, and some of the burdens of those whom they mislead without knowledge.

That is terrifying in its precision. Speech about truth is not weightless. Public contempt for revelation can travel into other hearts and become misguidance for which one is answerable.

So the surah exposes a chain:

inner arrogance,

dismissive speech,

misleading others,

then compounded burdens in the Hereafter.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask where arrogance may be hiding inside our own resistance to divine truth. Are there commands, limits, or beliefs we find ourselves shrinking, relabeling, or casually belittling because we do not want them to govern us? And have we ever treated speech about revelation as if it were only rhetoric, forgetting that words can become loads carried into the next life?

## Closing Synthesis

Allah begins by declaring His knowledge of what is hidden and what is made public, exposes the false gods as created and lifeless, states the simplicity of tawhid, diagnoses the deniers' hearts as rejecting and their posture as arrogant, then shows their contemptuous answer to revelation and the burdens that answer creates for themselves and those they mislead.

[PAUSE]

The structure is exact: hidden state, false object, plain truth, heart disease, public speech, final weight. The surah teaches that the gravest errors of worship and revelation are not detached from character. They grow in hearts that refuse humility before what Allah has made clear.

### Questions to Carry

- Where might pride be making you resist a truth that is already clear enough to act on?
- Have you ever tried to reduce an uncomfortable part of revelation by relabeling it inwardly instead of yielding to it?
- Do you treat your speech about truth as morally weighty, or as though public dismissal and private skepticism leave no lasting mark?
- What hidden resistance would look different if you remembered that Allah knows what you conceal and what you declare?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs show that rejection of tawhid and contempt for revelation arise from arrogant hearts, and that dismissing Allah's speech becomes a real burden carried by both the speaker and those he misleads.**

### Du'a

*Allahumma, protect our hearts from the pride that resists what You have made clear. Do not let us belittle any part of Your revelation inwardly or outwardly. Make us humble before Your oneness, truthful in our speech, and safe from carrying burdens born of arrogance or misguidance.*

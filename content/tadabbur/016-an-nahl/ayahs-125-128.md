# Call With Wisdom, Endure With Allah, and Know Who He Is With

## Surah An-Nahl (16:125-128)

[ayah:16:125] ٱدْعُ إِلَىٰ سَبِيلِ رَبِّكَ بِٱلْحِكْمَةِ وَٱلْمَوْعِظَةِ ٱلْحَسَنَةِ ۖ وَجَـٰدِلْهُم بِٱلَّتِى هِىَ أَحْسَنُ ۚ إِنَّ رَبَّكَ هُوَ أَعْلَمُ بِمَن ضَلَّ عَن سَبِيلِهِۦ ۖ وَهُوَ أَعْلَمُ بِٱلْمُهْتَدِينَ

## Introduction

A surah can end by summarizing its message.

And it can also end by teaching you how to carry that message into the world.

[PAUSE]

That is what An-Nahl does here.

After a long education in tawhid, favors, gratitude, law, parables, revelation, trial, and the paths of Ibrahim, the surah does not close with abstraction. It closes with posture. How do you speak? How do you answer hostility? How do you bear the pain of rejection? How do you stop grief from swallowing your mission? Where does patience actually come from? And when all of that feels too heavy, what final assurance does Allah leave in the heart of His servant?

These four ayahs answer all of that with astonishing balance. Call with wisdom, beauty, and the best mode of argument. If you answer harm, do not exceed justice. But know that patience is better. Then go even deeper: your patience is not from yourself anyway. It is from Allah. So do not let grief consume you, and do not let the scheming of opponents collapse your chest. The surah ends not by saying "You are alone, but endure." It ends by naming who Allah is with.

The hook of the passage is this: **An-Nahl closes by teaching that the servant's path in calling, responding, and enduring must be governed by wisdom, beauty, justice, and patience sourced in Allah, because the decisive strength of the believer is not self-possession but divine companionship.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Bi-al-Hikmah / al-Maw'izhah al-Hasanah / bi-allati hiya Ahsan**: Da'wah Is Not Only About Truth Said, but About the Mode in Which Truth Is Carried

The command begins:

**ud'u ila sabili rabbika**

Call to the way of your Lord.

Not your way. Not your ego. Not your victory. `Sabili rabbika` immediately purifies the call from self-ownership.

Then come three modes:

**bi-al-hikmah**

with wisdom,

**wa al-maw'izhati al-hasanah**

and good exhortation,

**wa jadilhum bi-allati hiya ahsan**

and argue with them in the way that is best.

[PAUSE]

The progression matters.

`Hikmah` is fittingness, putting things in their right place, speaking what is true in the right way, to the right person, at the right time.

`Maw'izhah` is admonition that reaches the heart, but it must be `hasanah` - beautiful, gentle, well-carried, not brutalized by ego or contempt.

Even `jidal`, debate or argument, is not left morally neutral. It must be done by what is `ahsan`, the best, the most beautiful, the most elevated way.

That means the Quran refuses a crude split between truth and character. The form of the call is itself part of obedience.

### 2. **Fa-'Aqibu bi-Mithli Ma 'Uqibtum Bihi**: Justice Places a Boundary on Retaliation

Then the passage turns to injury:

**wa in 'aqabtum fa-'aqibu bi-mithli ma 'uqibtum bihi**

If you retaliate, then retaliate with the like of what you were made to suffer.

The permission exists. The surah is not denying the reality of harm or the legitimacy of measured response.

But the word `mithl` is everything here. Like for like. Not escalation. Not revenge swollen by wounded ego. Not injury turned into moral theater for domination.

[PAUSE]

Justice is allowed. تجاوز is not.

Then the moral elevation comes immediately:

**wa la-in sabartum la-huwa khayrun li-al-sabirin**

But if you are patient, it is surely better for the patient.

The surah preserves justice while still leading the soul higher than it.

### 3. **Wa Ma Sabruka illa bi-Allahi**: Real Patience Is Borrowed Strength

Then Allah speaks directly to the Messenger:

**wa isbir**

Be patient.

Then one of the most relieving sentences in the surah:

**wa ma sabruka illa bi-Allahi**

Your patience is only through Allah.

[PAUSE]

This is enormous. It means patience is not a personality trait you manufacture by grim internal force. It is a state given, held, and sustained through Allah.

The ayah removes both arrogance and despair. You cannot boast in sabr as though it came from your own reservoir. And you also need not despair when your reservoir feels empty.

Then the emotional commands:

**wa la tahzan 'alayhim**

Do not grieve over them.

**wa la taku fi dayqin mimma yamkurun**

Do not be in constriction over what they scheme.

`Dayq` is tightness, chest-narrowing pressure. The surah cares not only about the external mission, but about the internal space from which the mission is being carried.

### 4. **Inna Allaha Ma'a alladhina Ittaqaw**: The Surah Ends With Presence, Not Mere Instruction

Then the closing promise:

**inna Allaha ma'a alladhina ittaqaw wa alladhina hum muhsinun**

Surely Allah is with those who have taqwa and those who are people of ihsan.

This is the summit.

`Ma'a` here is not spatial proximity in any crude sense. It is support, aid, care, sustaining nearness, protective companionship.

And it is given to two intertwined kinds of people:

those of `taqwa`, who guard themselves through reverent awareness of Allah,
and those of `ihsan`, who act beautifully and excellently before Him.

[PAUSE]

The surah that early on commanded `al-'adl` and `al-ihsan` now ends by placing divine companionship with the people who live that reality.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The Way We Carry Truth Is Part of the Truth We Carry

Many people think da'wah is mainly about being correct.

The Quran certainly does not permit falsehood or compromise in the content.

But these ayahs show that correctness without wisdom, beauty, and disciplined restraint is not yet Quranic carriage of truth.

[PAUSE]

Some people call harshness sincerity.

Some call humiliation "winning the argument."

Some use religious truth to discharge private anger.

Some become so obsessed with exposing error that they forget the soul they are speaking to.

An-Nahl cuts across all of this. Wisdom. Beautiful exhortation. The best mode of argument. This is not softness at the expense of truth. It is obedience in how truth travels.

That should humble anyone who speaks in Allah's name. The question is not only, "Was I right?" It is also, "Did I speak in a way that obeyed Allah while being right?"

### Theme 2: The Believer's Great Hidden Strength Is That Patience Does Not Rest on the Self Alone

There is an exhaustion that comes from trying to be patient in your own strength.

You feel the wound.

You replay the scheme.

You want to answer.

You are tired of being misread.

You are tired of carrying something beautiful through ugly reactions.

[PAUSE]

Then this ayah comes: `wa ma sabruka illa bi-Allahi`.

That changes everything.

Patience is no longer self-enclosure. It becomes dependence. You are not being asked to build a wall around your pain and survive by willpower. You are being asked to remain open to divine help while refusing to let pain corrupt your manner, your justice, or your trust.

And that is why the final verse matters so much. Allah is with the people of taqwa and ihsan. The servant is not enduring in an empty field. He is enduring under companionship.

## Closing Synthesis

These closing ayahs move from calling, to responding, to enduring, to companionship.

That sequence is the surah's final gift.

Call beautifully.

If you must answer harm, stay inside justice.

Know that patience is better.

Know that you cannot even do that patience except through Allah.

Then rest your heart in the fact that Allah is with the people who fear Him and act with excellence.

[PAUSE]

So An-Nahl ends where a whole life of faith must end:

not merely in information,
not merely in argument,
not merely in rules,
but in a servant learning how to move through the world under Allah.

### Questions to Carry

- When I speak truth, am I also obeying Allah in the way I speak it?
- Where does my desire for retaliation exceed the boundary of `mithl`?
- Do I still imagine patience as something I must generate alone?
- What would it mean for me to live today as someone who really believes Allah is with the people of taqwa and ihsan?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These final ayahs teach that the believer must carry truth with wisdom and beauty, restrain response within justice while preferring patience, and draw endurance from Allah Himself, because divine companionship belongs to the people of taqwa and ihsan.**

### Du'a

*Allahumma, teach us to call to You with wisdom and beauty, keep our responses just and our patience alive through You, protect our hearts from grief and constriction, and make us among the people of taqwa and ihsan whom You are with.*

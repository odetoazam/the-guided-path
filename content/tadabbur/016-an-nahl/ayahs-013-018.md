# He Spread Varied Colors, Opened the Sea, Set Mountains and Signs, Then Asked: Is the Creator Like One Who Does Not Create?

## Surah An-Nahl (16:13-18)

[ayah:16:13] وَمَا ذَرَأَ لَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مُخْتَلِفًا أَلْوَٰنُهُۥٓ ۗ إِنَّ فِى ذَٰلِكَ لَـَٔايَةً لِّقَوْمٍ يَذَّكَّرُونَ

[ayah:16:14] وَهُوَ ٱلَّذِى سَخَّرَ ٱلْبَحْرَ لِتَأْكُلُوا۟ مِنْهُ لَحْمًا طَرِيًّا وَتَسْتَخْرِجُوا۟ مِنْهُ حِلْيَةً تَلْبَسُونَهَا وَتَرَى ٱلْفُلْكَ مَوَاخِرَ فِيهِ وَلِتَبْتَغُوا۟ مِن فَضْلِهِۦ وَلَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ

[ayah:16:15] وَأَلْقَىٰ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ رَوَٰسِىَ أَن تَمِيدَ بِكُمْ وَأَنْهَـٰرًا وَسُبُلًا لَّعَلَّكُمْ تَهْتَدُونَ

**And what He has spread for you in the earth, differing in its colors. Indeed in that is surely a sign for a people who remember. And He is the One who has subjected the sea so that you may eat from it fresh meat and extract from it ornaments which you wear, and you see the ships plowing through it, and so that you may seek of His bounty and so that you may be grateful. Then is He who creates like one who does not create? Will you not then remember? And if you tried to count the favor of Allah, you could not enumerate it. Indeed Allah is surely All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.**

## Introduction

Some passages do not argue with abstract propositions first. They surround you with realities you already live inside until denial starts looking embarrassing.

That is what these ayahs do.

The earth with its varied colors.

The sea with food, adornment, ships, and trade.

Mountains, rivers, roads, landmarks, stars.

Then one devastating question: is the One who creates like one who does not create?

[PAUSE]

The surah is not merely cataloguing blessings. It is constructing moral pressure. The world keeps arriving as evidence, and then Allah asks the heart how it can keep distributing worship irrationally after walking through so much creation and so much care.

The hook of the passage is this: **An-Nahl teaches that creation is not only a field of enjoyment but a courtroom of tawhid, where every varied color, safe route, sea-born gift, and orienting sign testifies that the Creator cannot be equated with what creates nothing at all.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Mukhtalifan Alwanuhu**: Difference in Creation Is Meant to Awaken Remembrance

The passage begins:

**wa ma dhara'a lakum fi al-ardi mukhtalifan alwanuhu**

And what He spread for you in the earth, differing in its colors.

[PAUSE]

The word **dhara'a** carries the sense of scattering forth, spreading, bringing into multiplied existence. Then comes **mukhtalifan alwanuhu**: its colors differing, its varieties diverse.

This is a beautiful Quranic move. Difference is not presented as noise. It is presented as sign.

The earth is not monotonous. Fruits, animals, minerals, flowers, soils, textures, shades, seasons of appearance: all of this variation is meant to do inward work on the human being.

That is why the ayah closes with:

**li-qawmin yadhdhakkarun**

for a people who remember.

Remembrance here is not bare recollection. It is moral waking-up. The world is varied enough to shake the heart out of heedlessness if the heart lets itself be taught.

### 2. **Sakhkhara al-Bahr ... Mawakhira**: The Sea Is Not Only Powerful; It Has Been Made Serviceable

Then:

**wa huwa alladhi sakhkhara al-bahr**

He is the One who subjected the sea.

[PAUSE]

The sea is not naturally tame. It is immense, unstable, dangerous, beyond human mastery in itself. Yet the ayah speaks of it as **musakhkhar**, subjected, made serviceable by Allah.

Then the gifts unfold:

fresh meat,

ornament extracted and worn,

ships seen plowing through it,

the seeking of Allah's bounty through movement and trade,

and finally gratitude.

The word **mawakhira** for the ships is vivid: vessels cleaving, cutting, moving through the water's resistance. The sight itself becomes a sign that what looks overwhelming has been made traversable by divine permission.

### 3. **Afa-man Yakhluqu Ka-man La Yakhluqu**: After the Signs, the Argument Becomes Almost Humiliatingly Simple

Then the whole passage tightens into a question:

**a-fa-man yakhluqu ka-man la yakhluqu**

Is the one who creates like one who does not create?

[PAUSE]

The question is so simple it is almost humiliating. After all the previous ayahs, the surah does not offer a dense philosophical syllogism. It asks an elementary question whose answer should be obvious.

That simplicity is itself part of the rebuke. Shirk is not merely a difficult theological puzzle. Often it is a refusal to honor what is plain.

Then the closure:

**wa in ta'uddu ni'mata Allahi la tuhsuha**

If you tried to count the favor of Allah, you could not enumerate it.

The argument moves from one decisive difference to countless blessings. The Creator is unlike the non-creator not only because He creates, but because His favors overflow the servant's capacity even to count them properly.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Human Beings Often Enjoy Creation While Refusing the Conclusion Creation Is Pressing on Them

People love the sea without loving the Lord of the sea.

They enjoy food, beauty, movement, stability, maps, stars, landscapes, and arrival.

They build lives out of the dependability of a world they did not make.

And still many resist the simplest conclusion: the One who originated, ordered, and continues to sustain all this has the sole claim to worship.

[PAUSE]

This passage exposes that split with force. It does not let us consume reality without interpreting it. Every benefit becomes a question. Every route becomes a witness. Every sign of ease becomes a pressure against ingratitude.

One reason shirk is so ugly in the Quran is precisely that it happens in a universe already saturated with reminders. The problem is not lack of evidence. The problem is moral refusal to let evidence complete its journey into worship.

### Theme 2: We Are Too Small to Count Allah's Blessings, Yet He Meets Our Incomplete Gratitude With Forgiveness and Mercy

The closing is deeply tender:

**inna Allaha la-ghafurun rahim**

Indeed Allah is surely forgiving, merciful.

[PAUSE]

That may sound surprising after such a forceful tawhid argument, but it is exactly right. Allah knows the servant cannot fully enumerate, much less fully repay, His favors.

So the point is not that the believer must achieve mathematically perfect gratitude before Allah will be gentle with him. The point is that the servant should be humbled by the impossibility of total repayment and moved into ongoing gratitude, tawhid, repentance, and dependence.

This protects us from two distortions.

One is heedlessness: acting as though the blessings are small and countable and self-explanatory.

The other is despair: feeling that because we can never thank Allah adequately, the whole effort is pointless.

The ayah allows neither. You cannot count them. But your Lord is forgiving and merciful. So keep returning. Keep thanking. Keep remembering. Keep refusing to distribute His rights to what creates nothing.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask which blessings in your life have become so normal that they no longer argue for Allah inside you. Which routes, securities, provisions, and beauties have become background instead of evidence? And when was the last time the sheer uncountability of Allah's favor made you more humble rather than more numb?

## Closing Synthesis

An-Nahl spreads before the listener the earth's varied colors, the sea's food and adornment and commerce, the stability of mountains, the gift of rivers and roads, the orientation of landmarks and stars, then gathers all of that into one cutting question about the difference between the Creator and what creates nothing, before ending with the servant's incapacity to count Allah's blessings and Allah's own forgiveness and mercy.

[PAUSE]

The architecture is brilliant: wonder, usefulness, orientation, argument, humility. Creation is not left as scenery. It is turned into remembrance, gratitude, and a tawhid question simple enough that only stubbornness can resist it.

### Questions to Carry

- Which repeated blessings in your life no longer feel like arguments for Allah because you have grown too used to them?
- Are you letting creation remain scenery, or are you allowing it to complete its testimony inside your worship?
- What false objects of dependence in your life are being exposed by the question: "Is the one who creates like one who does not create?"
- Does the uncountability of Allah's favors make you more grateful and humble, or have you become strangely numb inside abundance?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs turn the world into a tawhid argument, teaching that every sign of provision, orientation, and beauty presses the heart toward the obvious truth that the Creator cannot be equated with what creates nothing, and that His favors overflow our ability to count them.**

### Du'a

*Allahumma, do not let Your signs become ordinary to us. Make the earth, the sea, the roads, the sky, and every repeated ease a witness that returns our hearts to tawhid. Humble us before the uncountability of Your favors, forgive our poor gratitude, and keep us turning back to You with remembering hearts.*

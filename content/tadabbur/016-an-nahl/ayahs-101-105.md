# When Revelation Is Accused of Fabrication, the Quran Turns the Charge Back on Its Deniers

## Surah An-Nahl (16:101-105)

[ayah:16:101] وَإِذَا بَدَّلْنَآ ءَايَةً مَّكَانَ ءَايَةٍ ۙ وَٱللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ بِمَا يُنَزِّلُ قَالُوٓا۟ إِنَّمَآ أَنتَ مُفْتَرٍۭ ۚ بَلْ أَكْثَرُهُمْ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ

[ayah:16:102] قُلْ نَزَّلَهُۥ رُوحُ ٱلْقُدُسِ مِن رَّبِّكَ بِٱلْحَقِّ لِيُثَبِّتَ ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ وَهُدًى وَبُشْرَىٰ لِلْمُسْلِمِينَ

[ayah:16:103] وَلَقَدْ نَعْلَمُ أَنَّهُمْ يَقُولُونَ إِنَّمَا يُعَلِّمُهُۥ بَشَرٌ ۗ لِّسَانُ ٱلَّذِى يُلْحِدُونَ إِلَيْهِ أَعْجَمِىٌّ وَهَـٰذَا لِسَانٌ عَرَبِىٌّ مُّبِينٌ

[ayah:16:104] إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِـَٔايَـٰتِ ٱللَّهِ لَا يَهْدِيهِمُ ٱللَّهُ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ

[ayah:16:105] إِنَّمَا يَفْتَرِى ٱلْكَذِبَ ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِـَٔايَـٰتِ ٱللَّهِ ۖ وَأُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْكَـٰذِبُونَ

*Wa idha baddalna ayatan makana ayah, wa Allahu a'lamu bima yunazzil, qalu innama anta muftar. Bal aktharuhum la ya'lamun.*

*Inna alladhina la yu'minuna bi-ayati Allahi la yahdihimu Allah wa lahum 'adhabun alim.*

**And when We replace one verse with another verse, and Allah knows best what He sends down, they say, "You are only a fabricator." Rather, most of them do not know. Say: The Holy Spirit has brought it down from your Lord in truth, to make firm those who believe, and as guidance and good news for the Muslims. And We certainly know that they say, "It is only a human being who teaches him." The tongue of the one they point toward is foreign, while this is a clear Arabic tongue. Surely those who do not believe in the signs of Allah, Allah will not guide them, and for them is a painful punishment. Only those who do not believe in the signs of Allah fabricate lies, and they are truly the liars.**

## Introduction

When a heart does not want revelation, it usually does not only refuse it.

It tries to explain it away.

[PAUSE]

That is what this passage unveils.

The Quran is not being met here by a careful listener wrestling honestly with meaning. It is being met by a threatened listener searching for a story that will let him dismiss it. If a ruling changes, they say that proves fabrication. If the Quran's language overwhelms them, they say a human must be feeding it to Muhammad. If its force unsettles them, they do not examine their resistance. They attack the source.

And the surah answers each of these evasions with remarkable calm. Allah knows best what He sends down. The Quran was brought by `Ruh al-Qudus` from your Lord in truth. It stabilizes believers rather than confusing them. The person they gesture toward has a foreign tongue, while this Quran stands as manifest Arabic. Then the passage does something morally devastating: it returns the accusation to its rightful owners. The real fabricators are not the ones carrying revelation, but the ones refusing Allah's signs.

The hook of the passage is this: **An-Nahl teaches that when people cannot bear the authority of revelation, they often invent alternative explanations for it, but the Quran strips those explanations away and shows that the deeper issue is not textual confusion but a heart already committed against belief.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Baddalna Ayatan Makana Ayah**: Changed Rulings Do Not Mean Changed Truth

The passage opens with a controversy:

**wa idha baddalna ayatan makana ayah**

When We replace one ayah in the place of another ayah.

[PAUSE]

The key thing is that the subject is Allah: `baddalna` - We replaced. The shift is not evidence of prophetic improvisation. It is evidence of divine governance over revelation.

Then comes the settling clause:

**wa Allahu a'lamu bima yunazzil**

And Allah knows best what He sends down.

That line is the theological center of the response. The One who reveals is also the One who knows the right timing, sequence, burden, and wisdom of what is revealed. The problem is not with revelation changing in administration. The problem is with listeners imagining that divine wisdom must look static in order to be trustworthy.

But they answer:

**innama anta muftar**

You are only a fabricator.

The accusation is quick because the soul under threat often chooses suspicion over submission. Then the ayah closes:

**bal aktharuhum la ya'lamun**

Rather, most of them do not know.

Their problem is not excess insight. It is ignorance masquerading as critique.

### 2. **Ruhu al-Qudus ... Li-Yuthabbita**: Revelation Comes Not to Destabilize the Believer but to Make Him Firm

Then Allah commands the Prophet:

**qul nazzalahu Ruhu al-Qudusi min rabbika bi-al-haqq**

Say: The Holy Spirit brought it down from your Lord in truth.

The tafsir here is clear: `Ruhu al-Qudus` is Jibril. So the Quran names both the heavenly carrier and the divine source, not to satisfy curiosity but to close the imagined human chain the deniers are trying to invent.

[PAUSE]

Then comes the purpose:

**li-yuthabbita alladhina amanu**

so that He may make firm those who believe.

This is profound. The enemies of revelation describe it as unstable, borrowed, suspicious. Allah describes it as stabilizing.

`Yuthabbita` is to make firm, steady, settled, rooted. Revelation does not merely inform the believer. It secures him.

Then the verse continues:

**wa hudan wa bushra li-al-muslimin**

and guidance and glad tidings for the Muslims.

So the Quran is not only defended as authentic. It is described by what it does in the receptive heart: it steadies, guides, and gladdens.

### 3. **Lisan ... A'jamiyyun / Lisan 'Arabiyyun Mubin**: The Excuse Collapses Under the Weight of the Quran's Own Speech

Then the surah exposes another accusation:

**innama yu'allimuhu bashar**

A human being teaches him.

Allah does not deny knowing the claim:

**wa la-qad na'lamu annahum yaqulun**

We certainly know that they say...

That alone is a mercy. Revelation is answering slander while making clear that the slander never escaped divine notice.

Then comes the devastating rebuttal:

**lisanu alladhi yulhiduna ilayhi a'jamiyyun wa hadha lisanun 'arabiyyun mubin**

The tongue of the one they incline toward is foreign, while this is a clear Arabic tongue.

[PAUSE]

`Yulhiduna ilayhi` has the feel of deviation, slanting, leaning away from the truth toward a crooked explanation. And the reply is elegantly obvious. The alleged source lacks the very linguistic medium in which the miracle stands manifest.

`Lisan` matters here. This is not merely vocabulary. It is expressive power, linguistic system, mode of articulation. The Quran's Arabic clarity is itself part of the proof.

`Mubin` returns again. Clear, clarifying, self-manifesting. The text carries its own refutation.

### 4. **Innama Yaftari al-Kadhiba**: Revelation Reverses the Moral Accusation

Then the passage reaches its sharpest turn.

First:

**inna alladhina la yu'minuna bi-ayati Allahi la yahdihimu Allah**

Those who do not believe in Allah's signs, Allah does not guide them.

This is not a random denial of grace. It is the spiritual consequence of persistent refusal.

Then:

**innama yaftari al-kadhiba alladhina la yu'minuna bi-ayati Allah**

Only those who do not believe in Allah's signs fabricate lies.

The charge is reversed.

[PAUSE]

The ones calling the Prophet a fabricator turn out to be the fabricators. Not because they merely made a mistaken inference, but because their entire opposition required invented explanations to protect them from what was plain.

And the verse seals it:

**wa ula'ika humu al-kadhibun**

Those are the liars.

The grammar gives them the label they tried to place on revelation itself.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: A Resistant Heart Often Prefers Alternative Explanations to Surrender

One of the most subtle forms of spiritual avoidance is not direct rejection, but explanatory deflection.

We do not say, "This is true and I refuse it."

We say, "There must be another reason for this."

[PAUSE]

Maybe the speaker is manipulative.

Maybe the text is derivative.

Maybe the change in application proves inconsistency.

Maybe the power I feel is just rhetoric.

Maybe the discomfort is cultural rather than moral.

Maybe the problem is not my heart but the source itself.

This passage reveals that such moves are ancient. The deniers around the Prophet did not merely argue details. They tried to build an alternate story for revelation that would let them keep distance without admitting rebellion.

And we are not as far from them as we might like to think. Whenever truth presses us and our first instinct is to search for a narrative that lowers its claim on us, we are standing dangerously close to the same pattern.

The believer must therefore learn to distrust not only obvious denial, but also the mind's talent for manufacturing explanations that protect the ego from surrender.

### Theme 2: The Same Revelation That Agitates the Deniers Steadies the Believers

There is a beautiful contrast in this passage.

The same Quran that provokes accusation in one heart produces `thabat` in another.

That matters.

[PAUSE]

It means revelation does not affect everyone in the same way because the decisive variable is not only the text, but the state of the receiver.

To the threatened ego, revelation feels destabilizing because it interrupts self-rule.

To the believer, revelation feels stabilizing because it reconnects him to reality.

That is why the Quran can be guidance, glad tidings, and firmness all at once. It is not merely a set of propositions to inspect. It is a divine address that settles the heart precisely by placing it under something higher than its own confusion.

And this should change how we come to the Quran. Not as critics searching for a safe distance, but as needy servants asking to be made firm. The believer does not merely ask, "What does this text mean?" He also asks, "How can this text stabilize my heart before Allah?"

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs begin with charges of replacement and end with a verdict about lying.

That movement is exact.

The deniers see changed rulings, heavenly revelation, linguistic miracle, and moral authority, and still reach for human explanations that would empty all of it of divine claim.

But the surah patiently strips each layer away.

[PAUSE]

Allah knows best what He sends down.

Jibril brought it from your Lord in truth.

Its purpose is to steady the believers.

Its Arabic clarity exposes the poverty of the alternative story.

And in the end, the lie returns to the liar.

### Questions to Carry

- When truth unsettles me, do I examine my heart first, or do I rush to construct a safer explanation?
- Have I learned to come to the Quran asking to be made firm, or mostly to confirm what I already prefer?
- What accusations or suspicions about revelation become attractive only when obedience feels costly?
- In my own life, where has divine speech clarified, guided, and stabilized me when my soul was scattered?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs show that revelation's deniers often invent explanations to escape its claim, while the Quran answers that it comes from Allah through Jibril in truth, steadies believers, and leaves the charge of fabrication resting finally on those who refuse Allah's signs.**

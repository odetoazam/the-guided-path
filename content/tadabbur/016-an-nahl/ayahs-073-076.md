# Do Not Give Allah Equals: The Quran Breaks False Comparison With Two Parables

## Surah An-Nahl (16:73-76)

[ayah:16:73] وَيَعْبُدُونَ مِن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ مَا لَا يَمْلِكُ لَهُمْ رِزْقًا مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ شَيْـًٔا وَلَا يَسْتَطِيعُونَ

## Introduction

Sometimes the Quran argues.

Sometimes it simply places two things side by side until the mind is embarrassed that it ever treated them as comparable.

[PAUSE]

That is what happens here.

The passage begins by stating the madness of shirk in direct language: people worship beings that cannot provide a single thing from heaven or earth. Then the surah warns us not to strike comparisons for Allah at all. And immediately after that warning, Allah Himself gives the only comparisons fit for the subject: not comparisons that reduce Him to creation, but parables that expose how absurd the false comparison already was.

An owned slave with no power is set beside one granted good provision and free spending.

A mute, useless burden is set beside one who commands justice and walks a straight path.

The answer is obvious before it is spoken.

The hook of the passage is this: **An-Nahl teaches that shirk survives by pretending the powerless and the all-providing can somehow be treated as equivalents, and the Quran shatters that illusion by forcing the soul to feel the humiliation of false comparison.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Ma La Yamliku Lahum Rizqan**: Shirk Begins by Depending on What Owns Nothing

The opening ayah is ruthlessly plain:

**wa ya'buduna min duni Allahi ma la yamliku lahum rizqan mina al-samawati wa al-ardi shay'an**

They worship besides Allah what possesses for them no provision at all from the heavens or the earth.

[PAUSE]

The issue is not being discussed in mystical abstraction. The surah takes it back to the most practical sign of lordship: provision. Rain, crops, fruits, milk, life, sustenance. The beings they worship control none of it.

Then the verse closes:

**wa la yastati'un**

nor are they able.

Not only do they not own provision. They do not even possess the capacity.

This is the Quran stripping false worship down to impotence.

### 2. **Fa-La Tadribu li-Allahi al-Amthal**: Do Not Manufacture Resemblances for the One Beyond Comparison

Then comes the prohibition:

**fa-la tadribu li-Allahi al-amthal**

So do not strike comparisons for Allah.

[PAUSE]

`Tadribu` is striking, setting forth, laying down. `Al-amthal` here are likenesses, equivalences, analogical pairings. The warning is not anti-thinking. It is anti-reduction. Do not make Allah manageable by bringing Him down into creaturely categories and rivalries.

Then the reason:

**inna Allaha ya'lamu wa antum la ta'lamun**

Surely Allah knows, and you do not know.

That line does two things at once. It humbles the intellect and recenters revelation. You are not in possession of the kind of knowledge that qualifies you to construct likenesses for the One who has no peer.

### 3. **Hal Yastawun**: The Quran Uses the Logic of Non-Equality to Break Shirk

After forbidding false comparisons, Allah gives two revelatory parables.

The first:

**'abdan mamlukan la yaqdiru 'ala shay'**

an owned slave, unable to do anything.

Set beside:

**wa man razaqnahu minna rizqan hasanan fa-huwa yunfiqu minhu sirran wa jahran**

one whom We provided from Ourselves with good provision, and he spends from it privately and openly.

[PAUSE]

Then the humiliating question:

**hal yastawun**

Are they equal?

Of course they are not.

And then:

**al-hamdu li-Allah**

All praise belongs to Allah.

It lands almost like relief after confusion. Praise belongs where non-equality has finally been admitted.

Then the second parable sharpens the matter:

**rajulayni ahaduhuma abkamu la yaqdiru 'ala shay' wa huwa kallun 'ala mawlahu**

two men, one of them mute, unable to do anything, and a burden upon his master.

`Kall` is heavy language. He is a weight on the one responsible for him.

Then:

**aynama yuwajjihhu la ya'ti bi-khayr**

wherever he is directed, he brings no good.

Set beside:

**wa man ya'muru bi-al-'adli wa huwa 'ala siratin mustaqim**

one who commands justice and is himself on a straight path.

[PAUSE]

Again the question is enough. The answer is already built into the contrast.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: False Worship Depends on a Refusal to Follow Obvious Unequal Realities to Their Conclusion

Shirk is not always kept alive by sophisticated philosophy.

Often it survives by emotional habit, inherited symbolism, social loyalty, and a refusal to follow obvious truths all the way through.

[PAUSE]

That is why these parables are so effective. They do not rely on obscure reasoning. They force the soul to admit what it already knows in ordinary life.

The powerless is not equal to the powerful.

The one who owns nothing is not equal to the one who gives.

The one who cannot produce good is not equal to the one who commands justice on a straight path.

Once those truths are admitted at the human level, the whole architecture of shirk starts collapsing under its own embarrassment. The only way to keep false worship alive is to protect it from this clarity.

And that still happens now. People may not bow to carved idols, but they do elevate created things into practical lords: systems, reputations, leaders, desires, markets, ideologies. Then they keep speaking as though these forces can secure, define, save, or provide in an ultimate sense.

The Quran's question still cuts through that fog: `hal yastawun`? Are they equal?

No.

But the soul often lives as though yes.

### Theme 2: The One Who Truly Resembles Divine Guidance Is Marked by Justice and Straightness

The second parable has a beautiful positive side many readers rush past.

It does not only negate the useless figure. It positively describes the sound one:

**ya'muru bi-al-'adl wa huwa 'ala siratin mustaqim**

he commands justice, and he himself is on a straight path.

[PAUSE]

That pairing matters.

Some people speak of justice but do not walk straight.

Some people claim personal rectitude but do not care about justice.

The Quran joins the two. Right command and right path. Outward truth and inward alignment. Speech and direction.

At the human level, this becomes a criterion of trustworthy moral beauty. The more a servant is shaped by Allah's guidance, the less uselessness, incoherence, and dependency of falsehood he carries. He becomes someone whose words point toward justice and whose life does not contradict the road he names.

That also deepens the anti-shirk argument. If even at the human level we can tell the difference between a burdened, unproductive impotence and an upright justice-bearing integrity, how could anyone flatten the distinction between Creator and idol, Lord and dependent, Giver and utterly empty claimant?

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs move from declaration to warning to parable with great force.

First, the surah states the truth plainly: the beings worshipped besides Allah own no provision from heaven or earth and are not even capable.

Then it warns: do not strike likenesses for Allah. He knows; you do not.

Then, to heal the diseased imagination, Allah gives two parables that expose what the soul should already know: the powerless is not equal to the powerful, the burdened useless one is not equal to the justice-bearing one on a straight path.

[PAUSE]

So the whole block is teaching us how to think rightly about worship. Tawhid is not only a revealed doctrine. It is also the restoration of proportion. Things must be placed at their true weight. Once that happens, praise returns naturally to Allah alone.

### Questions to Carry

- What created thing in my life am I treating with a weight it does not actually possess?
- Where have I been living as though powerless means could ultimately provide, secure, or define me?
- Does my understanding of guidance include both commanding justice and walking a straight path myself?
- What false equivalence does my heart still protect because it does not want the embarrassment of admitting the truth?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs show that shirk depends on pretending the powerless can stand beside the all-providing Lord, and the Quran destroys that illusion by forcing the soul to admit the obvious non-equality it already recognizes everywhere else.**

### Du'a

*Allahumma, strip false likenesses from our hearts, return us to clear proportion in how we see You and everything besides You, and make us people who love justice, walk a straight path, and give all praise to You alone.*

# You May Yearn for Their Guidance, but Allah Will Raise Them and Say: Be

## Surah An-Nahl (16:37-40)

[ayah:16:37] إِن تَحْرِصْ عَلَىٰ هُدَىٰهُمْ فَإِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِى مَن يُضِلُّ ۖ وَمَا لَهُم مِّن نَّـٰصِرِينَ

[ayah:16:38] وَأَقْسَمُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ جَهْدَ أَيْمَـٰنِهِمْ ۙ لَا يَبْعَثُ ٱللَّهُ مَن يَمُوتُ ۚ بَلَىٰ وَعْدًا عَلَيْهِ حَقًّا وَلَـٰكِنَّ أَكْثَرَ ٱلنَّاسِ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ

[ayah:16:39] لِيُبَيِّنَ لَهُمُ ٱلَّذِى يَخْتَلِفُونَ فِيهِ وَلِيَعْلَمَ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوٓا۟ أَنَّهُمْ كَانُوا۟ كَـٰذِبِينَ

[ayah:16:40] إِنَّمَا قَوْلُنَا لِشَىْءٍ إِذَآ أَرَدْنَـٰهُ أَن نَّقُولَ لَهُۥ كُن فَيَكُونُ

*Wa aqsamu bi-Allahi jahda aymanihim la yab'athu Allahu man yamut. Bala wa'dan 'alayhi haqqan wa lakinna akthara al-nasi la ya'lamun.*

**If you are eager for their guidance, then indeed Allah does not guide the one He lets go astray, and they will have no helpers. And they swore by Allah with the strongest of their oaths, "Allah will not resurrect one who dies." Yes indeed. It is a true promise binding upon Him, but most people do not know. So that He may make clear to them that over which they differ, and so that those who disbelieved may know that they were liars. Our word to a thing, when We intend it, is only that We say to it, "Be," and it is.**

## Introduction

There is a kind of grief that comes from wanting truth for someone who does not want it for himself.

You explain.

You warn.

You hope.

You watch denial harden anyway.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs begin inside that pain. The Prophet is told that even if he is intensely eager for their guidance, Allah does not guide the one who persists into misguidance. Then the surah turns immediately to one of the deniers' boldest claims: their strongest oaths are used not to confess truth but to swear that Allah will never resurrect the dead.

The answer is not only that resurrection is true. The answer is why it is true. It will come so disputed matters are made plain and so the liars know they were liars. Then the surah closes by lifting the whole question out of human scale. Resurrection feels impossible only to creatures. As for Allah, His command to a thing, when He intends it, is simply: **kun fayakun**.

The hook of the passage is this: **An-Nahl teaches that guidance remains in Allah's hand, resurrection is certain because truth must be unveiled and lying exposed, and what seems impossible to human weakness is effortless to the Lord whose command is simply "Be."**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **In Tahris 'Ala Hudahum**: The Quran Names the Prophet's Eagerness Without Letting It Become Burden

The passage opens:

**in tahris 'ala hudahum**

If you are eager for their guidance.

[PAUSE]

The verb `tahris` carries real intensity. This is not casual preference. It is concern that leans forward, concern that strains, concern that wants the other person rescued. The ayah does not rebuke that concern as though mercy itself were the problem. It acknowledges it.

That matters, because one of the quiet mercies of the Quran is that it lets the Messenger's human ache remain visible. He does not become prophetic by becoming emotionally flat. He wants guidance for people who are refusing it.

But then comes the boundary:

**fa-inna Allaha la yahdi man yudill**

Allah does not guide the one He lets go astray.

The structure is sobering. `Hudahum` at the beginning is what the Prophet longs for them. `La yahdi` in the answer shows that the matter does not finally sit in prophetic yearning. It sits with Allah's just guidance.

This does not mean human da'wah is meaningless. The whole surah has been insisting on prophetic conveyance. It means that no messenger, teacher, or loved one can force the opening of a heart that keeps refusing what has been made clear.

Then the ayah seals the abandonment of false hopes:

**wa ma lahum min nasirin**

They will have no helpers.

[PAUSE]

Once a person has insisted on standing outside guidance, no protective network remains that can shield him from truth at the final unveiling.

### 2. **Aqsamu Billahi Jahda Aymanihim**: They Use Maximum Oath to Protect Maximum Falsehood

Then the surah says:

**wa aqsamu billahi jahda aymanihim**

They swore by Allah with the utmost force of their oaths.

[PAUSE]

The wording is striking. `Jahda aymanihim` suggests the strongest, most exerted, most emphatic use of oath-language available to them. But what do they spend that seriousness on?

**la yab'athu Allahu man yamut**

Allah will not resurrect one who dies.

This is part of the Quran's moral exposure of the human being. People do not only lie lightly. Sometimes they recruit their most solemn language to defend their deepest falsehood.

And notice the irony: they swear by Allah in order to deny one of Allah's decisive acts.

Then comes the Quran's answer:

**bala**

Yes indeed.

One word, and the whole denial collapses.

Then:

**wa'dan 'alayhi haqqan**

a promise binding upon Him, true and real.

[PAUSE]

This is not resurrection as hypothetical possibility. It is resurrection as divine promise. The ayah wants certainty to replace scoffing. What they denied with their strongest oath, Allah affirms with His promise.

Yet the verse ends:

**wa lakinna akthara al-nasi la ya'lamun**

but most people do not know.

Their problem is not that Allah failed to speak. Their problem is that human beings often remain ignorant of realities larger than their current sensory confidence.

### 3. **Li-Yubayyina ... wa Li-Ya'lama ... Kun Fayakun**: Resurrection Clarifies, Exposes, and Costs Allah Nothing

Then the passage tells us why resurrection happens:

**li-yubayyina lahumu alladhi yakhtalifuna fihi**

so that He may make clear to them what they used to differ about.

[PAUSE]

This is profound. Resurrection is not only for recompense in a narrow sense. It is also for disclosure. Human life is full of disputes protected by fog: about truth, worship, accountability, revelation, the unseen, the meaning of obedience, and whether the mockers were actually right all along. The Hereafter does not leave those arguments suspended forever. Allah clarifies them.

Then:

**wa li-ya'lama alladhina kafaru annahum kanu kadhibin**

so that those who disbelieved may know that they were liars.

The final knowledge they refused in dunya becomes unavoidable in akhirah. Not merely that they were mistaken. That they were lying.

This is morally heavier than intellectual error. It suggests not innocent confusion alone, but active resistance to truth.

Then the closing ayah lifts us above creaturely difficulty:

**innama qawluna li-shay'in idha aradnahu an naqula lahu kun fa-yakun**

[PAUSE]

Our word to a thing, when We intend it, is only that We say to it, "Be," and it is.

The verse does not invite fantasy about mechanism. It dismantles the very scale by which humans judge divine action. We think in terms of process, effort, delay, and material limitation. The ayah answers from the side of divine command. The issue is not how hard resurrection looks from below. The issue is who is commanding from above.

For the One who created first, to recreate is not a strain.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: You Can Love Someone's Guidance Without Owning the Result

This passage is deeply merciful for anyone carrying da'wah grief.

There is a pain that comes when someone you love keeps refusing what is good for him. A parent feels it. A spouse feels it. A teacher feels it. Anyone who has tried to call another person gently toward Allah knows the ache of watching clarity arrive and then be pushed away.

[PAUSE]

The ayah does not mock that ache. It names eagerness. It acknowledges that wanting guidance for another person can become intense.

But it also frees the servant from a burden he was never given. You are commanded to convey, invite, embody, repeat, and pray. You are not given the key to another person's heart.

This matters spiritually, because without that boundary the caller can become crushed by outcomes, inflated by outcomes, or manipulative in pursuit of outcomes. The Quran protects the da'i from all three. Guidance is from Allah. Clear conveyance is from the messenger.

That balance is still ours. We should care deeply. We should not pretend indifference is piety. But our care must remain servanthood, not control.

### Theme 2: People Often Use Their Most Serious Language in Service of Their Deepest Denials

The deniers swear by Allah with the strongest of their oaths that resurrection will never happen.

This should sober us.

Intensity is not proof of truth.

Confidence is not proof of truth.

Religious vocabulary is not proof of truth.

[PAUSE]

Human beings can be passionately wrong.

We can use solemn tones, emphatic rhetoric, sacred phrases, even divine names, while defending something false. That is part of why humility matters so much in matters of creed and akhirah. The ego loves certainty when certainty serves its appetites.

And perhaps this is one wisdom in the verse's contrast. Their strongest oath says: resurrection will not happen. Allah's answer is not another human oath. It is His promise.

The believer survives confusion by learning whose word carries the real weight.

### Theme 3: Resurrection Is Necessary Because Truth Must Be Publicly Uncovered

Many people imagine the Hereafter mostly as a place of reward and punishment. It is that. But this passage adds another dimension: the Hereafter is where truth is clarified after history leaves so much apparently unresolved.

People die while major questions still look open.

Was revelation really true?

Did obedience matter?

Were the mockers actually cleverer than the believers?

Did injustice really win?

Was the unseen merely projection?

[PAUSE]

The ayah answers that resurrection comes `li-yubayyina` - to make clear.

This means Allah does not leave the moral and theological confusion of dunya as the final condition of reality. The unresolved will be resolved. The disputed will be clarified. The liar will know he lied. The truthful will see what they trusted become visible.

That gives the believer a different kind of patience. We do not need every dispute settled here in full. We live, act, worship, and witness under the certainty that Allah has not abandoned truth to permanent ambiguity.

### Theme 4: What Overwhelms Human Imagination Is Effortless for Divine Command

The closing `kun fayakun` is often quoted, but its place here matters.

It comes after denial of resurrection.

So it is not floating abstraction. It is the answer to a mockery.

[PAUSE]

The deniers look at death, decay, and distance in time and say: this cannot be brought back.

Allah answers: your impossibility comes from measuring My action by creaturely standards.

We live by effort. Allah acts by command.

We move through sequence. Allah is not trapped inside sequence.

We need means to produce effects. Allah creates means and effects alike.

This does not mean the believer becomes simplistic. It means the believer refuses to make human limitation the yardstick of divine power.

That has implications far beyond resurrection. Many acts of trust fail because we silently assume that if something looks closed at our scale, it must be closed absolutely. The ayah teaches us to think more reverently. Allah's promise is never restricted by our sense of what looks feasible from below.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs move from the Prophet's grief to Allah's final power in one unbroken line.

The Messenger may long intensely for people's guidance, but guidance remains with Allah and misled people will have no helpers. The deniers then swear with maximum force that resurrection will never happen, and Allah overturns their oath with a promise: yes, it will. Then He tells us why. Resurrection will clarify disputed truth and expose the liars as liars. Finally, the surah closes by dissolving the imagined difficulty altogether: for Allah, when He intends a thing, His command is only "Be," and it is.

[PAUSE]

So the architecture is exact: eagerness, denial, promise, purpose, command. Human yearning does not control hearts. Human oaths do not control reality. Human imagination does not limit divine power. The final word belongs to Allah, who guides justly, raises the dead truly, clarifies disputed truth openly, and brings into being whatever He wills without strain.

### Questions to Carry

- Where am I carrying grief over someone's guidance in a way that forgets the boundary between sincere care and trying to control hearts?
- Have I ever mistaken confidence, strong rhetoric, or sacred language for actual truthfulness?
- What unresolved truth in this world am I being asked to entrust to Allah's final clarification?
- Where has my sense of what is "impossible" quietly become larger than my sense of Allah's power?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs teach that guidance rests with Allah, resurrection is a true divine promise that will clarify disputed truth and expose lying, and what seems impossible to us is effortless for the Lord whose command is only "Be."**

### Du'a

*Allahumma, make us gentle and sincere in calling others without trying to take over what belongs to Your guidance. Give us certainty in the resurrection, humility before Your promise, and trust in Your power when our own imagination reaches its limit. Do not let us be among those who speak boldly against what they will one day see made clear.*

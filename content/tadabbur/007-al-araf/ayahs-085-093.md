*Wa ila Madyana akhahum Shu'ayban. Qala ya qawmi u'budu Allaha ma lakum min ilahin ghayruhu. Qad ja'atkum bayyinatun min rabbikum. Fa-awfu al-kayla wa al-mizana wa la tabkhasu al-nasa ashya'ahum wa la tufsidu fi al-ardi ba'da islahiha. Dhalikum khayrun lakum in kuntum mu'minin.*

*Wa la taq'udu bi-kulli siratin tu'iduna wa tasudduna 'an sabili Allahi man amana bihi wa tabghunaha 'iwajan. Wa-udhkuru idh kuntum qalilan fa-kaththarakum. Wa-unzuru kayfa kana 'aqibatu al-mufsidin.*

*Wa in kana ta'ifatun minkum amanu bi-alladhi ursiltu bihi wa ta'ifatun lam yu'minu fa-isbiru hatta yahkuma Allahu baynana. Wa huwa khayru al-hakimin.*

*Qala al-mala'u alladhina istakbaru min qawmihi la-nukhrijannaka ya Shu'aybu wa alladhina amanu ma'aka min qaryatina aw la-ta'udunna fi millatina. Qala a-wa law kunna karihin.*

*Qadi iftarayna 'ala Allahi kadhiban in 'udna fi millatikum ba'da idh najjana Allahu minha. Wa ma yakunu lana an na'uda fiha illa an yasha'a Allahu rabbuna. Wasi'a rabbuna kulla shay'in 'ilman. 'Ala Allahi tawakkalna. Rabbana iftah baynana wa bayna qawmina bi al-haqqi wa anta khayru al-fatihin.*

*Wa qala al-mala'u alladhina kafaru min qawmihi la'ini ittaba'tum Shu'ayban innakum idhan la-khasirun.*

*Fa-akhadhathumu al-rajfatu fa-asbahu fi darihim jathimin.*

*Alladhina kadhdhabu Shu'ayban ka'an lam yaghnaw fiha. Alladhina kadhdhabu Shu'ayban kanu humu al-khasirin.*

*Fa-tawalla 'anhum wa qala ya qawmi la-qad ablaghtukum risalati rabbi wa nasahtu lakum. Fa-kayfa asa 'ala qawmin kafirin.*

And remember when you were few and He multiplied you, and look how was the end of the corrupters. And if a group among you has believed in that with which I was sent and another group has not believed, then be patient until Allah judges between us, and He is the best of judges." The eminent ones who were arrogant among his people said, "We will surely expel you, O Shu'ayb, and those who believe with you, from our town, unless you return to our creed." He said, "Even if we are unwilling? We would have fabricated a lie against Allah if we returned to your creed after Allah has saved us from it. And it is not for us to return to it except that Allah, our Lord, should will. Our Lord encompasses everything in knowledge. Upon Allah we have relied. Our Lord, decide between us and our people in truth, and You are the best of those who decide." And the eminent ones who disbelieved among his people said, "If you follow Shu'ayb, indeed you would then be losers." So the quake seized them, and they became in their home fallen still. Those who denied Shu'ayb became as if they had never prospered there. Those who denied Shu'ayb were themselves the losers. So he turned away from them and said, "O my people, I had certainly conveyed to you the messages of my Lord and given you sincere advice. So how could I grieve over a disbelieving people?"**

## Introduction

The story of Shu'ayb is one of the clearest places in the Qur'an where tawhid proves that it was never only a matter of abstract belief. The call to worship Allah alone appears side by side with the command to give full measure, honor weight, stop shortchanging people, stop corrupting the earth, and stop occupying public pathways in order to threaten, intimidate, and turn believers away from Allah.

[PAUSE]

That means the surah is exposing a society whose corruption is theological, economic, and political all at once. Their scales are crooked. Their markets are unjust. Their streets are weaponized. Their public moral imagination wants the path of Allah itself bent into crookedness.

[PAUSE]

And when revelation confronts all of that, the elite eventually say what corrupt systems often say when they are running out of arguments: leave, or come back to our way. **Exile or assimilation.** That is the real political offer they make to Shu'ayb and the believers.

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. Tawhid Immediately Becomes Economic Justice

Shu'ayb begins with the familiar call:

*Ya qawmi u'budu Allaha ma lakum min ilahin ghayruhu.*

O my people, worship Allah; you have no god other than Him.

[PAUSE]

But then the passage moves at once into trade:

*Fa-awfu al-kayla wa al-mizan wa la tabkhasu al-nasa ashya'ahum.*

So fulfill the measure and the scale, and do not diminish people in their things.

[PAUSE]

This matters enormously. The Qur'an is teaching that a crooked creed eventually produces crooked scales. If Allah is not obeyed as Lord, people start treating other human beings as available for manipulation. Even ordinary exchange becomes a theater of domination.

The verb *tabkhasu* is powerful. It is not merely "do not make accounting mistakes." It is do not diminish people, do not reduce what is theirs, do not shave off their due because you can get away with it.

### 2. Corruption Includes Public Intimidation

Then the command intensifies:

*Wa la taq'udu bi-kulli siratin tu'iduna wa tasudduna 'an sabili Allahi man amana bihi wa tabghunaha 'iwajan.*

Do not sit on every path, threatening, turning away from Allah's path those who believe, and seeking to make it crooked.

[PAUSE]

This is not private immorality. This is organized pressure. "Sitting on every path" evokes control points, chokepoints, social intimidation, the strategic occupying of spaces where people move and decide. They threaten. They repel. They attempt to deform the perception of the straight path so that it appears bent.

The word *'iwajan* is spiritually revealing. The path is straight, but they want it to look crooked. One of the classic works of corruption is not only to sin, but to narrate righteousness as unreasonable.

### 3. Shu'ayb Models Patience Without Relativism

Then:

*Wa in kana ta'ifatun minkum amanu ... wa ta'ifatun lam yu'minu fa-isbiru hatta yahkuma Allahu baynana.*

If one group among you believed in what I was sent with and another group did not, then be patient until Allah judges between us.

[PAUSE]

This is not moral uncertainty. Shu'ayb is not saying both sides are equally valid. He is saying the believers must not panic. Let the matter be carried before the best of judges. This is patience rooted in certainty, not patience rooted in confusion.

### 4. The Elite Finally Reveal Their Real Terms: Exile or Return

Then the elite say:

*La-nukhrijannaka ... aw la-ta'udunna fi millatina.*

We will certainly expel you ... unless you return to our creed.

[PAUSE]

This is one of the rawest moments in the prophetic stories. When truth threatens the social order, the elites make the issue explicit: either leave the public space, or re-enter our ideological world. The problem was never just a theological disagreement. The problem was that the presence of believing people weakened the totality of the dominant order.

And Shu'ayb's response is magnificent:

*A-wa law kunna karihin.*

Even if we are unwilling?

That short question exposes the violence in their proposal. A coerced return is not truth. It is domination dressed as communal unity.

### 5. The Response Joins Creed, Humility, and Tawakkul

Then Shu'ayb says:

*Qadi iftarayna 'ala Allahi kadhiban in 'udna fi millatikum ba'da idh najjana Allahu minha.*

We would have fabricated against Allah a lie if we returned to your creed after Allah saved us from it.

[PAUSE]

This is not framed as a mere lifestyle preference. Returning would be *iftira'* - fabrication against Allah. Then comes a beautiful calibration:

*Wa ma yakunu lana an na'uda fiha illa an yasha'a Allahu rabbuna.*

It is not for us to return to it unless Allah our Lord should will.

This is not doubt in truth. It is adab before Allah's absolute will.

Then:

*Wasi'a rabbuna kulla shay'in 'ilman. 'Ala Allahi tawakkalna.*

Our Lord encompasses everything in knowledge. Upon Allah we have relied.

Knowledge and reliance come together. Because He knows fully, we can lean fully.

And then one of the great du'as of the Qur'an:

*Rabbana iftah baynana wa bayna qawmina bi al-haqqi wa anta khayru al-fatihin.*

Our Lord, open between us and our people in truth; You are the best of those who open/decide.

This is a plea for decisive divine judgment, for truth to be made manifest with clarity no human power can obstruct.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Economic Injustice Is a Spiritual Disease Before It Is a Social Problem

Shu'ayb's people are not rebuked only for doctrinal error. They are rebuked for markets built on diminution and streets built on intimidation. The Qur'an teaches here that cheating is not a secondary vice floating beside theology. It is theology worked out in public life.

[PAUSE]

If Allah is not feared, people will underfill scales, underpay labor, manipulate value, and narrate theft as cleverness. Tawhid, by contrast, should show up in honesty where money changes hands and in justice where others are vulnerable to being shortchanged.

### Theme 2: Corrupt Orders Often Demand Either Silence or Assimilation

The threat in 7:88 feels painfully recognizable. "We will expel you, or you return to our creed." This is how systems speak when they cannot bear principled dissent. They do not merely want coexistence. They want absorption. They want the believing remnant either removed or made to rejoin the moral fiction that keeps the system stable.

[PAUSE]

Shu'ayb answers with one of the believer's deepest freedoms: if returning would be a lie against Allah, then it does not become true because the majority demands it, the elite threaten it, or the town organizes itself around it.

### Theme 3: The True Losers May Look Secure Right Up Until the End

The elites explicitly warn the people:

*Innakum idhan la-khasirun.*

You will then be losers.

[PAUSE]

But when the story closes, the Qur'an turns that word back on them:

*Alladhina kadhdhabu Shu'ayban kanu humu al-khasirin.*

Those who denied Shu'ayb were themselves the losers.

This reversal matters. Corrupt power is often loudest in defining who the losers are. The Qur'an repeatedly teaches us not to accept that verdict too quickly.

## Closing Synthesis

Shu'ayb's passage gathers an entire society into view: false worship, economic cheating, public intimidation, distortion of the path, coercive communal pressure, and elite rhetoric that calls believers losers.

[PAUSE]

Against all of that stand a few luminous elements: fulfilled measure, refusal to diminish people, patience under pressure, refusal to return to falsehood, trust in Allah's encompassing knowledge, and the prayer that Allah Himself open the matter in truth.

[PAUSE]

Then the quake comes, the social confidence collapses, and the deniers are described as if they had never flourished there at all. The very permanence they weaponized turns out to be fragile dust.

[PAUSE]

And Shu'ayb's final line is heavy with both fulfillment and release: I conveyed. I advised. So how could I keep grieving over a disbelieving people?

[PAUSE]

May Allah make our worship visible in justice, our transactions clean, our hearts resistant to coercive falsehood, and our trust anchored in the One who opens between people in truth.

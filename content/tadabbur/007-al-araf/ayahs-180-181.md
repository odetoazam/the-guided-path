# Call Upon Him By Them

## Surah Al-Araf (7:180-181)

[ayah:7:180] وَلِلَّهِ ٱلْأَسْمَآءُ ٱلْحُسْنَىٰ فَٱدْعُوهُ بِهَا ۖ وَذَرُوا۟ ٱلَّذِينَ يُلْحِدُونَ فِىٓ أَسْمَـٰٓئِهِۦ ۚ سَيُجْزَوْنَ مَا كَانُوا۟ يَعْمَلُونَ

[ayah:7:181] وَمِمَّنْ خَلَقْنَآ أُمَّةٌ يَهْدُونَ بِٱلْحَقِّ وَبِهِۦ يَعْدِلُونَ

*Wa lillahi al-asma'u al-husna fa-id'uhu biha wa dharu alladhina yulhiduna fi asma'ihi. Sayujzawna ma kanu ya'malun.*

*Wa mimman khalaqna ummatun yahduna bi-al-haqqi wa bihi ya'dilun.*

**And to Allah belong the most beautiful names, so call upon Him by them, and leave those who deviate regarding His names. They will be repaid for what they used to do. And among those We created is a community who guide by the truth and by it act with justice.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

After speaking about hearts that do not understand, eyes that do not see, and ears that do not hear, the surah does something merciful.

It turns our attention away from the ruined creature and back to Allah Himself. It is as though the Qur'an is saying: if heedlessness is so deep, then do not begin by circling endlessly around the heedless self. Begin by turning toward the One whose names are all beautiful. Call upon Him by them. Then, before despair can settle in, the surah adds that among creation there is still a community that guides by truth and judges by it with justice.

[PAUSE]

So these two ayahs move from divine orientation to human embodiment. They tell us where the heart must turn upward and what truthful life must look like outwardly.

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Names Belong to Allah Before They Serve Us

Ayah 180 begins:

*Wa lillahi al-asma'u al-husna.*

And to Allah belong the most beautiful names.

[PAUSE]

The verse starts with ownership. *Lillahi.* To Allah belong. Before the names comfort us, inspire us, or appear in our dhikr, they are His. That matters because it protects us from treating the divine names as spiritual decorations or emotional vocabulary that we may handle however we wish. The names belong to the Named.

Then comes *al-asma'u al-husna* - the names, the most beautiful. Not merely beautiful in sound, but beautiful in truth, perfection, and fittingness. They do not contain distortion, deficiency, or ugliness. To know Allah through His names is therefore to be turned toward perfection rather than projection.

Then the command:

*Fa-id'uhu biha.*

So call upon Him by them.

This is practical immediately. The ayah does not say merely: know them, list them, or admire them. It says: call upon Him by them. The names are given for invocation. Ask the Merciful through His mercy. Seek forgiveness through the Forgiving. Seek opening through the Opener. Seek protection through the Protector. The names are not detached theology. They are doors of relationship.

### 2. Deviation in the Names Is a Form of Crookedness

The ayah continues:

*Wa dharu alladhina yulhiduna fi asma'ihi.*

And leave those who deviate regarding His names.

[PAUSE]

The verb *yulhiduna* is strong. Its root carries the sense of inclining away, veering off into a side-path, bending from the straight line. So the problem here is not only open mockery, though that is certainly included. It is every form of crookedness in relation to Allah's names - twisting them, emptying them, misusing them, denying what they mean, or handling them without reverence.

This is striking in context. The surah has just described people whose inner faculties fail to receive truth. Now it names one of the places that failure shows up most seriously: in how people speak about Allah Himself. When the heart becomes crooked, eventually even the divine names are not left untouched.

Then comes the warning:

*Sayujzawna ma kanu ya'malun.*

They will be repaid for what they used to do.

The ayah closes on action. Not merely speech. That tells us deviation in the names is not treated as an abstract intellectual slip. It belongs to the moral register. It is something one does.

### 3. There Is Still a Community That Lives by Truth

Then ayah 181 answers the darkness with a beautiful sentence:

*Wa mimman khalaqna ummatun yahduna bi-al-haqqi wa bihi ya'dilun.*

And among those We created is a community who guide by the truth and by it act with justice.

[PAUSE]

The phrasing matters. *Wa mimman khalaqna* - and among those We created. That means the surah is not imagining an angelic class outside ordinary human creation. These are created beings within the same world of trial, pressure, and possible heedlessness. Yet some of them become an ummah shaped by truth.

And what do they do? First: *yahduna bi-al-haqq* - they guide by the truth. Not by charisma, tribe, novelty, pressure, or mere sincerity. The instrument and standard of their guidance is *al-haqq*.

Then: *wa bihi ya'dilun* - and by it they act justly. The pronoun is powerful. By it. By the truth itself they do justice. Justice here is not free-floating moral instinct detached from revelation. Nor is truth left in the realm of speech alone. The same truth that guides becomes the measure of judgment.

That is a remarkable pairing. The ayah does not separate da'wah from fairness, or correct belief from upright dealing. A truthful community is one that both points by the truth and adjudicates by the truth.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The Cure for Heedlessness Begins With the Right Turning Toward Allah

The previous passage exposed the tragedy of human faculties left unused for truth. These ayahs answer that tragedy by redirecting the heart toward Allah's names.

That is deeply important. Spiritual recovery is not sustained by self-analysis alone. If a person stares only at his own heedlessness, he may become either numb or despairing. The Qur'an instead teaches a turning: look to Allah as He has named Himself. Call on Him by those names. Let the heart be educated by revelation about who its Lord is.

[PAUSE]

This also rescues us from inventing a god in our own emotional image. A person in fear imagines only harshness. A person in indulgence imagines only softness. A person in pain imagines only distance. But the names Allah gives Himself correct our projections. They teach us how to ask, how to hope, how to fear rightly, and how to stay inside revealed nearness rather than fantasy.

### Theme 2: Truth Must Become Communal Guidance and Justice, Not Private Sentiment

Ayah 181 is one of the most hopeful lines in this closing section of the surah.

After so many warnings about denial, shedding signs, self-wronging, heedlessness, and deviation, the Qur'an reminds us that there remains a real community among creation that lives differently. Truth does not disappear from the earth. It continues to be carried by people who both guide with it and judge with it.

[PAUSE]

That should sober us. We sometimes reduce truth to private correctness: having the right answer inwardly, belonging to the right camp, holding the right slogans. But this ayah gives a more demanding picture. If truth is real, it must appear in the way a community guides others and the way it deals justly among people. A claim to *al-haqq* that does not generate *'adl* has not yet completed what this ayah describes.

And the order is illuminating. They guide by truth, and by it they do justice. The just community is not improvising morality from mood. It is being governed by something higher than itself.

## Closing Synthesis

These two ayahs answer heedless collapse with orientation and embodiment.

Allah's names are declared the most beautiful, and the servant is commanded to call upon Him by them while leaving those who bend His names into deviation, with a warning that such crookedness will be repaid. Then the surah refuses despair by reminding us that among creation there still exists a community shaped by truth - a people who guide by it and by it establish justice.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: do Allah's names function in my life mainly as information, or as living doors of du'a and return? And if I claim to love truth, where does that truth actually show itself in the way I guide, judge, and deal with people?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the unit lands here: **after exposing the ruin of heedlessness, the surah turns the heart back to Allah through His most beautiful names and then shows that the truthful alternative to deviation is not private admiration alone but a community that is guided by the truth and carries that same truth into justice.**

# They Testified Against Themselves

## Surah Al-Araf (7:37-39)

[ayah:7:37] فَمَنْ أَظْلَمُ مِمَّنِ ٱفْتَرَىٰ عَلَى ٱللَّهِ كَذِبًا أَوْ كَذَّبَ بِـَٔايَـٰتِهِۦٓ ۚ أُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ يَنَالُهُمْ نَصِيبُهُم مِّنَ ٱلْكِتَـٰبِ ۖ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَا جَآءَتْهُمْ رُسُلُنَا يَتَوَفَّوْنَهُمْ قَالُوٓا۟ أَيْنَ مَا كُنتُمْ تَدْعُونَ مِن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ ۖ قَالُوا۟ ضَلُّوا۟ عَنَّا وَشَهِدُوا۟ عَلَىٰٓ أَنفُسِهِمْ أَنَّهُمْ كَانُوا۟ كَـٰفِرِينَ

[ayah:7:38] قَالَ ٱدْخُلُوا۟ فِىٓ أُمَمٍ قَدْ خَلَتْ مِن قَبْلِكُم مِّنَ ٱلْجِنِّ وَٱلْإِنسِ فِى ٱلنَّارِ ۖ كُلَّمَا دَخَلَتْ أُمَّةٌ لَّعَنَتْ أُخْتَهَا ۖ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَا ٱدَّارَكُوا۟ فِيهَا جَمِيعًا قَالَتْ أُخْرَىٰهُمْ لِأُولَىٰهُمْ رَبَّنَا هَـٰٓؤُلَآءِ أَضَلُّونَا فَـَٔاتِهِمْ عَذَابًا ضِعْفًا مِّنَ ٱلنَّارِ ۖ قَالَ لِكُلٍّ ضِعْفٌ وَلَـٰكِن لَّا تَعْلَمُونَ

[ayah:7:39] وَقَالَتْ أُولَىٰهُمْ لِأُخْرَىٰهُمْ فَمَا كَانَ لَكُمْ عَلَيْنَا مِن فَضْلٍ فَذُوقُوا۟ ٱلْعَذَابَ بِمَا كُنتُمْ تَكْسِبُونَ

*Fa-man azlamu mimmani iftara 'ala Allahi kadhiban aw kadhdhaba bi-ayatih. Ula'ika yanaluhum nasibuhum mina al-kitab. Hatta idha ja'at-hum rusuluna yatawaffawnahum qalu ayna ma kuntum tad'una min duni Allah. Qalu dallu 'anna wa shahidu 'ala anfusihim annahum kanu kafirin.*

*Qala ادخلوا fi umamin qad khalat min qablikum mina al-jinni wa al-insi fi al-nar. Kullama dakhalat ummatun la'anat ukhtaha. Hatta idha iddarakuu fiha jami'an qalat ukhrahum li-ulahum rabbana ha'ula'i adalluna fa-atihim 'adhaban di'fan mina al-nar. Qala li-kullin di'fun wa lakin la ta'lamun.*

*Wa qalat ulahum li-ukhrahum fa-ma kana lakum 'alayna min fadlin fa-dhuqu al-'adhaba bima kuntum taksibun.*

**So who is more unjust than one who invents a lie against Allah or denies His signs? Those will attain their portion from the decree until, when Our messengers come to take them in death, they will say, "Where are those you used to call upon besides Allah?" They will say, "They have departed from us," and they will testify against themselves that they were disbelievers. He will say, "Enter among nations which had passed on before you of jinn and mankind into the Fire." Every time a nation enters, it curses its sister until, when they have all overtaken one another therein, the later of them will say about the former of them, "Our Lord, these had led us astray, so give them a double punishment of the Fire." He will say, "For each is double, but you do not know." And the former of them will say to the later of them, "Then you had no favor over us, so taste the punishment for what you used to earn."**

## Introduction

False religion can feel socially powerful in the world. It can gather crowds, habits, slogans, leaders, and whole lineages of imitation. But these ayahs carry us to the moment when all that borrowed solidity collapses.

[PAUSE]

The first collapse happens at death. The ones who used to call on others besides Allah are asked where those objects of devotion are now. The answer is devastating in its brevity: they vanished from us. Then the second collapse happens in the Fire. The communities who once moved together no longer shield each other. They curse each other. Followers blame leaders. Leaders refuse to carry the excuse of the followers. Every false solidarity turns into accusation.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **a lie about Allah may accompany a person through his allotted span in dunya, but when death and the Fire arrive, every false object of trust disappears and every corrupt alliance becomes mutual blame.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Greatest Injustice Is Framed as Lying About Allah and Denying His Signs

The passage opens:

*Fa-man azlamu mimmani iftara 'ala Allahi kadhiban aw kadhdhaba bi-ayatih.*

Who is more unjust than one who invents a lie against Allah or denies His signs?

[PAUSE]

This is not merely private sin. It is distortion at the source: forging falsehood about Allah and then rejecting the truth He actually sent.

Then:

*Ula'ika yanaluhum nasibuhum mina al-kitab.*

Those people receive their share from the decree.

That is a sobering line. Falsehood does not always cut life short immediately. A person may still receive his allotted portion in the world. Time passing is not proof of divine approval.

### 2. Death Exposes the Emptiness of False Invocation

Then:

*Hatta idha ja'at-hum rusuluna yatawaffawnahum...*

Until Our messengers come to take them in death.

[PAUSE]

The question follows:

*Ayna ma kuntum tad'una min duni Allah.*

Where are those you used to call upon besides Allah?

And the answer:

*Qalu dallu 'anna.*

They vanished from us.

The verb is powerful because it reveals that what felt present enough to organize a life cannot remain present enough to help at death.

Then the most terrible honesty:

*Wa shahidu 'ala anfusihim annahum kanu kafirin.*

They testified against themselves.

The case no longer needs argument from outside. Their own selves become witnesses.

### 3. Shared Misguidance Turns Into Shared Cursing

Then the scene enters the Fire:

*Kullama dakhalat ummatun la'anat ukhtaha.*

Every time a nation enters, it curses its sister.

[PAUSE]

*Ukhtaha* - its sister. Even the word suggests prior nearness, a kind of shared lineage or companionship. But in punishment the bond rots into malediction.

Then:

*Qalat ukhrahum li-ulahum rabbana ha'ula'i adalluna.*

The later ones say of the earlier ones: our Lord, these led us astray.

This is the follower's instinct in final form: transfer the moral center upward.

### 4. Yet Shared Influence Does Not Cancel Personal Earning

The divine answer is:

*Li-kullin di'f.*

For each is double.

[PAUSE]

The leaders do not escape. But neither do the followers.

Then the earlier ones answer:

*Fa-ma kana lakum 'alayna min fadl.*

You had no superiority over us.

Meaning: you are not innocent merely because you came after us. You still chose. Then the close:

*Fa-dhuqu al-'adhaba bima kuntum taksibun.*

Taste the punishment for what you used to earn.

The language returns responsibility to earning.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Time in Dunya Can Be Mistaken for Safety

One of the passage's most searching lines is that they receive their portion from the decree. A liar about Allah may continue living, thriving, and taking his share of worldly allotment. That can deceive both him and those watching him.

[PAUSE]

But the ayah teaches that continued provision is not vindication. The decree can continue to unfold for a person even while he is storing up a meeting he has not prepared for. The fact that a false life remains functional is not the same as the fact that it is safe.

### Theme 2: Borrowed Religion Produces Borrowed Excuses

The later communities in the Fire immediately point upward: these are the ones who led us astray. There is truth in influence. Human beings do mislead one another. Yet the surah refuses to let influence become innocence. The leaders are guilty, but the followers are not blank pages.

[PAUSE]

That makes these ayahs painfully relevant. Many souls want a moral arrangement in which the one misled bears no burden because someone else spoke louder, looked more sacred, or came first. But the Qur'an teaches that once a person gives away moral agency, he may also inherit the punishment of having chosen whom to follow.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs strip false religion to its end.

The lie about Allah persists through a person's allotted life. Death comes. The invoked powers disappear. The self testifies. Then the social body of misguidance falls apart in the Fire into cursing, accusation, counter-accusation, and the final restoration of responsibility to what each one earned.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: is there anywhere in my religious life where I am leaning on inherited voices or admired figures in a way that is quietly training my soul to surrender responsibility for what I choose?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **when a person builds life on lies about Allah and borrowed allegiances besides Him, those supports may endure through his worldly allotment, but at death and in the Fire they collapse into disappearance, self-testimony, and mutual blame.**

# My Lord Has Only Forbidden

## Surah Al-Araf (7:33-36)

[ayah:7:33] قُلْ إِنَّمَا حَرَّمَ رَبِّىَ ٱلْفَوَٰحِشَ مَا ظَهَرَ مِنْهَا وَمَا بَطَنَ وَٱلْإِثْمَ وَٱلْبَغْىَ بِغَيْرِ ٱلْحَقِّ وَأَن تُشْرِكُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ مَا لَمْ يُنَزِّلْ بِهِۦ سُلْطَـٰنًا وَأَن تَقُولُوا۟ عَلَى ٱللَّهِ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ

**Say, "My Lord has only forbidden indecencies - what is apparent of them and what is concealed - and sin, and oppression without right, and that you associate with Allah that for which He has not sent down any authority, and that you say about Allah what you do not know." And for every community is an appointed term. So when their term comes, they will neither delay it by an hour nor advance it. O children of Adam, if messengers come to you from among you recounting to you My signs, then whoever is mindful of Allah and reforms - no fear will be upon them, nor will they grieve. But those who deny Our signs and wax arrogant toward them - they are the companions of the Fire; they will abide therein.**

## Introduction

After challenging invented prohibitions, the surah now does something morally cleansing. It names the actual forbidden things.

There is relief in that. Human beings become spiritually confused when religion fills with taboos Allah never made and falls silent where Allah spoke clearly. So the Qur'an gathers the matter with force: these are the things my Lord has forbidden. Then, before we can treat the clarification lightly, the passage reminds us that communities have appointed terms and that messengers will come reciting Allah's signs. The issue is not abstract ethics. It is how a people will respond before their time runs out.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **when Allah names what is truly forbidden, He is not narrowing life arbitrarily but clearing moral space before the arrival of a deadline, and the real divide thereafter is between those who receive the signs with taqwa and reform and those who answer them with denial and arrogance.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Surah Names the Actual Muharramat

The passage opens:

*Innama harrama rabbi...*

My Lord has only forbidden...

[PAUSE]

The structure is restrictive. Not everything people dramatize as sacred boundary is actually one. The ayah is reclaiming moral authority from human invention.

Then the list begins with:

*Al-fawahish ma zahara minha wa ma batana.*

Indecencies, what is outward of them and what is inward.

The sweep is total. Public obscenity and hidden obscenity alike. The sin visible to people and the sin cultivated in secrecy.

Then:

*Wa al-ithm wa al-baghya bi-ghayri al-haqq.*

And sin, and aggressive transgression without right.

The movement is important. The passage does not stop at sexual or shameless corruption. It includes broader moral guilt and wrongful overreach against others.

### 2. Shirk Without Proof and Speech About Allah Without Knowledge Stand at the Summit of Danger

Then:

*Wa an tushriku bi-Allahi ma lam yunazzil bihi sultanan.*

And that you associate with Allah that for which He sent down no authority.

[PAUSE]

Shirk is exposed not only as false worship but as worship without revealed warrant. There is no descending proof for it.

Then the final item:

*Wa an taqulu 'ala Allahi ma la ta'lamun.*

And that you say about Allah what you do not know.

The list closes where the previous passage also warned us to fear most. Speaking about Allah without knowledge becomes a capstone corruption because it can generate and justify all the others.

### 3. Moral Clarity Is Placed Under the Horizon of Appointed Time

Then:

*Wa likulli ummatin ajal.*

For every community is an appointed term.

[PAUSE]

The clarification of right and wrong is not detached from history. Peoples have deadlines. Then:

*Fa-idha ja'a ajaluhum la yasta'khiruna sa'atan wa la yastaqdimun.*

When their term comes, they can neither delay it an hour nor bring it forward.

This should sober every culture that thinks it can bargain with time indefinitely.

### 4. Messengerhood Creates the Human Fork

Then the address returns:

*Ya bani Adam imma ya'tiyannakum rusulun minkum yaqussuna 'alaykum ayati.*

O children of Adam, if messengers come to you from among you recounting to you My signs.

[PAUSE]

The messengers are from among you. The signs are recited to you. Revelation is brought near, humanly accessible, and clearly narrated.

Then the fork:

*Fa-man ittaqa wa aslaha...*

Whoever has taqwa and makes rectification.

Not only inward fear of Allah, but repair. Then the promise:

*Fa-la khawfun 'alayhim wa la hum yahzanun.*

No fear upon them, nor will they grieve.

### 5. The Opposite Is Not Mere Ignorance But Arrogant Rejection

Then the other side:

*Wa alladhina kadhdhabu bi-ayatina wa istakbaru 'anha.*

Those who deny Our signs and act arrogantly toward them.

[PAUSE]

The issue is not lack of exposure to truth here. It is takdhib and istikbar: calling the signs false and standing too large to bow before them.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Moral Confusion Often Begins by Majoring in the Wrong Prohibitions

These ayahs are a mercy because they clear away noise. Religious communities can become obsessed with invented boundaries while tolerating the very things Allah named as forbidden: hidden indecency, aggression, shirk, and reckless speech about Him.

[PAUSE]

The passage forces a reprioritization. Before multiplying taboos, listen to what Allah actually named. That is morally humbling, because it moves the eye from performative scruples toward the deeper corruptions of the heart, the tongue, and the structure of worship.

### Theme 2: The Signs Do Not Leave the Soul Static

Once the messengers come recounting Allah's signs, the human being does not remain in a neutral zone. The passage gives only two destinations. Either taqwa and reform emerge, producing a future without fear and grief, or denial and arrogance harden, leading to abiding fire.

[PAUSE]

That means revelation is not information added to an otherwise unchanged life. It is a dividing mercy. It exposes what a person will do when truth is recited clearly enough to require either surrender or refusal.

## Closing Synthesis

Al-A'raf moves here from false prohibitions to the true ones, from cultural noise to divine clarity, and from clarity to deadline.

My Lord has only forbidden these things. Every community has an appointed term. Messengers come from among you with the signs. Then the road forks: taqwa and reform, or denial and arrogance. That is the moral architecture of the passage.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with these ayahs is to ask: have I let religion train me to fear what people dramatize more than what Allah Himself explicitly named?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **Allah names the real forbidden things so that people may reorder their moral attention before their appointed term arrives, because once the signs are recited the soul moves either toward taqwa and repair or toward arrogant rejection.**

# Take It With Strength

## Surah Al-Araf (7:144-145)

[ayah:7:144] قَالَ يَـٰمُوسَىٰٓ إِنِّى ٱصْطَفَيْتُكَ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ بِرِسَـٰلَـٰتِى وَبِكَلَـٰمِى فَخُذْ مَآ ءَاتَيْتُكَ وَكُن مِّنَ ٱلشَّـٰكِرِينَ

[ayah:7:145] وَكَتَبْنَا لَهُۥ فِى ٱلْأَلْوَاحِ مِن كُلِّ شَىْءٍ مَّوْعِظَةً وَتَفْصِيلًا لِّكُلِّ شَىْءٍ فَخُذْهَا بِقُوَّةٍ وَأْمُرْ قَوْمَكَ يَأْخُذُوا۟ بِأَحْسَنِهَا ۚ سَأُو۟رِيكُمْ دَارَ ٱلْفَـٰسِقِينَ

*Qala ya Musa inni istafaytuka 'ala al-nasi bi-risalati wa bi-kalami fa-khudh ma ataytuka wa kun min al-shakirin.*

*Wa katabna lahu fi al-alwahi min kulli shay'in maw'izhatan wa tafsilan li-kulli shay'. Fa-khudhha bi-quwwatin wa'mur qawmaka ya'khudhu bi-ahsaniha. Sa-urikum dara al-fasiqin.*

**He said, 'O Musa, indeed I have chosen you over the people with My messages and My speech, so take what I have given you and be among the grateful. And We wrote for him upon the tablets of everything an admonition and an explanation for everything, so hold to them with strength and command your people to take the best of them. I will show you the home of the defiantly disobedient.'**

## Introduction

After the mountain, there is recommissioning.

That matters. Musa asks to see Allah, the mountain is leveled, Musa collapses, and he rises in repentance. The next scene is not embarrassment. It is not divine distance. It is Allah speaking again, naming Musa as chosen, and then placing revelation in his hands with renewed command.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs teach us how the Qur'an understands honor. Honor is not a glow around the self. It is a trust that must be carried. Musa is selected by Allah, but the proof of that selection is not that he gets to cherish a private experience. The proof is that he must take, hold, teach, and embody what he has been given. Selection becomes responsibility immediately.

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. Selection Is Named, but It Is Named Through Mission

Allah says:

*Inni istafaytuka 'ala al-nasi bi-risalati wa bi-kalami.*

I have chosen you over the people with My messages and My speech.

[PAUSE]

The verb *istafaytuka* carries selection, choosing with distinction. But notice how the distinction is defined. Musa is not told he is chosen because of private spiritual superiority. He is chosen *bi-risalati wa bi-kalami* - by the messages and by the speech.

One is plural and one is singular. *Risalati* points to the entrusted messages that must be conveyed outward. *Kalami* points to the exceptional nearness of direct speech from Allah. Outward mission and inward honor meet in one servant. The chosen one is therefore not the one who escapes service. He is the one pressed deeper into it.

Then the command comes at once:

*Fa-khudh ma ataytuka.*

So take what I have given you.

Revelation in the Qur'an is never an ornament. It is something to be taken.

### 2. Gratitude Is the First Interior Adab of Receiving Revelation

The ayah continues:

*Wa kun min al-shakirin.*

Be among the grateful.

[PAUSE]

This is striking. Allah does not say first: be among the proud, or the relieved, or the self-conscious bearers of distinction. He says: be among the grateful. That means the correct inner state for one who receives revelation is not ego but shukr.

Gratitude changes how a gift is held. The grateful person does not relate to what he has received as personal property. He relates to it as something entrusted from above, something whose source must remain visible in the heart. The moment guidance becomes a mirror for self-importance, its spirit is already being betrayed.

This matters far beyond prophethood. Every opening Allah gives - knowledge, clarity, access to the Qur'an, steadiness in worship, the ability to teach, even the desire to learn - is safest in the heart that translates it immediately into shukr. Gratitude keeps the gift from turning into a claim.

### 3. The Tablets Are Not Only Information; They Are Admonition, Detail, Strength, and Best-Taking

Ayah 145 says:

*Wa katabna lahu fi al-alwahi min kulli shay'in maw'izhatan wa tafsilan li-kulli shay'.*

We wrote for him upon the tablets of everything an admonition and an explanation for everything.

[PAUSE]

Two words stand out here. *Maw'izhah* is not cold data. It is admonition - truth that enters the heart to warn, soften, and turn it. *Tafsil* is detail, clarification, explanatory differentiation. Revelation therefore addresses both the heart and the mind. It admonishes, and it makes clear.

Then comes the command:

*Fa-khudhha bi-quwwah.*

So take it with strength.

This is one of the most important commands in the Sinai passage. Allah does not say merely: hold it. He says: hold it with *quwwah*. Revelation must be carried with seriousness, firmness, and moral resolve.

Then the circle widens beyond Musa himself:

*Wa'mur qawmaka ya'khudhu bi-ahsaniha.*

Command your people to take the best of it.

Not merely to take it. To take the best of it. That means revelation is not fulfilled by minimal compliance. The community is being trained toward the most beautiful uptake, the noblest register of obedience, the strongest ethical form of response.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The Real Test of Honor Is What You Carry After the Moment Passes

Many people imagine that spiritual life is about having a moment. A night that changes everything. A retreat. A powerful prayer. An encounter with the Qur'an that feels overwhelming. Those moments matter. But the Qur'an repeatedly asks a harder question: what do you carry after the moment ends?

Musa has already had the overwhelming moment. Mountain. Collapse. Recovery. If the story ended there, we would remember intensity. But Allah turns the whole scene toward burden. Take what I gave you. Be grateful. The tablets contain admonition and detail. Hold them with strength. Teach your people to take them in their best form.

[PAUSE]

That is a mercy for us too. It means our religion is not built on chasing peaks. It is built on carrying trusts. A person may have cried deeply one night in salah, but the real question comes later: did that crying become stronger obedience, steadier shukr, and more disciplined holding of what Allah has already revealed? If not, the moment was not false, but it was unfinished.

The chosen ones in the Qur'an are not those who merely feel most. They are those who carry most faithfully.

### Theme 2: Revelation Is Not Meant to Be Received at the Lowest Acceptable Level

The phrase *bi-ahsaniha* is quietly devastating. It suggests that one can, in practice, meet revelation at different moral heights. One can obey grudgingly, minimally, technically. Or one can take it in its best register - with love, beauty, sincerity, and energetic seriousness.

[PAUSE]

This is painfully relevant to modern religious life. We often ask: what is the minimum? What is the least I must do? How close can I stand to the border and still count as inside? But this ayah trains an entirely different instinct. Take the best of it. Let revelation pull you upward rather than let the nafs negotiate it downward.

That does not mean performative extremity. It means beautiful uptake. When the Qur'an commands truthfulness, take its best by becoming deeply honest. When it commands salah, take its best by guarding presence, not only procedure. When it commands repentance, take its best by returning quickly and sincerely rather than delaying until shame hardens.

This is why the ayah ends with:

*Sa-urikum dara al-fasiqin.*

I will show you the home of the defiantly disobedient.

The contrast is sharp. Either revelation is taken with strength and beauty, or the road of fisq is opened and its destination will eventually be shown.

## Closing Synthesis

These two ayahs take the most elevated spiritual scene and turn it into disciplined stewardship.

Musa is told he has been chosen by Allah's messages and speech. He is then commanded to take what he has been given and anchor that reception in gratitude. The tablets are described as both admonition and detailed guidance, and that guidance must be held with strength. Then the community is called not to bare minimum religion, but to the best uptake of revelation.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: do I love spiritual moments more than I love carrying divine instruction? Has knowledge made me grateful, or subtly proud? When Allah gives me guidance, do I reach for the minimum acceptable response, or do I try to take the best of it?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the unit lands here: **Allah's selection is proved by entrusted burden, so revelation must be received with gratitude, held with strength, and taken in its most beautiful form before the destination of fisq is shown.**

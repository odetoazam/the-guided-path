# And the Garment of Taqwa Is Better

## Surah Al-Araf (7:26-27)

[ayah:7:26] يَـٰبَنِىٓ ءَادَمَ قَدْ أَنزَلْنَا عَلَيْكُمْ لِبَاسًا يُوَٰرِى سَوْءَٰتِكُمْ وَرِيشًا ۖ وَلِبَاسُ ٱلتَّقْوَىٰ ذَٰلِكَ خَيْرٌ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ مِنْ ءَايَـٰتِ ٱللَّهِ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَذَّكَّرُونَ

[ayah:7:27] يَـٰبَنِىٓ ءَادَمَ لَا يَفْتِنَنَّكُمُ ٱلشَّيْطَـٰنُ كَمَآ أَخْرَجَ أَبَوَيْكُم مِّنَ ٱلْجَنَّةِ يَنزِعُ عَنْهُمَا لِبَاسَهُمَا لِيُرِيَهُمَا سَوْءَٰتِهِمَآ ۗ إِنَّهُۥ يَرَىٰكُمْ هُوَ وَقَبِيلُهُۥ مِنْ حَيْثُ لَا تَرَوْنَهُمْ ۗ إِنَّا جَعَلْنَا ٱلشَّيَـٰطِينَ أَوْلِيَآءَ لِلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ

*Ya bani Adama qad anzalna 'alaykum libasan yuwari saw'atikum wa rishan. Wa libasu al-taqwa dhalika khayrun. Dhalika min ayati Allahi la'allahum yadhdhakkarun.*

*Ya bani Adama la yaftinannakumu al-shaytanu kama akhraja abawaykum mina al-jannati yanzi'u 'anhuma libasahuma li-yuriyahuma saw'atihima. Innahu yarakum huwa wa qabiluhu min haythu la tarawnahum. Inna ja'alna al-shayatina awliya'a lilladhina la yu'minun.*

**O children of Adam, We have sent down upon you clothing to conceal your private parts and as adornment, but the garment of taqwa - that is better. That is from the signs of Allah so that they may remember. O children of Adam, let not Satan tempt you as he brought your parents out of Paradise, stripping from them their clothing to show them their private parts. Indeed, he sees you, he and his tribe, from where you do not see them. Indeed, We have made the devils allies of those who do not believe.**

## Introduction

After the shame of exposure in the Adam story, the Qur'an does something tender and searching. It does not leave the descendants of Adam staring only at what was lost. It tells them that clothing itself is a gift from Allah.

[PAUSE]

That matters. The first human experience of nakedness after sin could have become a permanent theology of humiliation. Instead, Allah names covering as a mercy sent down. He teaches that what protects dignity is not a trivial social accessory. It is part of divine care.

[PAUSE]

But then the ayah rises higher still. Beyond clothing that covers and adorns, there is *libas al-taqwa* - the garment of God-consciousness. Outer covering is honored, yet the inner covering is declared better. Then immediately the warning comes: do not let Shaytan repeat on you what he did to your parents. He still wants stripping. He still wants exposure.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **Allah responds to the human vulnerability of exposure by giving coverings for the body and a better covering for the soul, while Shaytan's old project remains the same - to strip both away.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. Clothing Is Described as Something Sent Down

The address opens:

*Ya bani Adam.*

O children of Adam.

[PAUSE]

The story has widened. We are no longer only watching our first parents. We are being addressed as their descendants.

Then:

*Qad anzalna 'alaykum libasan.*

We have sent down upon you clothing.

The wording is striking. Clothing is not described merely as something humans invented. Allah says He sent it down. He is naming it as provision from above, a mercy that belongs inside revelation's moral world rather than outside it.

### 2. Clothing Has Two Functions: Concealment and Adornment

Then:

*Yuwari saw'atikum wa rishan.*

To conceal your private parts, and as adornment.

[PAUSE]

The first function is satr: covering what should not be exposed. The second is beauty and embellishment. The Qur'an refuses the false choice between dignity and beauty. A believer's relationship to clothing is not only defensive concealment. There is also *rish* - adornment, presentability, grace.

But the order matters. Concealment comes first. Beauty is not permitted to cancel modesty.

### 3. Then the Ayah Names a Better Garment

Then:

*Wa libasu al-taqwa dhalika khayr.*

And the garment of taqwa - that is better.

[PAUSE]

This is one of the most beautiful transfers in the passage. We begin with cloth and are moved inward. A garment can cover the limbs while the heart remains exposed. A person can look assembled and still be spiritually stripped. So the ayah points us to a deeper covering: taqwa, the inward state that guards, restrains, and keeps the servant under Allah's watchful awareness.

The word *khayr* - better - does not abolish outer clothing. It ranks the deeper thing above it. Outer garments are real gifts. Inner God-consciousness is the greater one because without it even external covering can become costume.

### 4. Shaytan's Ancient Method Is Stripping

Then the second address:

*La yaftinannakumu al-shaytan.*

Let not Satan tempt you.

[PAUSE]

The warning is immediately connected to the family story:

*Kama akhraja abawaykum mina al-jannah yanzi'u 'anhuma libasahuma li-yuriyahuma saw'atihima.*

As he brought your parents out of the Garden, stripping from them their clothing to show them their private parts.

This is his old method in clear language. He strips. He removes. He wants the servant seen where he should have remained covered.

### 5. The Battle Includes an Unseen Asymmetry

Then comes the unsettling line:

*Innahu yarakum huwa wa qabiluhu min haythu la tarawnahum.*

He sees you, he and his tribe, from where you do not see them.

[PAUSE]

The asymmetry is real. The believer is not told to be naive about it. The enemy watches from an unseen angle.

Then the end:

*Inna ja'alna al-shayatina awliya'a lilladhina la yu'minun.*

We have made the devils allies for those who do not believe.

The danger is not only occasional whisper. It can harden into alliance, a relationship of orientation and companionship.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Modesty Is Not a Social Relic; It Is Part of Divine Mercy

These ayahs rescue the subject of clothing from triviality. In many settings it gets reduced either to fashion or to cultural debate. But the Qur'an roots it deeper than both. Clothing answers a human vulnerability first exposed in the Garden. It is part of Allah's caring response to creatures who can be shamed.

[PAUSE]

That means satr is not embarrassment about the body. It is protection of dignity. And because Allah Himself names both covering and adornment, the believer learns that beauty is not rejected, but it must remain under the order set by Allah rather than the appetites of exposure.

### Theme 2: Spiritual Warfare Often Looks Like Progressive Uncovering

The passage keeps tying Shaytan to the act of stripping. That should widen how we understand his temptations. He does not only tempt toward obvious disbelief. He works to remove coverings slowly: shame where there should be reserve, brazenness where there should be adab, self-display where there should be guardedness, and spiritual heedlessness where there should be taqwa.

[PAUSE]

So there are two kinds of nakedness to fear. One is bodily exposure. The other is the exposure of a heart emptied of taqwa, easy for every whisper to penetrate. The outer garment can hide one while the inner one is gone. That is why the Qur'an says the garment of taqwa is better.

## Closing Synthesis

The children of Adam are addressed with a memory and a mercy.

Remember what Shaytan did to your parents. Remember what he wanted to expose. Then recognize what Allah gave in response: garments that cover, garments that beautify, and a better garment still - taqwa. The same enemy remains, seeing from where we do not see, but Allah has not left the children of Adam defenseless.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: am I caring for only the garment people see, or am I also guarding the inward garment that keeps the heart covered before Allah?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **because Shaytan's old strategy is to strip the human being of covering and dignity, Allah teaches the children of Adam to receive both outer clothing and the inner garment of taqwa as mercies without which exposure becomes easy and remembrance fades.**

# To Him Belong Creation and Command

## Surah Al-Araf (7:54-56)

[ayah:7:54] إِنَّ رَبَّكُمُ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ فِى سِتَّةِ أَيَّامٍ ثُمَّ ٱسْتَوَىٰ عَلَى ٱلْعَرْشِ يُغْشِى ٱلَّيْلَ ٱلنَّهَارَ يَطْلُبُهُۥ حَثِيثًا وَٱلشَّمْسَ وَٱلْقَمَرَ وَٱلنُّجُومَ مُسَخَّرَٰتٍۭ بِأَمْرِهِۦٓ ۗ أَلَا لَهُ ٱلْخَلْقُ وَٱلْأَمْرُ ۗ تَبَارَكَ ٱللَّهُ رَبُّ ٱلْعَـٰلَمِينَ

[ayah:7:55] ٱدْعُوا۟ رَبَّكُمْ تَضَرُّعًا وَخُفْيَةً ۚ إِنَّهُۥ لَا يُحِبُّ ٱلْمُعْتَدِينَ

**Indeed, your Lord is Allah, who created the heavens and the earth in six days, then rose over the Throne. He covers the night with the day, chasing it swiftly, and the sun, the moon, and the stars are subjected by His command. Unquestionably, to Him belong creation and command. Blessed is Allah, Lord of the worlds. Call upon your Lord in humility and privately. Indeed, He does not love the transgressors. And do not cause فساد on the earth after its rectification, and call upon Him in fear and hope. Indeed, the mercy of Allah is near to the doers of good.**

## Introduction

These ayahs do something the Qur'an often does at exactly the right moment: after showing us human destinies, they turn us back to the Lord of those destinies.

Your Lord is Allah, the One who created the heavens and the earth, who governs night and day, and before whose command the sun, moon, and stars are subjected. Then, without any awkward transition, the surah moves from cosmology to du'a and ethics. If He is the Lord of creation and command, then call upon Him humbly and quietly. Do not transgress in supplication. Do not spread فساد in a world He already rectified.

[PAUSE]

That movement is the secret of the passage. Theology is not there as spectacle. It is there to teach posture. Once the heart sees who Allah is, it should know how to speak to Him and how to walk on His earth.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **the One who owns creation and command wants from His servants not noisy self-assertion but humble du'a, careful inhabiting of the earth, and a heart stretched between fear and hope beneath His near mercy.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Ayah Gathers Creation, Sovereignty, and Ongoing Governance

The opening is majestic:

*Inna rabbakumu Allahu alladhi khalaqa al-samawati wa al-arda...*

Your Lord is Allah, the One who created the heavens and the earth.

[PAUSE]

Then:

*Thumma istawa 'ala al-'arsh.*

Then He rose over the Throne.

The ayah is naming sovereignty after creation. Not only did He originate everything, He reigns over it.

Then the world is shown not as static but actively governed:

*Yughshi al-layla al-nahara yatlubuhu hathitha.*

He covers the night with the day, one chasing the other swiftly.

The cosmos is portrayed as ordered urgency, one sign pursuing another without fatigue.

### 2. The Great Sentence: To Him Belong Creation and Command

Then comes the line that gathers the whole ayah:

*Ala lahu al-khalqu wa al-amr.*

Unquestionably, to Him belong creation and command.

[PAUSE]

This is one of the great theological concentrations in the Qur'an. *Al-khalq*: bringing into being. *Al-amr*: authoritative command, governance, disposal, rule. The One who made is the One who rightly commands. No fracture is allowed between authorship and authority.

### 3. Du'a Is Framed by Humility and Hiddenness

Then the surah moves immediately into worship:

*Ud'u rabbakum tadarru'an wa khufyah.*

Call upon your Lord in humility and hiddenness.

[PAUSE]

*Tadarru'* is lowliness, pleading need, softened dependence. *Khufyah* is quietness, privacy, hiddenness from display. Together they define the adab of du'a. It is not performance. It is not spiritual volume for its own sake. It is a needy servant speaking to the Lord.

### 4. Corruption Is Condemned After Rectification Is Already Named

Then:

*Wa la tufsidu fi al-ardi ba'da islahiha.*

Do not corrupt the earth after its rectification.

[PAUSE]

The phrase *ba'da islahiha* matters. The earth has already been set right by Allah's ordering, by revelation, by the moral architecture He placed in it. فساد is therefore not creating chaos out of neutral matter; it is ruining what had been given form and rightness.

### 5. Fear and Hope Are Joined Under Near Mercy

Then:

*Wa ud'uhu khawfan wa tama'an.*

Call upon Him in fear and hope.

[PAUSE]

Not fear only. Not hope only. The believer's heart is stretched between both.

Then the close:

*Inna rahmata Allahi qaribun mina al-muhsinin.*

The mercy of Allah is near to the doers of good.

The mercy is not abstractly remote. It is near.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Theology Should Produce Posture

It is easy to read the first ayah as grand doctrine and the next two as practical advice, but the passage is more integrated than that. The majesty of Allah's creation and command is exactly what should humble the servant in du'a. Once you know who owns the heavens, the earth, the Throne, the cycles of time, and the stars, you stop performing need and start feeling it.

[PAUSE]

This is one reason spiritual life weakens when theology becomes merely conceptual. If *lahu al-khalq wa al-amr* does not reach the knees, the voice, the secret heart, and the way you move through the world, it has not yet been received in the Qur'anic way.

### Theme 2: فساد Often Means Damaging What Allah Already Put Right

The warning about فساد after rectification is searching because many people imagine corruption only as dramatic collapse. But the ayah suggests something subtler too: taking a world Allah ordered and living in it as though His order does not matter. That can include moral corruption, social distortion, environmental abuse, and spiritual vandalism.

[PAUSE]

The antidote is right here in the passage: humility in supplication, restraint from transgression, and ihsan. The earth is not healed by human dominance alone. It is healed by servants who know they are standing inside a world already authored and morally structured by Allah.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs gather majesty and nearness together.

Allah created the heavens and earth, governs the alternation of night and day, and owns both creation and command. Therefore the servant should call on Him quietly and humbly, refuse to corrupt what He set right, and live between fear and hope. The final word is not distance but nearness: Allah's mercy is near to the muhsinin.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: does my belief that Allah owns both creation and command actually make my du'a more humble and my footprint on His earth more careful?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **when the servant truly sees that creation and command both belong to Allah, worship becomes lowly and sincere, the earth is treated as a trust already set right by Him, and the heart learns to live between fear and hope under a mercy that is near.**

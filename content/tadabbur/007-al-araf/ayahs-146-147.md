# I Will Turn Away from My Signs

## Surah Al-Araf (7:146-147)

[ayah:7:146] سَأَصْرِفُ عَنْ ءَايَـٰتِىَ ٱلَّذِينَ يَتَكَبَّرُونَ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ بِغَيْرِ ٱلْحَقِّ وَإِن يَرَوْا۟ كُلَّ ءَايَةٍ لَّا يُؤْمِنُوا۟ بِهَا وَإِن يَرَوْا۟ سَبِيلَ ٱلرُّشْدِ لَا يَتَّخِذُوهُ سَبِيلًا وَإِن يَرَوْا۟ سَبِيلَ ٱلْغَىِّ يَتَّخِذُوهُ سَبِيلًا ۚ ذَٰلِكَ بِأَنَّهُمْ كَذَّبُوا۟ بِـَٔايَـٰتِنَا وَكَانُوا۟ عَنْهَا غَـٰفِلِينَ

[ayah:7:147] وَٱلَّذِينَ كَذَّبُوا۟ بِـَٔايَـٰتِنَا وَلِقَآءِ ٱلْـَٔاخِرَةِ حَبِطَتْ أَعْمَـٰلُهُمْ ۚ هَلْ يُجْزَوْنَ إِلَّا مَا كَانُوا۟ يَعْمَلُونَ

*Sa-asrifu 'an ayatiya alladhina yatakabbaruna fi al-ardi bi-ghayri al-haqq. Wa in yaraw kulla ayatin la yu'minu biha, wa in yaraw sabila al-rushdi la yattakhidhuhu sabila, wa in yaraw sabila al-ghayyi yattakhidhuhu sabila. Dhalika bi-annahum kadhdhabu bi-ayatina wa kanu 'anha ghafilin.*

*Wa alladhina kadhdhabu bi-ayatina wa liqa'i al-akhirati habitat a'maluhum. Hal yujzawna illa ma kanu ya'malun.*

**I will turn away from My signs those who are arrogant upon the earth without right. And if they see every sign, they will not believe in it; and if they see the way of right guidance, they will not take it as a way; but if they see the way of error, they will take it as a way. That is because they denied Our signs and were heedless of them. And those who denied Our signs and the meeting of the Hereafter - their deeds have become worthless. Are they recompensed except for what they used to do?**

## Introduction

The Qur'an sometimes warns us not only about what we do, but about what our doing will eventually do to our ability to see.

That is the terror of these ayahs. They are not describing people who lacked evidence. They are describing people whose inner posture became so distorted that evidence itself stopped helping them. They can see every sign and still not believe. They can recognize the road of right guidance and decline to adopt it. They can recognize the road of error and still choose it. By the time the verse finishes, even their deeds have been emptied.

[PAUSE]

This is the opposite of taking revelation with strength. When the heart meets divine signs with arrogance, Allah describes the punishment not only as future pain but as present diversion from the ayat themselves. The most frightening loss may be to stand in front of truth and no longer know how to receive it.

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Warning Begins with Diversion from the Signs

Allah says:

*Sa-asrifu 'an ayatiya...*

I will turn away from My signs...

[PAUSE]

The verb *sa-asrifu* carries turning away, diverting, causing something to be moved aside. The warning is not phrased merely as "they will turn away." It is phrased with divine action over the consequences of their condition. That should make us tremble.

This does not mean Allah wrongs them. The ayah itself explains the reason later: *because they denied Our signs and were heedless of them.* Their arrogance comes first; the diversion is its consequence. But the wording teaches that spiritual perception is not under our absolute control. If a person repeatedly meets truth with pride, Allah may leave that person unable to benefit from what is now plainly in front of him.

### 2. Perception Is Present, Adoption Is Missing

Then the ayah repeats one of the most devastating patterns in the surah:

*Wa in yaraw...*

And if they see...

[PAUSE]

They see every sign and do not believe. They see the path of *rushd* and do not take it as a path. They see the path of *ghayy* and adopt it as a path.

The repetition destroys a common excuse. The problem is not that guidance was invisible. The problem is adoption. The ayah does not say they never encountered the road. It says they encountered it and refused to make it their road. Likewise, the path of error is not stumbled into accidentally. It is taken.

Notice the paired language of *sabil al-rushd* and *sabil al-ghayy*. The Qur'an names both as roads. Human life is directional. We are always taking some path. The heart that refuses guidance does not remain neutral. It begins to normalize misguidance as its chosen route.

### 3. Denial and Heedlessness End by Emptying Deeds

The verse explains:

*Dhalika bi-annahum kadhdhabu bi-ayatina wa kanu 'anha ghafilin.*

That is because they denied Our signs and were heedless of them.

Denial is active. Heedlessness is dullness. Together they form a lethal pair: sometimes the heart fights the truth, and sometimes it simply stops attending to it. In the end both lead to the same disaster.

Then ayah 147 says:

*Habitat a'maluhum.*

Their deeds have become worthless.

[PAUSE]

The word *habitat* is chilling because it describes collapse from within. Outwardly there may still be motion, effort, labor, production. But the thing has lost its enduring value. Why? Because they denied the signs and the meeting of the Hereafter. Once the akhirah is no longer believed as an encounter one is truly moving toward, deeds lose their center of gravity.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Arrogance Is Not Just a Moral Flaw; It Is a Reading Disorder

We often speak of arrogance as though it were mainly a personality problem - a vice of tone, posture, or social behavior. The Qur'an treats it as something even deeper. It is a disorder in how reality gets read.

An arrogant person does not merely disobey. He misperceives. He overestimates himself, underestimates revelation, and interprets encounter through the filter of self-importance. That is why arrogance and ayat are such a dangerous combination. The more signs appear, the more threatened the arrogant self feels, because every sign implies an authority above it.

[PAUSE]

This helps explain why some people seem to become less reachable the more truth they hear. More reminders do not always soften. If the ego has decided in advance that surrender is unacceptable, every reminder becomes one more piece of resisted pressure. The problem is not informational deficiency. The problem is that the self has become committed to preserving its throne.

We should read this with fear for ourselves before reading it as a diagnosis of others. How often do we ignore a clear Qur'anic demand not because we do not understand it, but because we already know what obedience would cost our pride?

### Theme 2: Without the Hereafter, Human Work Eventually Hollows Out

The ayah joins denial of the signs to denial of *liqa' al-akhirah* - the meeting of the Hereafter. That phrase matters. The akhirah is not only an abstract doctrine about a future world. It is a meeting. An encounter. A coming face-to-face with what is true.

[PAUSE]

If that meeting fades from the heart, work changes. It may still look energetic. One may still build, speak, produce, argue, perform, and accumulate. But the axis has shifted. Deeds start orbiting self, reputation, appetite, immediate payoff, and worldly anxiety. They lose weight because they are no longer being sent forward toward Allah.

This is one reason heedlessness is so dangerous. It is quiet. It does not always announce itself as rebellion. Sometimes it just appears as a person slowly living as though there is no real meeting coming. And once that happens, even strong outward activity can become *habat* - work emptied of enduring yield.

These ayahs therefore do not merely threaten punishment. They teach us how to guard meaning. Humility before the signs and vivid remembrance of the Hereafter keep deeds alive.

## Closing Synthesis

These verses explain the interior catastrophe that follows arrogant refusal of revelation.

Allah warns that He will turn away from His signs those who grow arrogant in the earth without right. Such people can see every sign and still refuse belief, see the road of guidance and refuse to adopt it, and see the road of error and make it their own. That is because they denied the signs and lived heedlessly toward them, until denial of the Hereafter emptied their deeds altogether.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: where am I pretending the issue is confusion when the deeper issue is pride? Which path have I clearly recognized as right but still resisted adopting? Am I working in a way that is being sent toward the meeting with Allah, or am I slowly hollowing out my deeds by living as though no true meeting is coming?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the unit lands here: **arrogance first distorts how the heart receives the signs, then it reverses guidance and misguidance in lived choice, and finally it drains deeds of their value by severing them from the coming meeting with Allah.**

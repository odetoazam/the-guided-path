# Today We Forget Them

## Surah Al-Araf (7:50-51)

[ayah:7:50] وَنَادَىٰٓ أَصْحَـٰبُ ٱلنَّارِ أَصْحَـٰبَ ٱلْجَنَّةِ أَنْ أَفِيضُوا۟ عَلَيْنَا مِنَ ٱلْمَآءِ أَوْ مِمَّا رَزَقَكُمُ ٱللَّهُ ۚ قَالُوٓا۟ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ حَرَّمَهُمَا عَلَى ٱلْكَـٰفِرِينَ

[ayah:7:51] ٱلَّذِينَ ٱتَّخَذُوا۟ دِينَهُمْ لَهْوًا وَلَعِبًا وَغَرَّتْهُمُ ٱلْحَيَوٰةُ ٱلدُّنْيَا ۚ فَٱلْيَوْمَ نَنسَىٰهُمْ كَمَا نَسُوا۟ لِقَآءَ يَوْمِهِمْ هَـٰذَا وَمَا كَانُوا۟ بِـَٔايَـٰتِنَا يَجْحَدُونَ

*Wa nada ashabu al-nari ashab al-jannati an afidu 'alayna mina al-ma'i aw mimma razaqakumu Allah. Qalu inna Allaha harramahuma 'ala al-kafirin.*

*Alladhina ittakhadhu dinahum lahwan wa la'iban wa gharrathumu al-hayatu al-dunya. Fa-al-yawma nansahum kama nasu liqa'a yawmihim hadha wa ma kanu bi-ayatina yajhadun.*

**And the companions of the Fire will call to the companions of Paradise, "Pour upon us some water or some of what Allah has provided you." They will say, "Indeed, Allah has forbidden them both to the disbelievers" - those who took their religion as amusement and play and whom the worldly life deluded. So today We forget them as they forgot the meeting of this Day of theirs and because they used to reject Our signs.**

## Introduction

The request in these ayahs is painfully small.

The people of the Fire are not asking for dominion, relief without cost, or restored life. They ask for water, or for something from what Allah provided the people of Paradise. The plea is reduced to elemental need. That alone tells us how deep deprivation has become.

[PAUSE]

And the answer does not come from personal cruelty. The people of Paradise do not say, "We refuse you." They say: Allah forbade them both to the disbelievers. Then the Qur'an explains the moral history behind that answer. These were people who made religion into *lahw* and *la'ib* - amusement and play - while the worldly life deluded them until the meeting of this Day faded from seriousness.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **a soul that trains itself to treat religion as trivial and dunya as absorbing may eventually discover that what it once treated lightly has become the dividing line between nourishment and deprivation.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The People of Fire Ask for Flow

The request begins:

*An afidu 'alayna mina al-ma'.*

Pour upon us some water.

[PAUSE]

The verb *afidu* evokes outpouring, something flowing over and toward them. The people of the Fire are no longer bargaining from strength. They are pleading for even a poured share.

Then the request widens:

*Aw mimma razaqakumu Allah.*

Or some of what Allah provided you.

Even here the language remembers the true source of provision: Allah.

### 2. The Answer Grounds Deprivation in Divine Judgment

Then:

*Inna Allaha harramahuma 'ala al-kafirin.*

Indeed Allah has forbidden them both to the disbelievers.

[PAUSE]

The moral center is crucial. Paradise is not withholding out of petty vengeance. The deprivation is part of divine judgment.

### 3. Their Religion Was Reduced to Entertainment

Then the defining diagnosis:

*Alladhina ittakhadhu dinahum lahwan wa la'iban.*

Those who took their religion as amusement and play.

[PAUSE]

This is one of the most frightening descriptions in the Qur'an because it does not describe open hatred of religion only. It describes trivialization. Religion becomes spectacle, pastime, cultural performance, or an arena for ego rather than serious surrender before Allah.

### 4. Dunya Deluded Them Until Meeting Became Forgettable

Then:

*Wa gharrathumu al-hayatu al-dunya.*

The worldly life deluded them.

And the result:

*Fa-al-yawma nansahum kama nasu liqa'a yawmihim hadha.*

Today We forget them as they forgot the meeting of this Day of theirs.

[PAUSE]

The forgetting here is not loss of knowledge on Allah's part. It is abandonment, being left out of mercy and care in recompense for how they lived as though this meeting did not matter.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: One of the Deepest Forms of Kufr Is Trivialization

These ayahs force us to take seriously a danger many modern souls underestimate. Religion does not need to be hated in order to be emptied. It can be turned into amusement. Something consumed, styled, joked with, performed around, or sampled without surrender.

[PAUSE]

That is spiritually lethal because it trains the heart to remain entertained where it should tremble, and casual where it should prepare to meet Allah.

### Theme 2: Forgetting the Meeting Produces a Hollow Life Long Before the Final Day

The surah says they forgot the meeting of this Day. That is not just a future problem. A person who forgets the final meeting in the present gradually becomes easy prey for delusion. Dunya inflates, appetite expands, urgency decays, and even revelation can be handled with the posture of play.

[PAUSE]

Then one day what was treated as distant becomes immediate. The meeting arrives, and the soul discovers that its habits of trivialization were not neutral tastes. They were training for ruin.

## Closing Synthesis

The people of the Fire ask for water and provision. The people of Paradise answer by naming Allah's judgment. Then the Qur'an reveals the moral story underneath the deprivation: religion made into amusement, life in dunya taken at face value, meeting forgotten, signs rejected.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: where in my own relationship with faith have I allowed what should be weighty to become merely consumable, entertaining, or easy to put down?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **the people most deprived in the akhirah are described here as those who spent dunya training themselves to treat religion lightly, until the meeting they forgot became the reality that finally excluded them.**

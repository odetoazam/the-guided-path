# The Prophet Who Lifts Burdens

## Surah Al-Araf (7:157-158)

[ayah:7:157] ٱلَّذِينَ يَتَّبِعُونَ ٱلرَّسُولَ ٱلنَّبِىَّ ٱلْأُمِّىَّ ٱلَّذِى يَجِدُونَهُۥ مَكْتُوبًا عِندَهُمْ فِى ٱلتَّوْرَىٰةِ وَٱلْإِنجِيلِ يَأْمُرُهُم بِٱلْمَعْرُوفِ وَيَنْهَىٰهُمْ عَنِ ٱلْمُنكَرِ وَيُحِلُّ لَهُمُ ٱلطَّيِّبَـٰتِ وَيُحَرِّمُ عَلَيْهِمُ ٱلْخَبَـٰٓئِثَ وَيَضَعُ عَنْهُمْ إِصْرَهُمْ وَٱلْأَغْلَـٰلَ ٱلَّتِى كَانَتْ عَلَيْهِمْ ۚ فَٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ بِهِۦ وَعَزَّرُوهُ وَنَصَرُوهُ وَٱتَّبَعُوا۟ ٱلنُّورَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أُنزِلَ مَعَهُۥٓ ۙ أُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ

## Introduction

These ayahs arrive like a disclosure of what Allah's mercy looks like when it takes prophetic form.

The previous passage said that divine mercy would be written for people of taqwa, zakah, and iman in Allah's signs. Here the surah shows us the road by which that mercy is received in history: it is received by following the Messenger. Not a messenger who flatters appetite. Not a messenger who leaves people trapped inside inherited distortions. A messenger who commands what is recognized as good, forbids what is recognized as corrupt, makes lawful the wholesome, forbids the foul, and removes burdens and shackles.

[PAUSE]

Then the horizon widens even further. The address is no longer only inside an inherited scriptural conversation. It becomes: *ya ayyuha al-nas.* O mankind. The Messenger is sent to all of you. His authority is not tribal, regional, or civilizational. It is anchored in the Lord who owns the heavens and the earth, who gives life and causes death. So these two ayahs bind together two things we often separate: the gentleness of liberation and the grandeur of universal claim.

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The People of Mercy Are Described First as People Who Follow

Ayah 157 begins:

*Alladhina yattabi'una al-rasula...*

Those who follow the Messenger.

[PAUSE]

The opening matters. It does not begin by describing admiration, loose affiliation, or inherited label. It begins with *yattabi'una* - they follow. The root carries going after, taking a path behind someone, ordering your steps by another's lead. Mercy in this passage is not abstract agreement with prophetic truth. It is lived *ittiba'*. A person's feet, habits, moral choices, and loyalties begin to fall in behind the Messenger.

Then the ayah layers his description: *al-rasul, al-nabiyy, al-ummiyy.* Messenger, Prophet, unlettered Prophet. The juxtaposition is striking. He is not being authorized by elite literary machinery or by a borrowed scriptural performance from previous communities, and yet the verse says he is found *maktuban 'indahum* - written with them in the Torah and the Gospel. The one not manufactured by their institutions was already written in their revelation.

That pairing is spiritually important. Real truth does not need human prestige in order to be ancient in Allah's knowledge. The Messenger may appear in the world without worldly credentials that satisfy every gatekeeper, yet he arrives already inscribed inside the longer story of revelation.

### 2. Prophetic Guidance Is Described as Moral Clarity and Human Release

The ayah then unfolds the Prophet's work in a flowing sequence:

*Ya'muruhum bi-al-ma'ruf wa yanhahum 'an al-munkar wa yuhillu lahumu al-tayyibat wa yuharrimu 'alayhimu al-khaba'ith...*

He commands them to what is recognized as good, forbids them from what is rejected as wrong, makes lawful the good things, and forbids the foul things.

[PAUSE]

There is moral clarity here, but not cold severity. The verse uses *ma'ruf* and *munkar* - what is soundly recognized and what is soundly rejected. Then it speaks of *tayyibat* and *khaba'ith* - the wholesome and the foul. Prophetic law is not presented here as arbitrary difficulty. It is presented as a restoration of fitrah-level clarity. The Messenger does not scramble the moral world. He clears it.

Then comes one of the most tender descriptions of his mission:

*Wa yada'u 'anhum israhum wa al-aghlala allati kanat 'alayhim.*

He lifts from them their burden and the shackles that were upon them.

[PAUSE]

That means following the Prophet is not a movement from freedom into suffocation. It is a movement from false, inherited heaviness into divinely guided release. The verse does not deny that taklif exists. Revelation still commands and forbids. But it insists that the truest description of prophetic guidance is not imprisonment. It is burden-lifting.

Many of us feel religion first as weight because we encounter it after long histories of distortion, harshness, family damage, cultural performance, or our own undisciplined desires. This ayah tells us to distinguish between the burden of truth and the shackles people manufacture around truth. The Messenger removes shackles. If our version of religious life only crushes and never clarifies, only binds and never heals, we should tremble before this ayah.

### 3. Success Is a Whole Constellation: Belief, Honor, Support, and Following the Light

The verse does not stop at private conviction. It says:

*Fa-alladhina amanu bihi wa 'azzaruhu wa nasaruhu wa ittaba'u al-nura alladhi unzila ma'ahu ula'ika humu al-muflihun.*

Those who believe in him, honor him, support him, and follow the light sent down with him - those are the successful.

[PAUSE]

This is a rich sequence. *Amanu bihi* is inward assent and trust. *'Azzaruhu* carries honoring and strengthening him. *Nasaruhu* is active support. *Ittaba'u al-nur* is not only loyalty to a person but following the revealed light that comes with him. Success therefore is not a single emotion. It is a whole moral formation around the Messenger: belief, reverence, assistance, and sustained obedience to revelation.

Then ayah 158 widens the call:

*Qul ya ayyuha al-nasu inni rasulu Allahi ilaykum jami'an.*

Say: O mankind, indeed I am the Messenger of Allah to you all.

The word *jami'an* matters. All of you. The Prophet is not a local repair within one scriptural community only. The address now crosses every boundary. And the universal claim is grounded immediately in divine sovereignty: *alladhi lahu mulku al-samawati wa al-ard.* To Him belongs the dominion of the heavens and the earth. If the dominion is universal, the messengerhood sent by that Lord can also be universal.

Then the ayah adds a beautiful clause about the Messenger himself:

*Alladhi yu'minu bi-Allahi wa kalimatihi.*

The Prophet being followed is himself a believer in Allah and His words. He stands under the truth he proclaims. He is not above revelation. He is the first one shaped by it.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Following the Prophet Is Not the Loss of Freedom but the Recovery of It

Modern people are often trained to hear prophetic authority as a threat. The assumption runs deep: if I really follow, I will become smaller, narrower, more constrained, less myself. But this passage describes the Messenger in almost the opposite terms. He clarifies moral vision. He distinguishes wholesome from foul. He removes burdens and shackles.

[PAUSE]

That means one of the Qur'an's claims is that what we often call freedom may actually be another kind of chain. We can be shackled by appetite, by inherited confusion, by social pressure, by performative rebellion, by self-invented identities we are too fragile to question, by guilt patterns we do not know how to leave, by communities that make religion harder than Allah made it, and by communities that make sin look weightless until it hollows us out. The Prophet does not merely add one more controlling voice into that noise. He disentangles us from it.

This is why *ittiba'* matters so much. We are always following something already. Desire. Fashion. fear. family myth. public mood. wounded memory. The only real question is whether what we follow leads into light or into more hidden chains. The Qur'an answers here with remarkable confidence: follow the Messenger and the light sent with him, and you move toward *falah*.

### Theme 2: The Universal Call of the Prophet Rests on the Universal Lordship of Allah

Ayah 158 is not merely a missionary slogan. It is a theological argument.

The Prophet is told to say he is the Messenger of Allah to all people, then the verse immediately describes Allah as the possessor of the dominion of the heavens and the earth, the only deity, the One who gives life and causes death. In other words, universality of messengerhood flows from universality of Lordship.

[PAUSE]

This matters because many of us try to keep the Prophet at a respectful distance. We may admire him historically, ethically, even emotionally, while quietly resisting the idea that his call lays claim to our actual lives. But if Allah is the Lord of all life and all death, then no human being stands outside the relevance of His messenger. The verse does not permit us to treat prophetic guidance as one optional cultural path among many equally final roads.

And yet the call is not delivered in the language of conquest. It closes with hope: *la'allakum tahtadun* - so that you may be guided. The universal claim is not made to crush humanity under abstraction. It is made because guidance is needed everywhere humans live and die.

## Closing Synthesis

These two ayahs show what mercy looks like when it enters the world through a messenger.

The people written for mercy are those who follow the Messenger, the unlettered Prophet already found in earlier revelation. His work is described through moral command, purification of what is lawful and unlawful, and the lifting of burdens and shackles. Success is then defined through a full constellation of response: belief in him, honoring him, supporting him, and following the light sent with him. After that, the address opens to all humanity because the One who sent him owns the heavens and the earth, gives life and causes death, and therefore speaks with universal right over every human being.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: what am I actually following right now? Do I experience the Prophet's guidance as burden because I have confused divine discipline with human shackles? Have I reduced love of the Prophet to sentiment while resisting the hard beauty of *ittiba'*? Do I believe the Messenger himself is under Allah's words in a way that calls me to come under them too?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the unit lands here: **the Messenger sent by the Lord of all dominion does not come to imprison the human being but to reorder, purify, and release, so true success belongs to those who believe in him, stand with him, and follow the light sent with him until it becomes their road of guidance.**

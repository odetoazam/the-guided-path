# Our Lord, We Have Wronged Ourselves

## Surah Al-Araf (7:23-25)

[ayah:7:23] قَالَا رَبَّنَا ظَلَمْنَآ أَنفُسَنَا وَإِن لَّمْ تَغْفِرْ لَنَا وَتَرْحَمْنَا لَنَكُونَنَّ مِنَ ٱلْخَـٰسِرِينَ

[ayah:7:24] قَالَ ٱهْبِطُوا۟ بَعْضُكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّ ۖ وَلَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّ وَمَتَـٰعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍ

[ayah:7:25] قَالَ فِيهَا تَحْيَوْنَ وَفِيهَا تَمُوتُونَ وَمِنْهَا تُخْرَجُونَ

*Qala rabbana zalamna anfusana wa in lam taghfir lana wa tarhamna la-nakunanna mina al-khasirin.*

*Qala ihbitu ba'dukum li-ba'din 'aduwwun wa lakum fi al-ardi mustaqarrun wa mata'un ila hin.*

**They said, "Our Lord, we have wronged ourselves, and if You do not forgive us and have mercy upon us, we will surely be among the losers." He said, "Descend, some of you enemies to others. And for you on the earth is a dwelling place and enjoyment for a time." He said, "In it you will live, and in it you will die, and from it you will be brought out."**

## Introduction

The scene could have gone another way.

After being confronted, Adam and his wife could have argued. They could have emphasized the whisper, pointed to deception, spoken about intention, or tried to distribute responsibility outward. Instead, the first words placed on their tongues are among the most healing words in the Qur'an: *Rabbana zalamna anfusana.*

Our Lord, we have wronged ourselves.

[PAUSE]

That sentence is the opposite of Iblis. He explained himself. They confessed. He accused by implication. They returned by admission. He centered his grievance. They centered Allah's lordship and their own need.

[PAUSE]

Yet the passage is also sober. Forgiveness is sought, but descent still comes. Earth becomes the place of life, death, conflict, and temporary enjoyment. So the ayahs teach us a truth many souls resist: repentance is real, mercy is real, and worldly consequence can still remain real.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **the way back to Allah begins when the sinner stops defending the self and names the wrong as self-wronging, but returning to Allah does not mean escaping the conditions of earthly trial.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. They Begin With Lordship, Not Excuse

The ayah opens:

*Qala rabbana.*

They said: Our Lord.

[PAUSE]

Even after the fall, the relationship is not denied. They do not speak into emptiness. They speak to the One whose command they broke and whose lordship they still recognize. This is the first medicine of tawbah: not distance from Allah, but return to Him precisely as Lord.

Then:

*Zalamna anfusana.*

We have wronged ourselves.

This is a decisive phrase. They do not say first, "We were deceived." That was true, but it is not their opening. They name the moral reality from inside themselves. The wrong reaches them. Sin is not merely the breaking of a rule outside us; it is damage that loops back into the soul that commits it.

### 2. Forgiveness and Mercy Are Asked Together

Then:

*Wa in lam taghfir lana wa tarhamna la-nakunanna mina al-khasirin.*

If You do not forgive us and have mercy upon us, we will surely be among the losers.

[PAUSE]

The pairing matters. *Taghfir* is forgiveness, the covering over and pardoning of sin. *Tarhamna* is mercy, the tenderness and compassionate care without which a servant cannot be restored. They do not ask only for a legal cancellation. They ask for mercy too. They know they need not only acquittal, but healing.

Then the ending:

*La-nakunanna mina al-khasirin.*

We will surely be among the losers.

This is not theatrical despair. It is clarity. Without divine forgiveness and mercy, the human being is not merely flawed. He is in real loss.

### 3. Descent Does Not Cancel Lordship

Then the answer:

*Qala ihbitu.*

He said: Descend.

[PAUSE]

The decree is firm. Earthly life now begins under different conditions. Then:

*Ba'dukum li-ba'din 'aduww.*

Some of you are enemies to others.

The earth will be a field of opposition, conflict, and testing. The story of humanity outside the Garden is not the story of unbroken ease.

But then the ayah does not describe the earth only as curse. It says:

*Wa lakum fi al-ardi mustaqarrun wa mata'un ila hin.*

For you in the earth is a dwelling place and enjoyment for a time.

*Mustaqarr* is a place of settling, of taking up residence. *Mata'* is usable enjoyment, provision for the journey, not everlasting possession. Even in descent, Allah names habitability and provision.

### 4. Earth Is Named as the Place of the Whole Human Arc

Then:

*Fiha tahyawna wa fiha tamutuna wa minha tukhrajun.*

In it you will live, and in it you will die, and from it you will be brought out.

[PAUSE]

The earth becomes the site of the entire human cycle. Life in it. Death in it. Resurrection from it. That means dunya is not home in the absolute sense, but neither is it meaningless. It is the appointed ground on which the story of return unfolds.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Real Tawbah Sounds Like Self-Accusation Before It Sounds Like Explanation

Adam and his wife were actually deceived. The Qur'an itself told us that. But when they turn back, they do not begin with the serpent's biography. They begin with their own need and fault.

[PAUSE]

This is one of the deepest differences between repentance and ego-preservation. The ego wants a true-enough explanation that reduces the sting of responsibility. Tawbah wants Allah more than it wants self-justification, so it speaks plainly: we wronged ourselves.

This is why these words endure as a model. Every sinner knows the instinct to defend the self. These ayahs teach us another way: name the wrong without theatricality, admit the self-loss inside it, and ask for forgiveness and mercy together.

### Theme 2: Consequence in Dunya Does Not Mean the Door of Mercy Closed

Some people imagine repentance as a transaction that erases every trace of consequence immediately. These ayahs do not teach that. Adam and his wife turn back, and yet descent still comes. Earthly life with enmity, toil, mortality, and temporariness still begins.

[PAUSE]

But that does not mean repentance failed. It means dunya and akhirah are not identical categories. A servant may be forgiven before Allah while still living through the earthly unfolding of what has been decreed. In fact, part of mercy may be that the servant now lives that earthly life in return rather than estrangement.

The earth is described not only as battlefield but as *mustaqarr* and *mata'*. There is settlement there. There is provision there. There is life there, and after death there is being brought out. Even the place of exile is still under lordship.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs teach us how the human story continues after failure.

Not by denial. Not by self-exoneration. Not by pretending consequence is unreal. But by confession, appeal to forgiveness and mercy, acceptance of the earthly decree, and living the rest of the journey knowing that the One who sent you into the earth is still the One to whom you speak as *Rabbana*.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: when I fall, do I sound more like Adam in confession or more like Iblis in explanation?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **the servant returns to Allah by admitting that sin was first a wrong against his own soul, and even when life on earth remains a place of trial, mortality, and enmity, it remains fully under the mercy of the Lord to whom he can still say: Our Lord.**

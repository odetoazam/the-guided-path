# Allah Does Not Command Indecency

## Surah Al-Araf (7:28-30)

[ayah:7:28] وَإِذَا فَعَلُوا۟ فَـٰحِشَةً قَالُوا۟ وَجَدْنَا عَلَيْهَآ ءَابَآءَنَا وَٱللَّهُ أَمَرَنَا بِهَا ۗ قُلْ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَأْمُرُ بِٱلْفَحْشَآءِ ۖ أَتَقُولُونَ عَلَى ٱللَّهِ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ

[ayah:7:29] قُلْ أَمَرَ رَبِّى بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۖ وَأَقِيمُوا۟ وُجُوهَكُمْ عِندَ كُلِّ مَسْجِدٍ وَٱدْعُوهُ مُخْلِصِينَ لَهُ ٱلدِّينَ ۚ كَمَا بَدَأَكُمْ تَعُودُونَ

## Introduction

Not every moral collapse presents itself as rebellion.

Sometimes it wears the clothes of inheritance. Sometimes it hides under the phrase, "This is how we found our people." Sometimes it goes further and dares to speak in the name of Allah. That is the crisis these ayahs expose. People perform *fahishah* - an indecency, an obscenity, something shamelessly out of bounds - and then defend it first by ancestry and then by theology.

[PAUSE]

The Qur'an answers with unusual bluntness: Allah does not command indecency. Then it immediately replaces the lie with the truth. What Allah commands is *qist* - justice, uprightness, balance. He commands worship with the face set straight, and calling on Him with religion purified for Him alone. The passage ends with one of the most frightening diagnoses in the surah: people can take devils as allies and still think they are guided.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **one of the deepest forms of misguidance is not merely to commit wrong, but to inherit it, sacralize it, and still imagine oneself obedient to Allah while walking with devils as allies.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Problem Begins With Fahishah and a Double Justification

The ayah opens:

*Wa idha fa'alu fahishah.*

And when they commit an indecency.

[PAUSE]

*Fahishah* is not a small error. It carries the sense of something grossly shameful, glaringly out of moral proportion.

Then comes the defense:

*Qalu wajadna 'alayha aba'ana wa Allahu amarana biha.*

We found our fathers upon it, and Allah commanded us to do it.

This is a two-layer shield. First, inherited norm. Second, divine attribution. "This is tradition" becomes "this is religion."

### 2. The Qur'an Denies the Attribution Before Anything Else

Then:

*Qul inna Allaha la ya'muru bi al-fahsha'.*

Say: Allah does not command indecency.

[PAUSE]

The reply is categorical. It does not negotiate with the claim. It severs it.

Then:

*A-taquluna 'ala Allahi ma la ta'lamun.*

Do you say about Allah what you do not know?

This is more than a correction of behavior. It is an indictment of speech. The danger is not only doing the wrong, but speaking about Allah without knowledge. That is one of the gravest moral disorders in the Qur'an because it corrupts both action and the source people think they are obeying.

### 3. Allah's Actual Command Is Qist

Then the replacement truth:

*Qul amara rabbi bi al-qist.*

Say: My Lord has commanded justice.

[PAUSE]

*Qist* is balance, fairness, rectitude, giving things their due. This is the opposite of *fahishah*. One is moral ugliness without boundary. The other is upright measure.

Then:

*Wa aqimu wujuhakum 'inda kulli masjid.*

Set your faces at every place of prostration.

The face is the most forward part of the human being, the direction of presence and orientation. To set the face straight at every masjid is to come into worship without crookedness, divided allegiance, or ritual distortion.

### 4. Sincerity Purifies the Whole Act

Then:

*Wa ud'uhu mukhlisina lahu al-din.*

Call upon Him, sincere to Him in religion.

[PAUSE]

The problem in ayah 28 was false attribution and inherited corruption. The cure in ayah 29 is ikhlas, the purification of religion for Allah alone. Once religion is not for Allah alone, it becomes vulnerable to ancestry, desire, performance, and demonic suggestion.

Then the grounding reminder:

*Kama bada'akum ta'udun.*

Just as He began you, you will return.

Worship is placed under the horizon of return. The One who originated you is the One before whom your religion will be measured.

### 5. Misguidance Can Coexist With the Feeling of Being Right

Then the closing division:

*Fariqan hada wa fariqan haqqa 'alayhimu al-dalalah.*

A group He guided, and a group deserved upon them misguidance.

[PAUSE]

Then the diagnostic cause:

*Innahumu ittakhadhu al-shayatina awliya'a min duni Allahi wa yahsabuna annahum muhtadun.*

They took the devils as allies besides Allah while thinking they are guided.

This is a devastating ending. The tragedy is not only that they are astray. It is that they misperceive their condition. They are allied elsewhere while feeling secure in themselves.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Tradition Does Not Purify Falsehood

The first human defense named here is inherited precedent: we found our fathers upon it. That remains one of the strongest forces in moral life. People often feel that an old practice has earned innocence merely by being old.

[PAUSE]

But the Qur'an refuses that comfort. Age does not sanctify error. A behavior can be ancient and still indecent. A community can pass down distortion with the seriousness of ritual and still be lying about Allah.

That means loyalty to truth sometimes requires disobeying inherited moral atmosphere, even when that atmosphere feels sacred because it has wrapped itself around memory and belonging.

### Theme 2: The Most Dangerous Misguidance Is Self-Confident Misguidance

The passage ends not with open rebels boasting of rebellion, but with people who think they are guided. That is what makes the condition so dangerous. If a person knows he is astray, there is at least the beginning of alarm. But if he has taken devils as allies while imagining himself upright, then misguidance has entered the level of perception itself.

[PAUSE]

This is why the ayahs hold together *qist*, setting the face straight, ikhlas, and return to Allah. The cure for self-deceived religion is not self-confidence. It is sincere reorientation before the Lord who began us and to whom we return.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs expose one of the oldest corruptions in religion: doing something shameful, defending it by ancestry, then attaching Allah's name to it.

The Qur'an tears that apart and rebuilds the moral world around four truths: Allah does not command indecency. Allah commands justice. Worship must be rightly oriented and sincerely purified for Him. And the final measure of all this will be return to the One who began us.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: is there anything I have excused for so long through habit, culture, or inherited comfort that I no longer notice how easily the soul can start speaking about Allah without knowledge?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **misguidance becomes most dangerous when indecency is inherited, justified, and falsely attributed to Allah, because at that point the servant may already be walking with devils as allies while still feeling spiritually correct.**

# A Caller Announced Between Them

## Surah Al-Araf (7:44-45)

[ayah:7:44] وَنَادَىٰٓ أَصْحَـٰبُ ٱلْجَنَّةِ أَصْحَـٰبَ ٱلنَّارِ أَن قَدْ وَجَدْنَا مَا وَعَدَنَا رَبُّنَا حَقًّا فَهَلْ وَجَدتُّم مَّا وَعَدَ رَبُّكُمْ حَقًّا ۖ قَالُوا۟ نَعَمْ ۚ فَأَذَّنَ مُؤَذِّنٌۢ بَيْنَهُمْ أَن لَّعْنَةُ ٱللَّهِ عَلَى ٱلظَّـٰلِمِينَ

[ayah:7:45] ٱلَّذِينَ يَصُدُّونَ عَن سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ وَيَبْغُونَهَا عِوَجًا وَهُم بِٱلْـَٔاخِرَةِ كَـٰفِرُونَ

*Wa nada ashabu al-jannati ashab al-nari an qad wajadna ma wa'adana rabbuna haqqan fa-hal wajadtum ma wa'ada rabbukum haqqan. Qalu na'am. Fa-adhdhana mu'adhdhinun baynahum an la'natu Allahi 'ala al-zalimin.*

*Alladhina yasudduna 'an sabili Allahi wa yabghunaha 'iwajan wa hum bi al-akhirati kafirun.*

**And the companions of Paradise will call to the companions of the Fire, "We have certainly found what our Lord promised us to be true, so have you found what your Lord promised to be true?" They will say, "Yes." Then a caller will announce between them that the curse of Allah is upon the wrongdoers - those who turn people away from the path of Allah and seek to make it crooked while they are disbelievers in the Hereafter.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

Truth in the Qur'an is not only privately realized. Sometimes it is publicly announced.

These ayahs take us to one of those moments. The people of Paradise call across to the people of the Fire, not because they need information, but because reality has now become undeniable enough to be spoken aloud between both assemblies. What Allah promised was true. It was true in mercy and true in warning.

[PAUSE]

Then a caller rises between them and names the curse of Allah upon the wrongdoers. But the Qur'an does not leave *zulm* undefined here. It specifies its social form: they turned people away from Allah's path, sought to bend that path into crookedness, and denied the Hereafter. So the curse lands not only on private disbelief, but on obstructive religion and corruptive influence.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **when the akhirah arrives, truth is no longer an internal hunch or debated doctrine; it becomes a publicly acknowledged reality, and the gravest wrongdoers are exposed as those who not only missed the path but worked to warp it for others.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The People of Paradise Speak Across the Divide

The ayah begins:

*Wa nada ashabu al-jannati ashab al-nar.*

The companions of Paradise called to the companions of the Fire.

[PAUSE]

The scene is striking because the two final destinations are not sealed into muteness. There is an address across the divide. And the content is:

*Qad wajadna ma wa'adana rabbuna haqqan.*

We have found what our Lord promised us to be true.

The verb *wajadna* - we found - gives the sense of reality encountered and verified. Promise has become experience.

### 2. The Reply From the Fire Is Only One Word

Then the question:

*Fa-hal wajadtum ma wa'ada rabbukum haqqan.*

Have you found what your Lord promised to be true?

[PAUSE]

And the answer:

*Qalu na'am.*

They said: yes.

There is terrible force in the brevity. No argument remains. No delay remains. One word is enough.

### 3. Then a Public Announcement Interprets the Whole Scene

Then:

*Fa-adhdhana mu'adhdhinun baynahum.*

Then a caller announced between them.

[PAUSE]

The announcement is public, between the two assemblies. The meaning of the divide is named out loud:

*An la'natu Allahi 'ala al-zalimin.*

The curse of Allah is upon the wrongdoers.

This is not random doom. It is divine judgment interpreted.

### 4. The Wrongdoers Are Defined by Obstruction and Crookedness

Then the next ayah specifies:

*Alladhina yasudduna 'an sabili Allahi wa yabghunaha 'iwajan.*

Those who turn people away from the path of Allah and seek to make it crooked.

[PAUSE]

This is powerful because the wrong is not only failure to walk the path oneself. It is *sadd* - obstructing others from it. And not only obstructing, but *yabghunaha 'iwajan* - wanting the path itself bent, distorted, re-narrated into crooked form.

Then the root disease:

*Wa hum bi al-akhirati kafirun.*

While they are disbelievers in the Hereafter.

If there is no believed final accounting, crookedness becomes much easier to normalize.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: One Day the Truth Will Be Publicly Obvious

Much of dunya is lived in partial visibility. People debate revelation, argue about justice, reinterpret warnings, and treat certainty as if it belonged to nobody. These ayahs remind us that such ambiguity is temporary.

[PAUSE]

There is a day when both the saved and the ruined alike answer from inside what they have found. At that point truth is not merely believed. It is encountered.

### Theme 2: Crookedness Becomes Most Dangerous When It Tries to Rebrand the Straight Path

The description of the wrongdoers is especially searching. They do not merely wander off. They work against the path and seek to make it crooked. That means one of the gravest corruptions is not blunt hostility alone, but distortion that still remains close enough to the path to talk about it, reinterpret it, and mislead others concerning it.

[PAUSE]

This should make us fear any role we play in making Allah's path look smaller, more compromised, or more bent than it really is, especially when that distortion comes wrapped in intelligence, respectability, or inherited custom.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs stage one of the Qur'an's great public unveilings.

The people of Paradise confirm promise fulfilled. The people of Fire answer with a bare yes. Then a caller announces the meaning of it all: Allah's curse is upon the wrongdoers, especially those who obstructed His path, sought to bend it, and denied the Hereafter that would have exposed them.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: am I merely trying not to leave the path myself, or am I also careful not to speak, model, or normalize religion in a way that makes the path look crooked to others?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **when the final world arrives, truth becomes publicly undeniable, and among the most condemned are not only those who refused Allah's path, but those who spent life making that path harder to see straight.**

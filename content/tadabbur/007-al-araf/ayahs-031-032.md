# Take Your Adornment, But Do Not Commit Excess

## Surah Al-Araf (7:31-32)

[ayah:7:31] ۞ يَـٰبَنِىٓ ءَادَمَ خُذُوا۟ زِينَتَكُمْ عِندَ كُلِّ مَسْجِدٍ وَكُلُوا۟ وَٱشْرَبُوا۟ وَلَا تُسْرِفُوٓا۟ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ لَا يُحِبُّ ٱلْمُسْرِفِينَ

[ayah:7:32] قُلْ مَنْ حَرَّمَ زِينَةَ ٱللَّهِ ٱلَّتِىٓ أَخْرَجَ لِعِبَادِهِۦ وَٱلطَّيِّبَـٰتِ مِنَ ٱلرِّزْقِ ۚ قُلْ هِىَ لِلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ فِى ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا خَالِصَةً يَوْمَ ٱلْقِيَـٰمَةِ ۗ كَذَٰلِكَ نُفَصِّلُ ٱلْـَٔايَـٰتِ لِقَوْمٍ يَعْلَمُونَ

*Ya bani Adama khudhu zinatakum 'inda kulli masjidin wa kulu wa ishrabu wa la tusrifu. Innahu la yuhibbu al-musrifin.*

*Qul man harrama zinata Allahi allati akhraja li-'ibadihi wa al-tayyibati mina al-rizq. Qul hiya lilladhina amanu fi al-hayati al-dunya khalisatan yawma al-qiyamah. Kadhalika nufassilu al-ayati li-qawmin ya'lamun.*

**O children of Adam, take your adornment at every place of prostration, and eat and drink, but do not commit excess. Indeed, He does not love the excessive. Say, "Who has forbidden the adornment of Allah which He has brought forth for His servants and the good things of provision?" Say, "They are for those who believe during the worldly life, exclusively theirs on the Day of Resurrection." Thus do We detail the signs for a people who know.**

## Introduction

Once the Qur'an corrects shameless exposure, it does not swing into a religion of ugliness.

That is one of the passage's beauties. After warning against stripping and indecency, Allah tells the children of Adam to take their adornment at every place of prostration. Then He tells them to eat and drink. The surah is teaching balance. Worship is not meant to be careless and shabby, nor is piety meant to declare war on Allah's wholesome provision.

[PAUSE]

But the balance has a boundary. Eat and drink, yes. Adorn yourselves, yes. Yet do not commit excess. So the Qur'an refuses both distortions at once: false ascetic severity on one side and appetite without measure on the other.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **Allah does not purify His servants by forbidding beauty and provision in themselves, but by teaching them to receive both lawfully, gratefully, and without israf.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. Adornment Is Taken Into Worship

The ayah begins:

*Khudhu zinatakum 'inda kulli masjid.*

Take your adornment at every place of prostration.

[PAUSE]

The word *zinah* is not minimal coverage only. It carries adornment, presentability, what beautifies and dignifies outward appearance. So the ayah teaches that worship is not approached with neglectful indifference to the body. There is an adab to how one appears before Allah.

And the wording *khudhu* - take - suggests intentionality. Do not drift into worship carelessly. Bring your adornment with consciousness.

### 2. Lawful Enjoyment Is Commanded, But Excess Is Forbidden

Then:

*Wa kulu wa ishrabu wa la tusrifu.*

Eat and drink, but do not commit excess.

[PAUSE]

This is an extraordinary moral proportion. Allah does not say merely, "avoid food," or "fear delight." He commands eating and drinking as part of a sane human life. But then He places a limit: *la tusrifu*. Do not cross the bounds in quantity, attitude, wastefulness, or obsession.

Then the reason:

*Innahu la yuhibbu al-musrifin.*

Indeed He does not love the excessive.

The issue is no longer only the object consumed. It is the kind of person being formed: the *musrif*, the one who habitually spills beyond measure.

### 3. The Surah Rebukes False Piety That Forbids Allah's Beauty

Then:

*Qul man harrama zinata Allahi allati akhraja li-'ibadihi wa al-tayyibati mina al-rizq.*

Who has forbidden the adornment of Allah that He brought forth for His servants and the wholesome things from provision?

[PAUSE]

The rhetorical question cuts sharply. If Allah brought it forth, by what right does someone else outlaw it? The beauty is called *zinat Allah* in the sense that it belongs to His gift, His creation, His permission. And the provision is called *tayyibat*, wholesome and good, not morally suspect by default.

### 4. The Believers' Relationship to Dunya and Akhirah Is Clarified

Then:

*Hiya lilladhina amanu fi al-hayati al-dunya khalisatan yawma al-qiyamah.*

They are for those who believe in the worldly life, exclusively theirs on the Day of Resurrection.

[PAUSE]

In dunya, believers share the world of provision with others. But in the akhirah the purity of divine gift belongs to them without mixture, rivalry, or contamination. The verse teaches that lawful beauty here is not a betrayal of piety. It is a preview handled with restraint.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Islam Refuses Both Spiritual Shabbiness and Spiritual Gluttony

These ayahs are profoundly corrective because human beings often lurch toward extremes. Some imagine nearness to Allah requires hostility to beauty, comfort, or taste itself. Others baptize indulgence and call it gratitude. The Qur'an accepts neither.

[PAUSE]

Instead it teaches a liturgy of proportion: adornment in worship, nourishment in life, and a red line at israf. That is a much more searching ethic than simple denial, because it requires inner measure rather than outward theatrics.

### Theme 2: Forbidding What Allah Made Lawful Can Be Its Own Distortion

The rhetorical challenge in ayah 32 should unsettle every tendency to invent holiness through prohibition. Sometimes the ego feels safer with more rules than Allah made, because invented harshness can imitate seriousness. But the Qur'an asks: who forbade it?

[PAUSE]

That question keeps religion honest. Not everything restrained-looking is righteous, and not everything beautiful is suspect. The real issue is whether the beauty is Allah's lawful beauty and whether the one enjoying it remains inside gratitude, modesty, and measure.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs place a believer between two corruptions and teach him to walk between them.

Do not strip worship of beauty. Do not strip life of Allah's lawful provision. But do not cross into israf either. The children of Adam are not healed from shamelessness by becoming enemies of every adornment. They are healed by learning how to take beauty into worship and provision into life without letting either become excess.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: in my own habits, do I distort piety more by needless severity or by a quiet normalization of excess?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **Allah's religion does not sanctify ugliness or appetite, but teaches the children of Adam to receive adornment and provision as gifts that must remain under worship, gratitude, and measure.**

# No One Feels Secure From the Plan of Allah

## Surah Al-Araf (7:96-99)

[ayah:7:96] وَلَوْ أَنَّ أَهْلَ ٱلْقُرَىٰٓ ءَامَنُوا۟ وَٱتَّقَوْا۟ لَفَتَحْنَا عَلَيْهِم بَرَكَـٰتٍ مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَلَـٰكِن كَذَّبُوا۟ فَأَخَذْنَـٰهُم بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْسِبُونَ

[ayah:7:97] أَفَأَمِنَ أَهْلُ ٱلْقُرَىٰٓ أَن يَأْتِيَهُم بَأْسُنَا بَيَـٰتًا وَهُمْ نَآئِمُونَ

[ayah:7:98] أَوَأَمِنَ أَهْلُ ٱلْقُرَىٰٓ أَن يَأْتِيَهُم بَأْسُنَا ضُحًى وَهُمْ يَلْعَبُونَ

[ayah:7:99] أَفَأَمِنُوا۟ مَكْرَ ٱللَّهِ ۚ فَلَا يَأْمَنُ مَكْرَ ٱللَّهِ إِلَّا ٱلْقَوْمُ ٱلْخَـٰسِرُونَ

*Wa law anna ahla al-qura amanu wa-ittaqaw la-fatahna alayhim barakatin mina al-sama'i wa al-ard. Wa lakin kadhdhabu fa-akhadhnahum bima kanu yaksibun.*

*Afaa amina ahlu al-qura an ya'tiyahum ba'suna bayatan wa hum na'imun.*

*Aw amina ahlu al-qura an ya'tiyahum ba'suna duhan wa hum yal'abun.*

*Afaa aminu makra Allahi fa-la ya'manu makra Allahi illa al-qawmu al-khasirun.*

**And if only the people of the cities had believed and feared Allah, We would have opened upon them blessings from the heaven and the earth. But they denied, so We seized them for what they used to earn. Then did the people of the cities feel secure from Our punishment coming to them at night while they were asleep? Or did the people of the cities feel secure from Our punishment coming to them in the morning while they were at play? Then did they feel secure from the plan of Allah? But no one feels secure from the plan of Allah except the losing people.**

## Introduction

These ayahs arrive after a long procession of ruined peoples.

Nuh was denied. Hud was denied. Salih was denied. Lut was denied. Shu'ayb was denied. The surah has shown us arrogance, mockery, coercion, and destruction again and again. Then suddenly it stops narrating and speaks directly into the logic behind all of those stories. It tells us that none of those towns were destroyed because mercy was unavailable to them. The door had been open all along.

[PAUSE]

The unit begins with one of the Qur'an's most aching counterfactuals: if the people of the towns had believed and had taqwa, blessings would have been opened for them from the sky and the earth. Then, without letting us stay in wistfulness, the ayahs turn into interrogation. Did they feel safe while sleeping? Did they feel safe while playing in daylight? Did they feel safe from Allah's makr?

[PAUSE]

The hook of the passage is severe and tender at once: **the same settled life we treat as proof that all is well can either become an opening of barakah through iman and taqwa, or the very setting in which false security matures into loss.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. Iman and Taqwa Are Named as the Conditions of Opening

The ayah does not say that the towns merely needed better policy, better leadership, or better luck. It says:

*Amanu wa-ittaqaw.*

They believed, and they had taqwa.

[PAUSE]

The pairing matters. *Iman* is the inward settling of trust in Allah and His messengers. *Taqwa* carries the sense of guardedness, of placing a shield between yourself and what incurs Allah's anger. The root behind *ittaqaw* carries protection, caution, preservation. So the Qur'an is not describing a momentary emotional response. It is describing a society whose inner trust and outward restraint have both been reordered by revelation.

That is why the counterfactual hurts. The destroyed towns were not deprived of a path to flourishing. They were offered one and refused it.

### 2. Barakah Is Described as an Opening

Allah says:

*La-fatahna alayhim barakatin mina al-sama'i wa al-ard.*

We would surely have opened upon them blessings from the heaven and the earth.

[PAUSE]

The verb is from *fatha* - to open. Barakah here is not described as a random shower of goods dropped onto a people. It is described as an opening. Something closed would have been opened above them and beneath them. Rain from the sky. Provision from the earth. Ease in what grows. Good in what descends. Expansion in what sustains.

And the noun is plural: *barakat*. Not one blessing, but streams of blessing. The image is abundance with permeability. A life through which good can travel.

[PAUSE]

That also means the opposite is not only scarcity. Sometimes the opposite is closure. A life can still look furnished and yet be sealed. Money enters but peace does not. Information multiplies but wisdom does not. Productivity rises but hearts harden. Outward possession is not always the same thing as an opened life.

### 3. The Ayahs Move From Promise to Interrogation

Then the surah pivots with repeated questions:

*Afaa amina ahlu al-qura...*

Did the people of the towns feel secure...?

[PAUSE]

Twice the setting is named. Once at night while sleeping. Once in the brightness of *duha* while playing. The contrast is powerful because it spans two opposite moods of heedlessness. Sleep is lowered vigilance by weakness. Play is lowered vigilance by distraction. One belongs to the dark and one belongs to the bright part of day, but both describe the same illusion: that ordinary routine is evidence of safety.

Then all of it is gathered into the final line:

*Afaa aminu makra Allah.*

Did they feel secure from the makr of Allah?

[PAUSE]

In Qur'anic usage here, *makr Allah* is not the makr of creatures. It is Allah's overturning of the false security people build for themselves, sometimes through gradual respite, continuing provision, and the delayed arrival of consequence until they mistake postponement for approval. The tafsir tradition often names this dimension as *istidraj*: a person keeps receiving while thinking he is safe, and the very continuity of gift becomes the context of exposure.

That is why the ayah ends with *al-qawmu al-khasirun* - the losing people. They are not called the frightened people, or the unlucky people, or the misinformed people. They are the people of loss. Their reading of reality ruined their ledger.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Prosperity Is Not Self-Interpreting

One of the most dangerous habits of the soul is to let comfort explain itself.

If things are functioning, income is arriving, the body is mostly healthy, the schedule is full, and visible disaster is absent, we start reading all of that as a verdict. We assume the calm means acceptance. We assume continuity means safety. We assume the absence of collapse means the presence of pleasure from Allah. These ayahs break that habit with one stroke.

[PAUSE]

The towns could have had opened blessings, but opened blessings were tied to *iman* and *taqwa*. Without that pair, provision does not interpret itself. A comfortable life may indeed be mercy. It may also be delay. It may be space granted for return. It may be evidence being completed against us while we keep calling it stability.

That is a frightening thought if we hear it without tenderness. But the tenderness is already present in the counterfactual. Allah is telling us that blessing has a path. Believe. Guard yourselves. Return. Do not make your circumstances speak more loudly to you than revelation does.

### Theme 2: Heedlessness Loves Ordinary Time

The ayahs could have said simply that punishment may come suddenly. Instead they put us inside specific human states: sleeping and playing.

This is deeply diagnostic. Most of us imagine spiritual danger arriving with dramatic atmosphere. We picture warning signs, ominous moods, obvious unraveling. The Qur'an says danger may reach people while they are inside the most ordinary forms of unconsciousness: asleep in the night, amused in the brightness of morning, moving through routine with no expectation that anything is being measured.

[PAUSE]

That does not mean the believer is supposed to become paranoid. It means we are supposed to become awake. There is a difference. Wakefulness is not panic. It is moral sobriety. It is knowing that a heart can drift while the calendar remains full, that a household can look intact while gratitude has thinned out, that a society can call itself advanced while the conditions of destruction are quietly ripening underneath the surface.

And this is where *makr Allah* becomes personally relevant. The verse is not merely about ancient cities. It is about every soul that confuses deferment with endorsement. Every time we say, in effect, "If Allah were displeased, something obvious would have happened by now," we are walking near the edge of the very thought these ayahs condemn.

## Closing Synthesis

This passage stands like a verdict over every false reading of comfort.

If the people of the towns had believed and had taqwa, the heavens and the earth would have opened for them in blessing. But they denied, and what they kept earning overtook them. Then the surah asks whether any people really imagine themselves safe while sleeping, safe while playing, safe while moving through apparent normality. The final diagnosis is that only the losing people feel secure from Allah's makr.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with these ayahs is to ask: am I reading my present ease through revelation, or am I reading revelation through my present ease? When doors open for me, do I see barakah tied to gratitude, obedience, and taqwa, or do I let continuity become a narcotic?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the unit lands here: **faith and taqwa turn provision into opened barakah, while denial turns even ordinary life into a setting for false security, and only the losing soul mistakes delayed reckoning for real safety.**

# They Cannot Create or Follow

## Surah Al-Araf (7:191-193)

[ayah:7:191] أَيُشْرِكُونَ مَا لَا يَخْلُقُ شَيْـًٔا وَهُمْ يُخْلَقُونَ

[ayah:7:192] وَلَا يَسْتَطِيعُونَ لَهُمْ نَصْرًا وَلَآ أَنفُسَهُمْ يَنصُرُونَ

[ayah:7:193] وَإِن تَدْعُوهُمْ إِلَى ٱلْهُدَىٰ لَا يَتَّبِعُوكُمْ ۚ سَوَآءٌ عَلَيْكُمْ أَدَعَوْتُمُوهُمْ أَمْ أَنتُمْ صَـٰمِتُونَ

*Ayushrikuna ma la yakhluqu shay'an wa hum yukhlaqun.*

*Wa la yastati'una lahum nasran wa la anfusahum yansurun.*

*Wa in tad'uhum ila al-huda la yattabi'ukum. Sawa'un 'alaykum a-da'awtumuhum am antum samitun.*

**Do they associate as partners that which creates nothing while they themselves are created? And they are not able to help them, nor can they help themselves. And if you call them to guidance, they will not follow you. It is all the same for you whether you call them or you remain silent.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

After showing how blessing can be contaminated by shirk, the surah now attacks shirk at the level of plain reason.

It does not begin with a complicated philosophical system. It begins with humiliation by obvious facts. The things being associated with Allah do not create. They cannot help their worshippers. They cannot even help themselves. And if they are called toward guidance, they do not move.

[PAUSE]

The effect is devastating. Idolatry is not merely condemned as disobedience. It is exposed as dependence on what is fundamentally inert.

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. They Associate What Is Itself Created

Ayah 191 asks:

*Ayushrikuna ma la yakhluqu shay'an wa hum yukhlaqun.*

Do they associate that which creates nothing while they themselves are created?

[PAUSE]

The question form matters. The ayah is not merely informing. It is shaming false proportion. How can partnership with Allah be imagined for something that does not create even a single thing?

Then comes the final turn of the sentence: *wa hum yukhlaqun* - while they themselves are created. So the alleged partners are not merely non-creators. They are creatures. Their whole existence is derivative.

This is the first great collapse of shirk in the passage. The heart is being asked: why lean ultimate dependence upon what stands under the same created condition as everything else?

### 2. They Cannot Help Others or Themselves

Ayah 192 continues:

*Wa la yastati'una lahum nasran wa la anfusahum yansurun.*

They are not able to help them, nor can they help themselves.

[PAUSE]

The key word here is *yastati'una* - they are not able. Incapacity is the point.

Then the sentence moves in two circles. First, they cannot help their worshippers. That already destroys the logic of calling upon them. But the ayah then goes further: they cannot even help themselves. The second negation is harsher, because it removes even the illusion that they possess hidden reserve strength which they simply do not deploy.

So false gods fail both outwardly and inwardly. They cannot rescue others because they are themselves unrescuing.

### 3. They Do Not Even Respond to a Call Toward Guidance

Then ayah 193 says:

*Wa in tad'uhum ila al-huda la yattabi'ukum.*

If you call them to guidance, they will not follow you.

[PAUSE]

This is a remarkable line because it momentarily speaks to the idols as though they could be addressed. But the point is precisely that they do not and cannot respond.

Then comes the conclusion:

*Sawa'un 'alaykum a-da'awtumuhum am antum samitun.*

It is all the same for you whether you call them or you remain silent.

That means invocation itself has been emptied. The whole ritual act becomes exposed as ineffective motion. Speech toward them changes nothing. Silence changes nothing. The relation is spiritually sterile either way.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Shirk Thrives by Granting Ultimate Weight to What Is Creaturely and Helpless

These ayahs dismantle idolatry by attacking its hidden illusion: that something created can be treated as if it carries ultimate power.

The specific idol may differ across times. Stone, status, saint, symbol, system, ideology, wealth, image, influence. But the Qur'anic logic keeps returning: does it create? Can it truly help? Can it even sustain itself? If not, then the heart has given too much weight to what is creaturely.

[PAUSE]

This is why the ayah's argument never grows old. Modern people may not bow to carved images, but we still attach ourselves to created things as though they could finally secure us. The Qur'an does not let the heart hide behind updated forms. The criterion remains the same.

### Theme 2: One Sign of False Dependence Is That It Never Truly Answers Back

Ayah 193 adds a subtle but piercing layer. False gods do not merely lack cosmic power. They do not respond with guidance.

A relationship built on illusion can consume enormous emotional energy while yielding no true answer. A person keeps calling, sacrificing, adjusting, obsessing, and waiting, yet no real guidance comes back. The ritual may continue, but the dependency remains mute.

[PAUSE]

That is part of the mercy of this passage. It teaches us to ask not only whether something feels important to us, but whether it actually responds with what the soul most needs. Guidance belongs to Allah. What cannot guide cannot deserve ultimate reliance.

## Closing Synthesis

These three ayahs strip false gods down to their actual condition.

They do not create, though they themselves are created. They cannot help their worshippers, nor can they help themselves. And if they are addressed or called toward guidance, nothing changes: speech and silence are the same before what cannot respond.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: what created thing in my life have I burdened with more expectation than it was ever made to carry? Where am I treating means as if they were sources, or attachments as if they were saviors? And when I keep calling on something other than Allah for ultimate steadiness, has it ever truly answered with guidance, or only kept me cycling inside dependence?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the unit lands here: **the Qur'an exposes shirk as absurd reliance on what is itself created, incapable of help, incapable of self-defense, and incapable of response, so the heart is pushed back toward the only One whose creating, helping, and guiding are real.**

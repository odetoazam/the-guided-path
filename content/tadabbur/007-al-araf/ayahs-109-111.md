# He Wants to Drive You Out of Your Land

## Surah Al-Araf (7:109-111)

[ayah:7:109] قَالَ ٱلْمَلَأُ مِن قَوْمِ فِرْعَوْنَ إِنَّ هَـٰذَا لَسَـٰحِرٌ عَلِيمٌ

[ayah:7:110] يُرِيدُ أَن يُخْرِجَكُم مِّنْ أَرْضِكُمْ ۖ فَمَاذَا تَأْمُرُونَ

[ayah:7:111] قَالُوٓا۟ أَرْجِهْ وَأَخَاهُ وَأَرْسِلْ فِى ٱلْمَدَآئِنِ حَـٰشِرِينَ

*Qala al-mala'u min qawmi Fir'awna inna hadha la-sahirun alim.*

*Yuridu an yukhrijakum min ardikum fa-madha ta'murun.*

*Qalu arjih wa akhahu wa arsil fi al-mada'ini hashirin.*

**The eminent ones among the people of Fir'awn said, "Indeed, this is surely a learned magician. He wants to drive you out of your land, so what do you command?" They said, "Postpone him and his brother, and send into the cities gatherers."**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

There are moments when truth enters a room so clearly that power cannot answer it honestly.

It cannot simply say nothing, because the sign has been seen.

It cannot yield, because yielding would cost it too much.

So it does something else. It changes the category.

[PAUSE]

Musa has just spoken before Fir'awn with prophetic clarity and cast the signs Allah gave him. The court has seen enough that ordinary dismissal is no longer possible. So these ayahs show the next move of a threatened order. The chiefs do not ask whether Musa is true. They call him a skilled magician. They do not ask whether Allah has spoken. They recast him as a threat to land and order. Then the room turns to consultation: what procedure should now be taken?

That is the hook of the passage: **when false power feels truth pressing against its throat, it often stops arguing about truth and starts narrating danger, then buys time until it can reorganize the room around fear.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. `Al-mala'`: Truth Is First Reframed by the Filled Circle Around Power

The ayah begins:

`qala al-mala'u min qawmi Fir'awn`

The eminent ones from the people of Fir'awn said.

[PAUSE]

The Quran could have said simply that "they said." Instead it names the speaking class: `al-mala'`.

In Quranic usage, `al-mala'` is the notable circle, the chiefs, the public-facing elite whose presence fills the political room. They are the ones who stabilize the ruler's world by turning his fear into language, policy, and consensus. They are close enough to power to protect it, and powerful enough to teach the wider people how the moment is supposed to be interpreted.

That matters because falsehood often survives not by raw force alone, but by a class of interpreters who keep force feeling rational. Fir'awn has seen Musa's signs, but the chiefs are the ones who begin the collective reframing. Before the crowd can sit with what they just witnessed, the elite class steps in to tell them what it "really" was.

The first defense of a false order is very often not violence yet. It is narration.

### 2. `La-sahirun alim` and `yuridu an yukhrijakum`: The Sign Is Turned Into a Threat

Their line is striking:

`inna hadha la-sahirun alim`

This is surely a learned magician.

[PAUSE]

Notice they do not call Musa a fool, an amateur, or a confused man. They call him `sahir` and then intensify it with `alim` - one who knows, one who is technically accomplished, one who has command of his craft.

In one sense, this is slander. But in another sense, it is unwilling admiration. The sign was too weighty to be dismissed as clumsy trickery. So the accusation becomes more sophisticated. If we cannot deny the force of what was seen, we will explain it as elite-level manipulation.

Then the next ayah tells us why this label is being chosen:

`yuridu an yukhrijakum min ardikum`

He wants to drive you out of your land.

[PAUSE]

This is a revealing shift. Musa came speaking about Allah, truth, and the release of the oppressed. The chiefs answer in the language of territory, possession, and collective threat. The issue is moved from revelation to security.

And look at the pronouns: `yukhrijakum` and `ardikum` - drive **you** out of **your** land. Fear becomes effective when it is personalized. The court is being trained to hear Musa not as messenger but as invader, not as truth-bearer but as destabilizer of public life.

This is one of the oldest maneuvers in the moral history of rejection. If truth threatens the arrangement by which some people remain comfortable, the truth is accused of threatening society itself.

### 3. `Fa-madha ta'murun` / `arjih`: When Power Feels Pressure, It Seeks Delay

Then comes the consultative turn:

`fa-madha ta'murun`

So what do you command?

[PAUSE]

As al-Tabari notes, this is the moment the discourse turns to Fir'awn consulting his council: what do you advise, what should be done with this matter? The scene has moved fully into the grammar of control. We are no longer in the field of wonder, repentance, or listening. The room is asking what managerial action should now be taken.

And their answer is not "answer him." It is:

`arjih wa akhahu`

Postpone him and his brother.

The response to unsettling truth is delay.

Buy time.

Do not let the moment stay raw.

Do not let the sign remain direct.

Put some distance between what people saw and what they are being allowed to conclude.

[PAUSE]

Then:

`wa arsil fi al-mada'ini hashirin`

Send into the cities gatherers.

`Hashirin` are those who gather up the necessary people, assemble the network, pull in the practitioners, build the counter-scene. The system begins mobilizing. What revelation entered as a confrontation with conscience is now being converted into a managed public contest.

That is the logic of these ayahs: **label, alarm, delay, organize.** The court cannot erase what happened, so it decides to contain it.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Threatened Hearts Often Call Truth "Dangerous" Before They Call It False

There is a subtle but devastating spiritual insight here.

Sometimes when people feel exposed by the truth, they do not first say, "This is untrue." They say, "This is dangerous."

[PAUSE]

That sentence can appear in many forms.

This conversation is destabilizing.

This reminder is too intense.

This command will disrupt too much.

This call to obedience will cost people their place.

This repentance would change everything.

The point is not that every warning about danger is dishonest. The point is that the ego and the unjust order know how to wrap self-protection in the language of public concern.

Fir'awn's chiefs are not defending truth. They are defending arrangement. Musa threatens a whole structure of false belonging, false sovereignty, and ownership over other human beings. So his message is translated into danger to the land.

We should sit with that carefully in our own lives. Sometimes the thing we call "too disruptive" is simply the thing that would force us to stop living a lie. Sometimes we call an ayah, a command, or a needed repentance "complicated" because the alternative label - "I do not want to submit" - is too naked to bear.

### Theme 2: Delay Is One of Falsehood's Most Effective Defenses

`Arjih.`

Postpone him.

[PAUSE]

This is a small word, but it carries one of the oldest diseases of the soul.

Later.

Not now.

Let's return to this after things settle.

Let's gather more voices.

Let's put this in a different setting.

Let's create one more layer between ourselves and the demand of truth.

Falsehood does not always need immediate denial. Sometimes deferment is enough. If the heart can be kept from responding while the sign is still alive inside it, the moment may cool, fear may be reorganized, and the truth can be pushed into a frame more comfortable for ego and power.

[PAUSE]

That is why these ayahs are not only about an ancient court. They are about our interior life. How many times has Allah shown us something clearly - in prayer, in loss, in a conversation, in an ayah we knew was aimed straight at us - and instead of yielding, we asked our inner court to buy time?

The self is full of little ministers.

One says: not the right season.

Another says: this will cost too much.

Another says: wait until you understand everything first.

Another says: maybe gather more opinions until the urgency dissolves.

And before long, the soul has done its own version of `wa arsil fi al-mada'ini hashirin` - it has assembled enough inner noise to avoid the simple obedience the moment required.

## Closing Synthesis

These three ayahs show us the first organized reaction of Fir'awn's world after Musa's signs have entered the court.

The chiefs speak first. They relabel Musa as a highly skilled magician. They reinterpret his mission as a threat to collective possession and national order. Then the room shifts from truth to command, from wonder to administration. The recommendation is postponement and mobilization: delay him, gather people from the cities, and remake the confrontation on terms power thinks it can control.

[PAUSE]

This is not only ancient politics. It is a recurring anatomy of resistance to Allah's truth. A heart, a household, a community, or a ruling class feels the pressure of revelation and responds with the same sequence: rename, securitize, delay, organize.

The questions these ayahs leave with us are uncomfortable and necessary.

### Questions to Carry

- Where in your life have you changed the question from "Is this true?" to "What will this cost me if I obey?"
- What truth have you quietly labeled as disruptive because yielding to it would rearrange your comfort?
- What does `arjih` sound like in your own inner language of postponement?
- When Allah makes something clear, do you answer the clarity itself, or do you immediately start assembling reasons to defer it?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**When truth threatens a false order, it is often renamed as danger, then postponed until fear and procedure can reorganize the room against it.**

### Du'a

*Allahumma, protect us from hearts that hear truth and immediately translate it into threat. Save us from the delay that cools conviction and from the inner court that gathers excuses against obedience. When Your signs become clear to us, let us answer with surrender before fear has time to reorganize our souls.*

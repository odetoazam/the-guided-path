# Prepare Fully, But Incline to Peace When It Is Real

## Surah Al-Anfal (8:59-61)

[ayah:8:59] وَلَا يَحْسَبَنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ سَبَقُوٓا۟ ۚ إِنَّهُمْ لَا يُعْجِزُونَ

[ayah:8:60] وَأَعِدُّوا۟ لَهُم مَّا ٱسْتَطَعْتُم مِّن قُوَّةٍ وَمِن رِّبَاطِ ٱلْخَيْلِ تُرْهِبُونَ بِهِۦ عَدُوَّ ٱللَّهِ وَعَدُوَّكُمْ وَءَاخَرِينَ مِن دُونِهِمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَهُمُ ٱللَّهُ يَعْلَمُهُمْ ۚ وَمَا تُنفِقُوا۟ مِن شَىْءٍ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ يُوَفَّ إِلَيْكُمْ وَأَنتُمْ لَا تُظْلَمُونَ

[ayah:8:61] ۞ وَإِن جَنَحُوا۟ لِلسَّلْمِ فَٱجْنَحْ لَهَا وَتَوَكَّلْ عَلَى ٱللَّهِ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ هُوَ ٱلسَّمِيعُ ٱلْعَلِيمُ

*Wa la yahsabanna alladhina kafaru sabaqu. Innahum la yu'jizun.*

*Wa a'iddu lahum ma istata'tum min quwwatin wa min ribati al-khayli turhibuna bihi 'aduwwa Allahi wa 'aduwwakum wa akharina min dunihim la ta'lamunahumu Allahu ya'lamuhum. Wa ma tunfiqu min shay'in fi sabilillahi yuwaffa ilaykum wa antum la tuzlamun.*

*Wa in janahu li al-salmi fajnah laha wa tawakkal 'ala Allah. Innahu huwa al-sami'u al-'alim.*

**Let not those who disbelieve think they have escaped. They will never frustrate Allah. And prepare against them whatever force you can and tethered horses by which you may deter the enemy of Allah and your enemy and others besides them whom you do not know but Allah knows. Whatever you spend in the path of Allah will be repaid to you in full, and you will not be wronged. And if they incline to peace, then incline to it also and rely upon Allah. Surely He alone is the All-Hearing, All-Knowing.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

There is a kind of false piety that thinks trust in Allah means neglecting preparation.

There is another kind of hardness that thinks preparation means becoming incapable of peace.

These ayahs refuse both distortions at once.

[PAUSE]

First the surah shuts the door on a dangerous illusion: let not the disbelievers imagine they have gotten away, outrun consequence, or slipped beyond Allah's reach. Then, with no passivity, it commands the believers to prepare whatever force they can. Real capacity. Real readiness. Real expenditure. Real deterrence against visible enemies and even against others still unknown to them but fully known to Allah.

And then, just when the soul could mistake that preparation for a charter of perpetual hostility, the next ayah turns the direction of the heart: if they incline to peace, you incline too. Not because you stopped being prepared. Not because you are naive about the world. But because believers are not commanded to love conflict. They are commanded to be ready for it without worshipping it.

This sequence is one of the Qur'an's most balanced political and spiritual teachings. Strength is obligatory. Readiness matters. Spending for communal protection is not beneath tawakkul. But neither may the believer become so invested in hardness that genuine peace becomes intolerable. The final anchor is still reliance upon Allah, who hears what is said behind offers of peace and knows what is hidden beyond visible enemies.

The hook of the passage is this: **the Qur'an commands the believer to prepare with full seriousness and still remain inwardly free enough to accept real peace when it appears, because tawakkul is not laziness before threat nor addiction to conflict.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **La yahsabanna ... sabaqu**: The Qur'an First Corrects the Fantasy of Escape

Verse 59 begins by correcting a thought:

**wa la yahsabanna alladhina kafaru sabaqu**.

Let not those who disbelieve think they have outrun things.

[PAUSE]

The issue is not merely what they did. It is what they imagine about what they did. They think they have moved ahead, escaped consequence, gotten beyond capture, or left the believers behind. The Qur'an interrupts that mental picture at once.

Then:

**innahum la yu'jizun**.

They will not render powerless. They cannot frustrate, exhaust, or outstrip Allah.

So before the command to prepare even arrives, the verse relocates the entire field under divine supremacy. Human planning matters, but no disbelieving force has truly escaped the reach of Allah.

### 2. **Wa a'iddu lahum ma istata'tum min quwwah**: Preparation Is Commanded at the Limit of Capacity

Then the command:

**wa a'iddu lahum ma istata'tum**.

Prepare for them whatever you are able.

[PAUSE]

This wording is important. The verse does not ask for fantasy. It asks for maximum truthful capacity. **Ma istata'tum** ties preparation to real ability, sustained effort, and responsible use of means.

Then it names:

**min quwwah** - from force, capacity, strength.

The word is broad. It is larger than one weapon system or one historical form. The surah is not narrowing readiness down to a museum piece. It is commanding strength in whatever form the community can genuinely muster.

Then:

**wa min ribati al-khayl**.

And from tethered horses.

The image is vivid: readiness already held in place, already trained, already waiting. Not improvised panic after danger arrives. Preparedness before the confrontation.

### 3. **Turhibuna bihi**: The Goal of Readiness Is Deterrence, Not Bloodlust

Then the verse names the function of that preparedness:

**turhibuna bihi**.

By it you deter, you cast fear, you make aggression think twice.

[PAUSE]

This matters deeply. The goal is not frenzy, vengeance, or appetite for fighting. It is deterrence. Strength that prevents violation. Capacity that stops hostile calculation from becoming easy.

Then the field widens:

**'aduwwa Allahi wa 'aduwwakum wa akharina min dunihim la ta'lamunahum Allahu ya'lamuhum**.

The enemy of Allah, your enemy, and others besides them whom you do not know, but Allah knows.

This is one of the most sobering lines in the passage. The believers are told plainly that the visible threat is not the whole map. There are enemies outside current awareness. Allah's knowledge exceeds strategic sight.

So preparation is not paranoia. It is humble realism under divine omniscience.

### 4. **Wa ma tunfiqu ... yuwaffa ilaykum**: Expenditure for Protection Is Entered Into Allah's Ledger

Then the ayah moves from strategy to spending:

**wa ma tunfiqu min shay'in fi sabilillahi yuwaffa ilaykum**.

Whatever you spend of anything in Allah's path, it will be repaid to you in full.

[PAUSE]

This is spiritually beautiful. Readiness costs. Communities often hesitate at that point. The nafs feels defensive about money spent on protection, infrastructure, resilience, and communal safeguarding. The Qur'an answers by placing that spending inside Allah's own repayment.

You are not throwing resources into a dark hole. You are spending into a ledger held by Allah.

Then:

**wa antum la tuzlamun**.

You will not be wronged.

Nothing truly given for Allah's path disappears.

### 5. **Wa in janahu li al-salmi fajnah laha**: Readiness Must Not Kill the Capacity for Peace

Then comes the turn no merely worldly strategy would guarantee:

**wa in janahu li al-salmi fajnah laha**.

If they incline to peace, then you incline to it.

[PAUSE]

The root **janaha** suggests leaning, inclining, angling toward. Peace here is not treated as humiliation. It is a valid direction when it is genuinely taken by the other side.

And then the command is doubled back on the believer: if they lean toward it, you lean too. Do not remain addicted to the stance of confrontation once the path of peace is truly opened.

But the verse does not end there. It says:

**wa tawakkal 'ala Allah**.

And rely upon Allah.

Even peace is entered through tawakkul, because hearts, motives, and outcomes still belong to Him.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Strength in the Qur'an Is Not the Opposite of Reliance but One of Its Forms

Many people split the world in harmful ways.

If you prepare materially, they think you must be trusting the material.
If you trust Allah, they think you should stop preparing.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs heal that split. Allah Himself commands preparation at the edge of one's ability, names force and tethered readiness explicitly, then promises full repayment for what is spent in that path. This means readiness can itself be an act of obedience and tawakkul when it is kept inside Allah's command rather than worshipped as an independent savior.

That applies to more than warfare. Families, communities, institutions, and souls also need preparation. Contingency, discipline, skill, savings, training, planning, moral clarity. None of these are betrayals of tawakkul when they are subordinate to Allah. They are often part of it.

### Theme 2: The Heart Must Not Become So Militarized That It Forgets How to Lean Toward Peace

The second distortion is subtler. Sometimes after a season of betrayal, vigilance, and danger, the soul becomes attached to hardness itself. Conflict begins to feel clean. Peace feels suspicious simply because it requires softening a posture one has learned to inhabit.

[PAUSE]

The Qur'an will not allow that attachment. It commands real preparedness and then commands receptivity to peace when peace is real. This is spiritually demanding. It means believers may not build their identity around permanent hostility. They must remain servants of Allah, not servants of a hardened self-image.

And because peace can be approached with uncertainty, the ayah gives the final refuge: **wa tawakkal 'ala Allah**. Trust Him with what you cannot control once you have obeyed Him in both readiness and openness.

## Closing Synthesis

These three ayahs hold a rare balance.

The disbelievers are told not to imagine they have escaped. The believers are told to prepare every force they truly can, to maintain real deterrent power, and to spend for that readiness knowing Allah will not let the expenditure be lost. Then, without contradiction, the surah says that if the other side leans toward peace, the believers must lean as well and entrust the matter to Allah.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this unit is to ask: do I confuse tawakkul with unpreparedness? Do I spend seriously for what Allah has actually commanded me to safeguard? And once I have learned vigilance, am I still able to recognize and accept real peace, or has hardness become part of my identity?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of this unit lands here: **the Qur'an places believers between two corruptions - negligent piety and conflict-addicted hardness - and teaches them to prepare with full seriousness, accept real peace when it appears, and hold both under tawakkul upon Allah.**

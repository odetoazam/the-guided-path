# Do Not Say "We Heard" While Not Hearing

## Surah Al-Anfal (8:20-23)

[ayah:8:20] يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓا۟ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ وَلَا تَوَلَّوْا۟ عَنْهُ وَأَنتُمْ تَسْمَعُونَ

[ayah:8:21] وَلَا تَكُونُوا۟ كَٱلَّذِينَ قَالُوا۟ سَمِعْنَا وَهُمْ لَا يَسْمَعُونَ

[ayah:8:22] ۞ إِنَّ شَرَّ ٱلدَّوَآبِّ عِندَ ٱللَّهِ ٱلصُّمُّ ٱلْبُكْمُ ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يَعْقِلُونَ

## Introduction

One of the most frightening possibilities in the spiritual life is to remain physically close to truth while becoming inwardly unavailable to it.

The words still reach the ear. The lesson is still being delivered. The verses are still being recited. The tongue may even know what to say in response. But something essential has gone missing. Hearing is happening at the surface without reception in the core.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs speak directly into that danger. They do not address people who had no access to revelation. They address people who are already hearing. And the warning is severe precisely because proximity to truth can create the illusion of obedience when the heart is quietly turning away.

The passage moves in three steps. First: obey, and do not turn away while you hear. Second: do not become people who say "we heard" while not hearing. Third: understand how terrible spiritual deafness really is. By the end, the matter is not about information anymore. It is about the condition of the faculty itself.

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Warning Is Given to Those Already Hearing

Ayah 20 begins:

*Ya ayyuha alladhina amanu ati'u Allaha wa rasulahu wa la tawallaw 'anhu wa antum tasma'un.*

O you who believe, obey Allah and His Messenger, and do not turn away from him while you hear.

[PAUSE]

The phrase *wa antum tasma'un* is piercing. The prohibition is not against turning away in ignorance. It is against turning away while the sound is already reaching you.

That is what makes the warning heavier. The problem is not lack of contact. The problem is betrayal in the presence of contact.

Then notice the pairing: *ati'u Allaha wa rasulahu*. Obedience is not left as an abstract devotion to God while resisting the Messenger's concrete guidance. The verse joins them together, because revelation comes through the Messenger and must be received in the form Allah sent it.

### 2. Saying "We Heard" Can Be a Lie of the Soul

Ayah 21 deepens the warning:

*Wa la takunu ka-alladhina qalu sami'na wa hum la yasma'un.*

Do not be like those who said, "We heard," while they do not hear.

[PAUSE]

This is one of the Qur'an's most subtle diagnoses of self-deception. The problem is not open rejection alone. The problem can also be a verbal posture of compliance covering an inward refusal.

*Qalu sami'na* - they said, "we heard."

The mouth has the right script. The social performance is intact. The outward language sounds responsive.

But the verse ends: *wa hum la yasma'un* - while they do not hear.

This means real hearing in the Qur'anic sense is not mere acoustic reception. It is receptive hearing, morally consequential hearing, hearing that travels into obedience.

### 3. Spiritual Deafness Is Worse Than Animality

Then ayah 22 says:

*Inna sharra al-dawabbi 'inda Allahi al-summu al-bukmu alladhina la ya'qilun.*

Indeed, the worst of living creatures in Allah's sight are the deaf, the mute, those who do not reason.

[PAUSE]

The language is severe because the loss described is severe. *Al-summu al-bukmu* does not only mean physical disability. In context, it refers to the inward refusal of receptive hearing and responsive speech before truth.

Then comes *alladhina la ya'qilun* - those who do not reason.

This is crucial. The problem is not that revelation asks people to bypass reason. The problem is that the refusal of truth here is itself irrational. When hearing, speech, and intellect are all cut off from their proper use before revelation, the human being falls beneath the dignity these faculties were meant to serve.

### 4. Hearing Can Be Withheld as Judgment

Ayah 23 closes with a sobering line:

*Wa law 'alima Allahu fihim khayran la-asma'ahum.*

If Allah knew any good in them, He would have made them hear.

[PAUSE]

This does not deny human responsibility. It reveals the depth of the condition. Their state is such that even the granting of hearing in the fuller sense is bound up with whether there remains any goodness willing to receive it.

Then the ayah says:

*Wa law asma'ahum la-tawallaw wa hum mu'ridun.*

Even if He had made them hear, they would still have turned away while refusing.

The issue, then, is not simply access. It is aversion. The heart is not neutral before truth. It is actively leaning away.

## Part 2: Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Real Hearing in the Qur'an Is Moral, Not Merely Sensory

These ayahs force us to distinguish between exposure to revelation and reception of revelation.

Many people live very near the truth. They hear recitation, attend reminders, learn vocabulary, repeat formulas, and know what the right answer is supposed to sound like. But the Qur'an asks a harder question: did the hearing become obedience? Did it alter direction? Did it prevent turning away?

[PAUSE]

That is why the line *wa antum tasma'un* is so unsettling. It tells us that hearing itself can become part of the case against a person if it is repeatedly met with turning away. Nearness to revelation is not automatically a virtue. It becomes a virtue only if the soul responds.

And that should make us gentler and more vigilant with ourselves. The danger is not only hard disbelief "out there." The danger is the slow formation of a self that knows the language of response while withholding the substance of response.

### Theme 2: The Breakdown of Receptivity Is Also a Breakdown of Reason

The verse about *al-summu al-bukmu alladhina la ya'qilun* is especially important now, because many imagine that reason and submission sit opposite one another.

The Qur'an reverses that assumption. Here, failure before revelation is tied to a failure of *'aql*. The one who cannot receive truth properly is not being portrayed as too rational. He is being portrayed as not reasoning rightly at all.

[PAUSE]

This means spiritual refusal is not an intellectual achievement. It is often a fragmentation of the very faculties that were meant to bring a person into alignment: hearing, speech, and reason.

And that is what makes the warning so serious. A person can keep living, functioning, and even talking religiously while the inner faculty of hearing is collapsing. By the time the collapse becomes visible, the turning away may already be deeply practiced.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs teach that one of the gravest forms of corruption is to stay near revelation while becoming unavailable to it.

The believers are told to obey and not turn away while hearing. Then they are warned against becoming people who say "we heard" while not truly hearing. Then the warning intensifies into a description of spiritual deafness, muteness, and irrationality so severe that it becomes the worst use of the faculties Allah gave. Finally, the passage shows that the issue is not mere lack of access, but active inward aversion.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: where in my life have I become too used to hearing truth without moving under it? Are there places where my tongue still says "I know" or "I heard," but my choices keep turning away? And do I still approach revelation as something capable of interrupting me, or have I grown too practiced at standing near it without letting it enter?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of this unit lands here: **the Qur'an warns that hearing revelation without obeying it can harden into a terrifying condition where the soul says "we heard" while remaining inwardly deaf, mute, and unreasonable before the truth it is physically near.**

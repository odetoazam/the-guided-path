# The True Call Belongs to Him, and All Things Prostrate Even When Humans Resist

## Surah Ar-Ra'd (13:14-15)

[ayah:13:14] لَهُۥ دَعْوَةُ ٱلْحَقِّ ۖ وَٱلَّذِينَ يَدْعُونَ مِن دُونِهِۦ لَا يَسْتَجِيبُونَ لَهُم بِشَىْءٍ إِلَّا كَبَـٰسِطِ كَفَّيْهِ إِلَى ٱلْمَآءِ لِيَبْلُغَ فَاهُ وَمَا هُوَ بِبَـٰلِغِهِۦ ۚ وَمَا دُعَآءُ ٱلْكَـٰفِرِينَ إِلَّا فِى ضَلَـٰلٍ

[ayah:13:15] وَلِلَّهِ يَسْجُدُ مَن فِى ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ طَوْعًا وَكَرْهًا وَظِلَـٰلُهُم بِٱلْغُدُوِّ وَٱلْـَٔاصَالِ ۩

*Lahu da'watu al-haqq. Wa alladhina yad'una min dunihi la yastajibuna lahum bi-shay'in illa ka-basiti kaffayhi ila al-ma'i li-yablugha fahu wa ma Huwa bi-balighihi. Wa ma du'a'u al-kafirina illa fi dalal.*

*Wa li-Llahi yasjudu man fi al-samawati wa al-ardi taw'an wa karhan wa zilaluhum bi-al-ghuduwwi wa al-asal.*

**To Him belongs the true call. And those they call upon besides Him do not answer them in anything, except like one who stretches out his two hands toward water so that it might reach his mouth, but it never reaches it. And the call of the disbelievers is nothing but straying. And to Allah prostrates whoever is in the heavens and the earth, willingly and unwillingly, and their shadows as well, in the mornings and the evenings.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

After lightning, thunder, angels, and thunderbolts, Ar-Ra'd now asks the question of response.

If the sky is already glorifying, if the angels are already in fear, if creation is already speaking correctly, then where does the human being turn? To whom does he call? And what does his calling actually reach?

[PAUSE]

The answer is uncompromising. The true call belongs to Allah alone. Every rival invocation is pictured as failure before the image even finishes unfolding. A person reaches out toward water, desperate for it to arrive at his mouth, but it never does. Then the surah widens one more time and says that all things in the heavens and the earth are already in sujud to Allah, even when that surrender is not mirrored in conscious human willing. Even shadows obey their rhythm before Him.

The hook of the passage is this: **Ar-Ra'd teaches that worship is not first proved by what we say we need, but by where our need is directed, because only Allah owns the true answering call while the whole world is already bowing to Him.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Lahu da'watu al-haqq**: The True Call Does Not Merely Reach Him; It Belongs to Him

The ayah begins with beautiful compression:

**lahu da'watu al-haqq**

To Him belongs the true call.

[PAUSE]

Not merely that the true call should be directed to Him, though that is included. The structure is stronger. The true call is His. It belongs to Him in ownership, in worthiness, in rightness, in the reality of where response truly resides.

And **al-haqq** here matters just as it did earlier in the surah. It names the real, the sound, the rightly grounded. So invocation itself is divided by reality. There is a calling rooted in truth, and there are calls that only imitate it outwardly while being severed from actual response.

This gives du'a enormous seriousness. Calling is not spiritually neutral. Where you direct your helplessness reveals who you think truly governs benefit, harm, nearness, rescue, and provision.

### 2. **Ka-basiti kaffayhi ila al-ma'**: False Calling Is Like Reaching for Water That Never Arrives

Then the parable comes:

Those called upon besides Allah do not answer in anything,

**except like one stretching out both hands toward water so that it may reach his mouth - but it never reaches it.**

[PAUSE]

The image is painfully precise. The person is not indifferent. He is reaching. He wants life. He wants relief. He wants something essential. But the whole movement is misaligned.

The ayah does not say the water is unreal. Water is real. Need is real. Thirst is real. The tragedy is in the method and direction of access. The person has chosen a posture that gives the appearance of seeking while never actually arriving at reception.

That makes the parable more devastating than a simple image of emptiness. The problem is not that the seeker feels no desire. It is that desire has been attached to a non-answering route.

Then the verse seals it:

**"And the call of the disbelievers is nothing but in dalal."**

Not merely unsuccessful. In **dalal** - straying, misdirection, lostness. The path of calling itself is off-course.

### 3. **Wa li-Llahi yasjudu**: The Surah Moves From Call to Sujud

The next ayah answers the failed calls with a universal scene:

**"And to Allah prostrates whoever is in the heavens and the earth..."**

The move from **da'wah** to **sujud** is powerful. Calling is the language of need. Sujud is the posture of truth. Once Allah is named as the owner of the true call, the surah shows that the entire cosmos already lives in the logic of submission.

[PAUSE]

Then comes the pair:

**taw'an wa karhan**

willingly and unwillingly.

This means the created order does not wait for unanimous emotional consent before it submits to Allah's rule. Much of creation is already under His command in ways no creature can suspend.

And then one of the most subtle images in the surah:

**"and their shadows in the mornings and the evenings."**

Even shadows. The thing with no speech, no ego, no independent mass. The ayah teaches us to see the ordinary movement of shadow as a silent choreography of submission. Morning and evening become not only time-markers, but visual reports of created obedience.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Need Alone Does Not Purify Worship

People often assume that because their need is intense, their spiritual posture must therefore be sincere.

But Ar-Ra'd teaches otherwise. Need is not the same as direction. A person can be desperate and still misdirected. A person can be thirsty and still reaching wrongly. Desire by itself does not guarantee tawhid.

[PAUSE]

This is what makes the water image so searching. The person in the parable is not casual. He wants what he wants badly. But wanting is not enough if the route of calling is false.

That has implications far beyond explicit shirk in its obvious form. Whenever the heart quietly assigns final rescue, final security, final emotional salvation, or final preservation of worth to something besides Allah, it begins stretching its hands toward water that will not actually reach the mouth. The outward forms may vary - status, approval, wealth, relationships, control, intermediaries - but the structure of the delusion remains the same.

The surah does not mock need. It purifies it by returning it to the One who truly answers.

### Theme 2: The World Is Already More Submitted Than the Ego Wants to Be

The second theme is humbling in a different way.

The heavens and the earth are already in sujud. Shadows are already obeying their appointed movement. Morning and evening are already cycles of enacted servanthood. The created order is not standing in suspense over whether Allah deserves submission.

[PAUSE]

What resists most noisily is the human ego. That is why the ayah's mention of willing and unwilling prostration cuts so deeply. Much of reality is already under Allah's command whether the self likes it or not. The real question is whether we will join knowingly, lovingly, and truthfully what so much of creation is already doing by design.

Once that lands, worship stops feeling like an isolated religious activity laid on top of an otherwise neutral universe. It becomes alignment with the grain of reality itself.

## Closing Synthesis

Ar-Ra'd first says that the true call belongs to Allah alone, then it demolishes false calling with one unforgettable image: hands stretched toward water that never reaches the mouth. After that it lifts the gaze outward again and says the whole created world is already in sujud to Allah, willingly or unwillingly, even down to the shadows that move at dawn and evening.

[PAUSE]

The architecture is profound. True invocation and true submission belong together. If Allah alone answers rightly, then Allah alone deserves the bowed posture of the cosmos. The false call is empty not only because idols or rivals cannot answer, but because all of reality is already testifying that lordship belongs elsewhere.

[PAUSE]

So the passage asks us: where do my hands really stretch when I am thirsty? What do I quietly expect to save me, stabilize me, or finally satisfy me? And if even shadows keep better rhythm with Allah than I do, what would it mean for my worship to stop being occasional performance and become alignment with the deeper order already filling the world?

[PAUSE]

Ar-Ra'd teaches us here: **the true call belongs only to Allah, and everything from the heavens to the shadows is already proving it by bowing, while the misdirected heart keeps reaching for water that will never arrive.**

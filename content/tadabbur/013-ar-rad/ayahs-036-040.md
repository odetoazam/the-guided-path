# Some Rejoice in the Book, the Quran Comes as an Arabic Judgment, and Upon You Is Only the Conveyance

## Surah Ar-Ra'd (13:36-40)

[ayah:13:36] وَٱلَّذِينَ ءَاتَيْنَـٰهُمُ ٱلْكِتَـٰبَ يَفْرَحُونَ بِمَآ أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكَ ۖ وَمِنَ ٱلْأَحْزَابِ مَن يُنكِرُ بَعْضَهُۥ ۚ قُلْ إِنَّمَآ أُمِرْتُ أَنْ أَعْبُدَ ٱللَّهَ وَلَآ أُشْرِكَ بِهِۦٓ ۚ إِلَيْهِ أَدْعُوا۟ وَإِلَيْهِ مَـَٔابِ

## Introduction

As Ar-Ra'd moves toward its close, it keeps returning the Prophet to steadiness.

Not everybody responds the same way to revelation. Some from among the People of the Book recognize what has come down and rejoice in it. Others, gathered in factions, reject parts of it. The Prophet is not told to build a message that satisfies every camp. He is told to say with greater clarity what his mission always was: I was commanded to worship Allah alone, not associate anything with Him, call to Him, and return to Him.

Then the surah expands that clarification. This Quran comes as **hukman 'arabiyyan**. Earlier messengers were human beings with wives and children. No messenger ever had independent authority over signs. Allah erases and confirms what He wills, and with Him is the Mother of the Book. And whether the Prophet sees some promised outcome in his lifetime or not, his task remains the same: convey; Allah will reckon.

The hook of the passage is this: **Ar-Ra'd teaches that revelation does not become truer by universal approval, nor does the Messenger's role expand into controlling outcomes, because the Quran comes as Allah's judgment, signs remain under His permission, and the Prophet's honor is to worship, call, and convey while Allah keeps the reckoning.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Yafrahuna ... yunkiru ba'dahu**: Revelation Divides Hearers by Integrity, Not by Exposure

The first ayah of this unit is quietly profound.

Those given the Book rejoice in what was revealed to you.

But among the factions are those who deny part of it.

[PAUSE]

This matters because it tells us that contact with scripture does not flatten people into one response. Exposure alone is not the decisive thing. Integrity is.

The verb **yafrahuna** is not a cold intellectual acknowledgment. It is joy. There are people who hear the Quran and feel recognition, gladness, confirmation, because what has come down resonates with the truth they already know from Allah's earlier gift.

Set beside them are the **ahzab** - the factions, parties, aligned camps. The word itself gives a sense of grouped positioning. Not all opposition is solitary confusion. Sometimes it is collective, identity-bearing resistance.

Then the verse says some of them **deny part of it**.

That phrase is searching because it reveals a very durable human pattern: partial acceptance can become a strategy of self-protection. A person keeps the parts that leave his structure untouched and resists the parts that threaten it.

So the Prophet is commanded to answer not with reactive customization, but with purified summary:

I was commanded only to worship Allah,

not associate anything with Him,

to Him I call,

and to Him is my return.

That is the center that does not move when hearers split.

### 2. **Hukman 'arabiyyan ... li-kulli ajalin kitab**: The Quran Comes as Judgment Within Allah's Timed Order

Then the surah says:

**wa kadhalika anzalnahu hukman 'arabiyyan**

Thus We sent it down as an Arabic judgment.

[PAUSE]

The word **hukm** is rich. It is not merely "text" or "recitation" here. It carries judgment, ruling, authoritative determination. The Quran arrives not as suggestion but as Allah's governing word.

And it comes **'arabiyyan** - in Arabic, yes, but also in the intelligible tongue of the messenger to whom it came. The revelation is not alien to its first audience in form. The resistance, then, cannot be blamed on obscurity of language.

Then comes a stern warning:

If you were to follow their desires after what came to you of knowledge, you would have from Allah no protector and no defender.

The contrast is exact:

**al-'ilm**

knowledge from Allah,

versus

**ahwa'**

their desires, inclinations, wanted outcomes.

This is one of the Quran's recurring separations. The revealed thing may be opposed not by better knowledge, but by desire dressed as principle.

Then the passage broadens out:

earlier messengers had wives and offspring;

no messenger brings a sign except by Allah's permission;

for every term there is a decree.

These lines all work together. They humanize the messengers, de-exceptionalize the Prophet in the right way, and keep reattaching the whole economy of signs and timings to Allah's governance.

### 3. **Yamhu ... yuthbit ... wa 'alayna al-hisab**: The Final Word on Outcomes Stays With Allah

Then comes the brief, immense line:

**yamhu Allahu ma yasha'u wa yuthbit**

Allah erases what He wills and confirms.

**wa 'indahu ummu al-kitab**

and with Him is the Mother of the Book.

[PAUSE]

Whatever range of interpretation scholars discuss around the details, the spiritual force here is unmistakable: mutable things are under Allah's disposal, while the deepest source, the mother-origin of the decree and record, remains with Him.

This puts the servant back in his place without humiliation and puts the Prophet back in his place without burden. You are not managing the total architecture. Allah is.

Then the surah lands the lesson practically:

Whether We show you some of what We promise them,

or We take you in death,

your duty is only **al-balag**,

and upon Us is **al-hisab**.

The pairing is unforgettable.

**al-balag**

the conveyance, the delivery, the reaching of the message.

**al-hisab**

the reckoning, the accounting, the final settling of what each person did with what reached them.

That division is mercy. It protects the Messenger from confusing truthfulness of delivery with control over response. It also protects every caller to Allah after him from imagining that sincerity is measured by whether they can engineer outcomes that Allah kept for Himself.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: We Are Easily Shaken When We Want Everyone's Response to Validate the Truth

One of the deepest temptations in any life of da'wah, teaching, witness, or even ordinary faithful living is to let the mixed response of people become a referendum on the truth itself.

If some rejoice but others reject, if one camp receives and another camp denies, we begin to feel pressure to make the truth more palatable, more selective, more adjustable, more strategic in a way that slowly shifts it away from what it is.

Ar-Ra'd refuses that pressure.

[PAUSE]

The Quran is not made true by unanimous welcome. Some people of scripture rejoice in it. Some factions deny parts of it. Both responses exist at once, and revelation remains revelation.

That is not only a prophetic lesson. It is a major medicine for the believing heart. Much of our instability comes from wanting clarity to come with applause, or wanting obedience to arrive pre-accompanied by social reassurance. But Allah often lets responses remain divided so that we learn whether we are attached to the truth itself or to the comfort of collective validation.

The Prophet's answer is therefore stripped to its center: I was commanded to worship Allah alone, not associate anything with Him, call to Him, and return to Him. A life held there can survive mixed reception without beginning to deform revelation in order to be loved by every camp.

### Theme 2: There Is Freedom in Accepting the Border Between Conveyance and Control

The other major mercy in this passage is the boundary it sets.

Upon you is the conveyance.

Upon Us is the reckoning.

[PAUSE]

Human beings suffer badly when they keep trespassing this border. We say something true, call someone lovingly, parent faithfully, advise sincerely, teach carefully, warn gently, or witness clearly, and then we begin trying to carry the part that never belonged to us: forcing transformation, guaranteeing acceptance, accelerating divine timing, or accounting for the hearts of others.

That burden becomes spiritually crushing because it is stolen weight.

Ar-Ra'd returns it to Allah. He permits the signs. He times the decrees. He erases and confirms what He wills. He keeps the Mother of the Book. He brings some outcomes in your lifetime and withholds others. He holds the reckoning.

Once that lands, responsibility does not disappear. In fact, it becomes purer. We still have **balag** to fulfill. We still must worship without shirk, call toward Allah, and remain answerable for our own fidelity. But we no longer have to pretend to be lords over outcomes.

There is immense relief in that, and also immense seriousness. Relief, because the world is not resting on our control. Seriousness, because we lose the excuse of claiming helplessness while neglecting our actual task.

[PAUSE]

## Closing Synthesis

Ar-Ra'd closes this movement by showing divided responses to revelation: some from the People of the Book rejoice, some factions deny parts, and the Prophet is told to answer by restating pure tawhid, invitation, and return. Then the surah names the Quran as an Arabic judgment, warns against following desire after knowledge, reminds him that earlier messengers were human beings with families, binds every sign to Allah's permission and every term to its decree, opens the immense line of erasing and confirming under the Mother of the Book, and finally draws the boundary that steadies the Prophet's heart: upon you is conveyance; upon Us is reckoning.

[PAUSE]

The architecture is clarifying. Truth may be welcomed or resisted. Language may be understood and still opposed. Signs may be requested and still remain under Allah's exclusive permission. History may unveil some promised outcomes now and postpone others. But through all of it, the servant's path stays the same: worship Allah alone, call to Him, return to Him, and carry the message faithfully.

So the passage asks us: where have I been waiting for unanimous reception before standing more clearly inside the truth? Where have I confused others' response with the validity of what Allah revealed? What outcomes am I trying to control that belong to Allah's `hisab`, not my `balag`? And how would my life change if I really accepted that revelation remains true, even when people only accept parts of it, while my task is to worship, call, and return?

[PAUSE]

Ar-Ra'd teaches us here: **revelation is Allah's judgment, not a product to be reshaped by factions, and the believer is freed and burdened rightly when he learns that his work is faithful conveyance while Allah keeps the decree and the account.**

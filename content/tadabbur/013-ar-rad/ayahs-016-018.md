# Blindness Cannot Equal Sight, Foam Cannot Equal What Remains, and Only Response Endures

## Surah Ar-Ra'd (13:16-18)

[ayah:13:16] قُلْ مَن رَّبُّ ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ قُلِ ٱللَّهُ ۚ قُلْ أَفَٱتَّخَذْتُم مِّن دُونِهِۦٓ أَوْلِيَآءَ لَا يَمْلِكُونَ لِأَنفُسِهِمْ نَفْعًا وَلَا ضَرًّا ۚ قُلْ هَلْ يَسْتَوِى ٱلْأَعْمَىٰ وَٱلْبَصِيرُ أَمْ هَلْ تَسْتَوِى ٱلظُّلُمَـٰتُ وَٱلنُّورُ ۗ أَمْ جَعَلُوا۟ لِلَّهِ شُرَكَآءَ خَلَقُوا۟ كَخَلْقِهِۦ فَتَشَـٰبَهَ ٱلْخَلْقُ عَلَيْهِمْ ۚ قُلِ ٱللَّهُ خَـٰلِقُ كُلِّ شَىْءٍ وَهُوَ ٱلْوَٰحِدُ ٱلْقَهَّـٰرُ

[ayah:13:17] أَنزَلَ مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءً فَسَالَتْ أَوْدِيَةٌۢ بِقَدَرِهَا فَٱحْتَمَلَ ٱلسَّيْلُ زَبَدًا رَّابِيًا ۚ وَمِمَّا يُوقِدُونَ عَلَيْهِ فِى ٱلنَّارِ ٱبْتِغَآءَ حِلْيَةٍ أَوْ مَتَـٰعٍ زَبَدٌ مِّثْلُهُۥ ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ يَضْرِبُ ٱللَّهُ ٱلْحَقَّ وَٱلْبَـٰطِلَ ۚ فَأَمَّا ٱلزَّبَدُ فَيَذْهَبُ جُفَآءً ۖ وَأَمَّا مَا يَنفَعُ ٱلنَّاسَ فَيَمْكُثُ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ يَضْرِبُ ٱللَّهُ ٱلْأَمْثَالَ

[ayah:13:18] لِلَّذِينَ ٱسْتَجَابُوا۟ لِرَبِّهِمُ ٱلْحُسْنَىٰ ۚ وَٱلَّذِينَ لَمْ يَسْتَجِيبُوا۟ لَهُۥ لَوْ أَنَّ لَهُم مَّا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ جَمِيعًا وَمِثْلَهُۥ مَعَهُۥ لَٱفْتَدَوْا۟ بِهِۦٓ ۚ أُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ لَهُمْ سُوٓءُ ٱلْحِسَابِ وَمَأْوَىٰهُمْ جَهَنَّمُ ۖ وَبِئْسَ ٱلْمِهَادُ

*Qul man Rabbu al-samawati wa al-ard. Quli Allah. Qul afattakhadhtum min dunihi awliya'a la yamlikuna li-anfusihim naf'an wa la darran. Qul hal yastawi al-a'ma wa al-basir am hal tastawi al-zulumatu wa al-nur. Am ja'alu li-Llahi shuraka'a khalaqu ka-khalqihi fa-tashabaha al-khalqu 'alayhim. Quli Allahu Khaliqu kulli shay'in wa Huwa al-Wahidu al-Qahhar.*

*Anzala mina al-sama'i ma'an fa-salat awdiyatun bi-qadariha fa-ihtamala al-saylu zabadan rabiyan. Wa mimma yuqiduna 'alayhi fi al-nari ibtigha'a hilyatin aw mata'in zabadun mithluhu. Kadhalika yadribu Allahu al-haqqa wa al-batil. Fa-amma al-zabadu fa-yadhhabu jufa'an wa amma ma yanfa'u al-nasa fa-yamkuthu fi al-ard. Kadhalika yadribu Allahu al-amthal.*

*Lilladhina istajabu li-Rabbihimu al-husna. Wa alladhina lam yastajibu lahu law anna lahum ma fi al-ardi jami'an wa mithlahu ma'ahu la-ftadaw bihi. Ula'ika lahum su'u al-hisab wa ma'wahum Jahannam wa bi'sa al-mihad.*

**Say, "Who is the Lord of the heavens and the earth?" Say, "Allah." Say, "Have you then taken besides Him protectors who possess no benefit or harm even for themselves?" And those who did not answer Him, if they had everything in the earth and the like of it with it, they would surely offer it as ransom. Those will have the worst account, and their refuge is Hell, and wretched is the resting place.**

## Introduction

These ayahs do not simply argue for tawhid. They train the soul to stop pretending that opposites are somehow equal.

Ar-Ra'd asks question after question until the false balance collapses. Is the Lord of the heavens and the earth anyone other than Allah? Can protectors who cannot even manage benefit and harm for themselves be trusted? Can blindness equal sight? Can darkness equal light? Can created things that never created anything be treated as rivals to the Creator of everything?

[PAUSE]

Then, after breaking false equivalence in thought, the surah breaks it in image. Rain falls. Valleys carry it according to their measure. Foam rises on top. Metal is heated for ornament and use. Foam rises there too. The surface swells, but the swelling is not the thing that lasts. The froth looks visible, noisy, immediate. Yet it is the part that gets thrown away. What remains is what benefits.

Then the passage closes by saying that this is not merely an intellectual distinction. Human beings are being sorted by response. Some answer their Lord and find al-husna. Others refuse until the Day comes when they would spend the whole earth and another earth like it just to escape the account.

The hook of the passage is this: **Ar-Ra'd teaches that spiritual confusion often begins when we treat unlike things as though they were equal, but revelation restores sight by separating Creator from created, light from darkness, truth from foam, and genuine response from too-late regret.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Qul... qul... qul**: The Passage Advances by Forced Clarification

The ayah opens with repeated command:

**qul**

Say.

Then again:

**qul**

And again:

**qul**

[PAUSE]

The rhythm matters. This is not casual conversation. It is revelation forcing the false argument into clarity. The Prophet is commanded to keep pressing until the hidden contradiction becomes visible.

First:

**"Who is the Lord of the heavens and the earth?"**

Then the answer is supplied:

**"Allah."**

The structure is already devastating. If the answer is already clear, then everything built against it afterward is exposed as rebellion, not confusion.

Then the ayah turns:

**"Have you then taken besides Him awliya'..."**

Protectors, patrons, allies, objects of dependence.

And what is their condition?

**la yamlikuna li-anfusihim naf'an wa la darran**

They do not possess, even for themselves, benefit or harm.

That is one of the Quran's sharpest diagnostics of false reliance. Before asking whether something can save you, ask whether it even owns itself.

### 2. **Hal yastawi... am hal tastawi...**: Revelation Heals Us by Refusing False Equivalence

Then come the comparisons:

**hal yastawi al-a'ma wa al-basir**

Is the blind equal to the seeing?

**am hal tastawi al-zulumatu wa al-nur**

Or are the darknesses equal to the light?

[PAUSE]

Notice how the ayah does not treat truth and falsehood as subtle alternatives on the same level. It does not flatter disbelief by granting it equal standing inside some neutral marketplace of ideas. It names the difference as the difference between blindness and sight, darknesses and light.

And there is beauty in the wording:

**al-zulumat** comes in the plural,

while **al-nur** comes singular.

The paths of confusion are many. The truth is one.

This is not just a rhetorical flourish. It is spiritual medicine. Much of the self's disorder comes from treating harmful and beneficial things, clear and clouded things, living and dead things, as if they should all receive equal respect from the heart. Revelation heals by discriminating rightly.

Then the ayah reaches its final exposure:

**"Or have they assigned to Allah partners who created as He created, so that creation seemed alike to them?"**

This is almost a challenge of impossibility. If these alleged partners have no share in creation, then the entire emotional architecture of shirk collapses. The answer comes with finality:

**Allahu Khaliqu kulli shay'**

Allah is the Creator of everything.

And then:

**al-Wahid al-Qahhar**

the One, the Overwhelming.

Not merely one among many. The One before whom rivals are not only false but impossible.

### 3. **Zabadan rabiyan... fa-yadhhabu jufa'an**: Foam Swells First, But It Does Not Stay

Then comes one of the most profound parables in the surah.

Allah sends down water from the sky.

**fa-salat awdiyatun bi-qadariha**

The valleys flow according to their measure.

[PAUSE]

That alone is already contemplatively rich. The same rain falls, but the carrying differs according to the valley's capacity. The revelation and the signs may come broadly, but receptivity is not uniform. Some hearts carry much. Some carry little. The rain is not deficient. The measures differ.

Then:

**fa-ihtamala al-saylu zabadan rabiyan**

The torrent carries rising foam.

The word **zabad** is the froth, the scum, the surface swelling. **Rabiyan** gives the sense of rising, swelling upward, puffing above the stream.

That is precisely how falsehood often appears. It can look large by being on the surface. It can look dominant by being loud. It can look substantial by swelling.

Then the ayah adds a second image from smelted metal:

When people heat metal seeking ornament or use, foam appears there too.

So the parable is doubled. Whether in floodwater or in the furnace, there is always a disposable surface impurity that appears above what is actually useful.

[PAUSE]

Then comes the judgment:

**fa-amma al-zabadu fa-yadhhabu jufa'an**

As for the foam, it goes away, cast off.

**wa amma ma yanfa'u al-nasa fa-yamkuthu fi al-ard**

But what benefits people remains in the earth.

This is one of the Quran's most stabilizing descriptions of truth. Truth does not have to imitate foam in order to be true. It does not have to be flashy, swollen, immediate, or noisy. Its sign is not surface size. Its sign is benefit and staying.

### 4. **Istajabu li-Rabbihim**: The Whole Parable Ends in the Question of Response

Then the surah lands the lesson where it was always heading:

**lilladhina istajabu li-Rabbihimu al-husna**

For those who answered their Lord is al-husna.

[PAUSE]

The key verb is **istajabu** - they responded, they answered, they yielded to the call.

This matters because the passage began with the true Lord and false protectors, moved through true sight and false equivalence, moved through truth and foam, and now tells us the decisive human category: response.

Not admiration. Not awareness. Not verbal acknowledgment alone. Response.

Then the other side:

those who did not respond would, on that Day, give everything in the earth and its like with it as ransom.

That is how absolute the loss is. All the things once treated as sufficient become insufficient all at once.

Then:

**su'u al-hisab**

the worst account,

and

**bi'sa al-mihad**

wretched is the resting place.

Even the word **mihad** carries weight. A resting place is supposed to hold you in safety. Here the final resting place becomes the opposite of comfort.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Much of Misguidance Is a Failure to Distinguish Properly

We often imagine misguidance as beginning with aggressive denial. But these ayahs suggest that it often begins earlier, at the level of discernment.

We flatten things that should never be flattened. We give equal emotional weight to voices that are not equal. We give equal reverence to sources that are not equal. We treat clear and clouded, healing and harmful, revealed and invented, as though fairness requires us to hold them all at the same level.

[PAUSE]

But revelation is not embarrassed to distinguish. It says: blind and seeing are not the same. Darknesses and light are not the same. Creator and created are not the same. Foam and what benefits are not the same. Response and refusal are not the same.

This matters deeply for the inner life. Many of us stay stuck not because truth has failed to reach us, but because we keep granting falsehood more dignity than it deserves. We keep treating temptations, ego stories, borrowed idols, social applause, and divine guidance as though all of them should get equal voting rights in the soul.

Ar-Ra'd interrupts that politeness. It teaches us that one act of mercy from Allah is to sharpen our distinctions again.

### Theme 2: Truth Is Known Not Only by Brightness but by What Remains and Benefits

The parable of the foam is especially important for people living in ages of speed, noise, spectacle, and churn.

Foam has advantages if all you care about is immediate visibility. It sits on the surface. It looks active. It takes up attention quickly. But the ayah teaches us not to ask only, "What is most visible right now?" It teaches us to ask, "What is actually benefiting? What is actually remaining?"

[PAUSE]

That changes how we read our own lives too.

Some impulses feel huge because they are frothy. Some anxieties feel decisive because they swell on the surface. Some trends, arguments, and emotional surges look powerful because they are loud and moving fast. But that does not make them substantial. The Quran trains patience in discernment. Wait. Watch. What turns into nothing once the current settles? What remains in the earth? What nourishes, irrigates, purifies, builds, and outlasts performance?

That is also how we should think about our deeds. There are deeds that foam: dramatic, noticeable, instantly praised, and quickly gone. And there are deeds that remain: quiet obedience, answered calling, hidden sincerity, real benefit to others, durable truth in the heart. The ayah does not deny that foam appears. It just refuses to mistake it for the substance.

## Closing Synthesis

Ar-Ra'd opens this passage by shattering false equivalence: the Lord of the heavens and earth is Allah, false protectors cannot even control themselves, blindness is not sight, darknesses are not light, and supposed partners never created anything that could resemble His creation. Then the surah gives the parable of rain, valleys, torrent, metal, and foam: what swells on the surface is discarded, while what benefits people remains. Then it closes by saying that everything turns on whether the human being answers the Lord's call.

[PAUSE]

That movement is the whole spiritual map. See clearly. Distinguish rightly. Do not be deceived by swollen surfaces. And answer while answering is still possible.

[PAUSE]

So these ayahs ask us difficult questions. What have I been treating as equal that revelation has already separated? Where am I granting foam the emotional authority of substance? When something in me swells loudly, do I know how to ask whether it benefits or merely froths? And most importantly: have I actually responded to my Lord, or have I only hovered near the truth while postponing the answer?

[PAUSE]

Ar-Ra'd teaches us here: **tawhid restores discernment, the parable of foam restores patience, and the final distinction between those who answered and those who did not restores urgency before the Day when no ransom will work and only the real response will remain.**

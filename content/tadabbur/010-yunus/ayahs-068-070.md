# A Lie About Allah Can Glitter Briefly and Still Never Flourish

## Surah Yunus (10:68-70)

[ayah:10:68] قَالُوا۟ ٱتَّخَذَ ٱللَّهُ وَلَدًا ۗ سُبْحَـٰنَهُۥ ۖ هُوَ ٱلْغَنِىُّ ۖ لَهُۥ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۚ إِنْ عِندَكُم مِّن سُلْطَـٰنٍۭ بِهَـٰذَآ ۚ أَتَقُولُونَ عَلَى ٱللَّهِ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ

[ayah:10:69] قُلْ إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ يَفْتَرُونَ عَلَى ٱللَّهِ ٱلْكَذِبَ لَا يُفْلِحُونَ

[ayah:10:70] مَتَـٰعٌ فِى ٱلدُّنْيَا ثُمَّ إِلَيْنَا مَرْجِعُهُمْ ثُمَّ نُذِيقُهُمُ ٱلْعَذَابَ ٱلشَّدِيدَ بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْفُرُونَ

**They said: Allah has taken a child. Glory be to Him. He is the Self-Sufficient. To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is in the earth. You have no authority for this. Do you say about Allah what you do not know? Say: those who fabricate lies about Allah will never succeed. A brief enjoyment in the worldly life, then to Us is their return. Then We will make them taste the severe punishment because they used to disbelieve.**

## Introduction

There are errors that come from weakness, and there are errors that become crimes because they are spoken about Allah.

The latter are never small. Once the tongue begins assigning qualities, relationships, permissions, or needs to Allah without revelation, the issue is no longer mere confusion. It becomes trespass in the most sacred field possible.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs confront one of the greatest of those trespasses: claiming offspring for Allah. The response is immediate and cleansing. Glory be to Him. He is the Self-Sufficient. Everything already belongs to Him. There is no proof. There is only ignorant speech about Allah. Then the surah gives a sobering verdict: fabricated religion may produce temporary worldly use, but it never truly flourishes.

The hook of the passage is this: **Yunus teaches that claims about Allah without knowledge are not spiritually harmless opinions but dangerous fabrications, and that even when such lies seem to survive or benefit people in dunya, they never amount to real success before Allah.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Qalu ittakhadha Allahu waladan**: A Claim Is Quoted Before It Is Crushed

The ayah opens by naming the statement:

**qalu ittakhadha Allahu waladan**.

They said: Allah has taken a child.

[PAUSE]

The Qur'an does not soften the ugliness of the claim by leaving it vague. It quotes it. This matters because sacred falsehood often survives by remaining ambient, inherited, and emotionally normalized. The Qur'an brings it into the light as explicit speech.

### 2. **Subhanahu**: Tanzih Arrives Before Extended Discussion

Then, immediately:

**subhanahu**.

Glory be to Him.

[PAUSE]

This is one of the most beautiful reflexes in the Qur'an. Before prolonged argument, there is purification. Allah is declared far above the claim. The heart is taught an instinct here: when something unworthy is attributed to Allah, tanzih must rush in.

The lie is not merely incorrect. It is beneath His perfection.

### 3. **Huwa al-ghaniyy**: Divine Self-Sufficiency Destroys the Logic of Offspring

Then:

**huwa al-ghaniyy**.

He is the Self-Sufficient.

[PAUSE]

This is not random. Offspring in created life are tied to need, continuity, inheritance, support, likeness, or extension. But Allah is **al-ghaniyy** - utterly free of need. The claim collapses not only because revelation rejects it, but because divine self-sufficiency makes the whole framework unfitting.

### 4. **Lahu ma fi al-samawati wa ma fi al-ard**: Ownership Leaves No Room for This Myth

Then the ayah expands:

**lahu ma fi al-samawati wa ma fi al-ard**.

To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is in the earth.

[PAUSE]

This is a recurring move in Yunus. Allah's ownership is brought forward not as abstraction but as demolition of false theology. If everything belongs to Him, then all beings are creatures under His possession, not relatives who complete Him or emerge from need in Him.

### 5. **In 'indakum min sultanin bi-hadha**: The Qur'an Demands Proof, Not Inherited Confidence

Then:

**in 'indakum min sultanin bi-hadha**.

You have no authority for this.

[PAUSE]

The word **sultan** here is powerful. It carries proof, warrant, evidentiary authority. The surah is teaching us that inherited religious claims are not exempt from the demand for truth. Confidence is not evidence. Tradition is not automatically proof. Emotional attachment is not sultan.

### 6. **A-taquluna 'ala Allahi ma la ta'lamun**: Ignorance Becomes Sin When It Speaks Upward

Then the indictment lands:

**a-taquluna 'ala Allahi ma la ta'lamun**.

Do you say about Allah what you do not know?

[PAUSE]

This is the deeper crime. Not merely ignorance, but ignorance speaking in God's direction. The issue is not only bad theology as an intellectual error. It is moral audacity before the Lord.

### 7. **La yuflihun**: Fabrication May Function Socially, But It Never Truly Succeeds

Ayah 69 gives the verdict:

**inna alladhina yaftaruna 'ala Allahi al-kadhiba la yuflihun**.

Those who fabricate lies about Allah do not flourish.

[PAUSE]

The word **falah** is more than bare survival. It carries real success, lasting flourishing, arriving safely at what truly matters. The ayah does not deny that fabricators may appear effective for a while. It denies that any of it amounts to actual success.

### 8. **Mata'un fi al-dunya**: Temporary Utility Is Not Vindication

Then ayah 70 compresses their apparent gain:

**mata'un fi al-dunya**.

A brief enjoyment in the worldly life.

[PAUSE]

That is all. The surah reduces the whole outward advantage of falsehood to passing use. Not triumph. Not vindication. Not permanence. A little enjoyment.

And then:

**thumma ilayna marji'uhum**.

Then to Us is their return.

Everything re-enters the field where appearances fall away.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The Tongue Becomes Most Dangerous When It Talks Easily About Allah

People often fear major sins of the body while taking major sins of speech lightly. Yet the Qur'an repeatedly returns to one especially grave danger: speaking about Allah without knowledge.

[PAUSE]

Why? Because once this boundary is crossed, religion itself is corrupted at the source. The soul is no longer merely disobeying within revelation. It is trying to rewrite revelation, or supplement it, or speak over it. That is why these ayahs sound so severe.

### Theme 2: Apparent Religious Success Can Be a Mirage

One of the ways falsehood survives is by pointing to worldly durability. People believe something for generations, institutions form around it, emotional power attaches to it, worldly benefits accumulate, and then many assume the endurance itself is proof.

[PAUSE]

The Qur'an refuses that logic. **Mata'un fi al-dunya**. Some falsehoods may indeed produce temporary enjoyment, advantage, cohesion, or prestige. But that never converts fabrication into truth. A lie about Allah remains a lie even if it travels well.

## Closing Synthesis

The passage quotes the claim that Allah has taken a child, then immediately cleanses the field with tanzih. Allah's self-sufficiency and total ownership make the claim spiritually and rationally unfit. The absence of proof is exposed, ignorant speech about Allah is indicted, and fabricators are told plainly that their path will never amount to real flourishing, however much brief worldly enjoyment it seems to provide.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with these ayahs is to ask: do I tremble enough before saying things about Allah, His religion, His intentions, or His commands? Where have I mistaken familiarity with religious language for actual knowledge? And have I secretly started treating worldly traction as proof that a religious claim must be sound?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **Allah is too perfect to be spoken about carelessly, too self-sufficient to be imagined in created terms, and too just for fabricated speech about Him to become real success even if it enjoys a brief season in dunya.**

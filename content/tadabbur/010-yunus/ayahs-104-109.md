# The Surah Ends by Returning the Prophet to Pure Worship, Pure Dependence, and Patient Following

## Surah Yunus (10:104-109)

[ayah:10:104] قُلْ يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ إِن كُنتُمْ فِى شَكٍّ مِّن دِينِى فَلَآ أَعْبُدُ ٱلَّذِينَ تَعْبُدُونَ مِن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ وَلَـٰكِنْ أَعْبُدُ ٱللَّهَ ٱلَّذِى يَتَوَفَّىٰكُمْ ۖ وَأُمِرْتُ أَنْ أَكُونَ مِنَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ

*Qul ya ayyuha al-nasu in kuntum fi shakkin min dini fa-la a'budu alladhina ta'buduna min duni Allahi wa lakin a'budu Allaha alladhi yatawaffakum. Wa umirtu an akuna mina al-mu'minin.*

**Say: O mankind, if you are in doubt about my religion, then I do not worship those whom you worship besides Allah, but I worship Allah who takes your souls, and I have been commanded to be among the believers. And follow what is revealed to you, and be patient until Allah judges. And He is the best of judges.**

## Introduction

After all the arguments, warnings, stories, rescues, refusals, signs, and patterns of history, the surah ends in a strikingly simple place: worship Allah alone, depend on Him alone, and keep following revelation patiently until He judges.

That is deeply beautiful. It means the architecture of Yunus is not ultimately trying to leave us dazzled by narrative or overwhelmed by polemic. It is trying to return us to the clean center.

[PAUSE]

These final ayahs gather the whole surah into a closing stance. The Prophet defines his religion by refusal to worship what others worship besides Allah and by worship of the One who takes souls. He is commanded to set his face uprightly toward the deen, not call on what cannot benefit or harm, rest in Allah's exclusive control over harm and favor, clarify that guidance and misguidance return upon the self, and then continue following revelation with patience until Allah's judgment comes.

The hook of the passage is this: **Yunus ends by reducing the whole struggle to its pure center: exclusive worship, upright orientation, refusal of powerless dependencies, sober personal responsibility, and patient obedience under revelation until Allah Himself decides the matter.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Fa-la a'budu... wa lakin a'budu Allah**: Religion Is Defined by Both Refusal and Devotion

The Prophet is told to say:

**fa-la a'budu alladhina ta'buduna min duni Allah wa lakin a'budu Allah**.

I do not worship what you worship besides Allah, but I worship Allah.

[PAUSE]

This is important because tawhid is not left as private sentiment. It has a double shape: renunciation and attachment. False worship is refused, and true worship is positively affirmed.

### 2. **Alladhi yatawaffakum**: The One Worshipped Is Named Through Death

Then Allah is described as:

**alladhi yatawaffakum**.

The One who takes your souls.

[PAUSE]

This is powerful. At the surah's end, Allah is not described here by a distant abstraction. He is named through the reality none can escape. The One you should worship is the One in whose hand your final taking rests.

### 3. **Aqim wajhaka li-l-dini hanifan**: The Face Must Be Set Straight

Then:

**aqim wajhaka li-l-dini hanifan**.

Set your face for the religion uprightly inclined.

[PAUSE]

The face is the site of direction, identity, and turning. To set the face is to commit the whole self's orientation. And **hanif** carries the beautiful sense of inclining away from crookedness toward pure truth.

### 4. **Ma la yanfa'uka wa la yadurruka**: False Objects Are Exposed by Their Inability

Then:

**wa la tad'u min duni Allahi ma la yanfa'uka wa la yadurruka**.

Do not call on besides Allah what neither benefits you nor harms you.

[PAUSE]

This returns us to one of the great Qur'anic tests of worthiness. If something cannot truly benefit or harm, then turning to it with the acts of dependence that belong to worship is zulm.

### 5. **Wa in yamsaska Allahu bi-durr... wa in yuridka bi-khayr**: Harm and Good Are Both Returned to Him

Then the passage deepens:

**wa in yamsaska Allahu bi-durrin fa-la kashifa lahu illa Hu. Wa in yuridka bi-khayrin fa-la radda li-fadlihi**.

If Allah touches you with harm, none removes it but Him; if He intends good for you, none repels His favor.

[PAUSE]

This is one of the surah's purest summaries of dependence. The whole field of fear and hope is recollected into Allah.

### 6. **Fa-mani ihtada fa-innama yahtadi li-nafsih**: Guidance Returns to the Self

Then:

**fa-mani ihtada fa-innama yahtadi li-nafsih. Wa man dalla fa-innama yadillu 'alayha**.

Whoever is guided is guided for himself, and whoever goes astray goes astray against it.

[PAUSE]

This restores moral responsibility after all the passages on divine permission. The servant cannot claim that the stakes are elsewhere. Guidance and misguidance return upon the self.

### 7. **Wa-ttabi' ma yuha ilayka wa-isbir**: The Final Practical Command Is Follow and Endure

The surah ends:

**wa-ttabi' ma yuha ilayka wa-isbir hatta yahkuma Allah**.

Follow what is revealed to you and be patient until Allah judges.

[PAUSE]

This is a magnificent ending. Not: control everything. Not: close the matter yourself. Not: make the whole world respond now. Follow revelation. Be patient. Wait for Allah's judgment.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The End of Spiritual Complexity Is Usually Spiritual Simplicity

Yunus has carried us through arguments with shirk, stories of prophets, scenes of rescue, human haste, too-late belief, consolation, warning, and cosmic signs. Yet at the end, the path itself is simple: worship Allah, face the deen, do not call upon powerless things, remember who controls harm and good, follow revelation patiently.

[PAUSE]

This is a mercy. The Qur'an does not increase complexity forever. It leads us back to a purified center.

### Theme 2: Patience Is Part of Tawhid

The final command to **isbir** is not disconnected from the tawhid language before it. If Allah alone controls harm and good, if He alone takes souls, if He alone judges, then patience under His timing becomes part of what it means to truly worship Him.

[PAUSE]

Impatience often hides a subtle theological problem: we want resolution before Allah gives it. The surah ends by training that impulse back into obedient waiting.

## Closing Synthesis

The final movement of Yunus has the Prophet define his religion through exclusive worship of Allah and rejection of all worship besides Him. He is commanded to set his face uprightly toward the deen, never call upon what cannot benefit or harm, rest in Allah's sole control over harm and favor, clarify that guidance and misguidance return upon the self, and then close the entire surah with two lived duties: follow what is revealed and remain patient until Allah judges.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this ending is to ask: after all my complexity, what is my life actually oriented toward? Have I made my face upright toward the deen, or am I still inwardly turned toward other powers, fears, and hopes? And where do I most need to obey the final command of Yunus right now: follow what is revealed to you, and be patient until Allah judges?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the surah lands here: **after every warning and every story, Yunus returns the servant to the clean work of tawhid - worship Allah alone, depend on Him alone, accept responsibility for your response, and keep following revelation with patience until the best of judges decides the matter.**

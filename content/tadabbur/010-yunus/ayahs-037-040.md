# They Rejected the Quran Before They Had Circled It With Knowledge

## Surah Yunus (10:37-40)

[ayah:10:37] وَمَا كَانَ هَـٰذَا ٱلْقُرْءَانُ أَن يُفْتَرَىٰ مِن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ وَلَـٰكِن تَصْدِيقَ ٱلَّذِى بَيْنَ يَدَيْهِ وَتَفْصِيلَ ٱلْكِتَـٰبِ لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ مِن رَّبِّ ٱلْعَـٰلَمِينَ

[ayah:10:38] أَمْ يَقُولُونَ ٱفْتَرَىٰهُ ۖ قُلْ فَأْتُوا۟ بِسُورَةٍ مِّثْلِهِۦ وَٱدْعُوا۟ مَنِ ٱسْتَطَعْتُم مِّن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ إِن كُنتُمْ صَـٰدِقِينَ

[ayah:10:39] بَلْ كَذَّبُوا۟ بِمَا لَمْ يُحِيطُوا۟ بِعِلْمِهِۦ وَلَمَّا يَأْتِهِمْ تَأْوِيلُهُۥ ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ كَذَّبَ ٱلَّذِينَ مِن قَبْلِهِمْ ۖ فَٱنظُرْ كَيْفَ كَانَ عَـٰقِبَةُ ٱلظَّـٰلِمِينَ

## Introduction

One of the most revealing things the Qur'an shows us is that people do not always reject revelation after careful understanding. Often they reject it too early.

They hear enough for the ego to feel threatened, enough for inherited loyalties to tighten, enough for social instinct to say this is dangerous, and then the refusal comes rushing out before the mind has really circled the truth of what it heard. The verdict arrives before the comprehension.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs enter exactly there. First they name what the Qur'an actually is: not fabricated speech, but confirming revelation and detailing the Book from the Lord of the worlds. Then they answer the accusation of fabrication with challenge. And then they expose the deeper disease: these people denied what they had not yet encompassed in knowledge, before its unfolding had even reached them.

That is a profoundly human sickness. We sometimes reject by instinct what we have not yet had the patience to understand.

The hook of the passage is this: **Yunus teaches that the Qur'an stands as divine confirmation and clarification, while much denial of revelation is not born from mastered knowledge but from premature refusal before the truth has been comprehended and lived long enough to unfold.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Ma kana hadha al-Qur'anu an yuftara**: Fabrication Is Not Fitting to This Book

The passage opens:

**wa ma kana hadha al-Qur'anu an yuftara min duni Allah**.

This Qur'an was not such that it could be fabricated apart from Allah.

[PAUSE]

The phrasing is powerful. It does not merely say, "This Qur'an was not fabricated." It says it is not of the kind that could be fabricated in the way they imagine. The accusation is not only false historically. It is unfitting to the reality of the Book.

The word **yuftara** names invented speech, forged attribution, constructed claim. The ayah is saying the Qur'an's nature itself resists that category. Its texture, coherence, weight, relation to prior revelation, and truth-claims all stand against the charge.

### 2. **Tasdiqa alladhi bayna yadayhi wa tafsila al-kitab**: Revelation Arrives as Confirmation and Clarification

Then the Book is named through two functions:

**tasdiqa alladhi bayna yadayhi**.

Confirming what is before it.

And:

**tafsila al-kitab**.

A detailed clarification of the Book.

[PAUSE]

This is crucial. The Qur'an does not descend into a vacuum. It confirms the authentic line of revelation before it, and it clarifies, details, unfolds, and distinguishes.

That means revelation here is not random interruption. It is continuity and precision together. The Qur'an enters history not as an isolated spiritual event but as divine speech that both recognizes the prior river of truth and clarifies what human beings had blurred.

### 3. **La rayba fihi min rabbi al-'alamin**: Its Certainty Is Tied to Its Source

Then:

**la rayba fihi min rabbi al-'alamin**.

There is no doubt in it, from the Lord of the worlds.

[PAUSE]

The absence of doubt is not grounded in human consensus. It is grounded in source. **Min rabbi al-'alamin.** From the Lord of the worlds.

This matters because people often search for certainty by measuring revelation against social comfort, prevailing taste, or inherited permission structures. The Qur'an keeps returning us to a different logic: certainty comes from knowing who spoke.

### 4. **Fa'tu bi-suratin mithlihi**: The Response to the Charge Is Productive Challenge

Then the accusation is quoted:

**am yaquluna iftarahu**.

Or do they say: he fabricated it?

And the response:

**qul fa'tu bi-suratin mithlihi**.

Then bring a surah like it.

[PAUSE]

Notice the dignity of this answer. The Qur'an does not panic before the accusation. It turns it outward into challenge. If you are truthful, produce even one surah of comparable force.

Then it widens the field:

**wa ادعوا man استطعتum min duni Allah**.

Call whoever you can besides Allah.

The challenge is not private artistry. It is public impossibility. Gather your aids if you wish. The claim will still collapse.

### 5. **Lam yuhitu bi-'ilmihi**: They Denied Before Surrounding It With Knowledge

Then comes the diagnosis:

**bal kadhdhabu bima lam yuhitu bi-'ilmihi**.

Rather, they denied what they had not encompassed in knowledge.

[PAUSE]

This is one of the most psychologically precise lines in the surah. The verb **yuhitu** suggests surrounding, enclosing, comprehending from the edges, taking something in fully enough to judge it responsibly.

They did not do that. Their denial outran their understanding. They reached a verdict before their knowledge had circled the matter.

How much of human arrogance lives in that gap? We dislike, fear, or feel threatened by something, and we name it false before we have really sat with it long enough to know what it is.

### 6. **Wa lamma ya'tihim ta'wiluhu**: Time Had Not Yet Brought Its Unfolding

Then:

**wa lamma ya'tihim ta'wiluhu**.

Its unfolding had not yet come to them.

[PAUSE]

This means part of revelation's truth becomes clearer in lived arrival, consequence, fulfillment, and historical unfolding. They denied before allowing time to testify.

That is important because some truths are not exhausted in the instant of hearing. They unfold in the soul, in history, in judgment, in the proving of promises and warnings. Impatient denial refuses revelation both its meaning and its time.

### 7. **Wa minhum man yu'minu bihi...**: Responses Split, but Allah Already Knows the Corrupting Work

Finally:

**wa minhum man yu'minu bihi wa minhum man la yu'minu bihi**.

Among them are those who will believe in it, and among them are those who will not.

[PAUSE]

The surah refuses simplification. The audience is not one flat block. Some hearts will open. Some will remain shut.

Then it closes:

**wa rabbuka a'lamu bi-al-mufsidin**.

Your Lord knows best the corrupters.

Not merely the doubters. The corrupters. Because denial of revelation is not always private nonacceptance. It often becomes active distortion, obstruction, contamination of the field for others.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Premature Rejection Is a Form of Arrogance

There is a kind of arrogance that does not look loud. It looks quick.

[PAUSE]

It appears when a person decides too early that something divine cannot possibly be true because it disturbs the life he already arranged. He does not investigate with humility. He does not linger with patience. He does not let the matter surround him. He dismisses before understanding.

These ayahs teach that such rejection is not intellectual seriousness. It is often ego-protection disguised as judgment. The person wants the relief of a verdict more than the burden of truthful comprehension.

We should not confine that disease to ancient deniers. Even believers can practice a smaller version of it whenever revelation presses against a cherished habit, social instinct, or self-image. We may not say "it is false," but we can still quietly refuse to let the ayah fully encircle our life with knowledge and consequence.

### Theme 2: Revelation Needs Submission, but It Also Demands Time

The phrase **wa lamma ya'tihim ta'wiluhu** teaches patience in a subtle way. Not patience because the truth is weak, but patience because some dimensions of truth unfold over time.

[PAUSE]

A person can hear an ayah today and only later discover how accurate it was about the self. A community can mock a warning and only later realize it had been living toward its fulfillment. A civilization can laugh at divine scale because immediate consequence has not arrived yet.

This should make the heart slower to dismiss and quicker to wait before Allah. Not all refusal is bold denial. Sometimes it is the quiet insistence that if I do not yet see the full unfolding, I am entitled to suspend reverence. The ayah says otherwise. The delay in unfolding is no license for contempt.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs declare that the Qur'an is not speech that could be fabricated apart from Allah, but a confirming and clarifying revelation with no doubt in it from the Lord of the worlds. When the accusation of fabrication is repeated, the passage answers with challenge: produce a surah like it and gather your helpers. Then it names the deeper disease behind denial: they rejected what they had not encompassed in knowledge before its unfolding came to them. Even so, the audience will divide between faith and refusal, while Allah knows the corrupters perfectly.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: where have I rushed to verdict before allowing revelation to really surround my understanding? What truths have I wanted to dismiss quickly because they threatened a life-structure I preferred? And when Allah's speech unsettles me, do I slow down into humility and waiting, or speed up into premature judgment?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **the Qur'an stands with the calm authority of divine confirmation and clarification, while false denial reveals itself less as mastered critique than as a hurried refusal to let truth be known deeply enough, long enough, and honestly enough to unfold.**

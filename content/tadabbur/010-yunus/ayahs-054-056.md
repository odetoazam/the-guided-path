# When Regret Comes, the Whole Earth Would Not Be Enough to Buy One Soul Back

## Surah Yunus (10:54-56)

[ayah:10:54] وَلَوْ أَنَّ لِكُلِّ نَفْسٍ ظَلَمَتْ مَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ لَٱفْتَدَتْ بِهِۦ ۗ وَأَسَرُّوا۟ ٱلنَّدَامَةَ لَمَّا رَأَوُا۟ ٱلْعَذَابَ ۖ وَقُضِىَ بَيْنَهُم بِٱلْقِسْطِ ۚ وَهُمْ لَا يُظْلَمُونَ

[ayah:10:55] أَلَآ إِنَّ لِلَّهِ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ ۗ أَلَآ إِنَّ وَعْدَ ٱللَّهِ حَقٌّ وَلَـٰكِنَّ أَكْثَرَهُمْ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ

[ayah:10:56] هُوَ يُحْىِۦ وَيُمِيتُ وَإِلَيْهِ تُرْجَعُونَ

*Wa law anna li-kulli nafsin zalamat ma fi al-ardi la-iftadat bihi. Wa asarru al-nadamata lamma ra'awu al-'adhab. Wa qudiya baynahum bi-al-qist wa hum la yuzlamun.*

*Ala inna li-llahi ma fi al-samawati wa al-ard. Ala inna wa'da Allahi haqq, wa lakinna aktharahum la ya'lamun.*

**If every soul that wronged possessed all that is in the earth, it would offer it as ransom. They will conceal regret when they see the punishment, and judgment will be passed between them with justice, and they will not be wronged. Surely to Allah belongs whatever is in the heavens and the earth. Surely the promise of Allah is true, but most of them do not know. He gives life and causes death, and to Him you will be returned.**

## Introduction

There are moments in life when a person suddenly realizes he mispriced reality.

Something he treated lightly turns out to have been immense. Something he thought he could postpone turns out to have been urgent. Something he imagined he could repair later reveals itself as a loss no wealth can undo. The heart, in those moments, feels a form of panic deeper than fear: the panic of discovering too late what something was actually worth.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs enter exactly that zone. The wronging soul would hand over the whole earth if that could buy release. Regret arrives. But it arrives after sight of punishment, not before. Then the surah steps back and grounds the entire scene in first principles: all that is in the heavens and earth belongs to Allah, His promise is true, He alone gives life and death, and to Him all are returned.

The hook of the passage is this: **Yunus teaches that once the truth of return is unveiled, the human being would pay anything to reverse self-wrong, but by then the matter stands inside Allah's ownership, justice, and irreversible decree over life, death, and return.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **La-iftadat bihi**: The Soul Would Ransom Itself With the Whole Earth

The passage opens with one of the most severe hypothetical scenes in the surah:

**wa law anna li-kulli nafsin zalamat ma fi al-ardi la-iftadat bihi**.

If every soul that wronged possessed all that is in the earth, it would ransom itself with it.

[PAUSE]

This is staggering because the ayah imagines total possession only to strip it of value at the decisive moment. Everything in the earth. All property, status, accumulation, control, gathered means. And all of it would be thrown forward as ransom if ransom were still possible.

The point is not that such a bargain will be accepted. The point is that the soul's internal valuation reverses completely. What it spent life collecting becomes acceptable to lose instantly if that could purchase escape.

### 2. **Asarru al-nadamah**: Regret Becomes Too Deep Even for Speech

Then:

**wa asarru al-nadamah lamma ra'awu al-'adhab**.

They concealed regret when they saw the punishment.

[PAUSE]

That verb is striking. **Asarru**. They kept it hidden, held it inside, secreted it. The regret is real, immense, and undeniable, but it does not emerge as liberating confession. It sits inwardly as a sealed pain under the sight of punishment.

This is different from repentance. Tawbah opens before Allah. Nadamah here comes too late and under compulsion. It has the sting of truth without the opening of rescue.

### 3. **Wa qudiya baynahum bi-al-qist**: The Final Scene Is Not Chaos but Exact Justice

Then:

**wa qudiya baynahum bi-al-qist**.

Judgment is passed between them with justice.

[PAUSE]

This is essential. The atmosphere is terrifying, but the ayah anchors it in **qist**. Exact justice. Not rage detached from truth. Not arbitrary crushing. The surah keeps bringing us back to the moral structure of Allah's judgment.

And it closes the line with:

**wa hum la yuzlamun**.

They are not wronged.

Even here. Even under terror. Even after too-late regret. The verdict remains just.

### 4. **Ala inna li-llahi ma fi al-samawati wa al-ard**: Ownership Explains Why Ransom Cannot Work

Then the surah pulls us back to theological ground:

**ala inna li-llahi ma fi al-samawati wa al-ard**.

Surely to Allah belongs whatever is in the heavens and the earth.

[PAUSE]

This explains why the imagined ransom is ultimately futile. You cannot purchase yourself out of judgment using what was never yours independently to begin with. The entire field of wealth, possession, and leverage already belongs to Allah.

The human being spends life feeling like an owner. The ayah interrupts that illusion at its root.

### 5. **Wa'da Allahi haqq**: The Promise Is Not Dramatic Language but Reality

Then:

**ala inna wa'da Allahi haqq**.

Surely the promise of Allah is true.

[PAUSE]

This is the line that forces the entire scene into clarity. The warning was never symbolic exaggeration. The promise was **haqq**. Reality-level truth. The regret of ayah 54 is not tragic because Allah surprised them unfairly. It is tragic because what He promised was true all along.

### 6. **Huwa yuhyi wa yumit wa ilayhi turja'un**: Life, Death, and Return Are One Divine Circle

Finally:

**huwa yuhyi wa yumit wa ilayhi turja'un**.

He gives life and causes death, and to Him you are returned.

[PAUSE]

This final ayah is almost frightening in its simplicity. Life begins with Him. Death comes by His decree. Return ends with Him. The entire human arc is enclosed.

That is the architecture beneath the whole passage. The soul cannot negotiate outside the One who authored its beginning, set its end, and receives its return.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: One Sign of Spiritual Delusion Is Continuously Mispricing What Matters

Many human beings are not indifferent to value. We are constantly valuing things. The problem is that we often value them wrongly.

[PAUSE]

We overvalue what can only help in passing and undervalue what will matter at return. We guard image more than sincerity, accumulation more than repentance, timing in dunya more than readiness for liqa' Allah. Then reality turns and exposes the distortion. The soul would pay the whole earth for what it once deferred.

These ayahs teach that one of the deepest forms of guidance is learning true scale before compulsion teaches it to you painfully.

### Theme 2: Regret Is Not the Same Thing as Return

The ayah's hidden sorrow is that regret finally appears, but not as tawbah. That distinction matters.

[PAUSE]

A person can feel terrible about truth after it becomes inescapable and still have missed the window of loving return. Nadamah in this passage does not open into transformed obedience. It sits under the unveiled punishment.

That should make us grateful for every present moment in which regret still can become tawbah. As long as warning is still heard in the unseen register, sorrow can still heal. Once reality has fully arrived, sorrow may remain, but its function has changed.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs picture the wronging soul willing to ransom itself with the whole earth once punishment is seen, yet regret arrives as concealed inner agony rather than saving repentance. Even then judgment is passed with exact justice and no one is wronged. The surah then grounds everything in Allah's ownership of the heavens and earth, the truth of His promise, and His exclusive power over life, death, and return.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: what am I still mispricing because dunya has not yet exposed its real scale to me? What do I treat as negotiable that I would later give everything to recover? And is my present regret over sin still the kind that opens toward Allah, or am I playing with the possibility of a regret that will come only after the matter has already been decided?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **the soul that wronged itself eventually learns that no created wealth can ransom it from a truth already made visible, because the whole field always belonged to Allah, His promise was always real, and life, death, and return were always enclosed by Him alone.**

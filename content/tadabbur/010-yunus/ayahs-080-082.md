# Allah Does Not Repair Corruption by Letting Falsehood Win the Stage

## Surah Yunus (10:80-82)

[ayah:10:80] فَلَمَّا جَآءَ ٱلسَّحَرَةُ قَالَ لَهُم مُّوسَىٰٓ أَلْقُوا۟ مَآ أَنتُم مُّلْقُونَ

[ayah:10:81] فَلَمَّآ أَلْقَوْا۟ قَالَ مُوسَىٰ مَا جِئْتُم بِهِ ٱلسِّحْرُ ۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ سَيُبْطِلُهُۥٓ ۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يُصْلِحُ عَمَلَ ٱلْمُفْسِدِينَ

[ayah:10:82] وَيُحِقُّ ٱللَّهُ ٱلْحَقَّ بِكَلِمَـٰتِهِۦ وَلَوْ كَرِهَ ٱلْمُجْرِمُونَ

*Fa-lamma ja'a al-saharatu qala lahum Musa alqu ma antum mulqun.*

*Fa-lamma alqaw qala Musa ma ji'tum bihi al-sihr. Inna Allaha sa-yubtiluhu. Inna Allaha la yuslihu 'amala al-mufsidin.*

*Wa yuhiqqu Allahu al-haqqa bi-kalimatihi wa law kariha al-mujrimun.*

**So when the magicians came, Musa said to them: cast what you are going to cast. Then when they cast, Musa said: what you have brought is magic. Allah will surely nullify it. Indeed Allah does not set right the work of the corrupters. And Allah establishes the truth by His words, even if the criminals dislike it.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

There are moments when falsehood wants a stage.

It wants to be seen at full scale, under public attention, with every advantage of theater, timing, coordination, and emotional effect. In such moments, many hearts become shaken not only by the substance of falsehood, but by its display. Spectacle can make corruption feel strong.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs teach us how revelation sees such moments. Musa does not panic before the magicians' display. He allows the act to appear, names it for what it is, and places its collapse with Allah. Then the surah gives one of its great durable principles: Allah does not repair the work of corrupters. He establishes the truth by His words, whether criminals like that or not.

The hook of the passage is this: **Yunus teaches that falsehood may gather force and spectacle, but Allah does not grant corruption lasting rectification; He nullifies it and establishes the truth through His own words.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Alqu ma antum mulqun**: Musa Does Not Fear the Unveiling of Falsehood

When the magicians arrive, Musa says:

**alqu ma antum mulqun**.

Cast what you are going to cast.

[PAUSE]

This is extraordinary composure. He does not rush to conceal the field. He does not need falsehood prevented from appearing in order for truth to remain true. Sometimes the servant of Allah can stand calmly because he knows the real contest is not between equal powers.

### 2. **Ma ji'tum bihi al-sihr**: The First Duty Is Correct Naming

Then after they cast, Musa says:

**ma ji'tum bihi al-sihr**.

What you have brought is magic.

[PAUSE]

This sentence is powerful because the category has now been reversed. Earlier, truth was called magic. Here, actual magic is called what it is. Revelation does not merely defeat falsehood materially; it exposes it verbally.

Correct naming is moral clarity. If falsehood can keep its borrowed names, it keeps some of its power.

### 3. **Inna Allaha sa-yubtiluhu**: Nullification Belongs to Allah

Then:

**inna Allaha sa-yubtiluhu**.

Allah will surely nullify it.

[PAUSE]

The future tense here matters. The outcome is coming. Falsehood can flare before it collapses. A believer sometimes has to endure the interval between display and nullification. But the ayah anchors confidence in what Allah will do, not in what the eye sees at first glance.

### 4. **La yuslihu 'amala al-mufsidin**: Allah Does Not Give Corruption a Good End

Then comes the principle:

**inna Allaha la yuslihu 'amala al-mufsidin**.

Allah does not set right the work of the corrupters.

[PAUSE]

This is a remarkable formulation. The word **islah** means setting right, repairing, bringing into soundness. The ayah is not only saying that corruption is bad. It is saying Allah does not grant the corrupt project true rectification or healthy completion.

This is deeply comforting. Corruption may spread, impress, and wound. But it cannot finally be made sound by Allah while remaining corruption.

### 5. **Wa yuhiqqu Allahu al-haqqa bi-kalimatihi**: Truth Is Made Firm by Divine Words

Then the counterpart:

**wa yuhiqqu Allahu al-haqqa bi-kalimatihi**.

Allah establishes the truth by His words.

[PAUSE]

The verb here carries making true, making firm, causing to stand as real and vindicated. And the means named are **bi-kalimatihi**, by His words. Not merely by raw force, but by decree, revelation, promise, and command.

Truth is not self-securing because humans are better strategists. It is secured because Allah has words under which it stands.

### 6. **Wa law kariha al-mujrimun**: Criminal Dislike Does Not Delay Divine Vindication

The ayah ends:

**wa law kariha al-mujrimun**.

Even if the criminals dislike it.

[PAUSE]

This is almost liberating in its bluntness. The truth does not wait for criminal approval. It does not need to become emotionally palatable to the corrupt before Allah establishes it. Their dislike is real, but it is not sovereign.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: We Are Often More Impressed by the Theater of Falsehood Than the Qur'an Is

Part of spiritual immaturity is being overly moved by what is dramatic. We see coordination, money, messaging, influence, public display, polished technique, and the soul begins to assume depth must lie underneath.

[PAUSE]

The Qur'an disciplines that reaction. It teaches us to ask simpler and more devastating questions: what is this really, and what does Allah do with this kind of thing? Much of falsehood's power depends on our willingness to be dazzled before we discern.

### Theme 2: Not Every Temporarily Effective Project Is Something Allah Is Repairing

People often confuse temporary expansion with divine approval. If something is visible, persuasive, influential, or growing, they assume it must be somehow being helped toward completion.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs challenge that assumption sharply. Allah does not **yuslih** the work of corrupters. A project can move, spread, and still be spiritually doomed. Its very momentum may be part of the moment before its nullification.

## Closing Synthesis

The magicians arrive, and Musa calmly lets them cast. Once their act appears, he names it precisely as magic and places its nullification with Allah. The surah then gives two enduring principles: Allah does not set corrupt work right, and He establishes the truth by His own words even when criminals hate that outcome.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: where am I letting spectacle distort my judgment? Do I know how to name falsehood plainly, or do I keep speaking about it in borrowed, flattering categories? And when corruption appears organized and impressive, do I still believe that Allah does not give it a truly sound ending?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **falsehood may seize attention for a moment, but Allah neither repairs corruption nor abandons truth; He nullifies the one and makes the other stand by His words.**

# The Friends of Allah Are Not Defined by Spectacle but by Faith and Taqwa

## Surah Yunus (10:62-64)

[ayah:10:62] أَلَآ إِنَّ أَوْلِيَآءَ ٱللَّهِ لَا خَوْفٌ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا هُمْ يَحْزَنُونَ

[ayah:10:63] ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ وَكَانُوا۟ يَتَّقُونَ

[ayah:10:64] لَهُمُ ٱلْبُشْرَىٰ فِى ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا وَفِى ٱلْـَٔاخِرَةِ ۚ لَا تَبْدِيلَ لِكَلِمَـٰتِ ٱللَّهِ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ هُوَ ٱلْفَوْزُ ٱلْعَظِيمُ

*Ala inna awliya'a Allahi la khawfun 'alayhim wa la hum yahzanun.*

*Alladhina amanu wa kanu yattaqun.*

*Lahumu al-bushra fi al-hayati al-dunya wa fi al-akhirah. La tabdila li-kalimati Allah. Dhalika huwa al-fawzu al-'azim.*

**Surely, the friends of Allah - there will be no fear upon them, nor will they grieve. Those who believed and used to have taqwa. For them is good news in the worldly life and in the Hereafter. There is no altering the words of Allah. That is the great triumph.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

After ayahs of warning, judgment, regret, witness, and total record, the heart needs to know what safety looks like.

Not imagined safety. Not the safety of denial. Not the safety of control. Real safety before Allah.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs answer with breathtaking simplicity. The awliya' of Allah are not introduced by hidden powers, private myths, or dramatic claims. They are introduced by two things so central and so demanding that the ego often looks past them: faith and taqwa. Then the surah gives them what every anxious heart wants most: no fear over what is coming, no grief over what has passed, and good news in both worlds.

The hook of the passage is this: **Yunus teaches that nearness to Allah is not a mystical category detached from obedience but a lived station of iman and taqwa, and that the real security flowing from that nearness is a divinely guaranteed freedom from ultimate fear and grief.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Awliya' Allah**: Nearness Is Named Through Belonging and Loyal Closeness

The passage opens:

**ala inna awliya'a Allah**.

Surely, the friends / allies / close ones of Allah.

[PAUSE]

The word **awliya'** carries nearness, loyalty, protective closeness, allied attachment. It is a deeply relational word. But the ayahs immediately protect it from vagueness. They do not let us romanticize the title without definition.

### 2. **La khawfun 'alayhim wa la hum yahzanun**: Security Covers Future and Past Together

Then:

**la khawfun 'alayhim wa la hum yahzanun**.

No fear upon them, nor do they grieve.

[PAUSE]

This is one of the Qur'an's great formulas of security. Fear usually reaches forward toward what may come. Grief reaches backward toward what has passed or been lost. The ayah covers both horizons.

That does not mean awliya' Allah never feel human emotion in dunya. It means the decisive existential fear and grief are removed from them under Allah's friendship.

### 3. **Alladhina amanu wa kanu yattaqun**: The Definition Refuses Spectacle

Then the ayah explains who they are:

**alladhina amanu wa kanu yattaqun**.

Those who believed and used to have taqwa.

[PAUSE]

This is stunning in its clarity. The awliya' are defined by iman and ongoing taqwa. Not by aura. Not by strangeness. Not by self-advertised spiritual specialness.

And the form **kanu yattaqun** gives continuity. This was not a passing mood. Taqwa became a lived pattern.

### 4. **Lahumu al-bushra fi al-hayati al-dunya wa fi al-akhirah**: Good News Is Given in Both Homes

Then:

**lahumu al-bushra fi al-hayati al-dunya wa fi al-akhirah**.

For them is good news in the worldly life and in the Hereafter.

[PAUSE]

This is beautiful because it refuses a split imagination. Nearness to Allah is not only for later. The bushra reaches them already in dunya, and then continues into the akhirah.

The exact forms of that good news may vary, but the point is that Allah does not reserve all reassurance for the final moment. He lets traces of divine glad tidings touch His people already here.

### 5. **La tabdila li-kalimati Allah**: Their Security Rests on the Unalterable

Then:

**la tabdila li-kalimati Allah**.

There is no altering the words of Allah.

[PAUSE]

This is what makes the bushra stable. It is not fragile optimism. It is tied to words no one can revise. The believer's security rests not on mood, but on divine speech.

### 6. **Dhalika huwa al-fawzu al-'azim**: Triumph Is Redefined

Finally:

**dhalika huwa al-fawzu al-'azim**.

That is the great triumph.

[PAUSE]

The ayah redefines success. Real triumph is not gathered status, invulnerability, image, or worldly control. It is to stand under Allah's wilayah with the fear-and-grief problem fundamentally resolved by His promise.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: We Often Romanticize Wilayah and Underestimate Taqwa

Many people are attracted to the idea of being close to Allah, but less attracted to the daily discipline by which the Qur'an actually describes that closeness.

[PAUSE]

We like the language of spiritual intimacy, but we often imagine it detached from ordinary obedience. These ayahs refuse that fantasy. The awliya' are those who believed and kept practicing taqwa.

That means wilayah is nearer to honest faith, careful obedience, hidden sincerity, guarded boundaries, and repeated return than to anything theatrical. The Qur'an is rescuing the concept from spiritual vanity.

### Theme 2: The Deepest Human Longing Is Freedom From Fear and Grief

Under many of our surface goals is one deeper longing: to be finally safe. To stop fearing what is ahead. To stop grieving in a way that swallows the heart. To rest.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs teach that this rest is not reached by control, accumulation, or denial. It comes through relationship with Allah built on iman and taqwa. Only then can the soul receive a kind of security that touches both future and past - no fear, no grief - because it is rooted in the nearness of the One who governs both.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs announce the awliya' of Allah and immediately grant them freedom from ultimate fear and grief. They then define these close ones with disarming simplicity as those who believed and kept practicing taqwa. For them is bushra in this life and the next, and that promise is secured by words of Allah that cannot be altered, making this the truly great triumph.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: have I made nearness to Allah feel more mysterious than the Qur'an makes it here? What would it look like for my longing for safety to be answered through deeper iman and steadier taqwa instead of through more control? And where do I still seek triumph in forms that cannot solve the fear-and-grief problem at its root?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **the friends of Allah are not a hidden class beyond the reach of ordinary believers, but those who live faith and taqwa long enough to stand under His nearness, His bushra, and His unalterable promise of ultimate security.**

# Not Even the Smallest Unseen Movement Escapes His Witness

## Surah Yunus (10:61)

[ayah:10:61] وَمَا تَكُونُ فِى شَأْنٍ وَمَا تَتْلُوا۟ مِنْهُ مِن قُرْءَانٍ وَلَا تَعْمَلُونَ مِنْ عَمَلٍ إِلَّا كُنَّا عَلَيْكُمْ شُهُودًا إِذْ تُفِيضُونَ فِيهِ ۚ وَمَا يَعْزُبُ عَن رَّبِّكَ مِن مِّثْقَالِ ذَرَّةٍ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَلَا فِى ٱلسَّمَآءِ وَلَآ أَصْغَرَ مِن ذَٰلِكَ وَلَآ أَكْبَرَ إِلَّا فِى كِتَـٰبٍ مُّبِينٍ

*Wa ma takunu fi sha'nin wa ma tatlu minhu min Qur'anin wa la ta'maluna min 'amalin illa kunna 'alaykum shuhudan idh tufiduna fihi. Wa ma ya'zubu 'an rabbika min mithqali dharratin fi al-ardi wa la fi al-sama'i wa la asghara min dhalika wa la akbara illa fi kitabin mubin.*

**You are not engaged in any matter, nor do you recite any Quran from it, nor do you do any deed, except that We are witnesses over you when you are immersed in it. Not even the weight of a tiny particle escapes your Lord in the earth or in the heaven, nor anything smaller than that nor greater, except that it is in a clear Book.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

Some truths about Allah do not come to frighten us only. They come to dissolve the illusion of private moral space.

We often live as though there are zones of our life that remain functionally unwitnessed: half-formed intentions, passing choices, online habits, quiet compromises, private speech, inner drift, unrecorded motives, the small moments that never become public story. The ego survives partly by acting like scale protects secrecy.

[PAUSE]

This ayah enters that illusion with breathtaking totality. It begins with the Prophet's state, then recited Qur'an, then all human action, and then descends beneath visible deed into the weight of a particle, smaller than that, greater than that, all of it inside a clear Book. It is one of the Qur'an's great dismantlings of imagined privacy before Allah.

The hook of the passage is this: **Yunus teaches that Allah's witnessing is not intermittent observation but total accompaniment, and that nothing in our affairs - not recitation, not action, not even the smallest hidden weight - ever leaves His knowledge or His written record.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Ma takunu fi sha'n**: No State of Occupation Falls Outside the Field

The ayah begins:

**wa ma takunu fi sha'n**.

You are not in any matter.

[PAUSE]

This is broad on purpose. **Sha'n** can carry affair, concern, state, occupation, situation. The passage does not begin only with explicit worship or sin. It begins with whatever you are in.

That breadth matters because people often reserve divine awareness for obviously "religious" moments. The ayah starts earlier than that. Any matter.

### 2. **Ma tatlu minhu min Qur'an**: Even Recitation Happens Under Witness

Then:

**wa ma tatlu minhu min Qur'an**.

Nor do you recite any Qur'an from it.

[PAUSE]

The ayah moves from general state into revelation itself. Even recitation - the holiest speech on the servant's tongue - is named under witnessing. That means the point is not to catch only transgression. It is to establish uninterrupted divine accompaniment across every register of life.

### 3. **Wa la ta'maluna min 'amalin**: The Field Widens From the Prophet to the Community

Then the wording shifts outward:

**wa la ta'maluna min 'amalin**.

Nor do you do any deed.

[PAUSE]

The move is subtle and beautiful. From "you are not in any matter," to "you recite no Qur'an," to "you all do no deed." The field widens from prophetic state to communal human action.

It is as though the ayah is saying: this is not a private prophetic assurance. It is the structure of reality for all of you.

### 4. **Illa kunna 'alaykum shuhudan idh tufiduna fihi**: Witnessing Happens at the Moment of Immersion

Then comes the heart of the ayah:

**illa kunna 'alaykum shuhudan idh tufiduna fihi**.

Except that We are witnesses over you when you are immersed in it.

[PAUSE]

The phrase **tufiduna fihi** is especially powerful. It is not merely when the matter brushes past you. It is when you are pouring into it, engaging it, spreading into it, moving inside it.

This means Allah's witnessing is not delayed until after the deed is complete. It accompanies the very immersion.

### 5. **Ma ya'zubu... min mithqali dharratin**: Not Even a Particle's Weight Goes Missing

Then the scale tightens:

**wa ma ya'zubu 'an rabbika min mithqali dharratin**.

Not even the weight of a tiny particle escapes your Lord.

[PAUSE]

The verb **ya'zubu** means to be absent, to slip away, to vanish from view. The ayah negates that completely. Nothing at particle scale leaves His field of knowledge.

And not just the particle itself, but **mithqal** - its weight. Its measure. Its tiniest real significance.

### 6. **Wa la asghara min dhalika wa la akbara**: The Ayah Breaks the Human Habit of Hiding in Scale

Then:

**wa la asghara min dhalika wa la akbara**.

Nor anything smaller than that nor greater.

[PAUSE]

This is one of the most comprehensive phrases in the surah. Human beings try to hide in scale both ways. We excuse what feels too small to matter, and we compartmentalize what feels too large to face. The ayah closes both doors.

Smaller. Greater. All within the same witnessed field.

### 7. **Illa fi kitabin mubin**: What Is Witnessed Is Also Written

Finally:

**illa fi kitabin mubin**.

Except that it is in a clear Book.

[PAUSE]

The passage ends not only with knowledge but with inscription. What is witnessed is not drifting in unstructured awareness. It is placed in clear record.

This should produce both awe and relief. Awe, because nothing is lost. Relief, because nothing righteous is lost either.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The Soul Often Hides in "Small Enough Not to Matter"

Many of the compromises that reshape a life do not arrive announcing themselves as major. They come small. A little dishonesty. A little vanity. A little looking. A little hardness. A little negligence in intention. A little tolerated hypocrisy in speech. The ego loves the phrase: it's not that serious.

[PAUSE]

This ayah dismantles that refuge. If even a particle's weight, smaller than that or greater, does not escape Allah, then scale cannot be our excuse for moral numbness. The little things are not little because Allah missed them. They are little only in our distorted self-description.

### Theme 2: Total Witnessing Is Also a Mercy for the Believer

It is easy to hear this ayah only as threat. But it is also immense mercy.

[PAUSE]

It means your hidden patience is seen. Your uncelebrated restraint is seen. Your quiet tawbah is seen. Your effort to leave a sin nobody else knows about is seen. Your recitation when nobody praises it is seen. Your moments of sincerity in the middle of messy affairs are seen.

The same total witness that terrifies the heedless protects the believer from despair over being unseen.

## Closing Synthesis

This ayah moves from any matter the servant is in, to Qur'an recitation, to every deed, and declares that Allah is witness over His servants precisely when they are immersed in those affairs. It then tightens the scale until not even the weight of a tiny particle - smaller or greater - can escape the Lord's knowledge, and closes by placing all of it in a clear Book.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: what parts of my life do I still treat as if they are too small, too private, or too interior to matter before Allah? What righteous things have I been tempted to think were unseen and therefore maybe wasted? And if Allah is witnessing me in the very moment I am immersed in my affairs, how would that change the way I enter them?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **Allah's witnessing is total not only in range but in timing and scale, so the servant lives inside a world where nothing hidden is ever truly unheld, and nothing sincere is ever unrecorded.**

# After the Truth, What Is Left Except Misguidance?

## Surah Yunus (10:31-32)

[ayah:10:31] قُلْ مَن يَرْزُقُكُم مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ أَمَّن يَمْلِكُ ٱلسَّمْعَ وَٱلْأَبْصَـٰرَ وَمَن يُخْرِجُ ٱلْحَىَّ مِنَ ٱلْمَيِّتِ وَيُخْرِجُ ٱلْمَيِّتَ مِنَ ٱلْحَىِّ وَمَن يُدَبِّرُ ٱلْأَمْرَ ۚ فَسَيَقُولُونَ ٱللَّهُ ۚ فَقُلْ أَفَلَا تَتَّقُونَ

[ayah:10:32] فَذَٰلِكُمُ ٱللَّهُ رَبُّكُمُ ٱلْحَقُّ ۖ فَمَاذَا بَعْدَ ٱلْحَقِّ إِلَّا ٱلضَّلَـٰلُ ۖ فَأَنَّىٰ تُصْرَفُونَ

*Qul man yarzuqukum mina al-sama'i wa al-ard, amman yamliku al-sam'a wa al-absar, wa man yukhriju al-hayya mina al-mayyiti wa yukhriju al-mayyita mina al-hayy, wa man yudabbiru al-amr. Fa-sayaquluna Allah. Fa-qul afala tattaqun.*

*Fa-dhalikumu Allah rabbukumu al-haqq. Fa-madha ba'da al-haqq illa al-dalal. Fa-anna tusrafun.*

**Say: who provides for you from the heaven and the earth? Or who owns hearing and sight? And who brings the living out of the dead and brings the dead out of the living? And who governs the affair? They will say: Allah. Then say: will you not then have taqwa? That is Allah, your true Lord. So what is there after the truth except misguidance? Then how are you turned away?**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

Some arguments in the Qur'an do not begin by proving what the listener has never heard. They begin by cornering the listener with what he already knows.

That is what these ayahs do. They do not first ask the mushrik to admit resurrection, revelation, or the finer points of creed. They ask about what he already lives inside every day: provision, senses, life, death, and the governing of affairs. Then the Qur'an waits for the answer it knows will come.

[PAUSE]

Allah.

Once that answer is spoken, the real pressure of the passage begins. Because now the issue is no longer lack of information. It is moral inconsistency. If Allah is the One providing, owning, bringing forth, and governing, then what exactly is left for shirk, hesitation, or divided loyalty to stand on?

The hook of the passage is this: **Yunus teaches that many people already know enough about Allah's lordship to surrender, and that once the truth is admitted, turning away from its claim is not intellectual neutrality but misguidance.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Man yarzuqukum mina al-sama'i wa al-ard**: The Argument Begins With Dependency, Not Abstraction

The first question is:

**man yarzuqukum mina al-sama'i wa al-ard**.

Who provides for you from the heaven and the earth?

[PAUSE]

This is how the Qur'an pulls theology out of debate-club distance and back into lived dependence. **Rizq** is not a speculative concept. It is what keeps you alive. Rain, crops, openings, breath, capacity, means, timing, possibility. The question does not ask, "Who is theoretically supreme?" It asks: who is feeding your existence right now?

That matters because many spiritual disorders survive only when dependency is forgotten. The human being can talk like a servant and live like a functional atheist or a practical mushrik. The ayah starts where such illusions are weakest: provision.

### 2. **Amman yamliku al-sam'a wa al-absar**: Even the Instruments of Perception Are Owned

Then:

**amman yamliku al-sam'a wa al-absar**.

Who owns hearing and sight?

[PAUSE]

Not who created them once and then stepped away. Who **owns** them. **Yamliku**.

This is subtle and devastating. The ayah is not only reminding us that the senses are gifts. It is reminding us that they remain under possession. The faculties by which you navigate the world are not self-owned territory. They are entrusted capacities inside another's dominion.

So even the act of looking away from truth uses eyes you do not own. Even the act of refusing to hear uses hearing you were lent. The rebellion is already happening inside borrowed property.

### 3. **Yukhriju al-hayya mina al-mayyit...**: Life and Death Are Not Closed Systems

Then the ayah asks:

**wa man yukhriju al-hayya mina al-mayyit wa yukhriju al-mayyita mina al-hayy**.

Who brings the living out of the dead and the dead out of the living?

[PAUSE]

The question widens the field from provision and perception to transformation itself. Allah is naming realities human beings witness without controlling: emergence, reversal, generation, ending, transition, unexpected life from what seemed inert, and deadness from what once pulsed with vitality.

The point is not to reduce this to one narrow example. The point is that the boundaries we experience as final are not final before Allah. He crosses them constantly.

That makes shirk look even more irrational. Why attach ultimate dependence to what cannot even move between life and death, while ignoring the One who continuously does?

### 4. **Wa man yudabbiru al-amr**: Behind Events Is Ongoing Governance

Then comes the question that gathers the rest:

**wa man yudabbiru al-amr**.

Who governs the affair?

[PAUSE]

This is not only creation. It is administration. Not only origin. Ongoing management. The unfolding of things, the measuring, directing, sequencing, and turning of events.

That verb matters because many people are willing to give Allah the beginning of things while quietly refusing Him the living present. Yes, He created. But who is handling my actual outcomes now? Who really determines where things go? The ayah will not let the heart split those apart.

### 5. **Fa-sayaquluna Allah**: The Answer Is Known Before It Is Spoken

Then the Qur'an says:

**fa-sayaquluna Allah**.

They will say: Allah.

[PAUSE]

That future verb is piercing because it shows that the argument is not uncertain. The Qur'an knows the confession is available to them. The truth is not absent from their minds. It is already close enough to predict.

This is one of the most frightening states a human being can live in: not ignorance of truth, but possession of enough truth to be accountable without the willingness to submit to it.

### 6. **Afala tattaqun** and **fa-madha ba'da al-haqq illa al-dalal**: Confession Must Become Taqwa

Then the Messenger is told to say:

**afala tattaqun**.

Will you not then have taqwa?

And then:

**fa-madha ba'da al-haqq illa al-dalal**.

What is there after the truth except misguidance?

[PAUSE]

This is the passage's turning point. The Qur'an refuses to let admitted truth remain a decorative statement. If Allah is confessed as Provider, Owner, Life-Giver, and Governor, then the required next step is taqwa, lived guarding of oneself before Him.

And once that is laid down, the options are reduced to two. Truth, or misguidance. Not truth, or one more respectable alternative. Not truth, or a spiritually mixed arrangement. The ayah is clearing the field.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Many Hearts Are Not Starved of Evidence but Unwilling to Accept Its Consequences

We often imagine the central religious problem as lack of proof. If only more evidence arrived, more clarity, more demonstration, then surely surrender would follow. But the Qur'an repeatedly shows that the human problem often lies somewhere else.

[PAUSE]

People already know who feeds them. They already know they did not author their senses. They already know life and death exceed them. They already know that affairs do not finally obey their plans. They are not blank slates staring at an empty sky. They are recipients of relentless signs.

What the ego resists is not always information. It is implication. Because once the answer "Allah" is spoken honestly, a whole architecture of self-rule begins to crack. Preferences must be judged. Loyalties must be examined. Fear must be reordered. Taqwa is no longer optional decoration. It becomes the honest consequence of what one already admitted.

### Theme 2: After Truth, the Soul Is Usually Turned Away Rather Than Reasoned Away

The last line says:

**fa-anna tusrafun**.

How then are you turned away?

[PAUSE]

That wording is tender and severe together. It suggests deviation is not always the result of superior reasoning. Often the soul is turned. Redirected. Pulled away. Distracted from the straight implication of what it already knows.

That is spiritually illuminating. Many of us do not lose clarity because we found a stronger truth. We lose it because appetite, fear, social pressure, vanity, habit, or love of control slowly rotated us away from the claim of the truth we already recognized.

So the battle is not merely intellectual. It is directional. What is turning me? What bends me away from the obedience my own tongue already knows is owed to Allah?

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs ask who provides from heaven and earth, who owns hearing and sight, who brings living from dead and dead from living, and who governs the affair. The expected answer is spoken: Allah. Then the passage presses that confession into its consequence: will you not have taqwa? That is Allah, your true Lord, and after the truth there remains nothing but misguidance. So how are you being turned away?

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: what truths about Allah have I already admitted without letting them fully govern my life? Where have I treated confession as enough while withholding taqwa? And what is presently turning me away from the obedience that should naturally follow from what I already know about my Lord?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **once Allah's lordship over provision, perception, life, death, and governance is admitted, the path of truth has already been identified, and everything that pulls the soul elsewhere is not a second truth but a turning into misguidance.**

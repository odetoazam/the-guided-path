# There Comes a Point When a Tyrant's Adornments Are Seen Only as Fuel for Misguidance

## Surah Yunus (10:88-89)

[ayah:10:88] وَقَالَ مُوسَىٰ رَبَّنَآ إِنَّكَ ءَاتَيْتَ فِرْعَوْنَ وَمَلَأَهُۥ زِينَةً وَأَمْوَٰلًا فِى ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا رَبَّنَا لِيُضِلُّوا۟ عَن سَبِيلِكَ ۖ رَبَّنَا ٱطْمِسْ عَلَىٰٓ أَمْوَٰلِهِمْ وَٱشْدُدْ عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِهِمْ فَلَا يُؤْمِنُوا۟ حَتَّىٰ يَرَوُا۟ ٱلْعَذَابَ ٱلْأَلِيمَ

[ayah:10:89] قَالَ قَدْ أُجِيبَت دَّعْوَتُكُمَا فَٱسْتَقِيمَا وَلَا تَتَّبِعَآنِّ سَبِيلَ ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ

*Wa qala Musa rabbana innaka atayta Fir'awna wa mala'ahu zinatan wa amwalan fi al-hayati al-dunya rabbana li-yudillu 'an sabilik. Rabbana itmis 'ala amwalihim wa-shdud 'ala qulubihim fa-la yu'minu hatta yarawu al-'adhaba al-alim.*

*Qala qad ujibat da'watukuma fa-staqima wa la tattabi'anni sabila alladhina la ya'lamun.*

**And Musa said: Our Lord, You have given Fir'awn and his elite adornment and wealth in the worldly life, our Lord, so that they may mislead from Your path. Our Lord, obliterate their wealth and harden upon their hearts so that they do not believe until they see the painful punishment. He said: your prayer has been answered, so remain upright, and do not follow the path of those who do not know.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

There are moments when mercy toward an oppressor no longer means asking that his worldly power be preserved.

The Qur'an teaches that wealth and adornment are not always signs of health. Sometimes they become instruments through which others are misled, dazzled, bought off, or spiritually broken. At that point, a prophet may begin to see the tyrant's visible blessings not as neutral gifts, but as extensions of corruption.

[PAUSE]

That is the gravity of this passage. Musa reads Fir'awn's adornment and wealth through the harm they are doing. His du'a is severe, but it is not petty. Then Allah answers with something just as weighty: your prayer has been answered, so remain upright and do not drift into the path of the ignorant.

The hook of the passage is this: **Yunus teaches that when worldly splendor becomes a vehicle of mass misguidance, prophetic vision stops admiring it and instead asks Allah to break its power - while remembering that answered du'a still requires patient istiqamah.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Atayta Fir'awna wa mala'ahu zinatan wa amwalan**: Wealth Is Named as a Given, Not Self-made Glory

Musa begins:

**innaka atayta Fir'awna wa mala'ahu zinatan wa amwalan**.

You gave Fir'awn and his elite adornment and wealth.

[PAUSE]

This is important. Even the tyrant's possessions are still traced back to Allah as ultimately given. The believer never adopts a theology in which the oppressor escapes Allah's dominion. But tracing them back to Allah does not make them signs of approval.

### 2. **Li-yudillu 'an sabilik**: Musa Reads the Social Consequence of Their Splendor

Then:

**rabbana li-yudillu 'an sabilik**.

Our Lord, so that they may mislead from Your path.

[PAUSE]

Musa is not only reacting to private discomfort. He is reading consequence. Their adornment and wealth are functioning in history. They are helping produce misguidance. Splendor is becoming da'wah for falsehood.

### 3. **Itmis 'ala amwalihim**: Break the Outward Machinery of Corruption

Then the du'a sharpens:

**rabbana itmis 'ala amwalihim**.

Our Lord, obliterate their wealth.

[PAUSE]

This is strong language. The prayer is asking that the tyrant's resources be wiped of their operative beauty and usefulness. When wealth becomes a weapon of misguidance, part of justice is that its glamour be broken.

### 4. **Wa-shdud 'ala qulubihim**: A Hardened End for Hardened Hearts

Then:

**wa-shdud 'ala qulubihim**.

And harden upon their hearts.

[PAUSE]

This is not an early-stage prayer. This comes after prolonged proof, rejection, tyranny, and continued corruption. The hardness asked for here corresponds to a history of self-chosen hardening. Musa is not asking for innocent hearts to be blocked. He is asking that a criminal trajectory be carried through to its just end.

### 5. **Qad ujibat da'watukuma**: The Answer Arrives Before the Outcome Is Seen

Then Allah says:

**qad ujibat da'watukuma**.

Your prayer has been answered.

[PAUSE]

This is spiritually important. The answer is declared before all consequences are visibly unfolded. A believer may receive divine answer at the level of unseen decree before the eye sees historical completion.

### 6. **Fa-staqima**: Answered Du'a Does Not Cancel the Need for Steadiness

Then:

**fa-staqima**.

So remain upright.

[PAUSE]

What a beautiful correction to impatience. Even after answered prayer, the servant is told to keep steady. Acceptance in Allah's court does not mean immediate closure in worldly time.

### 7. **Wa la tattabi'anni sabila alladhina la ya'lamun**: Ignorance Has a Path, Not Only Isolated Mistakes

Finally:

**wa la tattabi'anni sabila alladhina la ya'lamun**.

Do not follow the path of those who do not know.

[PAUSE]

The word **sabil** matters. Ignorance is not merely a momentary slip. It can become a road, a way of reacting, a habitual pattern. The believers are warned not to drift into that road through impatience, shallowness, or misreading delay.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Not Every Beautiful Thing Is Spiritually Beautiful

One of the most important lessons here is that adornment and wealth must be read morally, not merely aesthetically. Fir'awn's world can look impressive and still be deeply diseased.

[PAUSE]

This matters for us because we are easily catechized by glamour. We assume that what shines must be blessed, what scales must be healthy, and what attracts must be sound. Musa teaches a harsher reading: if it is serving misguidance, its beauty is already corrupted.

### Theme 2: Answered Prayer Still Leaves a Season for Istiqamah

Many people imagine that once du'a is accepted, all waiting should disappear. But this ayah teaches otherwise. Allah can affirm the answer and still command continued steadiness.

[PAUSE]

This is a mercy, because it teaches the servant how to live between divine decree and visible unfolding. The answer may be settled in heaven while the road on earth still requires patience.

## Closing Synthesis

Musa names Fir'awn's adornment and wealth as divine givens that have become instruments of misguidance, then asks Allah to wipe out that wealth and carry hardened hearts to their just end. Allah answers that the prayer has indeed been accepted, but immediately pairs that answer with istiqamah and a warning not to slip into the path of the ignorant.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: what kinds of worldly beauty still tempt me into moral confusion? Do I know how to read wealth not only by how it looks, but by what it is doing to souls? And when I ask Allah intensely for change, am I ready for the season of istiqamah that may still be required before I see the answer unfold?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **when worldly splendor is clearly serving misguidance, prophetic du'a may ask Allah to break it, yet even an answered prayer does not remove the servant's obligation to remain upright and refuse the road of ignorant impatience.**

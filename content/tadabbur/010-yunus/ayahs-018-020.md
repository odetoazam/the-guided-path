# False Religion Tries to Inform Allah

## Surah Yunus (10:18-20)

[ayah:10:18] وَيَعْبُدُونَ مِن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ مَا لَا يَضُرُّهُمْ وَلَا يَنفَعُهُمْ وَيَقُولُونَ هَـٰٓؤُلَآءِ شُفَعَـٰٓؤُنَا عِندَ ٱللَّهِ ۚ قُلْ أَتُنَبِّـُٔونَ ٱللَّهَ بِمَا لَا يَعْلَمُ فِى ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَلَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۚ سُبْحَـٰنَهُۥ وَتَعَـٰلَىٰ عَمَّا يُشْرِكُونَ

[ayah:10:19] وَمَا كَانَ ٱلنَّاسُ إِلَّآ أُمَّةً وَٰحِدَةً فَٱخْتَلَفُوا۟ ۚ وَلَوْلَا كَلِمَةٌ سَبَقَتْ مِن رَّبِّكَ لَقُضِىَ بَيْنَهُمْ فِيمَا فِيهِ يَخْتَلِفُونَ

[ayah:10:20] وَيَقُولُونَ لَوْلَآ أُنزِلَ عَلَيْهِ ءَايَةٌ مِّن رَّبِّهِۦ ۖ فَقُلْ إِنَّمَا ٱلْغَيْبُ لِلَّهِ فَٱنتَظِرُوٓا۟ إِنِّى مَعَكُم مِّنَ ٱلْمُنتَظِرِينَ

*Wa ya'buduna min duni Allahi ma la yadurruhum wa la yanfa'uhum wa yaquluna ha'ula'i shufa'a'una 'inda Allah. Qul a-tunabbi'una Allaha bima la ya'lamu fi al-samawati wa la fi al-ard. Subhanahu wa ta'ala 'amma yushrikun.*

*Wa ma kana al-nasu illa ummatan wahidatan fa-ikhtalafu. Wa lawla kalimatun sabaqat min rabbika la-qudiya baynahum fima fihi yakhtalifun.*

*Wa yaquluna lawla unzila 'alayhi ayatun min rabbihi. Fa-qul innama al-ghaybu li-Allah fa-intaziru inni ma'akum mina al-muntazirin.*

**They worship, besides Allah, what neither harms them nor benefits them, and they say: these are our intercessors with Allah. Say: are you informing Allah of something He does not know in the heavens or on the earth? Glorified is He and exalted above what they associate. People were not but one community, then they differed. Had it not been for a word that had already preceded from your Lord, judgment would have been made between them regarding that over which they differ. And they say: why has no sign been sent down upon him from his Lord? So say: the unseen belongs only to Allah. So wait; indeed I am with you among those who wait.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

There is a kind of religiosity that looks humble on the surface but is deeply arrogant underneath.

It speaks the language of intermediaries, sacred figures, spiritual access, special paths, hidden arrangements. It sounds like reverence, but the Qur'an uncovers something more dangerous: it is actually making claims about Allah that Allah Himself never authorized. It is building an imagined court around Him and then treating that fiction as devotion.

[PAUSE]

That is why this passage is so sharp. It does not merely say their worship is ineffective. It asks a humiliating question: are you informing Allah of something He does not know? In other words, your false theology is not just mistaken ritual. It is an act of presumptuous speech about the unseen.

The hook of the passage is this: **Yunus teaches that shirk is not only misdirected worship but unauthorized speech about Allah's court, and that impatient demands for signs ignore the fact that the unseen, the timing of judgment, and the final settling of difference all belong to Him alone.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Ma la yadurruhum wa la yanfa'uhum**: They Give Devotion to What Has No Real Power

The passage begins by naming the objects of worship as things that:

**la yadurruhum wa la yanfa'uhum**.

Neither harm them nor benefit them.

[PAUSE]

That is a devastating reduction. People may surround false objects with mythology, emotion, and inherited prestige, but the Qur'an strips them down to actual capacity. Can they harm? Can they benefit? If not, why are they receiving what belongs to Allah?

### 2. **Shufa'a'una 'inda Allah**: False Worship Often Hides Behind Intercession Language

Then they say:

**ha'ula'i shufa'a'una 'inda Allah**.

These are our intercessors with Allah.

[PAUSE]

This is important because their defense is not raw atheism. It is mediated piety. They are not saying these beings are independent creators. They are saying these beings will get us access. The Qur'an exposes how often shirk survives by dressing itself in the language of nearness.

### 3. **A-tunabbi'una Allaha**: The Question Is Meant to Shame the Pretension

Then comes the reply:

**a-tunabbi'una Allaha bima la ya'lamu**.

Are you informing Allah of something He does not know?

[PAUSE]

This question is thunderous. It reveals the hidden arrogance inside false theology. You are acting as though you know the structure of divine permission better than Allah has disclosed. You are supplying heaven with information. The ayah tears down that fantasy in a single sentence.

### 4. **Subhanahu wa ta'ala**: Tanzih Arrives After Corrupted Theology

Then:

**subhanahu wa ta'ala**.

Glorified is He and exalted is He.

[PAUSE]

This is not decorative praise inserted at random. It is a cleansing response. Once a false idea about Allah has been voiced, the Qur'an immediately re-sanctifies the field. Allah is above the structures people project onto Him. Above the imagined partners. Above the invented procedures of access.

### 5. **Ummatan wahidatan fa-ikhtalafu**: Human Difference Is Real, but Not Sovereign

Verse 19 widens the scope:

**wa ma kana al-nasu illa ummatan wahidatan fa-ikhtalafu**.

People were only one community, then they differed.

[PAUSE]

Difference is real. Human fracture is real. But it is not ultimate. It exists within Allah's knowing and decree. The ayah neither romanticizes primordial unity nor absolutizes current division. It places both under divine sovereignty.

### 6. **Al-ghaybu li-Allah**: The Unseen Is Not Yours to Control

Then the demand for another sign is answered with:

**innama al-ghaybu li-Allah**.

The unseen belongs only to Allah.

[PAUSE]

That is the final correction. The timing of signs, the exposure of truth, the settling of disputes, the arrival of judgment, all of it belongs to the One who owns the unseen. Human impatience does not move that timetable.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Religious Error Often Begins When We Become Too Comfortable Speaking About Allah

One of the most dangerous states of the heart is speaking about Allah casually, as if His court, His permission, His acceptance, and His unseen dealings are obvious to us. The Qur'an teaches the opposite posture: fear, humility, revelation-bound speech.

[PAUSE]

The people in this passage are not merely worshipping badly. They are narrating a spiritual arrangement and then expecting everyone else to accept it. That temptation survives in every age. Whenever we become bolder in our claims than revelation has allowed, we are walking toward the same cliff.

### Theme 2: Delay Does Not Mean Absence of Judgment

Verse 19 is a mercy and a warning together. Had it not been for a word already decreed, judgment would have been settled immediately. The fact that it is not yet visibly settled does not mean reality is unclear. It means Allah has given time according to a wisdom that belongs to Him.

[PAUSE]

That matters for us because we often interpret delay as ambiguity. The Qur'an interprets delay as decree.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs expose people who worship powerless things and defend that worship by calling them intercessors with Allah. The Qur'an answers by asking whether they are informing Allah of something He does not know, then declares Him exalted above what they associate. It then places human difference under a prior divine word and answers the demand for more signs by returning the unseen entirely to Allah.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: where have I become too confident in speaking about what Allah accepts, prefers, or permits without clear evidence from revelation? Where have I confused delay with divine indecision? And when I do not receive the kind of sign or closure I want, do I humble myself before the unseen, or do I start quietly resenting the terms on which Allah reveals?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **false religion becomes especially dangerous when it speaks presumptuously about Allah, while faith learns to stop where revelation stops and to surrender the hidden timetable of judgment and disclosure to the Lord of the unseen.**

# Even After Rescue, Provision, and a Good Settlement, Knowledge Can Become a New Site of Division

## Surah Yunus (10:93)

[ayah:10:93] وَلَقَدْ بَوَّأْنَا بَنِىٓ إِسْرَٰٓءِيلَ مُبَوَّأَ صِدْقٍ وَرَزَقْنَـٰهُم مِّنَ ٱلطَّيِّبَـٰتِ فَمَا ٱخْتَلَفُوا۟ حَتَّىٰ جَآءَهُمُ ٱلْعِلْمُ ۚ إِنَّ رَبَّكَ يَقْضِى بَيْنَهُمْ يَوْمَ ٱلْقِيَـٰمَةِ فِيمَا كَانُوا۟ فِيهِ يَخْتَلِفُونَ

*Wa la-qad bawwa'na Bani Isra'ila mubawwa'a sidqin wa razaqnahum mina al-tayyibat. Fa-ma ikhtalafu hatta ja'ahumu al-'ilm. Inna rabbaka yaqdi baynahum yawma al-qiyamati fima kanu fihi yakhtalifun.*

**And We certainly settled the Children of Isra'il in a good settlement and provided them from the good things. Then they did not differ until knowledge came to them. Indeed your Lord will judge between them on the Day of Resurrection concerning that over which they used to differ.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

We often imagine that once oppression ends and provision arrives, the hardest part is over.

But the Qur'an regularly shows that some of the deepest moral tests begin after rescue. A people can be delivered from tyrants, settled in goodness, fed from what is wholesome, and still discover that the next trial is not persecution from outside, but division from within.

[PAUSE]

This ayah is brief, but severe. Bani Isra'il are given a truthful settlement and good provision. Yet their اختلاف emerges not in the absence of knowledge, but after knowledge comes. The unresolved matter is then handed to Allah's final judgment.

The hook of the passage is this: **Yunus teaches that external rescue and generous provision do not automatically produce inward unity, and that knowledge itself can become the arena of division when hearts are not healed enough to receive it rightly.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Bawwa'na... mubawwa'a sidqin**: Settlement Is Described Through Truthfulness

The ayah says:

**bawwa'na Bani Isra'ila mubawwa'a sidqin**.

We settled the Children of Isra'il in a settlement of truth.

[PAUSE]

This is beautiful. The dwelling is not described only as safe or comfortable. It is marked by **sidq**. Truthfulness, soundness, uprightness. The place they are given is not morally ambiguous gift. It is a real, good, fitting settlement.

### 2. **Wa razaqnahum mina al-tayyibat**: Provision Comes in a Wholesome Register

Then:

**wa razaqnahum mina al-tayyibat**.

We provided them from the good things.

[PAUSE]

The word **tayyibat** is morally textured. Not just abundance, but wholesome goodness. The Qur'an is establishing that what they received from Allah carried both generosity and purity.

### 3. **Fa-ma ikhtalafu hatta ja'ahumu al-'ilm**: Division Erupts After Knowledge

Then the startling line:

**fa-ma ikhtalafu hatta ja'ahumu al-'ilm**.

They did not differ until knowledge came to them.

[PAUSE]

This overturns an easy excuse. We often imagine people divide only because they do not know enough. But here knowledge arrives, and difference follows. That means the issue cannot be reduced to mere informational deficiency. Hearts, desires, status, ego, and unwillingness to submit all have to be considered.

### 4. **Yaqdi baynahum**: Some Differences Are Deferred to the Final Court

Then:

**inna rabbaka yaqdi baynahum yawma al-qiyamati**.

Your Lord will judge between them on the Day of Resurrection.

[PAUSE]

This returns the matter to Allah. Not every historical dispute is resolved inside history. Some are carried all the way to the final court, where neither inherited claim nor communal self-justification will protect anyone.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Rescue From External Tyranny Does Not Cure Internal Disease

It is tempting to think that if only hostile circumstances were removed, a community would automatically become healthy. The Qur'an repeatedly refuses that simplification.

[PAUSE]

This ayah warns us that a people can inherit good space and wholesome provision while still carrying inner conditions that later rupture into division. Environment matters, but it is not the whole story.

### Theme 2: Knowledge Without Purified Hearts Can Multiply Dispute

The ayah's sequence is haunting precisely because it names knowledge before division. We should let that unsettle us. More access, more literacy, more discourse, more detail, more argument - none of these automatically produce unity if the soul is still dominated by rivalry, ego, or selective submission.

[PAUSE]

The danger is especially subtle because people can then use knowledge itself as the material of their conflict while imagining they are honoring it.

## Closing Synthesis

Bani Isra'il are given a settlement described through truthfulness and are provided from wholesome things. Yet their later اختلاف does not wait for ignorance; it follows the arrival of knowledge. The ayah then leaves the unresolved matter with Allah, who will judge between them on the Day of Resurrection concerning what they kept disputing.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: do I still imagine that more knowledge alone will heal every division? What inner diseases in me could turn even good gifts and true knowledge into new occasions of conflict? And do I remember that some unresolved disputes are not ultimately settled by louder argument, but by Allah's final judgment?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **Allah may give a community truthful settlement, wholesome provision, and knowledge, yet if the heart is not disciplined those very gifts can become the setting of division whose final resolution belongs only to Allah.**

# What Cannot Guide You Does Not Deserve to Be Followed

## Surah Yunus (10:33-36)

[ayah:10:33] كَذَٰلِكَ حَقَّتْ كَلِمَتُ رَبِّكَ عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ فَسَقُوٓا۟ أَنَّهُمْ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ

[ayah:10:34] قُلْ هَلْ مِن شُرَكَآئِكُم مَّن يَبْدَؤُا۟ ٱلْخَلْقَ ثُمَّ يُعِيدُهُۥ ۚ قُلِ ٱللَّهُ يَبْدَؤُا۟ ٱلْخَلْقَ ثُمَّ يُعِيدُهُۥ ۖ فَأَنَّىٰ تُؤْفَكُونَ

[ayah:10:35] قُلْ هَلْ مِن شُرَكَآئِكُم مَّن يَهْدِىٓ إِلَى ٱلْحَقِّ ۚ قُلِ ٱللَّهُ يَهْدِى لِلْحَقِّ ۗ أَفَمَن يَهْدِىٓ إِلَى ٱلْحَقِّ أَحَقُّ أَن يُتَّبَعَ أَمَّن لَّا يَهِدِّىٓ إِلَّآ أَن يُهْدَىٰ ۖ فَمَا لَكُمْ كَيْفَ تَحْكُمُونَ

[ayah:10:36] وَمَا يَتَّبِعُ أَكْثَرُهُمْ إِلَّا ظَنًّا ۚ إِنَّ ٱلظَّنَّ لَا يُغْنِى مِنَ ٱلْحَقِّ شَيْـًٔا ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ عَلِيمٌۢ بِمَا يَفْعَلُونَ

## Introduction

There is a stage of error where the problem is no longer lack of evidence. The problem is that the soul has become willing to live by crooked judgment.

These ayahs come after the mushrik has already been made to admit that Allah provides, owns, gives life, takes life, and governs the affair. So the surah now asks the obvious next question: if that is already known, then why are you still following what cannot create, restore, or guide?

[PAUSE]

This is one of the Qur'an's most unsettling moves. It does not let false religion hide behind inherited language or emotional momentum. It tests the alleged objects of following by basic capacities. Can they begin creation? Can they repeat it? Can they guide to truth? If not, what exactly are you doing giving them the station of leadership over your heart?

Then the passage lands its diagnosis. Most of them are not following knowledge. They are following **zann**. Conjecture. Religious life without certainty, loyalty without proof, imitation without light.

The hook of the passage is this: **Yunus teaches that what cannot originate, restore, or guide has no right to be followed, and that much of spiritual deviation survives not on knowledge but on the flimsy authority of conjecture.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Haqqat kalimatu rabbika**: Some Refusals Ripen Into Confirmed Exposure

The passage opens:

**ka-dhalika haqqat kalimatu rabbika**.

Thus has your Lord's word come due.

[PAUSE]

This is a sobering opening because it describes a matter settling into inevitability. The divine word is no longer merely warned about. It has become due against a people whose inner direction has hardened.

Then they are named:

**alladhina fasaqu**.

Those who broke out defiantly.

The root of **fisq** carries the sense of exiting, bursting past a boundary. This matters because disbelief here is not pictured as passive confusion. It is connected to moral breach. They stepped out of the limits truth required.

### 2. **Man yabda'u al-khalqa thumma yu'iduhu**: Leadership Belongs to the One Who Handles Beginning and Return

Then the first challenge:

**hal min shuraka'ikum man yabda'u al-khalqa thumma yu'iduhu**.

Is there among your partners anyone who originates creation then repeats it?

[PAUSE]

This is devastating because the Qur'an tests their supposed partners at the level of ultimate capacity. Not decoration. Not sentiment. Not tribal prestige. Can they begin existence? Can they restore it after its first unfolding?

Then the Prophet is told not to wait for them to improvise:

**quli Allahu yabda'u al-khalqa thumma yu'iduhu**.

Allah originates creation then repeats it.

The sentence is short because the truth is not complicated. It is human crookedness that makes it feel complicated.

### 3. **Fa-anna tu'fakun**: The Problem Is Not Subtlety but Being Turned From the Obvious

Then:

**fa-anna tu'fakun**.

How are you being turned away?

[PAUSE]

That question carries both astonishment and exposure. The truth is near, plain, already admitted in its foundations, and yet the heart is still being diverted. The passage is teaching us that misguidance often looks less like deep inquiry and more like successful redirection.

We should hear ourselves in that. How often do we already know what belongs to Allah, yet still let our loyalties, fears, or practical dependencies drift toward created things?

### 4. **Man yahdi ila al-haqq**: The Real Test of Following Is Guidance

Then the argument deepens:

**hal min shuraka'ikum man yahdi ila al-haqq**.

Is there among your partners anyone who guides to the truth?

[PAUSE]

Here the passage moves from cosmic capacity to spiritual authority. Even if someone claims reverence for a thing that cannot create, perhaps he still imagines it can lead him. The Qur'an cuts that off too. Can it guide to the truth?

Then comes the answer:

**quli Allahu yahdi lil-haqq**.

Allah guides to the truth.

Not merely Allah knows the truth. Allah guides toward it. Reaching truth is not only an issue of data. It is an issue of being led.

### 5. **Afaman yahdi ila al-haqqi ahaqqu an yuttaba'...**: Following Is a Moral Verdict

Then the passage asks:

**afaman yahdi ila al-haqqi ahaqqu an yuttaba'a amman la yahiddi illa an yuhda**.

Is One who guides to the truth more worthy to be followed, or one who does not guide unless he himself is guided?

[PAUSE]

This is one of the clearest questions in the surah, and therefore one of the hardest to escape. **Ahaqqu an yuttaba'** means more deserving to be followed. Following is not neutral preference. It is a verdict about worthiness.

And the contrast is humiliating. One side guides to truth. The other cannot even guide itself unless guided. How then can the second be raised into a station of ultimate following?

The ayah is not only arguing against idols of stone. It is arguing against every false center of gravity the human heart is tempted to follow absolutely.

### 6. **Fa-ma lakum kayfa tahkumun** and **zann**: Wrong Religion Is Also Wrong Judgment

Then:

**fa-ma lakum kayfa tahkumun**.

What is wrong with you? How do you judge?

And after that:

**wa ma yattabi'u aktharuhum illa zannan**.

Most of them follow nothing but conjecture.

[PAUSE]

This is crucial. The Qur'an is not saying their problem is only bad information. It is saying their judgment process itself is broken. Their following rests on **zann**, not on **haqq**.

Then the verdict:

**inna al-zanna la yughni mina al-haqqi shay'a**.

Conjecture avails nothing against the truth at all.

False certainty can feel socially reinforced, emotionally inherited, and culturally sacred. But if it is only conjecture, it cannot stand in for truth before Allah.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: We Follow More Than We Admit

One of the subtle mercies of the Qur'an is that it keeps asking not only what we say we worship, but what we actually follow. Because following reveals trust, dependency, valuation, and surrendered authority in a way slogans often do not.

[PAUSE]

A person can insist that Allah alone is Lord, yet practically follow appetite, status, inherited custom, admired personalities, ideology, or collective mood whenever those things conflict with revealed truth. The heart hands them a role they did not earn.

That is why this passage feels so alive. It asks: can the thing you are following create? restore? guide? If it cannot even direct itself without being directed, why are you leaning your soul onto it? The Qur'an is not merely smashing ancient idols. It is teaching us how to audit every rival authority inside the self.

### Theme 2: Conjecture Often Wears the Clothes of Confidence

**Zann** is dangerous precisely because it often does not feel weak from the inside. It can feel inherited, common, respectable, intuitive, even spiritually warm. Entire worlds of practice can be built on it while people speak with complete confidence.

[PAUSE]

But these ayahs teach that confidence is not the same as knowledge. Majorities are not the same as truth. Familiarity is not the same as proof. A repeated story is not the same as guidance.

This matters in religious life because the soul often prefers suggestive comfort over demanding truth. Conjecture lets us keep the shape of devotion without the surrender of certainty. It lets us borrow the language of religion while staying protected from the harder implications of following Allah as the sole Guide to truth.

The ayah tears that protection away. **Al-zanna la yughni mina al-haqqi shay'a.** Conjecture does not cover the gap. It does not fill in for truth. It does not rescue a false path by sounding plausible.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs begin with the word of Allah becoming due against those who broke past moral limits until they would not believe. They then challenge the false partners at the most basic levels: can they originate creation and restore it, can they guide to the truth, and do they deserve to be followed if they cannot even guide themselves without being guided? The passage ends by diagnosing the root of the problem: most of them are following conjecture, and conjecture cannot stand in for truth.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: what do I follow with the practical obedience of my heart, not just the speech of my tongue? Where have I granted decisive authority to something that cannot truly guide? And in what areas of my life am I still living on inherited assumption, emotional comfort, or social conjecture instead of the haqq Allah revealed?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **only the One who creates, restores, and guides to truth is worthy of being followed without reserve, while everything built on conjecture eventually exposes not depth of knowledge but the poverty of judgment.**

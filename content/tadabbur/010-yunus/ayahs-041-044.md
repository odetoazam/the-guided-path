# Listening Is Not the Same as Receiving

## Surah Yunus (10:41-44)

[ayah:10:41] وَإِن كَذَّبُوكَ فَقُل لِّى عَمَلِى وَلَكُمْ عَمَلُكُمْ ۖ أَنتُم بَرِيٓـُٔونَ مِمَّآ أَعْمَلُ وَأَنَا۠ بَرِىٓءٌ مِّمَّا تَعْمَلُونَ

[ayah:10:42] وَمِنْهُم مَّن يَسْتَمِعُونَ إِلَيْكَ ۚ أَفَأَنتَ تُسْمِعُ ٱلصُّمَّ وَلَوْ كَانُوا۟ لَا يَعْقِلُونَ

[ayah:10:43] وَمِنْهُم مَّن يَنظُرُ إِلَيْكَ ۚ أَفَأَنتَ تَهْدِى ٱلْعُمْىَ وَلَوْ كَانُوا۟ لَا يُبْصِرُونَ

[ayah:10:44] إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَظْلِمُ ٱلنَّاسَ شَيْـًٔا وَلَـٰكِنَّ ٱلنَّاسَ أَنفُسَهُمْ يَظْلِمُونَ

But can you make the deaf hear, even if they do not reason? And among them are those who look at you. But can you guide the blind, even if they do not see? Indeed Allah does not wrong people in the least, but people wrong themselves.**

## Introduction

One of the hardest experiences in da'wah, in parenting, in friendship, and even in self-reform is discovering that physical nearness to truth is not the same as inward reception.

Someone can be right there. Listening. Looking. Watching your sincerity, hearing your words, observing the signs. And still something in the interior remains shut. From the outside it appears as contact. From the inside it is still refusal.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs speak into exactly that pain. The Prophet is told what to say when denial persists. Then the surah names a devastating reality: some of them listen to you, and some of them look at you. Yet the Qur'an still describes them through deafness and blindness. The problem is not bare sensory exposure. It is the collapse of inner receiving.

Then the passage closes the moral field with immense clarity. Allah is not wronging people at all. People are wronging themselves.

The hook of the passage is this: **Yunus teaches that truth can be heard and seen outwardly without being received inwardly, and that when hearts remain closed under such exposure, the injustice is not from Allah but from what people do to themselves.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Li 'amali wa lakum 'amalukum**: The Prophet Is Taught Moral Separation Without Panic

The opening response to denial is:

**li 'amali wa lakum 'amalukum**.

For me is my deed, and for you are your deeds.

[PAUSE]

This is a dignified sentence. It does not plead for validation. It does not collapse into bitterness. It draws a line of accountability. My work is mine before Allah. Yours is yours before Allah.

Then it deepens:

**antum bari'una mimma a'malu wa ana bari'un mimma ta'malun**.

You are free of what I do, and I am free of what you do.

The root of **bara'ah** carries dissociation, clearing, separation. The Prophet is being taught that persistent denial does not require him to carry their chosen burden.

### 2. **Yastami'una ilayk**: Listening Can Happen Without Arrival

Then the Qur'an says:

**wa minhum man yastami'una ilayk**.

Among them are those who listen to you.

[PAUSE]

This makes the next line hit harder, because the verb suggests actual listening. There is audible contact. Words are reaching the ear.

Yet immediately:

**afa-anta tusmi'u al-summ**.

Can you make the deaf hear?

The paradox is intentional. They listen, yet they are deaf. Meaning: the hearing being denied is not raw sound reception. It is receptive hearing, hearing that becomes inward opening and response.

### 3. **Wa law kanu la ya'qilun**: The Ear Is Blocked by More Than Sound

Then:

**wa law kanu la ya'qilun**.

Even if they do not reason.

[PAUSE]

This matters because the blockage is not portrayed as purely emotional. The inward faculty of binding understanding is absent too. They hear, but the hearing does not settle into thoughtful apprehension.

The Qur'an often links hearing, reason, and response. Here their severance is exposed. The sound entered. The inner tying-together did not happen.

### 4. **Wa minhum man yanzuru ilayk**: Looking Can Happen Without Vision

Then the visual side:

**wa minhum man yanzuru ilayk**.

Among them are those who look at you.

[PAUSE]

Again the same pain. They are not absent. They are watching. The Prophet is in front of them. His character, speech, patience, and signs are visible.

Yet:

**afa-anta tahdi al-'umya**.

Can you guide the blind?

The Qur'an is teaching that seeing and insight are not the same event. A person can look directly at truth and still remain blind in the decisive sense.

### 5. **Wa law kanu la yubsirun**: The Real Sight Missing Is Inner Perception

Then:

**wa law kanu la yubsirun**.

Even if they do not see.

[PAUSE]

This is not mere repetition. It completes the distinction. There is outer looking, but no inner seeing. There is outer listening, but no inner hearing. The sensory surface remains intact while the spiritual faculty is incapacitated.

That is one of the Qur'an's sharpest anthropologies: the crisis of the human being is not always absence of exposure. It is failure of reception.

### 6. **La yazlimu al-nasa shay'an**: Allah Is Cleared Completely of Injustice

Then the passage resolves the tension:

**inna Allaha la yazlimu al-nasa shay'an**.

Allah does not wrong people in the least.

[PAUSE]

Not even **shay'an**. A thing. The smallest amount. The passage closes every door to blaming divine injustice for human refusal.

And then:

**wa lakinna al-nasa anfusahum yazlimun**.

But people wrong themselves.

The self is named as the victim of its own misdirection. When truth is heard outwardly and refused inwardly, the deepest wound circles back upon the one refusing.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Nearness to Truth Does Not Replace Surrender to Truth

There is a religious illusion that proximity itself will save us. We imagine that because we sit near good people, hear good words, attend good spaces, or remain around revelation, something decisive must be happening inside us automatically.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs refuse that comfort. Some listened to the Prophet himself and still the Qur'an called them deaf. Some looked directly at him and still the Qur'an called them blind. If nearness to the Messenger did not force inward reception, then our own proximity to religious language, community, and symbols certainly cannot guarantee it either.

This should humble us. It is possible to be culturally close to Islam and spiritually distant. Possible to hear khutbah after khutbah, ayah after ayah, reminder after reminder, and yet protect the ego from the inward implications of all of it. The soul learns to coexist with truth without surrendering to it.

### Theme 2: Self-Wrong Is Often the Refusal to Let Truth Enter Fully

When we hear "people wrong themselves," we often think immediately of spectacular sins. But these ayahs suggest a quieter and perhaps more frightening self-wronging: refusing inward reception when the truth is already reaching you.

[PAUSE]

That refusal injures the self on multiple levels. It keeps the person from the life that guidance brings. It trains the interior to resist what would heal it. It creates a habit of outward contact without inward change. And over time, that habit can become a spiritual numbness that feels normal.

This is why the Qur'an does not sentimentalize passive exposure to reminders. The issue is not whether truth passed by your ears and eyes. The issue is whether you let it pass into the places from which judgment, surrender, and transformation actually rise.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs teach the Prophet how to answer ongoing denial with moral clarity and separation of responsibility: my deed is mine, and yours is yours. They then unveil a painful reality: some people truly listen to him and look at him, yet remain deaf and blind because the inward faculties of receiving, reasoning, and seeing are absent. The sequence closes by clearing Allah completely of injustice and locating the wrong inside the human being's treatment of the self.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: where in my life have I been near truth without truly surrendering to it? What reminders have I heard repeatedly without letting them become inward hearing? And when I remain unchanged under clear exposure to Allah's guidance, am I still tempted to look anywhere outside myself for the reason?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **physical contact with truth is not yet guidance, and when the heart refuses to receive what the ears and eyes were already given, the deepest injustice is not done to Allah or even first to others, but to the self.**

# When Love for Family and Comfort Competes with Allah and His Messenger

## Surah At-Tawbah (9:23-24)

[ayah:9:23] يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ لَا تَتَّخِذُوٓا۟ ءَابَآءَكُمْ وَإِخْوَٰنَكُمْ أَوْلِيَآءَ إِنِ ٱسْتَحَبُّوا۟ ٱلْكُفْرَ عَلَى ٱلْإِيمَـٰنِ ۚ وَمَن يَتَوَلَّهُم مِّنكُمْ فَأُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلظَّـٰلِمُونَ

[ayah:9:24] قُلْ إِن كَانَ ءَابَآؤُكُمْ وَأَبْنَآؤُكُمْ وَإِخْوَٰنُكُمْ وَأَزْوَٰجُكُمْ وَعَشِيرَتُكُمْ وَأَمْوَٰلٌ ٱقْتَرَفْتُمُوهَا وَتِجَـٰرَةٌ تَخْشَوْنَ كَسَادَهَا وَمَسَـٰكِنُ تَرْضَوْنَهَآ أَحَبَّ إِلَيْكُم مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِۦ وَجِهَادٍ فِى سَبِيلِهِۦ فَتَرَبَّصُوا۟ حَتَّىٰ يَأْتِىَ ٱللَّهُ بِأَمْرِهِۦ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ لَا يَهْدِى ٱلْقَوْمَ ٱلْفَـٰسِقِينَ

*Ya ayyuha alladhina amanu la tattakhidhu aba'akum wa ikhwanakum awliya'a ini istahabbu al-kufra 'ala al-iman. Wa man yatawallahum minkum fa-ula'ika humu al-zalimun.*

*Qul in kana aba'ukum wa abna'ukum wa ikhwanukum wa azwajukum wa 'ashiratukum wa amwalun iqtaraftumuha wa tijaratun takhshawna kasadaha wa masakinu tardawnaha ahabba ilaykum mina Allahi wa rasulihi wa jihadin fi sabilihi fa-tarabbasu hatta ya'tiya Allahu bi-amrih. Wa Allahu la yahdi al-qawma al-fasiqin.*

**O you who believe, do not take your fathers and your brothers as intimate allies if they prefer disbelief over faith. Whoever among you takes them as allies, then such are the wrongdoers. Say: if your fathers, your sons, your brothers, your spouses, your clan, the wealth you have acquired, the trade whose decline you fear, and the homes you delight in are more beloved to you than Allah, His Messenger, and striving in His path, then wait until Allah brings His command. And Allah does not guide the defiantly disobedient people.**

## Introduction

Some of the hardest idols to recognize are not ugly things. They are beloved things.

This passage comes after the surah has already ranked faith, migration, and striving above sacred-adjacent service. Now the test moves from public religious ranking into the private rooms of the heart. Fathers. Sons. Brothers. Spouses. Clan. Wealth we worked for. Trade we are afraid to lose. Homes we feel soothed by. None of these are named because they are evil. They are named because they are powerful loves.

[PAUSE]

The Qur'an is not teaching emotional numbness. It is teaching hierarchy. When family loyalty begins to align with kufr over iman, or when beloved comforts become more cherished than Allah, His Messenger, and striving in His path, the heart has slipped into a false order. This is why the passage is so piercing. It does not ask whether you love these things. It asks what happens when they compete.

And notice the tenderness of the realism. The trade is not abstract trade. It is the trade whose slowdown you fear. The homes are not any homes. They are the homes you delight in. Revelation names the felt world exactly where it grips us. That is why the verse cuts so deeply. It is not arguing against a cartoon version of dunya. It is naming the actual attachments by which love gets disordered.

The hook of the passage is this: **At-Tawbah teaches that the real test of love is not whether we possess family, wealth, work, or homes, but whether any of them become more beloved to us than Allah, His Messenger, and the costly path that loyalty to them requires.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **La tattakhidhu ... awliya'**: Love Becomes Dangerous When It Turns into Misplaced Wilayah

Verse 23 says:

**la tattakhidhu aba'akum wa ikhwanakum awliya'**.

Do not take your fathers and your brothers as awliya'.

[PAUSE]

The issue is not bare biological relationship. The issue is **wilayah**: taking them into the place of intimate protective alignment and determining loyalty.

Then comes the condition:

**ini istahabbu al-kufra 'ala al-iman**.

If they prefer disbelief over faith.

So the verse is not abolishing family feeling. It is refusing a loyalty order in which kinship outranks truth.

### 2. **Wa man yatawallahum minkum**: This Choice Reveals Something About the Self

Then:

**wa man yatawallahum minkum fa-ula'ika humu al-zalimun**.

Whoever among you takes them as allies, such are the wrongdoers.

[PAUSE]

The zulm here is deep. It is not merely "you made a hard emotional choice." It is misplacing a right, putting love and loyalty in the wrong order, giving created ties a priority that belongs to Allah.

### 3. **Qul in kana ...**: The Verse Expands from Family into the Full Ecology of Attachment

Verse 24 widens the field:

**aba'ukum wa abna'ukum wa ikhwanukum wa azwajukum wa 'ashiratukum**.

Your fathers, your sons, your brothers, your spouses, your clan.

[PAUSE]

Then it moves beyond people:

**wa amwalun iqtaraftumuha**.

Wealth you acquired.

**wa tijaratun takhshawna kasadaha**.

Trade whose decline you fear.

**wa masakinu tardawnaha**.

Homes you are pleased with.

The verse is naming the emotional economy of a settled life.

### 4. **Ahabba ilaykum mina Allahi wa rasulihi wa jihadin fi sabilihi**: The Test Is Comparative Love

Then comes the core:

**ahabba ilaykum mina Allahi wa rasulihi wa jihadin fi sabilihi**.

More beloved to you than Allah, His Messenger, and striving in His path.

[PAUSE]

This is one of the most searching questions in the Qur'an. It does not ask whether you verbally affirm Allah. It asks how love is comparatively ordered when obedience becomes costly.

And the inclusion of **jihadin fi sabilihi** matters. The love of Allah and His Messenger is tested by willingness to accept the burden of their path.

### 5. **Fa-tarabbasu**: If Love Is Disordered, Wait for Allah's Command

Then the warning:

**fa-tarabbasu hatta ya'tiya Allahu bi-amrih**.

Then wait until Allah brings His command.

[PAUSE]

This is chilling. If the attachments named have become more beloved than Allah and His Messenger, then the person is left to wait for what Allah's judgment will bring.

And the close:

**wa Allahu la yahdi al-qawma al-fasiqin**.

Allah does not guide the defiantly disobedient people.

So disordered love is not emotionally neutral. It is part of fisq, stepping outside the proper order set by Allah.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The Greatest Tests Often Arrive Through Legitimate Loves

Many people imagine that spiritual testing always arrives through obviously forbidden things. But often the deeper test comes through legitimate loves that begin to claim illegitimate priority.

[PAUSE]

Family is good. Provision is good. Trade is necessary. Homes are mercy. The verse does not deny any of that. It asks what happens when the good thing demands from you a loyalty that belongs to Allah first.

### Theme 2: Fear of Loss Often Sits Under Disordered Love

The verse's naming of trade "whose slowdown you fear" is so psychologically exact. Much of what we call prudence is actually fear of contraction. We fear losing income, status, stability, comfort, approval.

[PAUSE]

And because we fear loss, we quietly rearrange our loyalties. The Qur'an names that mechanism openly so we can no longer hide from it.

## Closing Synthesis

This unit forbids taking fathers and brothers into a loyalty-bond that sides with kufr over iman, then expands the examination to the whole ecology of beloved things: parents, children, siblings, spouses, clan, acquired wealth, feared-for trade, and pleasing homes. If these become more beloved than Allah, His Messenger, and jihad in His path, then the person is told to wait for Allah's command, because such disordered love belongs to fisq rather than guidance.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with these verses is to ask: what legitimate love in my life most tempts me toward illegitimate priority? Where does fear of financial loss, family strain, or comfort's collapse quietly weaken my loyalty to Allah? And if Allah were to expose the actual order of my loves today, would I find that He and His Messenger truly sit highest?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of this unit lands here: **the Qur'an does not call believers to love nothing, but to love rightly, so that even the most beautiful created bonds never outrank Allah, His Messenger, and the costly path of fidelity to them.**

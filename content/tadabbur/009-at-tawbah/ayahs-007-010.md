# Do Not Be Fooled by Mouths When Their Hearts Refuse

## Surah At-Tawbah (9:7-10)

[ayah:9:7] كَيْفَ يَكُونُ لِلْمُشْرِكِينَ عَهْدٌ عِندَ ٱللَّهِ وَعِندَ رَسُولِهِۦٓ إِلَّا ٱلَّذِينَ عَـٰهَدتُّمْ عِندَ ٱلْمَسْجِدِ ٱلْحَرَامِ ۖ فَمَا ٱسْتَقَـٰمُوا۟ لَكُمْ فَٱسْتَقِيمُوا۟ لَهُمْ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يُحِبُّ ٱلْمُتَّقِينَ

[ayah:9:8] كَيْفَ وَإِن يَظْهَرُوا۟ عَلَيْكُمْ لَا يَرْقُبُوا۟ فِيكُمْ إِلًّا وَلَا ذِمَّةً ۚ يُرْضُونَكُم بِأَفْوَٰهِهِمْ وَتَأْبَىٰ قُلُوبُهُمْ وَأَكْثَرُهُمْ فَـٰسِقُونَ

[ayah:9:10] لَا يَرْقُبُونَ فِى مُؤْمِنٍ إِلًّا وَلَا ذِمَّةً ۚ وَأُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُعْتَدُونَ

*Kayfa yakunu lil-mushrikina 'ahdun 'inda Allahi wa 'inda rasulihi illa alladhina 'ahadtum 'inda al-masjidi al-haram. Fa-ma istaqamu lakum fasta qimu lahum. Inna Allaha yuhibbu al-muttaqin.*

*Kayfa wa in yazharu 'alaykum la yarqubu fikum illan wa la dhimmah. Yurdunakum bi-afwahihim wa ta'ba qulubuhum wa aktharuhum fasiqun.*

**How can there be for the polytheists a covenant with Allah and with His Messenger, except for those with whom you made a treaty near the Sacred Mosque? So long as they remain upright toward you, remain upright toward them. Surely Allah loves the people of taqwa. How can it be, when if they gain the upper hand over you, they would not honor in you any bond or treaty? They please you with their mouths while their hearts refuse, and most of them are defiantly disobedient. They traded Allah's signs for a small price, then turned people away from His path. How evil is what they used to do. They do not honor with a believer any bond or treaty, and they are the transgressors.**

## Introduction

One of the most painful things to learn about people is that some forms of politeness are not peace.

Some mouths know how to reassure while the heart is already planning otherwise. Some agreements are kept only as long as weakness requires them. Some smiles are simply tactics buying time. These ayahs are not teaching permanent paranoia. They are teaching moral literacy. The believer is being taught how to tell the difference between uprightness that deserves reciprocal uprightness and performative speech that conceals an unchanged heart.

[PAUSE]

That is why the passage does two things at once. It preserves the standing of those who remain straight in their treaty near the Sacred Mosque. But then it asks, almost with astonishment: **how** could covenant stand with those who, if they gained the upper hand, would honor no sacred bond and no pledged obligation? This is not a rejection of all treaties. It is an exposure of those for whom treaty is only temporary convenience.

And the passage goes deeper than behavior into moral appetite. They sold Allah's signs for a small price. That is the root diagnosis. A person who can cheaply sell truth will cheaply sell trust. Once the signs of Allah become marketable, every public bond becomes unstable.

The hook of the passage is this: **At-Tawbah teaches the believer to honor those who are upright, but never to confuse soothing language with real fidelity when the heart has already priced truth cheaply.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Fa-ma istaqamu lakum fasta qimu lahum**: Uprightness Must Be Answered with Uprightness

Verse 7 begins by reopening the covenant question:

**kayfa yakunu lil-mushrikina 'ahdun 'inda Allahi wa 'inda rasulihi**.

How can there be for the polytheists a covenant with Allah and with His Messenger?

[PAUSE]

The question is not asked because all possibility of treaty is inherently void. The exception immediately proves otherwise:

**illa alladhina 'ahadtum 'inda al-masjidi al-haram**.

Except those with whom you made a treaty near the Sacred Mosque.

Then comes the rule:

**fa-ma istaqamu lakum fasta qimu lahum**.

So long as they remain upright toward you, remain upright toward them.

This is one of the cleanest ethical reciprocities in the Qur'an. Uprightness is not to be met with suspicion for its own sake. If they keep straight, you keep straight.

### 2. **Kayfa wa in yazharu 'alaykum**: Their Character Is Exposed Through Hypothetical Power

Verse 8 begins again:

**kayfa**.

How?

[PAUSE]

The repeated question presses the moral absurdity of trusting the wrong people.

Then:

**wa in yazharu 'alaykum**.

If they gain the upper hand over you.

This is revealing. The passage does not simply ask how they behave while constrained. It asks how they would behave if power shifted. Character is often exposed most clearly by imagined or actual advantage.

And what would they do?

**la yarqubu fikum illan wa la dhimmah**.

They would honor in you neither sacred bond nor pledged treaty.

The pair is heavy. **Ill** suggests sacred or close bond. **Dhimmah** suggests protected covenantal obligation. They would spare neither the intimate moral claim nor the formal public claim.

### 3. **Yurdunakum bi-afwahihim wa ta'ba qulubuhum**: Mouth and Heart Split Apart

Then comes the devastating line:

**yurdunakum bi-afwahihim wa ta'ba qulubuhum**.

They please you with their mouths, while their hearts refuse.

[PAUSE]

This is one of the clearest Qur'anic descriptions of manipulative speech. The mouth performs reassurance. The heart remains in refusal.

And the verb **ta'ba** is strong. The hearts do not merely hesitate. They refuse, recoil, resist. The problem is not lack of communication skill. It is inward unwillingness.

Then:

**wa aktharuhum fasiqun**.

Most of them are defiantly disobedient.

So the split between word and heart is not accidental. It belongs to a wider condition of fisq, of stepping outside rightful bounds.

### 4. **Ishtaraw bi-ayati Allahi thamanan qalilan**: Betrayal Began Earlier, at the Level of Value

Verse 9 diagnoses the deeper economy:

**ishtaraw bi-ayati Allahi thamanan qalilan**.

They bought with Allah's signs a small price.

[PAUSE]

This is spiritually devastating language. The issue is not merely that they made one bad political decision. They sold revelation cheaply. They exchanged the signs of Allah for something trivial and near.

Then:

**fa-saddu 'an sabilihi**.

So they turned others away from His path.

Corruption of value becomes corruption of guidance. Once someone has priced the signs cheaply, blocking others from Allah's path becomes easier.

### 5. **La yarqubuna fi mu'minin illan wa la dhimmah**: The Passage Ends by Naming Them as Transgressors

Verse 10 repeats the earlier pattern, but now narrows the focus:

**la yarqubuna fi mu'minin illan wa la dhimmah**.

They do not honor in a believer any bond or treaty.

[PAUSE]

The المؤمن is singled out. Their refusal is not random. It is especially pointed toward the believer.

Then comes the judgment:

**wa ula'ika humu al-mu'tadun**.

They are the transgressors.

Not merely imperfect negotiators. Not merely people with a hard past. They cross bounds.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Real Integrity Is Measured Not by Speech Alone but by What Power Would Reveal

These ayahs are a deep education in discernment. We often judge people by what they say under pressure, but the Qur'an asks a sharper question: what would they do if they were stronger than you?

[PAUSE]

That question strips away many illusions. Some people speak gently because they are constrained, not because they are upright. Some agreements survive only because the balance of power currently requires them. Revelation is teaching the believers not to build trust on pleasant speech alone.

### Theme 2: Cheapening Revelation Corrupts Every Other Bond

The line about selling Allah's signs for a small price is the key moral diagnosis in the passage. When truth itself can be exchanged for something little, all other loyalties become negotiable too.

[PAUSE]

That is why spiritual corruption and social betrayal are so close in the Qur'an. The person who will cheaply sell revelation will not find treaties sacred for long. He has already priced the highest thing too low.

## Closing Synthesis

This passage preserves the obligation to remain upright with those who remain upright, but it exposes the inner structure of the treaty-breakers: if they had advantage, they would honor neither sacred bond nor formal pledge; they soothe with their mouths while their hearts refuse; they sold Allah's signs for a tiny price and then obstructed His path; and in the end they are named outright as transgressors.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with these ayahs is to ask: where in my life have I confused verbal reassurance with real integrity? Do I know how to honor uprightness without becoming gullible before flattery? And have I noticed how every smaller betrayal in a person often traces back to a deeper cheapening of what Allah made weighty?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of this unit lands here: **the Qur'an teaches believers to answer straightness with straightness, but it also teaches them that when hearts have already sold truth cheaply, pleasing words are not enough to carry the weight of trust.**

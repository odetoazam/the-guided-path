# Believing Men and Women Are Protecting Friends of One Another

## Surah At-Tawbah (9:71-72)

[ayah:9:71] وَٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنَـٰتُ بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلِيَآءُ بَعْضٍ ۚ يَأْمُرُونَ بِٱلْمَعْرُوفِ وَيَنْهَوْنَ عَنِ ٱلْمُنكَرِ وَيُقِيمُونَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَيُؤْتُونَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَيُطِيعُونَ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥٓ ۚ أُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ سَيَرْحَمُهُمُ ٱللَّهُ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ عَزِيزٌ حَكِيمٌ

[ayah:9:72] وَعَدَ ٱللَّهُ ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنَـٰتِ جَنَّـٰتٍ تَجْرِى مِن تَحْتِهَا ٱلْأَنْهَـٰرُ خَـٰلِدِينَ فِيهَا وَمَسَـٰكِنَ طَيِّبَةً فِى جَنَّـٰتِ عَدْنٍ ۚ وَرِضْوَٰنٌ مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ أَكْبَرُ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ هُوَ ٱلْفَوْزُ ٱلْعَظِيمُ

*Wa al-mu'minuna wa al-mu'minatu ba'duhum awliya'u ba'd. Ya'muruna bi-al-ma'rufi wa yanhawna 'an al-munkari wa yuqimuna al-salata wa yu'tuna al-zakata wa yuti'una Allaha wa rasulahu. Ula'ika sayarhamuhumu Allah. Inna Allaha 'azizun hakim.*

*Wa'ada Allahu al-mu'minina wa al-mu'minati jannatin tajri min tahtiha al-anharu khalidina fiha wa masakina tayyibatan fi jannati 'Adn. Wa ridwanun mina Allahi akbar. Dhalika huwa al-fawzu al-'azim.*

**And the believing men and believing women are protecting friends of one another: they command what is right, forbid what is wrong, establish prayer, give zakah, and obey Allah and His Messenger. Those are the ones Allah will soon cover with mercy. Surely Allah is Mighty, Wise. Allah has promised the believing men and believing women gardens beneath which rivers flow, abiding therein, and good dwellings in gardens of lasting residence. But the good pleasure of Allah is greater. That is the great triumph.**

## Introduction

After showing us the architecture of hypocrisy, the surah now shows us the architecture of belief.

The contrast is exact and beautiful. Hypocrite men and women were said to be of one another in a community of inversion. Here believing men and women are said to be **awliya'** of one another. They protect, support, guard, and stand for one another. The difference is not merely doctrinal. It is social, moral, economic, devotional, and relational all at once.

[PAUSE]

Then the verse unfolds the believing community's shape: commanding right, forbidding wrong, establishing prayer, giving zakah, obeying Allah and His Messenger. This is not a sentimental brotherhood. It is a disciplined community of worship and moral care. Then comes mercy. Then paradise. Then, after all the gardens and dwellings, something greater still: Allah's ridwan.

That final ascent is breathtaking. The ayah does not let the believer stop at created reward. It lifts him beyond the gardens to the pleasure of the One who gives them. Paradise is real and glorious, but the surah still says there is something greater.

The hook of the passage is this: **At-Tawbah teaches that the believing community is built through protective loyalty, moral care, prayer, zakah, and obedience, and that its ultimate reward rises beyond gardens themselves into the pleasure of Allah.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Ba'duhum awliya'u ba'd**: Faith Makes a Protecting Fellowship

Verse 71 says:

**wa al-mu'minuna wa al-mu'minatu ba'duhum awliya'u ba'd**.

The believing men and women are awliya' of one another.

[PAUSE]

This is more than friendliness. **Wilayah** carries protection, nearness, support, and aligned concern. The believing community is not meant to be a crowd of isolated worshippers.

### 2. **Ya'muruna bi-al-ma'ruf wa yanhawna 'an al-munkar**: Their Social Pressure Faces the Right Direction

Then:

**ya'muruna bi-al-ma'ruf wa yanhawna 'an al-munkar**.

[PAUSE]

This is the perfect reversal of the previous hypocritical portrait. Believers do not merely avoid evil privately. They help create a moral environment that faces the right way.

### 3. **Yuqimuna al-salata wa yu'tuna al-zakata**: Worship and Social Care Remain Joined

Then:

**wa yuqimuna al-salata wa yu'tuna al-zakata**.

[PAUSE]

Again the Qur'an joins devotion and material purification. Salah keeps the bond with Allah alive; zakah keeps the hand open and the community supplied with mercy.

### 4. **Ula'ika sayarhamuhumu Allah**: Mercy Is the Immediate Divine Answer

Then:

**ula'ika sayarhamuhumu Allah**.

Those are the ones Allah will soon have mercy on.

[PAUSE]

The first named divine response is mercy. Before the details of paradise, the ayah tells us how Allah regards such a people.

### 5. **Wa ridwanun mina Allahi akbar**: The Reward Climbs Beyond Created Beauty

Verse 72 culminates in:

**wa ridwanun mina Allahi akbar**.

The pleasure of Allah is greater.

[PAUSE]

Greater than what? Greater than the gardens just named. Greater than the dwellings. Greater than the flowing rivers. The verse educates the believer's appetite so that even in yearning for paradise he does not stop short of Allah Himself.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Real Community Is Formed by Shared Obedience, Not Vague Affection

These ayahs push back against shallow notions of togetherness. The bond among believers is sustained by worship, moral seriousness, and mutual concern for one another's standing before Allah.

[PAUSE]

Without those, "community" can become little more than warm atmosphere.

### Theme 2: The Heart Must Be Trained to Want More Than Paradise's Features

The move from gardens to ridwan is one of the Qur'an's most refining moments. It teaches the believer that created rewards, however immense, are not the final summit. The deepest triumph is that Allah is pleased.

[PAUSE]

That is what makes paradise paradise.

## Closing Synthesis

These two verses present the believing men and women as protective allies of one another who command right, forbid wrong, establish prayer, give zakah, and obey Allah and His Messenger. Such a people are promised divine mercy, gardens, rivers, and beautiful dwellings, but the passage culminates higher still: the pleasure of Allah is greater, and that is the great triumph.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this unit is to ask: does my belonging among believers have the weight of wilayah, or only the sentiment of friendliness? Am I helping create a moral atmosphere that faces the right direction? And when I imagine my hoped-for end with Allah, do I stop at the gifts, or do I still know that His pleasure is greater?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of this unit lands here: **belief forms a protected, worshipping, morally serious community whose true triumph is not only paradise received, but Allah's pleasure resting upon it.**

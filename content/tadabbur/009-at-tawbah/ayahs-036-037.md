# Do Not Bend Allah's Calendar to Fit Your Desires

## Surah At-Tawbah (9:36-37)

[ayah:9:36] إِنَّ عِدَّةَ ٱلشُّهُورِ عِندَ ٱللَّهِ ٱثْنَا عَشَرَ شَهْرًا فِى كِتَـٰبِ ٱللَّهِ يَوْمَ خَلَقَ ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ مِنْهَآ أَرْبَعَةٌ حُرُمٌ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ ٱلدِّينُ ٱلْقَيِّمُ ۚ فَلَا تَظْلِمُوا۟ فِيهِنَّ أَنفُسَكُمْ ۚ وَقَـٰتِلُوا۟ ٱلْمُشْرِكِينَ كَآفَّةً كَمَا يُقَـٰتِلُونَكُمْ كَآفَّةً ۚ وَٱعْلَمُوٓا۟ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ مَعَ ٱلْمُتَّقِينَ

[ayah:9:37] إِنَّمَا ٱلنَّسِىٓءُ زِيَادَةٌ فِى ٱلْكُفْرِ ۖ يُضَلُّ بِهِ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ يُحِلُّونَهُۥ عَامًا وَيُحَرِّمُونَهُۥ عَامًا لِّيُوَاطِـُٔوا۟ عِدَّةَ مَا حَرَّمَ ٱللَّهُ فَيُحِلُّوا۟ مَا حَرَّمَ ٱللَّهُ ۚ زُيِّنَ لَهُمْ سُوٓءُ أَعْمَـٰلِهِمْ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ لَا يَهْدِى ٱلْقَوْمَ ٱلْكَـٰفِرِينَ

*Inna 'iddata al-shuhuri 'inda Allahi ithna 'ashara shahran fi kitabi Allah yawma khalaqa al-samawati wa al-ard, minha arba'atun hurum. Dhalika al-dinu al-qayyim. Fa-la tazlimu fihinna anfusakum. Wa qatilu al-mushrikina kaffatan kama yuqatilunakum kaffah. Wa'lamu anna Allaha ma'a al-muttaqin.*

*Innama al-nasi'u ziyadatun fi al-kufr. Yudallu bihi alladhina kafaru yuhillunahu 'aman wa yuharrimunahu 'aman li-yuwati'u 'iddata ma harrama Allah fa-yuhillu ma harrama Allah. Zuyyina lahum su'u a'malihim. Wa Allahu la yahdi al-qawma al-kafirin.*

**Surely the number of months with Allah is twelve months in the Book of Allah from the day He created the heavens and the earth; of them, four are sacred. That is the upright religion, so do not wrong yourselves during them. And fight the polytheists altogether as they fight you altogether, and know that Allah is with the people of taqwa. Indeed, postponing the sacred months is only an increase in disbelief. Those who disbelieve are led astray by it: they make it lawful one year and unlawful another year so as to match the number Allah made sacred, thereby making lawful what Allah made unlawful. The ugliness of their deeds has been beautified for them, and Allah does not guide the disbelieving people.**

## Introduction

One of the oldest ways people rebel against Allah is not by denying the sacred outright, but by adjusting it to fit convenience.

That is the heart of this passage. The sacred months are not tribal customs floating loose in history. They are rooted in Allah's book from the day He created the heavens and the earth. Time itself has a divinely set order. But the human ego hates limits it cannot renegotiate, so it invents religious-sounding adjustments. One year the month is sacred; another year it is moved. Formally the number is preserved, but in reality the command has been emptied and rewritten.

[PAUSE]

That is why the Qur'an speaks so strongly. This is not clever calendrical flexibility. It is an increase in kufr. Why? Because the deeper problem is not the math. The deeper problem is the will to keep the appearance of obedience while making lawful what Allah made unlawful. The passage is exposing a spiritual habit far wider than calendars: we preserve the symbolic count, the outer structure, the public respectability, while quietly shifting the command until it serves appetite.

And the ayah places all of this inside a majestic frame. Twelve months. Four sacred. Written with Allah from the day the heavens and earth were created. This is not negotiable because it is woven into the moral architecture of reality.

The hook of the passage is this: **the moment we start moving Allah's fixed boundaries around to suit our strategy while pretending the sacred number remains intact, we have already begun turning religion from surrender into manipulation.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **'Inda Allah ... yawma khalaqa al-samawati wa al-ard**: The Calendar Is Rooted in Creation

Verse 36 begins:

**inna 'iddata al-shuhuri 'inda Allah ithna 'ashara shahran**.

The number of months with Allah is twelve months.

[PAUSE]

Then:

**fi kitabi Allah yawma khalaqa al-samawati wa al-ard**.

In the Book of Allah, from the day He created the heavens and the earth.

This is breathtaking. The calendar is not merely a human coordination device. It is tied to divine writing and cosmic creation. The sacred order of time belongs to Allah before human communities organize around it.

### 2. **Minha arba'atun hurum**: Some Time Is Marked with Added Sanctity

Then:

**minha arba'atun hurum**.

Of them, four are sacred.

[PAUSE]

The ayah doesn't present time as flat. Some times carry extra sanctity, extra limits, extra moral charge.

Then it says:

**dhalika al-dinu al-qayyim**.

That is the upright religion.

The ordering of sacred time is not a side issue. It belongs to the uprightness of الدين itself.

### 3. **Fa-la tazlimu fihinna anfusakum**: Violating Sacred Time Is Self-Wronging

Then:

**fa-la tazlimu fihinna anfusakum**.

Do not wrong yourselves in them.

[PAUSE]

This is striking. The first way the ayah frames violation is not simply as wronging a rule, but as wronging the self. Sacred boundaries are protections. Crossing them injures the soul first.

### 4. **Innama al-nasi'u ziyadatun fi al-kufr**: Manipulating Sacred Time Is Not Neutral

Verse 37 turns:

**innama al-nasi'u ziyadatun fi al-kufr**.

Postponement is only an increase in disbelief.

[PAUSE]

That is strong language because the corruption is deep. **Al-nasi'** is delaying, deferring, rearranging the sacred months to suit human plans.

Why call it an increase in kufr? Because it is rebellion wearing religious formatting.

### 5. **Li-yuwati'u 'iddata ma harrama Allah**: Keeping the Count While Killing the Command

Then the logic is exposed:

**li-yuwati'u 'iddata ma harrama Allah**.

So that they match the number of what Allah made sacred.

[PAUSE]

This is the key. They preserve the count. The number still looks right. But then:

**fa-yuhillu ma harrama Allah**.

So they make lawful what Allah made unlawful.

That is one of the Qur'an's sharpest exposures of performative obedience. The shell is preserved; the command is gutted.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Humans Love Symbolic Obedience When Real Obedience Costs Too Much

This passage is about a specific calendrical abuse, but it also reveals a general religious pathology. We often want to keep the label, the number, the public sign, or the formal count while quietly shifting the actual command into something easier for us.

[PAUSE]

That is what makes the passage so contemporary. The ego has not changed. It still prefers cosmetic submission to costly surrender.

### Theme 2: Sacred Boundaries Protect Us Even When We Want to Renegotiate Them

The phrase "do not wrong yourselves" should make us read every divine limit differently. We often experience sacred boundaries as restraints on our freedom. The Qur'an describes violating them as self-wronging.

[PAUSE]

That means the limit was mercy before it felt like a limit. We only perceive it as restrictive because desire wants fluidity where Allah decreed form.

## Closing Synthesis

This unit roots the twelve-month order and the four sacred months in Allah's book from the day of creation itself, calls that structure part of the upright religion, and warns believers not to wrong themselves in those sacred times. It then exposes the practice of postponing sacred months as an increase in kufr because it preserves the number outwardly while making lawful what Allah made unlawful.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with these ayahs is to ask: where in my life am I tempted to preserve the appearance of obedience while quietly moving Allah's limits around to fit my convenience? Which parts of revelation do I still treat like negotiable time slots instead of sacred structure? And do I understand that every time I manipulate the boundary, I am not only playing with a rule but wronging my own soul?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of this unit lands here: **Allah's sacred order cannot be faithfully honored by people who keep the count but move the boundary, because true religion is surrender to the form He set, not cleverness about how to bypass it.**

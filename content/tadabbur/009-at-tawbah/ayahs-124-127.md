# The Same Revelation Raises One Heart and Pollutes Another

## Surah At-Tawbah (9:124-127)

[ayah:9:124] وَإِذَا مَآ أُنزِلَتْ سُورَةٌ فَمِنْهُم مَّن يَقُولُ أَيُّكُمْ زَادَتْهُ هَـٰذِهِۦٓ إِيمَـٰنًا ۚ فَأَمَّا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ فَزَادَتْهُمْ إِيمَـٰنًا وَهُمْ يَسْتَبْشِرُونَ

## Introduction

Revelation does not enter every heart the same way.

The same surah descends. The same words are heard. The same room receives the same recitation. Yet one heart rises, another hardens, one feels glad tidings, another feels inconvenience, one stays, another looks around for the nearest exit. The Qur'an is not only light; it is exposure. It reveals what the heart already wants from the truth.

[PAUSE]

That is why these closing verses of At-Tawbah are so searching. They do not mainly ask, "Did revelation come?" It did. They ask, "What did it do to you?" Did it increase iman, or did it only add filth to filth? Did yearly tests wake you up, or did you live through them unread? When the surah was recited, did you lean in, or did your eyes start scanning the room for a way to leave unseen?

Al-Tabari reads these verses as a devastating disclosure of the hypocrites' interior state: revelation itself became the occasion through which their hidden disease showed itself more clearly. Ibn Kathir keeps the same contrast sharp. The believers heard the surah and increased; the hypocrites heard the same surah and only accumulated more doubt, more impurity, and more exposure. Al-Muyassar makes the emotional texture especially plain: the healthy heart receives the surah with understanding, reflection, and joy, while the diseased heart receives it with mockery, denial, and tightening resistance.

[PAUSE]

These verses matter now because many of us still ask the wrong first question of revelation. We ask whether a passage was interesting, eloquent, intense, relevant, or intellectually satisfying. The Qur'an here asks something more dangerous: what direction did it move you in? Closer, softer, more alert, more willing to repent? Or more defended, more bored, more irritated, more eager to slip away before the truth becomes personally expensive?

So the hook of the passage is this: **At-Tawbah teaches that revelation is never neutral; it either increases a receptive heart in faith and gladness or pushes a diseased heart deeper into impurity, and repeated tests become useless only when a person refuses repentance and deep understanding.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Zadat-hu hadhihi imanan**: Revelation Is Measured by What It Adds

The first question the hypocrites ask is not entirely foolish on the surface: **ayyukum zadat-hu hadhihi imanan**. Which of you has this increased in faith? The root **z-y-d** is about increase, addition, surplus, something becoming more than it was. So even their mocking question unintentionally acknowledges a profound truth: revelation does not simply sit in front of a person unchanged. It adds. It acts. It alters the interior.

That is why the question is so exposing. The hypocrite asks it as if the matter were dubious or worth sarcastic testing, but the Qur'an answers it without hesitation: yes, revelation does increase. The real difference lies not in the Book's power but in the condition of the receiver.

[PAUSE]

The wording also matters because the ayah does not ask first whether the surah increased information. It asks whether it increased **iman**. Revelation is not being evaluated at the level of novelty or data. It is being evaluated by what it does to trust, surrender, certainty, and living response before Allah.

This already destabilizes a common modern posture toward the Qur'an. We often treat it as a text to analyze while leaving open whether it is allowed to change us. The hypocrites in the ayah sound almost analytical too. They ask the question from a distance. The believers answer it by being changed.

### 2. **Fa-zadathum imanan wa hum yastabshirun**: The Believer Feels Increase and Glad News Together

The believing response is brief but full: **fa-zadathum imanan wa hum yastabshirun**. It increased them in faith while they rejoice with glad tidings. The healthy heart does not only process revelation correctly. It receives it as bushra, as something secretly life-giving.

The verb **yastabshirun** comes from the field of good news, brightening, receiving something that gladdens from within. The verse could have said only that faith increased and stopped there. Instead it pairs increase with visible gladness. This is important. The believer may still find a surah demanding, frightening, or exposing, but beneath even that seriousness there is recognition: Allah spoke again. He did not abandon me to darkness. He addressed me once more.

[PAUSE]

Al-Tabari describes the increase here as deeper confirmation and stronger truthfulness in relation to Allah and His signs. Al-Muyassar adds a helpful layer: the increase comes through knowing the surah, reflecting upon it, believing it, and acting by it. So the Qur'an is not describing an undefined spiritual mood. It is describing a living process in which revelation becomes knowledge, reflection, conviction, and obedient motion.

This also teaches us that gladness at revelation is itself a health sign. Not performative excitement. Not aesthetic appreciation alone. But that inward sense that even a hard verse is mercy because Allah has not left the servant unaddressed.

### 3. **Rijsan ila rijsihim**: Disease Does Not Stay Still Under Revelation

Then the mirror turns: **wa amma alladhina fi qulubihim maradun fa-zadathum rijsan ila rijsihim**. Those in whose hearts is disease were increased in defilement upon their defilement. The root contrast is devastating. The same descending surah produced **iman** in one heart and **rijs** in another.

**Marad** in the heart is not presented here as a mild spiritual weakness. It is an active condition that distorts reception. And **rijs** is not neutral dirt. It carries the sense of foulness, impurity, corruption, moral defilement. The diseased heart does not simply fail to benefit. It worsens under exposure.

[PAUSE]

That is one of the most frightening things the Qur'an says about long-term inner resistance. A person can reach a point where the medicine itself becomes, through his refusal, another layer of sickness. Ibn Kathir reads this as increase in doubt and suspicion. Al-Tabari names it as added filth and hypocrisy joined to what was already there. The problem is not that revelation lacked clarity. The problem is that the heart kept turning clarity into one more reason to resist.

And then the verse closes with tragic finality: **wa matu wa hum kafirun**. They died while disbelieving. The point is not only that they were sick. It is that they never interrupted the trajectory.

### 4. **Yuftanuna fi kulli 'amin marratan aw marratayn**: Repeated Tests Keep Returning as Invitations

Verse 126 asks: **a-wa la yarawna annahum yuftanuna fi kulli 'amin marratan aw marratayn**. Do they not see that they are tested every year once or twice? The passive form matters. Tests keep coming upon them. They are not scarce. They are recurring mercies in severe dress.

The root **f-t-n** covers testing, exposing, proving, placing something in a fire so its reality shows. The ayah is therefore not merely saying, "bad things happen to them." It is saying that Allah keeps submitting them to realities meant to uncover and possibly awaken them.

[PAUSE]

The phrase **marratan aw marratayn** is especially searching. Once or twice every year. Not once in a lifetime. Not one unforgettable catastrophe. Regularly. Life keeps returning with evidence, friction, exposure, humiliation, need, fear, loss, or inner shaking. Yet the verse says they still neither repent nor take heed.

Al-Tabari preserves the rebuke whether read as directed to the hypocrites themselves or as directed to the believers observing them. Ibn Kathir cites the early view that they were tested by drought, hunger, and exposures of hypocrisy. Al-Muyassar makes the same point plainly: Allah kept showing them signs in hardship and exposure, but they did not turn.

### 5. **Nazara ba'duhum ila ba'd ... thumma insarafu**: The Body Confesses Before the Tongue Does

Then the surah becomes almost cinematic: **wa idha ma unzilat suratun nazara ba'duhum ila ba'd**. When a surah is sent down, they look at one another. The eyes move before the feet do. Hypocrisy appears here not first in a formal speech but in shared body language.

That detail is merciless in its accuracy. Before they leave, they silently ask permission from one another. "Can we get out now?" "Did anyone see?" "Is there a path out that won't expose us?" The Qur'an catches the whole choreography.

[PAUSE]

Then comes the whispered concern: **hal yarakum min ahad**. Does anyone see you? Not "is this false?" Not "is this from Allah?" Their anxiety is social detection. They want to avoid the shame of being seen leaving revelation, not the reality of leaving revelation itself.

Then: **thumma insarafu**. Then they turned away. And immediately the divine mirror answers: **sarafa Allahu qulubahum**. Allah has turned their hearts away. The outward act and the inward consequence are joined by the same root. Repeated departure from truth with the body becomes a training in departure from truth with the heart.

### 6. **Bi-annahum qawmun la yafqahun**: The Final Wound Is Not Ignorance Alone but Refusal of Deep Understanding

The closing diagnosis is not merely that they lack data. It is: **bi-annahum qawmun la yafqahun**. They are a people who do not deeply understand. The root **f-q-h** is not shallow knowing. It is penetrating grasp, inner comprehension, understanding that reaches consequence.

That is the final irony of the passage. Earlier the hypocrites asked a quasi-intellectual question about increase in faith. Here Allah reveals the real problem: they do not possess the kind of understanding that lets revelation land beneath the surface. They can hear words without fiqh. They can witness tests without fiqh. They can even sit in the room of revelation without fiqh.

[PAUSE]

So the linguistic architecture of the whole unit moves from increase, to bifurcation, to repeated testing, to bodily evasion, to turned hearts, to the final diagnosis of absent deep understanding. Revelation's descent is stable. The hearts' reception is what splits.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Revelation Is a Mirror Before It Is a Topic

Many people approach the Qur'an mainly as information. These verses show it also as disclosure. It does not only tell you something about Allah, history, law, or the world. It tells you something about yourself by what it awakens or irritates in you.

[PAUSE]

That means one of the most important questions after hearing Qur'an is not simply, "Did I understand the content?" but, "What happened to my heart while it was being recited?" Did the passage create hunger, humility, repentance, gratitude, trembling, relief, longing, or seriousness? Or did it create defensiveness, boredom, irritation, sarcasm, and the urge to slip inwardly away before the address could become personal?

This is hard for modern readers because we are trained to prize critical distance. We know how to discuss a text, compare translations, identify themes, and extract ideas. But a mirror does not care how sophisticated your commentary is if you keep refusing to look at your own face. At-Tawbah 9:124-125 says the decisive event is not that a surah descended. The decisive event is that hearts were revealed by what the surah did inside them.

Al-Tabari's framing helps here. He treats the hypocrites' question not as a sincere search but as a disclosure of their own inner posture. Revelation had already encountered them enough times that the issue was no longer lack of evidence. The issue was the kind of heart listening. Ibn Kathir sharpens the contrast in the same way: the believers increased in faith; the diseased increased in doubt. The same sun softens wax and hardens clay. The ayah is not making a cheap analogy, but it is naming that kind of divergence.

[PAUSE]

The Qur'an elsewhere confirms this mirror-function. In 2:26 Allah says that many are guided by a parable and many are led astray by it, though He does not thereby mislead except the defiantly disobedient. In 17:82 the Qur'an is described as healing and mercy for the believers while the wrongdoers increase only in loss. In 74:31 the number guarding Hell becomes, by Allah's decree, a strengthening of faith for believers and a fitnah for diseased hearts and disbelievers. Across these verses the pattern is stable: revelation does not become something else from one listener to another; rather, listeners are exposed by how they receive the same revelation.

That has unsettling implications for us. A person can spend years around Qur'an without letting Qur'an name him. He can enjoy its sound, admire its eloquence, build identity around it, teach parts of it, even defend it polemically, while still quietly refusing the places where it would require surrender. In such a case the Qur'an does not become powerless. It becomes a witness against selective listening.

[PAUSE]

This is why healthy believers often experience even difficult verses with a hidden gladness. The verse says: **wa hum yastabshirun**. They rejoice. That does not mean every ayah feels emotionally easy. Some ayat terrify, strip, correct, and confront. But beneath that there is a deep relief: Allah is still addressing me. He is not leaving me to my own blindness. The command itself can feel like mercy because it means abandonment has not occurred.

By contrast, the diseased heart experiences revelation as inconvenience, accusation, exposure, or obstruction. The problem is not only that it disagrees. The problem is that it increasingly wants distance from the very voice that could save it. That is what **rijsan ila rijsihim** means at the experiential level. The heart grows spiritually dirtier not because the Qur'an contaminated it, but because the heart kept converting mercy into another occasion for refusal.

[PAUSE]

We can watch this in ourselves in smaller ways long before it reaches hypocrisy's full ugliness. A verse touches an idol we cherish, and suddenly we become technical, evasive, dismissive, or oddly tired. A recitation names a habit we do not want to surrender, and we start searching for softer interpretations before we have even allowed the wound to land. A reminder arrives repeatedly about our speech, our money, our pride, our family obligations, or our laziness, and instead of asking why Allah keeps sending that ayah into our life, we focus on whether the speaker's tone was ideal. These are not yet the full disease described in the passage, but they are directions of the same kind.

So the practical demand of this theme is difficult and simple: when Qur'an comes, ask first what it is revealing in you. Do not rush to master the verse before letting the verse master at least one false comfort in your interior world. Revelation is not merely something to understand. It is something before which the heart is judged and healed.

### Theme 2: The Great Spiritual Danger Is Living Through Repeated Signs Without Letting Them Become Tawbah

Verse 126 may be one of the most psychologically precise condemnations in the surah: they are tested every year once or twice, yet they neither repent nor remember. This is not rebellion after no warning. It is rebellion after recurring warning.

[PAUSE]

That may be one of the darkest forms of heedlessness: to pass through repeated exposures and keep treating each one as temporary inconvenience instead of commentary from Allah on the state of your heart. A person loses something, is shaken, returns briefly, then hardens again. He is exposed publicly, feels shame, becomes soft for a week, then reconstructs the same self. He goes through illness, betrayal, collapse, fatigue, failure, fear, financial tightening, the death of illusion, the loss of control, and each time life almost opens him. Almost. Then he returns to the old arrangement as though nothing in the event had been addressed to him.

That is exactly the terror of **thumma la yatubun wa la hum yadhdhakkarun**. The tragedy is not that no event occurred. The tragedy is that event after event failed to become repentance or living remembrance.

The classical comments give this texture. Ibn Kathir preserves early reports that the hypocrites were tested by drought, hunger, and the repeated uncovering of what they tried to hide. Al-Tabari allows the rebuke to remain broad enough to include recurring forms of exposure and trial. Al-Muyassar captures the blunt mercy of it: Allah kept trying them with hardship and disclosure, but they still did not return. These are not random knocks of fate. They are yearly invitations.

[PAUSE]

The Qur'an elsewhere insists on this pattern. In 32:21 Allah says He will let them taste the nearer punishment before the greater one so that perhaps they will return. In 7:94-95 peoples are visited by hardship and adversity so they might humble themselves, yet many only move from pain to careless ease. In 39:8 man calls on his Lord when harm touches him, then once favor is granted he forgets what he had cried for. The Book keeps naming the same human failure: we are often emotionally moved by trial without allowing trial to become conversion.

This is especially relevant in our age because we have become very skilled at metabolizing crisis without meaning. We can therapize it, brand it, narrate it, optimize after it, joke about it, and continue. All of these may have their place in limited forms, but none of them equal tawbah. A person may become more self-aware after a trial without becoming more surrendered. He may even become more articulate about his wounds while remaining protected from Allah at the deepest level.

[PAUSE]

The ayah therefore asks a severe question: what has Allah already sent you more than once? Which lesson has come yearly, or seasonally, or relationally, or through repeating collapse? Where has He kept touching the same structural weakness in your life? Your speech? Your money? Your dependence on praise? Your refusal to pray on time? Your private indulgence? Your inability to forgive? Your tendency to leave truth when it becomes uncomfortable? Repetition itself is a clue. Sometimes the most merciful interpretation of recurring pain is that Allah is not finished inviting you to read it correctly.

There is another layer here too. The verse says **yadhdhakkarun** as well as **yatubun**. They do not repent and they do not remember. Tawbah is the turning; tadhakkur is the inward taking-to-heart. Some people momentarily regret without remembering. They feel bad, make a temporary adjustment, and then allow the event to become inert history. The Qur'an wants more. It wants the event to stay alive as insight, as memory with consequence, as an enduring rearrangement of perception.

[PAUSE]

That is why repeated tests become dangerous if unread. Each unread test can train a person in spiritual numbness. The first warning shook him. The second irritated him. The third became normal background noise. By then the issue is no longer just the trial itself. The issue is that his heart has learned how to survive revelation without yielding to it.

So the mercy of this passage is that it warns us while time remains. If Allah has been repeating a lesson in your life, do not ask only when it will stop. Ask what it has been trying to say. Ask what repentance would look like if you finally took the repetition seriously. The hypocrites' disaster was not that life stopped addressing them. It was that they kept remaining unaddressed inside being addressed.

### Theme 3: Leaving the Room With Your Body Teaches the Heart to Leave With It

Verse 127 is one of the most frighteningly accurate portraits of spiritual evasion in the Qur'an because it captures the moment before departure. They look at one another. They ask whether anyone sees them. Then they slip away. Then Allah turns their hearts away.

[PAUSE]

The sequence matters. Hypocrisy is not only false doctrine. It is practiced withdrawal. A person keeps choosing absence from truth at the moment truth becomes uncomfortable, and those outward gestures slowly tutor the heart. The body teaches the interior what to love, what to avoid, and what to flee from.

This is why the ayah's social detail is so devastating. They do not openly stand up and announce rejection. They glance. They coordinate. They seek invisibility. They want a departure without consequence. This is profoundly modern. Many people do not dramatically reject revelation. They simply learn how to leave it without seeming to leave it. They keep religious vocabulary, outward affiliation, maybe even public usefulness, but in the moments where Qur'an becomes expensive, exposing, or identity-threatening, something in them slips away.

Al-Tabari's explanation of the hypocrites' glances preserves that bodily secrecy: they feared that their reactions to what the surah exposed about them would be seen. Al-Muyassar makes the humiliation even sharper by describing the furtive signals and fear of exposure. Ibn Kathir joins verse 127 closely to the recurring trials of verse 126, which means the departure is not isolated. It comes after repeated warning. This was practiced behavior.

[PAUSE]

The Qur'an gives us powerful parallels to this spiritual law. In 61:5, when they deviated, Allah caused their hearts to deviate. In 6:110, because they refused belief the first time, their hearts and eyes are turned away. These are not arbitrary punishments disconnected from human action. They are divine judgments that ratify repeated chosen evasions. At-Tawbah 9:127 stands in that same terrifying grammar. **Thumma insarafu. Sarafa Allahu qulubahum.** They turned away. Allah turned their hearts away. The verbal echo is the lesson.

This should make us nervous about habits of selective absence. Not every act of distraction is hypocrisy, of course. Not every tired heart that struggles to stay present has fallen into this disease. The Qur'an is more merciful than that. But when a person repeatedly withdraws from the very places, people, ayat, and conversations that would threaten his falseness, he is training himself. He is teaching the heart what kind of truth is unwelcome.

[PAUSE]

You can watch this in ordinary religious life. A reminder begins moving toward your actual sin, and suddenly your phone matters urgently. A teacher nears the idol you protect, and you mentally leave before your body leaves. A spouse tells the truth about your pride, and you reorganize the conversation around their tone rather than your condition. A verse keeps surfacing in your life, and instead of dwelling under it, you rush toward easier passages that do not ask for death of self. Each instance may feel small. But repetition turns them into a direction.

That is why the verse ends not with "they do not know" but with **la yafqahun**. They do not deeply understand. Deep understanding is not intelligence. It is a moral and spiritual capacity to remain under truth long enough for truth to penetrate. The hypocrites fail there. They can physically hear a surah without fiqh because fiqh requires remaining long enough for the ayah to become command, mirror, judgment, and mercy.

[PAUSE]

There is a positive implication too. The opposite path also works by repetition. Staying in the room when the truth hurts trains the heart differently. Not escaping every exposing ayah. Not fleeing every uncomfortable reminder. Not abandoning the conversation the moment you feel seen. Presence under revelation becomes one of the ways Allah heals the heart's evasive reflexes. The believers in verse 124 stayed long enough to be increased and to rejoice. The hypocrites in verse 127 left too quickly and were turned away.

So the question this theme presses on us is very personal: where do you keep slipping away? Which truths make you glance sideways, search for cover, and look for a version of religion that asks less? The answer may reveal not only your current weakness but the very route by which your heart is being trained either toward Allah or away from Him.

## Closing Synthesis

### The Architecture of the Passage

These four ayahs are built as a double descent around revelation's arrival. Verse 124 opens with the descent of a surah and shows the believing heart ascending through increase and gladness. Verse 125 mirrors the same descent into the diseased heart, but now the movement is downward into filth upon filth and a death sealed in disbelief. Verse 126 interrupts with a yearly cycle of repeated testing, showing that life itself keeps offering corrective descent after descent. Then verse 127 returns to the descent of another surah, but this time the hypocrites' body language reveals practiced evasion, and the passage lands in the most terrifying mirror of all: they turn away outwardly and Allah turns their hearts away inwardly. The whole unit therefore moves from revealed increase, to revealed corruption, to repeated warning, to enacted departure.

The architecture teaches that revelation is not the variable. Descent stays descent. What changes is the heart, and then the body's response, and then the heart again.

### Questions to Carry

When Qur'an comes to you, what does it usually add first: hunger, humility, gladness, seriousness, resistance, boredom, or the wish for a softer version? Do not answer from theory. Answer from your most recent real encounters.

Which repeated trial in your life may already be Allah's yearly commentary on a part of your heart you still have not surrendered? If the same lesson keeps coming, perhaps the unanswered question is no longer "Why this again?" but "What repentance have I still refused?"

Where do you quietly leave the room when truth becomes expensive? The body may remain seated, but the heart may already have started scanning for exits long before the conversation ends.

What truths do you mostly prefer as topics rather than mirrors? A verse can remain academically interesting for years while never being allowed to expose the idol it was sent to strike in you.

Whose company helps you stay present under revelation instead of slipping away from it? Diseased hearts coordinate departure; healthy hearts need company that helps them remain long enough to be increased.

### One-Sentence Distillation

**At-Tawbah 9:124-127 shows that the same descending surah can raise a believing heart into faith and gladness or drive a diseased heart deeper into impurity, until repeated unread warnings and practiced evasions end with Allah turning away hearts that kept turning away from Him.**

### The Closing Invitation

The next time Qur'an reaches you, resist the urge to ask first whether it was eloquent, intense, or intellectually interesting. Ask what it is doing to you. Stay in the room a little longer where the truth hurts. Let the repeated lesson stop being a nuisance and become a message. If Allah has been sending the same warning through verses, people, and events, do not waste another year surviving it without tawbah. Choose to remain until revelation becomes increase, not evidence against you.

### Du'a

O Allah, make Your Book increase me in faith, not merely in familiarity.  
Do not let me hear revelation so often that I learn how to resist it politely.  
When You test me again and again, let the repetition become repentance, not numbness.  
Do not let my body slip away from truths my heart most needs.  
Keep me in the room of Your address until understanding becomes surrender.  
And turn my heart toward You before my own evasions teach it how to turn away.

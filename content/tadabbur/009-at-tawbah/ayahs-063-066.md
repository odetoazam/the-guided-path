# Do Not Say: We Were Only Joking

## Surah At-Tawbah (9:63-66)

[ayah:9:63] أَلَمْ يَعْلَمُوٓا۟ أَنَّهُۥ مَن يُحَادِدِ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ فَأَنَّ لَهُۥ نَارَ جَهَنَّمَ خَـٰلِدًا فِيهَا ۚ ذَٰلِكَ ٱلْخِزْىُ ٱلْعَظِيمُ

[ayah:9:64] يَحْذَرُ ٱلْمُنَـٰفِقُونَ أَن تُنَزَّلَ عَلَيْهِمْ سُورَةٌ تُنَبِّئُهُم بِمَا فِى قُلُوبِهِمْ ۚ قُلِ ٱسْتَهْزِءُوٓا۟ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ مُخْرِجٌ مَّا تَحْذَرُونَ

[ayah:9:65] وَلَئِن سَأَلْتَهُمْ لَيَقُولُنَّ إِنَّمَا كُنَّا نَخُوضُ وَنَلْعَبُ ۚ قُلْ أَبِٱللَّهِ وَءَايَـٰتِهِۦ وَرَسُولِهِۦ كُنتُمْ تَسْتَهْزِءُونَ

[ayah:9:66] لَا تَعْتَذِرُوا۟ قَدْ كَفَرْتُم بَعْدَ إِيمَـٰنِكُمْ ۚ إِن نَّعْفُ عَن طَآئِفَةٍ مِّنكُمْ نُعَذِّبْ طَآئِفَةًۢ بِأَنَّهُمْ كَانُوا۟ مُجْرِمِينَ

*A-lam ya'lamu annahu man yuhadidi Allaha wa rasulahu fa-anna lahu nara Jahannama khalidan fiha? Dhalika al-khizyu al-'azim.*

*Yahdharu al-munafiquna an tunazzala 'alayhim suratun tunabbi'uhum bima fi qulubihim. Quli istahzi'u inna Allaha mukhrijun ma tahdharun.*

*Wa la-in sa'altahum la-yaqulunna innama kunna nakhudu wa nal'ab. Qul a-bi-Allahi wa ayatihi wa rasulihi kuntum tastahzi'un.*

*La ta'tadhiru qad kafartum ba'da imanikum. In na'fu 'an ta'ifatin minkum nu'adhdhib ta'ifatan bi-annahum kanu mujrimin.*

**Do they not know that whoever opposes Allah and His Messenger, for him is the fire of Hell, abiding therein? That is the great disgrace. The hypocrites fear that a surah might be sent down about them, informing them of what is in their hearts. Say: go on mocking; Allah will surely bring out what you fear. And if you ask them, they will surely say, 'We were only talking idly and playing.' Say: was it Allah, His signs, and His Messenger that you were mocking? Do not make excuses. You have disbelieved after your faith. If We pardon a group of you, We will punish another group because they were criminals.**

## Introduction

These ayahs are among the Qur'an's most severe warnings against treating the sacred as material for entertainment.

The progression is chilling. First the surah reminds them of the stakes: whoever opposes Allah and His Messenger faces Hell and immense disgrace. Then it tells us that the hypocrites are already afraid. Afraid of what? That revelation will expose what is in their hearts. So the fear is there before the public incident. They already know there is something in them worth hiding.

[PAUSE]

Yet despite that fear, they still mock. And when confronted, they retreat into the most familiar excuse of unserious hearts: we were only talking, only joking, only passing time. The Qur'an refuses that refuge. Was it Allah, His signs, and His Messenger that you were mocking? Then do not make excuses. You have disbelieved after your faith.

This is a crucial lesson for every age that likes irony more than reverence. There are subjects a believer cannot safely turn into play. Once the sacred becomes a toy for self-display, group amusement, or cleverness, the tongue may reveal what the heart still wanted to keep hidden. These verses are not against joy. They are against desecration disguised as humor.

The hook of the passage is this: **At-Tawbah teaches that mockery of Allah, His signs, and His Messenger is not neutralized by saying "we were joking," because joking can be the very form by which hidden disbelief steps into speech.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Man yuhadidi Allaha wa rasulahu**: The Passage Opens with Boundary Language

Verse 63 says:

**man yuhadidi Allaha wa rasulahu**.

Whoever opposes Allah and His Messenger.

[PAUSE]

The verb carries the sense of setting oneself at a hostile boundary against them. This is not casual distance. It is adversarial positioning.

### 2. **Yahdharu al-munafiquna**: They Already Fear Exposure

Verse 64 begins:

**yahdharu al-munafiquna**.

The hypocrites fear.

[PAUSE]

This matters deeply. Fear is already present. They are not carefree innocents who accidentally crossed a line. Somewhere inside, they know revelation can expose them.

Then:

**tunabbi'uhum bima fi qulubihim**.

A surah informing them of what is in their hearts.

The real theater of the passage is the heart.

### 3. **Inna Allaha mukhrijun ma tahdharun**: Allah Brings Out What They Try to Conceal

Then:

**inna Allaha mukhrijun ma tahdharun**.

Allah will bring out what you fear.

[PAUSE]

That is one of the most searching statements in the surah. Revelation does not merely tell us new things. It also draws hidden things outward.

### 4. **Innama kunna nakhudu wa nal'ab**: The Standard Excuse of Irreverence

Verse 65 reports their defense:

**innama kunna nakhudu wa nal'ab**.

We were only talking idly and playing.

[PAUSE]

This is the excuse of unserious hearts. They want to remove moral weight by redefining the act as atmosphere rather than conviction.

### 5. **La ta'tadhiru qad kafartum ba'da imanikum**: The Excuse Is Rejected, and the Matter Is Named

Verse 66 answers:

**la ta'tadhiru**.

Do not make excuses.

Then:

**qad kafartum ba'da imanikum**.

You have disbelieved after your faith.

[PAUSE]

The Qur'an does not accept the retreat into "just kidding." Speech can cross real theological lines.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Humor Does Not Automatically Make a Sacred Violation Light

Modern people often assume that humor reduces seriousness. These ayahs challenge that assumption. Sometimes joking is precisely how seriousness is concealed from the speaker himself. The heart wants to taste irreverence without paying for it, so it borrows the costume of play.

[PAUSE]

The Qur'an tears that costume off.

### Theme 2: Revelation Exposes More Than It Announces

The fear in verse 64 is deeply instructive. Hypocrisy is haunted by exposure. A believer should let that line teach him to fear not only obvious sin, but hidden corrosion that revelation may one day bring into the light through the tongue, the face, or the circumstance.

[PAUSE]

Better to expose oneself to Allah in tawbah than to be exposed by Him in scandal.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs open by naming the stakes of opposing Allah and His Messenger, then reveal that the hypocrites already fear a surah exposing what is in their hearts. When their mockery is uncovered, they retreat into the language of idle talk and play, but Allah rejects the excuse, asks whether it was Allah, His signs, and His Messenger they were mocking, and names the act as kufr after faith.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: what have I allowed my tongue to become casual around? Do I ever use irony or humor to move near lines I would be embarrassed to cross seriously? And if revelation were to bring out what I most fear being exposed, would it find a heart that has already been honest with Allah in repentance?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of this unit lands here: **the sacred cannot be safely turned into play, because the claim "we were only joking" does not erase mockery when the heart has already let irreverence become speech.**

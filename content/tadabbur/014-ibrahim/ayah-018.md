# Their Deeds Are Like Ashes Struck by Wind on a Stormy Day

## Surah Ibrahim (14:18)

[ayah:14:18] مَّثَلُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ بِرَبِّهِمْ ۖ أَعْمَـٰلُهُمْ كَرَمَادٍ ٱشْتَدَّتْ بِهِ ٱلرِّيحُ فِى يَوْمٍ عَاصِفٍ ۖ لَّا يَقْدِرُونَ مِمَّا كَسَبُوا۟ عَلَىٰ شَىْءٍ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ هُوَ ٱلضَّلَـٰلُ ٱلْبَعِيدُ

*Mathalu alladhina kafaru bi-rabbihim a'maluhum ka-ramadin ishtaddat bihi al-rihu fi yawmin 'asif. La yaqdiruna mimma kasabu 'ala shay'. Dhalika huwa al-dalalu al-ba'id.*

**The example of those who disbelieved in their Lord is that their deeds are like ashes upon which the wind blows fiercely on a stormy day. They have no power over anything of what they earned. That is the far error.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

This ayah is about wasted effort.

Not laziness.

Not absence of action.

But a whole life of doing, building, achieving, gathering, earning, and producing that reaches the Day of Allah only to discover that what seemed substantial has the density of ash in a storm.

The hook of the ayah is this: **the tragedy of disbelief is not only punishment for evil, but the exposure of a life's apparent substance as spiritually unholdable once it is severed from the Lord for whom deeds were meant to stand.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **A'maluhum ka-ramad**: The Ayah Chooses Ash, Not Stone, Dust, or Leaves

The parable does not compare their deeds to something merely broken.

It compares them to:

**ramad**

ash.

[PAUSE]

Ash is what remains after something has already burned. It suggests prior activity, prior energy, prior material. There was once form here. There was once weight. There was once something that looked combustible, useful, or warm.

But now what remains is the faint residue after consumption.

This matters because the ayah is not talking about people who never acted at all. It is talking about deeds that looked like deeds, labor that looked productive, effort that could be pointed to, perhaps admired. Yet when separated from true belief in the Lord, the final remainder is ash.

### 2. **Ishtaddat bihi al-rih fi yawmin 'asif**: The Image Is Not Mild Wind, but Violent Exposure

Then the ash meets:

**al-rih**

the wind,

and not in an ordinary scene, but

**fi yawmin 'asif**

on a storming, violently windy day.

[PAUSE]

The verb **ishtaddat** intensifies the force. This is not a breeze passing over a quiet surface. The parable is built for removal. Ash is chosen because it cannot withstand such a day.

That gives the image its spiritual force. Some things appear stable only because the testing atmosphere has not yet arrived. They keep their shape in calm weather. But when the true wind comes, their reality is revealed.

The Day of Judgment is not only a courtroom. It is also a climate in which false substance cannot remain gathered.

### 3. **La yaqdiruna mimma kasabu 'ala shay'**: The Real Disaster Is Powerlessness Over What They Earned

The ayah then interprets its own parable:

**la yaqdiruna mimma kasabu 'ala shay'**

they have no power over anything of what they earned.

[PAUSE]

This is devastating. The deeds are described as something they **kasabu** - acquired, earned, worked for. There was exertion. Intention at some level. Ownership in the worldly sense.

But on that Day, they no longer possess any ability over what they once thought belonged to them. The labor has slipped out of their hands.

The issue is not merely that the deeds are few. It is that they are unusable. Unretrievable. Unholdable.

Then the ayah closes:

**dhalika huwa al-dalalu al-ba'id**

that is the far error.

Far, because the person did not merely miss the road by one step. He built an entire economy of effort away from its Lord.

[PAUSE]

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Many People Fear Failure, but the Ayah Warns About Futility

Worldly people often fear visible collapse.

We fear embarrassment, loss, humiliation, public failure, not being recognized, not finishing what we started. But this ayah warns of something deeper than failure: futility.

Failure still assumes the thing had weight and simply did not reach its target. Futility is worse. Futility means the thing never had the kind of substance you thought it did.

[PAUSE]

A person may pour years into work, identity, reputation, generosity, architecture, family legacy, ideological struggle, or self-made significance. From the ground, it may look impressive. But if it is severed from right relation to the Lord, the Day can reveal it not as a collapsed building, but as ash caught in wind.

This does not make action meaningless. It makes belief and orientation decisive.

### Theme 2: The Soul Needs More Than Activity; It Needs Weight Before Allah

The ayah does not attack action itself. It attacks action cut off from its Rabb.

That should sober believers too. We can become fascinated by visible output while neglecting the hidden matter that gives deeds weight before Allah: belief, sincerity, submission, truthfulness, and orientation to Him.

[PAUSE]

There is a version of religious life that also chases ash: doing what looks substantial in public while neglecting the inner root that would let the deed stand in divine weather.

So the question is not only: what are you doing?

It is: what will remain of what you are doing when the wind rises?

What in your life has the density to stay?

What would still matter if applause vanished, biography vanished, memory vanished, and only Allah's judgment remained?

[PAUSE]

## Closing Synthesis

The parable is simple and mercilessly clear.

There are deeds.

They look like something.

But their true composition is ash.

Then comes the day built for disclosure: fierce wind, storm atmosphere, no sheltering calm. And the people have no power left over anything they thought they earned.

That is the far error, because it is not only moral rebellion. It is existential misinvestment. A whole life can be spent manufacturing what will not remain.

### Questions to Carry

- Which parts of your life are built for appearance in calm weather but would not survive the wind of truth?
- Are you more concerned with doing much, or with doing what will actually remain before Allah?
- What current effort in your life most needs renewed sincerity before it becomes ash in a storm?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**This ayah warns that disbelief does not merely invite punishment; it can turn a lifetime of apparent achievement into ash that the first real wind of the Hereafter scatters beyond recovery.**

### Du'a

*Allahumma, do not let our deeds become ash. Give our actions truth, sincerity, and weight before You, and protect us from spending our lives earning what we will have no power to hold on the Day it matters most.*

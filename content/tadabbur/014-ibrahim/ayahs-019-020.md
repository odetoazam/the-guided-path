# He Created the Heavens and the Earth in Truth, and Replacing You Is Not Difficult for Him

## Surah Ibrahim (14:19-20)

[ayah:14:19] أَلَمْ تَرَ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ خَلَقَ ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ بِٱلْحَقِّ ۚ إِن يَشَأْ يُذْهِبْكُمْ وَيَأْتِ بِخَلْقٍ جَدِيدٍ

[ayah:14:20] وَمَا ذَٰلِكَ عَلَى ٱللَّهِ بِعَزِيزٍ

*A-lam tara anna Allaha khalaqa al-samawati wa al-arda bi-l-haqq. In yasha' yudhhibkum wa ya'ti bi-khalqin jadid.*

*Wa ma dhalika 'ala Allahi bi-'aziz.*

**Have you not seen that Allah created the heavens and the earth in truth? If He wills, He can remove you and bring a new creation. And that is not difficult for Allah.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

After showing the unbeliever's deeds as ash in violent wind, the surah does something bracing: it expands outward until the entire sky and earth are in view.

Why?

Because the human being overestimates his indispensability. We imagine our rebellion is a dramatic force, our presence is a fixed necessity, and the world somehow needs us to continue as we are. These ayahs correct that delusion by placing us inside a creation made in truth by Allah, then stating with complete ease that He can remove us and bring another creation.

The hook of the passage is this: **once the heavens and earth are seen as created in truth, the human being's imagined centrality collapses, and what remains is the sobering realization that Allah is under no strain at all in replacing a creation that refuses its Lord.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Khalaqa al-samawati wa al-arda bi-l-haqq**: Creation Is Not Random Material, but a Truth-Bearing Act

The ayah begins with:

**a-lam tara**

have you not seen?

[PAUSE]

This is not only physical sight. It is an invitation to notice what should already be evident.

Then:

**khalaqa al-samawati wa al-arda bi-l-haqq**

He created the heavens and the earth **in truth**.

The phrase **bi-l-haqq** matters immensely. Creation is not described as accidental, decorative, or morally empty. It is bound up with truth: right purpose, real meaning, just order, non-futility.

That means the world is not neutral ground on which disbelief and obedience are equally at home. The world itself was made in a way that testifies to reality. To rebel against Allah inside such a creation is not simply to break a rule. It is to live against the grain of what the world was made to mean.

### 2. **In yasha' yudhhibkum wa ya'ti bi-khalqin jadid**: The Human Being Is Not the Fixed Point

Then comes the sentence that cuts pride down to size:

**in yasha' yudhibhkum wa ya'ti bi-khalqin jadid**

If He wills, He can remove you and bring a new creation.

[PAUSE]

The verb **yudhibhkum** carries the force of taking away, making disappear, causing to go. Your continued presence is not self-secured.

Then:

**wa ya'ti**

and He brings.

The removal is followed immediately by replacement. The universe does not stall when a people are taken away. Allah is not left with an empty plan because a generation refused Him.

Then the phrase:

**bi-khalqin jadid**

a new creation.

This is part of what makes the verse so humbling. Not only can you be removed; you can be replaced. The moral universe is not built around your permanence.

### 3. **Wa ma dhalika 'ala Allahi bi-'aziz**: What Feels Immense to Us Is Not Hard for Him

Then the second ayah closes the thought:

**wa ma dhalika 'ala Allahi bi-'aziz**

and that is not difficult, not inaccessible, not beyond Him at all.

[PAUSE]

The word **'aziz** can carry senses of might, strength, and also something hard to attain or overcome. Here the negation tells you that replacing a people, overturning a creation-order, or bringing forth new creation is not some immense burden for Allah.

To us, replacing worlds feels unimaginable.

To Allah, it is not hard.

That is the scale correction these ayahs impose.

[PAUSE]

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The Ego Lives as Though Allah's Project Depends on It

This can show up in arrogant or subtle ways.

Sometimes a civilization lives as though its power is permanent.

Sometimes a sinner lives as though tomorrow is guaranteed.

Sometimes even a believer serves as though religion itself would be poorer without his contribution.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs sweep all of that away.

Allah created the heavens and the earth in truth. He was not lonely before you. He is not trapped by you now. And if He wills, He can remove you and bring another creation.

This is not meant to produce despair. It is meant to produce sobriety, humility, and relief from self-importance. You are invited into Allah's truth. You are not holding it up.

### Theme 2: Seeing Creation in Truth Changes How We Read Our Own Lives

If the heavens and earth are made **bi-l-haqq**, then your life is not floating in meaninglessness.

Your choices matter.

Your rebellion matters.

Your repentance matters.

Your response to Allah matters because you are living inside a creation already stamped with purpose and truth.

[PAUSE]

And that means the worst delusion is not merely sin. It is trivialization. Treating life as though it were casual, self-authored, and disposable while standing inside a world made with haqq.

These ayahs wake the servant up. They say: you are not in a toy world, and you are not its center. You are in Allah's truthful creation. So return to the truth before you learn your replaceability too late.

[PAUSE]

## Closing Synthesis

The surah has just shown deeds scattered like ash. Now it shows the doer himself in true scale.

The heavens and the earth were made in truth.

You were placed inside that truth, not above it.

And the One who made all this can remove you and bring another creation without difficulty at all.

That is not only a threat. It is a liberation from delusion. We do not need to inflate ourselves, secure ourselves, or imagine ourselves indispensable. We need to align ourselves with the haqq in which we were placed.

### Questions to Carry

- Where do you still live as though your continued presence is guaranteed rather than given?
- What would change if you felt, deeply, that Allah's religion and world do not depend on your ego?
- In what part of your life are you resisting the truth while standing inside a creation already made by it and for it?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs humble the servant by placing him inside a world created in truth and reminding him that the Lord who made the heavens and the earth can replace a refusing creation without effort at all.**

### Du'a

*Allahumma, do not let us live with the arrogance of imagined permanence. Show us the truth of Your creation, make us small in the right way before Your greatness, and keep us among those who align with the haqq before we are removed from the chance to do so.*

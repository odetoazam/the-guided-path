# For Allah Is the Conclusive Proof

## Surah Al-Anam (6:148-150)

[ayah:6:148] سَيَقُولُ ٱلَّذِينَ أَشْرَكُوا۟ لَوْ شَآءَ ٱللَّهُ مَآ أَشْرَكْنَا وَلَآ ءَابَآؤُنَا وَلَا حَرَّمْنَا مِن شَىْءٍ ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ كَذَّبَ ٱلَّذِينَ مِن قَبْلِهِمْ حَتَّىٰ ذَاقُوا۟ بَأْسَنَا ۗ قُلْ هَلْ عِندَكُم مِّنْ عِلْمٍ فَتُخْرِجُوهُ لَنَآ ۖ إِن تَتَّبِعُونَ إِلَّا ٱلظَّنَّ وَإِنْ أَنتُمْ إِلَّا تَخْرُصُونَ

**Those who associated others with Allah will say, "If Allah had willed, we would not have associated, nor would our fathers, nor would we have forbidden anything." Thus did those before them deny until they tasted Our punishment. Say, "Do you have any knowledge, then bring it out for us. You follow nothing but assumption, and you do nothing but lie." ## Introduction

There is a way people use true theology to hide from moral responsibility.

They take something real about Allah - His will, His decree, His comprehensive power - and then twist it into an excuse for their own rebellion. Instead of trembling before the fact that nothing happens outside His will, they weaponize that truth against His commands. If He did not want us to do this, they say, we would not have done it. So our shirk, our fathers' shirk, and even our invented prohibitions become dressed up as destiny rather than confessed as disobedience.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs cut through that maneuver with remarkable sharpness. First, the surah predicts the excuse before it is spoken. Then it answers by asking a devastating question: do you actually have any knowledge for this claim? If so, produce it. Otherwise you are following nothing but *zann* and *khars* - conjecture and fabrication. Then the matter is settled in principle: the conclusive proof belongs to Allah alone. Finally, the surah strips false religious testimony of all authority. Bring your witnesses. If they testify that Allah forbade this, do not join them. Their witness is not knowledge. It is desire speaking in sacred language.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **Allah's decree can never be used to cancel human accountability, and any religious claim not grounded in divine proof remains only desire, conjecture, and false witness no matter how confidently it is spoken.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Surah Anticipates the Excuse Before It Is Offered

The opening is striking:

*Sa-yaqulu alladhina ashraku...*

Those who associated others with Allah will say...

[PAUSE]

The Qur'an names the excuse before it arrives. There is something spiritually exposing about that. Human evasions often feel clever from inside the self, but revelation meets them already knowing their shape.

Then the excuse:

*Law sha'a Allahu ma ashrakna wa la aba'una wa la harramna min shay'.*

If Allah had willed, we would not have committed shirk, nor our fathers, nor would we have forbidden anything.

Notice how they bundle multiple wrongs together under one appeal to decree: shirk, inherited tradition, and fabricated law. The point is not sincere theological inquiry. The point is self-exoneration.

Then the surah replies historically:

*Kadhalika kadhdhaba alladhina min qablihim hatta dhaqu ba'sana.*

Thus did those before them deny until they tasted Our might.

So this is not a new sophistication. It is an old pattern of denial.

### 2. The Qur'an Forces the Argument Back Onto Knowledge

Then comes the decisive question:

*Qul hal 'indakum min 'ilmin fa-tukhrijuhu lana.*

Say: do you have any knowledge? Then bring it out for us.

[PAUSE]

The issue is moved immediately from rhetoric to epistemology. Not: do you have a mood, an inherited narrative, a plausible-sounding formula, or a philosophical shield. Do you have *'ilm*?

Then the verdict:

*In tattabi'una illa al-zann wa in antum illa takhrusun.*

You follow nothing but assumption, and you do nothing but fabricate.

*Zann* here is not a humble probable judgment where certainty is unavailable. It is conjecture masquerading as authority. And *khars* carries the sense of guessing, fabricating, speaking without grounded measure. The surah is unmasking a whole style of religious speech that sounds bold but has no revealed basis.

### 3. Allah's Proof Reaches Its Mark

Then:

*Qul fa-lillahi al-hujjatu al-balighah.*

Say: for Allah belongs the conclusive proof.

[PAUSE]

The phrase is powerful. *Hujjah* is proof, argument, decisive case. *Balighah* is that which reaches, arrives, penetrates fully. Allah's proof is not partial or stranded. It reaches its target.

Then:

*Fa-law sha'a la-hadakum ajma'in.*

Had He willed, He would have guided you all.

This preserves divine sovereignty without handing the sinner an excuse. Allah's will is true, but the servant is still answerable to the commands, proofs, warnings, and choices placed before him. The ayah does not let qadar be turned into a hiding place from taklif.

### 4. False Witness Is Just Desire in Formal Dress

Then the surah says:

*Qul halumma shuhada'akum alladhina yashhaduna anna Allaha harrama hadha.*

Bring your witnesses who testify that Allah forbade this.

[PAUSE]

It is almost courtroom language now. Produce the testimony. Name the evidence. Present the witnesses for your claim that Allah spoke this prohibition.

Then comes a remarkable instruction:

*Fa-in shahidu fala tashhad ma'ahum.*

If they testify, do not testify with them.

Even if they line up, even if they speak in unison, even if they produce social consensus, repeated assertion does not become truth.

Then the inner source of their false witness is named:

*Wa la tattabi' ahwa'a alladhina kadhdhabu bi-ayatina...*

Do not follow the desires of those who denied Our signs...

At the root of the counterfeit witness is *hawa* - desire. That is why the passage ends with denial of the Hereafter and setting equals beside the Lord. The epistemic corruption is tied to a deeper devotional corruption.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Appeals to Destiny Often Hide a Refusal to Repent

These ayahs expose a disease that is still very alive. When confronted with wrongdoing, people sometimes appeal to Allah's decree not to deepen surrender, but to escape confession. If Allah did not want this, they imply, it would not have happened. So why blame me?

[PAUSE]

But the Qur'an does not allow that move. Allah's will encompasses all things, yes. Yet He still sends commands, warnings, messengers, signs, and proofs. The moral question for the servant is never whether something could happen outside Allah's will. It is whether he obeyed what Allah commanded and responded honestly when he disobeyed.

The people condemned here are not being rebuked for affirming qadar. They are being rebuked for using qadar as a shield against repentance and as a justification for inherited falsehood.

### Theme 2: Religious Confidence Without Proof Is Spiritually Dangerous

The repeated demand for knowledge is deeply important.

People can be very certain about things Allah never said. Communities can generate witnesses, experts, elders, traditions, and slogans. But the Qur'an keeps asking the same question: where is the proof from Allah? If it is not there, then social reinforcement cannot transform desire into revelation.

[PAUSE]

That should humble us whenever we speak strongly in Allah's name. It should also steady us when false confidence surrounds us. We are not required to join a testimony just because many voices repeat it. If a claim about Allah lacks proof, we must not witness with it.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs strip false religion down to its inner machinery.

First comes the excuse: if Allah had willed, we would not have done this. Then comes the exposure: you have no knowledge, only conjecture and fabrication. Then comes the principle: the conclusive proof belongs to Allah. Then comes the command: do not stand with false witnesses, and do not follow desires that deny the signs and the Hereafter.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: when I am confronted by a truth I do not want, do I respond by seeking Allah's proof, or by reaching for a clever excuse that protects my desire?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **the servant is not saved by sounding theological while resisting repentance; he is saved by yielding to Allah's proof, refusing false witness, and not letting desire speak in the language of destiny.**

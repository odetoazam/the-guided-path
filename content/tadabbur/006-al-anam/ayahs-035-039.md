# Only the Truly Living Respond

## Surah Al-Anam (6:35-39)

[ayah:6:35] وَإِن كَانَ كَبُرَ عَلَيْكَ إِعْرَاضُهُمْ فَإِنِ ٱسْتَطَعْتَ أَن تَبْتَغِىَ نَفَقًا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ أَوْ سُلَّمًا فِى ٱلسَّمَآءِ فَتَأْتِيَهُم بِـَٔايَةٍ ۚ وَلَوْ شَآءَ ٱللَّهُ لَجَمَعَهُمْ عَلَى ٱلْهُدَىٰ ۚ فَلَا تَكُونَنَّ مِنَ ٱلْجَـٰهِلِينَ

## Introduction

There is a kind of sorrow that tempts a person into thinking that if only he could do more, push harder, show one more proof, find one more way in, then hearts would finally open.

If only I had the perfect argument.
If only I had the unforgettable sign.
If only I could reach them from some impossible angle.
If only I could make truth undeniable enough.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs meet that temptation directly.

The Prophet is told that if their turning away weighs heavily on him, then let the imagination run to the furthest edges: a tunnel through the earth, a ladder into the sky, another sign brought from beyond ordinary reach. But even that is not where the matter finally rests. If Allah had willed, He would have gathered them all upon guidance. Then the surah tells us who actually responds: those who truly hear. The dead are a different matter. They will be raised by Allah. The demand for more signs returns, but again the problem is not divine inability. It is human ignorance. Then, unexpectedly, the horizon widens to the animals and birds, each as communities under divine record and eventual gathering. Finally the deniers are pictured not as sharp skeptics but as deaf and mute in layered darkness.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **guidance does not come from piling up spectacles before a refusing heart, because only the inwardly living can truly answer, while the whole creation already stands inside Allah's knowledge, ordering, and final gathering.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Ayah Lets the Pain Speak, Then Corrects It

The passage opens:

*Wa in kana kabura 'alayka i'raduhum...*

If their turning away feels immense upon you.

[PAUSE]

The verb *kabura* conveys heaviness, magnitude, something becoming great in the sense of hard to bear. And *i'rad* is not simple ignorance. It is turning away, facing aside, refusing engagement. So the pain named here is the pain of seeing people deliberately pivot away from what would save them.

Then the ayah gives an almost impossible image:

*Fa-in استطعت an tabtaghiya nafaqan fi al-ardi aw sullaman fi al-sama'.*

If you can seek a tunnel into the earth or a ladder into the sky.

This is the language of exhausting every imaginable path. A *nafaq* is a tunnel, a hidden passage driven downward and through. A *sullam* is a ladder or means of ascent upward. Down into the earth, up into the sky: the whole vertical field of possibility is being named.

And yet the ayah ends by settling the matter where it truly belongs:

*Wa law sha'a Allahu la-jama'ahum 'ala al-huda.*

If Allah had willed, He would have gathered them upon guidance.

That is a profound corrective for anyone carrying da'wah, truth, parenting, teaching, or even concern for a loved one. We work, plead, clarify, and serve. But we do not own the secret of opened hearts.

Then the warning:

*Fa-la takunanna mina al-jahilin.*

Do not be among the ignorant.

Ignorance here is not lack of data. It is forgetting where guidance actually comes from and slipping into the illusion that enough human effort can independently force it open.

### 2. Only Those Who Truly Hear Can Respond

Then comes one of the sharpest lines in the surah:

*Innama yastajibu alladhina yasma'un.*

Only those who hear respond.

[PAUSE]

The restriction particle *innama* tightens the meaning. Not everyone exposed to truth answers it. Not everyone in the room is really hearing. The hearing meant here is not bare sound reception. The previous ayahs have already shown us people who listened outwardly while remaining blocked inwardly. Here the contrast becomes decisive: the people who answer are the ones who hear in a living way.

Then the other side is named:

*Wa al-mawta yab'athuhumu Allah.*

As for the dead, Allah will raise them.

This is breathtaking. The passage moves from hearing to death, showing that the deepest issue is not informational but vital. Some people are not merely unconvinced; they are spiritually deadened. Their true awakening belongs to resurrection and judgment if they refuse life now.

And then:

*Thumma ilayhi yurja'un.*

Then to Him they are returned.

The sentence keeps folding everything back to Allah. Response, resurrection, return.

### 3. The Problem Was Never Allah's Ability to Send a Sign

The objection repeats:

*Lawla nuzzila 'alayhi ayatun min rabbihi.*

Why was not a sign sent down to him from his Lord?

[PAUSE]

The Qur'an does not answer by saying signs are impossible. Quite the opposite:

*Qul inna Allaha qadirun 'ala an yunazzila ayah.*

Say: Allah is fully able to send down a sign.

Divine power is not the issue. The issue is named at the end:

*Wa lakinna aktharahum la ya'lamun.*

But most of them do not know.

That ignorance includes not knowing what signs actually do, what happens when overwhelming signs are sent and still denied, and where the real problem of refusal lives. A refusing heart often imagines that one more miracle would settle everything, when in truth it may only intensify accountability.

### 4. The Entire Created Field Already Lives Under Divine Ordering

Then the surah makes a stunning move:

*Wa ma min dabbatin fi al-ardi wa la ta'irin yatiru bi-janahayhi illa umamun amthalukum.*

There is no creature crawling on the earth, nor bird flying with its two wings, except that they are communities like you.

[PAUSE]

This widens the human eye beyond its self-importance. The human being is not the only organized creature under Allah's care. Life teems with ordered communities, each under divine knowledge, provision, pattern, and destiny.

And then:

*Ma farratna fi al-kitabi min shay'.*

We have not neglected anything in the Record.

This statement is awe-inducing and humbling. Nothing in the ordering, registration, and encompassing knowledge of Allah is forgotten, omitted, or dropped. The deniers keep asking for extra proofs as though the cosmos were somehow unattended. The ayah answers by showing a creation already densely held inside divine care and account.

Then the ending of the ayah:

*Thumma ila rabbihim yuhsharun.*

Then to their Lord they will be gathered.

Even the creatures are gathered. The whole world is moving toward return.

### 5. The Deniers Are Trapped in Layered Darkness

Then the moral image closes the passage:

*Wa alladhina kadhdhabu bi-ayatina summun wa bukmun fi al-zulumat.*

Those who deny Our signs are deaf and dumb in darknesses.

[PAUSE]

This is not merely one sensory deficiency. It is total communicative collapse inside plural darknesses.

*Summ* - deaf.
*Bukm* - mute.
*Fi al-zulumat* - in darknesses, not just one darkness.

The plural matters. Darkness upon darkness. Confusion, desire, pride, inherited habit, fear of change, social pressure, arrogance before revelation - all the layered obscurities by which the soul loses its ability to hear clearly, speak truthfully, and walk straight.

Then the ayah ends where tawhid requires it to end:

*Man yasha'i Allahu yudlilhu wa man yasha' yaj'alhu 'ala siratin mustaqim.*

Whom Allah wills, He leaves astray; and whom He wills, He places on a straight path.

This does not cancel responsibility. It cancels self-sovereignty. Guidance is not a human possession. It is a gift placed by Allah upon the one He wills, and misguidance is a terrifying abandonment when a soul insists on darkness.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Love for People's Guidance Must Not Turn into Illusions of Control

These ayahs dignify grief but dismantle control.

You are allowed to care deeply.
You are allowed to feel the heaviness of someone's turning away.
You are allowed to wish there were some path, some word, some opening that would break through.

[PAUSE]

But you are not allowed to forget who owns guidance.

That is one of the most painful maturities in spiritual life. We can love, serve, explain, pray, and endure. But we cannot tunnel our way into another person's heart, and we cannot climb a ladder into the sky to force faith down into him. When the soul forgets that, concern mutates into a subtle claim of control. The ayah calls that ignorance.

So the believer has to learn a purified kind of concern: intense in mercy, tireless in effort, but free of the illusion that success belongs to his own ability to engineer openings.

### Theme 2: The Real Division Is Not Between the Informed and the Uninformed, but Between the Living and the Dead

*Innama yastajibu alladhina yasma'un.*

Only those who hear respond.

[PAUSE]

This ayah is devastating because it tells us that the deepest human crisis is not lack of exposure but lack of life. Many people have information. Many people have access. Many people can repeat the vocabulary of truth. But not all of them are alive enough inwardly to answer.

That should make us very serious about our own hearing. We often assume that because we are near the Qur'an, we must be among its hearers. But the surah has already shown how possible it is to listen without receiving, argue without yielding, and stand near truth while drifting into death.

The question is not only whether I heard the reminder. It is whether anything in me was still alive enough to respond.

### Theme 3: The Whole Creation Is Already a Sign, and Darkness Is a Moral Condition

The move to animals and birds is not random. It breaks the illusion that signs are scarce.

The world is already thick with order.
It is already populated by communities.
It is already carried inside divine record.
It is already moving toward gathering.

[PAUSE]

If someone still keeps demanding one more decisive sign while standing inside that world, the issue is not absence of signs. The issue is darkness.

And darkness here is not simply intellectual limitation. It is moral-spiritual enclosure. A person can become deaf to what should shake him, mute before the truth he should confess, and trapped in layers of obscurity he helped build through repeated refusal.

That is why the end of the passage returns everything to Allah's will. Guidance is light. And light is not extracted by force from darkness. It is granted.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs move from grief to clarity.

The Prophet is told that if their turning away feels unbearably heavy, even the most dramatic imagined search for a new sign would not alter the fact that guidance belongs to Allah.
Then the surah defines the true responders as those who really hear.
The spiritually dead are left to resurrection and return.
The demand for more signs is answered by affirming Allah's power and exposing human ignorance.
The field of vision widens to creatures and birds as communities held inside divine record and eventual gathering.
And the deniers are finally shown as deaf and dumb in layered darkness, with straight guidance only where Allah places it.

[PAUSE]

That is a hard mercy. It relieves the soul of false control, but only by making it face how serious the matter of life and death truly is. The right response is neither passivity nor panic. It is patient labor joined to total dependence, deep respect for the mystery of guidance, and humble fear for our own hearing. We do not ask only whether others have seen enough signs. We ask whether we ourselves are alive enough to answer the signs already surrounding us.

### Questions to Carry

1. Where has sincere concern in me started to slip into the illusion that I can control another person's guidance?
2. Am I actually hearing the Qur'an in a way that produces response, or only staying near it outwardly?
3. What parts of creation have I trained myself to pass by without reading them as communities and signs under Allah's care?
4. Which layered darknesses in my own life are making it harder to hear clearly, speak honestly, and walk straight?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs teach that guidance is not forced open by multiplying spectacles, because only the inwardly living truly respond, while the whole creation already testifies under Allah's care and final gathering.**

### Du'a

Allahumma, keep our concern for others sincere without letting it turn into false claims of control. Make us among those who truly hear and respond while there is still life in us. Remove the layered darknesses from our hearts, show us Your signs in the whole creation around us, and place us firmly upon the straight path by Your mercy. Amin.

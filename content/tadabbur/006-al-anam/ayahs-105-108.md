# Follow What Was Revealed to You

## Surah Al-Anam (6:105-108)

[ayah:6:105] وَكَذَٰلِكَ نُصَرِّفُ ٱلْـَٔايَـٰتِ وَلِيَقُولُوا۟ دَرَسْتَ وَلِنُبَيِّنَهُۥ لِقَوْمٍ يَعْلَمُونَ

## Introduction

One of the hardest parts of carrying truth is learning that clarity does not guarantee fairness.

Sometimes the signs are plain, the argument is clean, the heart of the message is merciful, and still the response comes back twisted. People mislabel it, mock it, reduce it, weaponize it, or refuse it altogether. The temptation then is to start carrying burdens Allah did not assign us: to force hearts open, to control reactions, to answer every insult with a heavier insult, to treat escalation as strength.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs retrain the bearer of revelation. Allah says that He Himself diversifies the signs, yet some will still answer, *darasta* - "you've merely studied this elsewhere." The Prophet is then told what remains his real work: follow what has been revealed to you. Turn away from the mushrikin in the right way. Remember that their shirk did not escape Allah's will. And do not say true things in a way that predictably becomes fuel for greater falsehood. The da'wah ethic here is not compromise. It is disciplined obedience under divine sovereignty.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **faithful witness is not measured by controlling people's reactions, but by staying bound to revelation while refusing forms of speech that only deepen blindness and hostility.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Signs Are Diversified, and Still Some Will Twist Them

The passage begins:

*Wa kadhalika nusarrifu al-ayati.*

Thus We diversify, turn, vary, and set forth the signs in different ways.

[PAUSE]

The verb *nusarrifu* from the root *s-r-f* suggests turning something around, presenting it from multiple angles, distributing it in varied forms. The Qur'an is not monotonous. Allah rotates proofs, scenes, arguments, warnings, stories, inner and outer signs so that the truth becomes difficult to miss.

Yet then comes the sobering line:

*Wa li-yaqulu darasta.*

So that they may say, "You have studied."

The response to clarified signs is not always surrender. Sometimes it is cynical reframing. The truth is reclassified as borrowed, rehearsed, second-hand, or merely humanly sourced so that its claim can be dismissed without being faced.

Then the ayah gives the other side:

*Wa li-nubayyinahu li-qawmin ya'lamun.*

And so that We may make it clear for a people who know.

The same diversified signs generate opposite responses depending on the heart. One group looks for a label to escape the message. Another receives clarification.

### 2. The Prophet Is Told to Stay With Revelation, Not With the Crowd's Demands

Then comes the command:

*Ittabi' ma uhiya ilayka min rabbik.*

Follow what has been revealed to you from your Lord.

[PAUSE]

This is the center. Not: manage impressions. Not: chase acceptance. Not: soften truth until it costs nothing. Follow what was revealed.

Then the creed is restated immediately:

*La ilaha illa huwa.*

There is no god except Him.

Meaning: the command to follow revelation stands inside tawhid itself. If there is no god but Him, then there is no rival authority whose approval can overrule what He revealed.

Then:

*Wa a'rid 'ani al-mushrikin.*

Turn away from the polytheists.

This is not abandonment of da'wah. It is refusal of certain futile entanglements. Sometimes fidelity requires disengaging from argument patterns that only recycle resistance.

### 3. You Are Not Assigned Control Over Hearts

Then Allah relieves the Messenger:

*Wa law sha'a Allahu ma ashraku.*

Had Allah willed, they would not have associated others with Him.

[PAUSE]

This does not excuse shirk. It re-situates the Prophet's burden. Their disbelief is not evidence that the message failed or that Allah lost control of events. It unfolds under divine sovereignty.

Then the limits are named:

*Wa ma ja'alnaka 'alayhim hafizan. Wa ma anta 'alayhim bi-wakil.*

We did not make you over them a keeper, nor are you over them a disposer of affairs.

*Hafiz* suggests guardship, preserving, watching over outcomes. *Wakil* suggests management, disposal of affairs, delegated control. The Prophet is not assigned either role over their hearts. He is responsible for conveyance, not coercion.

That distinction is mercy for anyone who tries to carry truth today. We are not asked to generate guidance in people. We are asked to be faithful in delivery.

### 4. Not Every True Statement Is Wise in Every Form

Then comes one of the most important adab principles in da'wah:

*Wa la tasubbu alladhina yad'una min duni Allah.*

Do not insult those they call upon besides Allah.

[PAUSE]

The issue is not that their objects of worship deserve reverence. They do not. The issue is consequence:

*Fa-yasubbu Allaha 'adwan bi-ghayri 'ilm.*

Lest they insult Allah in hostility without knowledge.

The Qur'an is teaching us that speech is judged not only by whether its content is true, but by whether its form and timing predictably trigger a greater violation.

Then Allah explains a deeper human pattern:

*Kadhalika zayyanna li-kulli ummatin 'amalahum.*

Thus We have made pleasing to every community their deed.

People are not neutral about what they inherited and normalized. They are emotionally invested in it. What they practice becomes beautified in their sight.

Then the closing horizon:

*Thumma ila rabbihim marji'uhum fa-yunabbi'uhum bima kanu ya'malun.*

Then to their Lord is their return, and He will inform them of what they used to do.

The final reckoning belongs to Allah, not to the heat of the argument.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Clarification Does Not Remove the Trial of Response

We often imagine that if we could only explain things better, say them more clearly, or present the evidence from just the right angle, people would finally respond fairly. These ayahs complicate that hope.

Allah Himself says He diversifies the signs, and still some people answer with reduction: *darasta*. That is important because it protects us from a subtle despair. When people respond unfairly to truth, we may assume either that the truth was unclear or that we personally failed. Sometimes neither is true. Sometimes the heart simply refuses what would cost it too much to admit.

[PAUSE]

This does not make explanation unimportant. The ayah still says: *wa li-nubayyinahu li-qawmin ya'lamun*. Clarification matters deeply. But it also teaches us that explanation does not abolish the moral drama of response. Signs reveal people as much as they inform them.

### Theme 2: Wisdom in Speech Includes Refusing Predictable Escalation

The prohibition against insulting false gods is one of the clearest proofs that Islamic witness is not built on raw reaction. There are truths you can say in ways that become strategically foolish, spiritually corrosive, and socially disastrous.

The Qur'an is not embarrassed by truth. It is teaching mastery of delivery.

[PAUSE]

In our time this matters constantly. It is easy to confuse bluntness with courage, escalation with sincerity, humiliation of the other side with defense of the deen. But if the foreseeable result is that Allah is cursed more, hearts harden further, and the conversation becomes less reachable, then the form of speech has betrayed the purpose of truth.

That requires maturity. Not every provocation must be answered at the same temperature at which it was delivered. Sometimes the stronger act is restraint governed by what actually serves Allah's cause.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs leave the believer with a demanding but freeing ethic.

Allah diversifies the signs. Some will still twist them. Your task is not to control that. Your task is to follow what was revealed, remember who truly governs hearts, and refuse forms of speech that turn witness into needless escalation.

[PAUSE]

So the bearer of revelation walks a narrow but beautiful line: uncompromising in source, restrained in ego, clear in creed, wise in delivery, and unburdened by outcomes Allah never assigned him to manage.

Perhaps one way to sit with this passage is to ask: where have I confused faithfulness with winning, or courage with provocation, when Allah may be asking me instead for steadier obedience and wiser speech?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **clarity belongs to revelation, guidance belongs to Allah, accountability belongs to the Hereafter, and our work in between is to keep following what was revealed with enough wisdom that truth does not become fuel for deeper ignorance.**

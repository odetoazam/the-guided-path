# They Benefited From One Another

## Surah Al-Anam (6:128-132)

## Introduction

Some relationships feel mutually beneficial right up until the Day they are exposed.

One side gains influence, appetite, permission, thrill, or power. The other side gains followers, instruments, bodies, voices, or willing agents. Both call it advantage. Both feel they got something. But the Qur'an shows that some forms of mutual enjoyment are really systems of shared ruin whose full meaning only appears when Allah gathers everyone at once.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs move through that unveiling. The jinn are addressed for having multiplied themselves through human beings. Human allies admit that they benefited from one another. Then the surah immediately gives the governing principle: Allah allies some wrongdoers with others because of what they used to earn. After that comes the great justice question. Were they warned? Did messengers come? Did verses get recited? Yes. They testify against themselves. The dunya had deceived them. And the passage closes by naming the moral architecture beneath all of it: Allah does not destroy communities while they are unwarned, and every being receives degrees according to what it actually did.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **evil alliances survive by making corruption feel useful, but on the Day of gathering every false benefit will be exposed as earned ruin under a judgment that was preceded by warning, witnessed by the self, and measured with total precision.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Day of Gathering Reveals What Mutual Enjoyment Was Hiding

The passage opens:

*Wa yawma yahshuruhum jami'an...*

And on the Day when He gathers them all...

[PAUSE]

*Hashr* is gathering for reckoning, drawing scattered beings into one unavoidable assembly. What was hidden across different lives, eras, whispers, and actions is now brought into a single scene.

Then the address:

*Ya ma'shara al-jinni qad istakthartum mina al-insi.*

O company of jinn, you have made much of humankind.

The expression suggests multiplication, taking many, expanding one's reach through them. The jinn are not being portrayed here as isolated tempters of a few individuals. The image is broader: a harvested human field.

Then the human allies answer:

*Rabbana istamta'a ba'duna bi-ba'din.*

Our Lord, some of us benefited from others.

[PAUSE]

That line is chilling because it is so honest. They do not deny the relationship. They describe it in terms of utility and enjoyment. Corruption often persists because both sides feel compensated.

Then:

*Wa balaghna ajalana alladhi ajjalta lana.*

We have reached the term You appointed for us.

The enjoyment had a clock on it. It always does.

### 2. Wrongdoers Are Given to One Another

Then comes the governing principle:

*Wa kadhalika nuwalli ba'da al-zalimina ba'dan bima kanu yaksibun.*

Thus We make some of the wrongdoers allies of others because of what they used to earn.

[PAUSE]

The root *w-l-y* here is sobering. Alliance itself can be a judgment. Companions, patrons, protectors, and systems can become mirrors of what a people have earned.

The ayah does not present corrupt alliances as random accidents. They correspond to acquisition: *bima kanu yaksibun*.

What we earn inwardly and outwardly shapes what we are handed relationally.

### 3. The Self Ultimately Testifies

Then the address widens:

*Ya ma'shara al-jinni wa al-insi alam ya'tikum rusulun minkum...*

O company of jinn and humankind, did messengers not come to you from among you...?

They recited the verses. They warned of this Day. So the question is not whether there was access to truth. The question is what was done with it.

Then:

*Qalu shahidna 'ala anfusina.*

They say: we testify against ourselves.

[PAUSE]

That is one of the heaviest moments in the Qur'an. No external prosecutor is needed to invent a case. The self knows.

Then the explanation:

*Wa gharrathumu al-hayatu al-dunya.*

The worldly life deluded them.

Its nearness, pleasures, urgencies, rewards, and visible textures distorted proportion until the warning of the Day felt unreal.

### 4. Allah Does Not Destroy the Unwarned

Then comes a principle of divine justice:

*Dhalika an lam yakun rabbuka muhlika al-qura bi-zulmin wa ahluha ghafilun.*

That is because your Lord would not destroy the towns unjustly while their people were unaware.

[PAUSE]

The ayah is remarkable. Punishment does not descend out of divine arbitrariness. Warning precedes reckoning. Clarification precedes consequence.

Allah is not unjust, and ghaflah does not count as informed defiance until the truth has actually come.

### 5. Degrees Correspond to Deeds

Then the passage closes:

*Wa li-kullin darajatun mimma 'amilu.*

For each are degrees according to what they did.

Not vague outcomes. Not flattened judgment. Degrees.

Then:

*Wa ma rabbuka bi-ghafilin 'amma ya'malun.*

Your Lord is not unaware of what they do.

Nothing slips past Him: not the public scheme, not the private pact, not the inner consent, not the outward action.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Some of the Most Dangerous Relationships Are Built on Reciprocal Use

The confession *istamta'a ba'duna bi-ba'din* is spiritually diagnostic.

Not every alliance is rooted in love of truth or mutual elevation. Some are rooted in exchange: you feed my appetite, I reinforce your rebellion; you give me status, I give you permission; you normalize my darkness, I amplify yours. Each side names the arrangement as beneficial while it is active.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs force us to examine whether some relationships, systems, and loyalties in our lives are held together mainly by reciprocal corruption. The Qur'an is telling us that utility is not innocence. Two parties can both feel served and still both be moving toward Fire.

### Theme 2: Divine Justice Includes Warning, Witness, and Precision

The closing movement is deeply important because it protects us from imagining Allah's judgment as impulsive.

Messengers came. Verses were recited. Warning was given. The self testifies. Communities are not destroyed in blind neglect while unaware. Then, finally, degrees correspond to deeds.

[PAUSE]

This means the final judgment is not only severe. It is morally intelligible. It is preceded by communication, clarified by testimony, and calibrated with perfect awareness.

For the believer, that should produce two things at once: fear, because nothing is lost; and trust, because nothing is mishandled.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs expose a whole world of corrupt companionship and then place it under a perfectly just sky.

Jinn and human beings can become partners in ruin. Wrongdoers can be given to one another. The dunya can make destructive arrangements feel profitable. But warning comes. The self testifies. Allah does not punish blindly. And each one receives his degree according to what he actually did.

[PAUSE]

Perhaps one way to sit with this passage is to ask: where in my life have I mislabeled mutual corruption as benefit simply because it gave something to both sides in the short term?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **evil companionship is judged not by the temporary benefits it circulates, but by the warning it ignored, the self-testimony it will finally force, and the exact degree of ruin it truly earned under Allah's unerring justice.**

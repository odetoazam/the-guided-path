# When Mocked Truth Returns as News

## Surah Al-Anam (6:5-6)

[ayah:6:5] فَقَدْ كَذَّبُوا۟ بِٱلْحَقِّ لَمَّا جَآءَهُمْ ۖ فَسَوْفَ يَأْتِيهِمْ أَنۢبَـٰٓؤُا۟ مَا كَانُوا۟ بِهِۦ يَسْتَهْزِءُونَ

[ayah:6:6] أَلَمْ يَرَوْا۟ كَمْ أَهْلَكْنَا مِن قَبْلِهِم مِّن قَرْنٍ مَّكَّنَّـٰهُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مَا لَمْ نُمَكِّن لَّكُمْ وَأَرْسَلْنَا ٱلسَّمَآءَ عَلَيْهِم مِّدْرَارًا وَجَعَلْنَا ٱلْأَنْهَـٰرَ تَجْرِى مِن تَحْتِهِمْ فَأَهْلَكْنَـٰهُم بِذُنُوبِهِمْ وَأَنشَأْنَا مِنۢ بَعْدِهِمْ قَرْنًا ءَاخَرِينَ

*Faqad kadhdhabu bi-al-haqqi lamma ja'ahum. Fa-sawfa ya'tihim anba'u ma kanu bihi yastahzi'un.*

*A-lam yaraw kam ahlakna min qablihim min qarnin makkannahum fi al-ardi ma lam numakkin lakum wa arsalna al-sama'a 'alayhim midraran wa ja'alna al-anhara tajri min tahtihim fa-ahlaknahum bi-dhunubihim wa ansha'na min ba'dihim qarnan akharin.*

**They have already denied the truth when it came to them, so soon there will come to them the news of what they used to mock. Have they not seen how many generations before them We destroyed? We had established them in the earth in ways We have not established for you, and We sent the sky upon them in abundant rain, and We made rivers flow beneath them. Then We destroyed them for their sins and raised up after them another generation.**

## Introduction

There is a stage of rejection where the soul no longer merely hesitates. It becomes casual.

Truth arrives, and instead of trembling before it, a person reduces it to something to dismiss, distort, or laugh at. Once that happens, the issue is no longer lack of access. The truth has already come. What remains is the moral shape of the heart receiving it.

That is where these ayahs begin.

They do not say the people simply failed to encounter guidance. They say the truth came to them, and they denied it. More than that, they turned it into an object of mockery. So the Qur'an tells them something chilling: what you are mocking now will come back to you as *anba'* - as news, as report, as disclosed reality. What you treated lightly will one day arrive with the weight you refused to grant it.

[PAUSE]

Then the passage does something powerful. It refuses to leave the warning in abstraction. It points backward into history. Look at the generations before you. They were not weak scraps of humanity barely surviving. They were established. They had rain. They had rivers. They had civilizational strength beyond what you have been given. And none of that prevented their destruction when their sins matured.

So the hook of the passage is this: **truth denied and mocked does not disappear; it returns with consequence, and history is full of stronger people than us who learned too late that abundance cannot shield a people from the results of their sins.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Truth Came First, Then Denial Came After

The first ayah begins:

*Faqad kadhdhabu bi-al-haqqi lamma ja'ahum.*

They have denied the truth when it came to them.

The order matters. The truth is not absent. It *came*. It arrived, confronted, presented itself. Only then came *takdhib* - denial, declaring it false, pushing it away as unworthy of assent.

[PAUSE]

And the object of denial is *al-haqq*.

Not merely an opinion.
Not merely an unfamiliar religious claim.
The truth. That which is real, rightful, stable, and binding.

So the ayah is not narrating a neutral disagreement between competing perspectives. It is exposing a moral reaction to something that had already come carrying its own claim.

That should make us pause with ourselves as well. Many of our refusals are narrated inwardly as though we were still waiting for clarity, still considering, still gathering data. But sometimes the truth has already arrived, and what remains is whether we will yield to it or protect ourselves from it.

### 2. What Is Mocked Now Will Return as *Anba'*

Then the ayah says:

*Fa-sawfa ya'tihim anba'u ma kanu bihi yastahzi'un.*

Soon there will come to them the news of what they used to mock.

[PAUSE]

This is a devastating phrase. The root *n-b-'* carries news, report, weighty information that becomes known. Something not yet fully unfolded in their sight will become reality they cannot ignore.

And notice the link between denial and mockery. The ayah does not say only that they rejected the truth. It says they were *yastahzi'un* - mocking it, treating it with ridicule and contempt. Mockery is often the ego's way of protecting itself from truth it does not want to obey. If you can make the claim look small, maybe you won't have to feel small before it.

But the Qur'an says: it will come back as *news*. Meaning the day is coming when what was laughed at will no longer be a target of derision. It will be disclosed consequence.

That movement is one of the Qur'an's recurring warnings. Reality does not become lighter because you joke about it. You only postpone the moment when its real weight lands.

### 3. Historical Strength Does Not Cancel Moral Exposure

Then the second ayah opens with a question:

*A-lam yaraw...*

Have they not seen?

The Qur'an is pulling them toward historical vision. Look. Think. Notice what is already in the world.

Then:

*Kam ahlakna min qablihim min qarn.*

How many generations before them We destroyed.

[PAUSE]

The word *qarn* matters. It is not just an isolated person. It is a generation, a civilizational grouping, a people with social continuity and presence. So the ayah is not speaking about random private misfortune. It is speaking about whole communities erased from the scene.

Then comes the shock:

*Makkannahum fi al-ardi ma lam numakkin lakum.*

We established them in the earth in ways We have not established for you.

This is crucial. The people being warned are not allowed to console themselves with the myth that collapse only happens to the weak. The ayah says those earlier peoples had more establishment than you. More rootedness. More worldly stability. More reasons, in human terms, to think they were secure.

So history here is not an antique anecdote. It is an argument against false security.

### 4. Rain, Rivers, and Ruin: Blessing Misread as Immunity

Then the ayah fills in the scene:

*Wa arsalna al-sama'a 'alayhim midraran wa ja'alna al-anhara tajri min tahtihim.*

We sent the sky upon them in abundant rain, and We made rivers flow beneath them.

[PAUSE]

This is language of flourishing. The sky is generous. Water descends repeatedly. Rivers run below them. The image is one of provision, agricultural vitality, civilizational strength, sustained abundance.

And then the turn:

*Fa-ahlaknahum bi-dhunubihim.*

Then We destroyed them for their sins.

This is one of the passage's most important corrections. Blessing is not proof of immunity. Abundance is not a certificate of divine approval. Rain can fall on a people who are still moving toward destruction if their sins continue unrepented and their truth-denial hardens.

Then finally:

*Wa ansha'na min ba'dihim qarnan akharin.*

And We brought forth after them another generation.

The world moves on. The place they thought was secured for them alone receives others after them. History does not stop for the self-important.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Mockery Is Often a Form of Cowardice Before Truth

Many people imagine mockery as strength.

The one who jokes, shrugs, satirizes, and trivializes can appear relaxed, above it all, too clear-eyed to be morally threatened by what others take seriously. But the Qur'an sees something deeper. Often mockery is not the mark of freedom. It is the strategy of a threatened ego.

[PAUSE]

Truth asks things from us.
It exposes us.
It calls us to submit.
It asks us to admit that we are not self-legislating creatures.

That can feel humiliating to the nafs. So rather than bow, it laughs. It frames seriousness as naivete. It turns the uncomfortable claim into a joke. It trains the social atmosphere to treat reverence as weakness.

But these ayahs show how unstable that posture really is. What you mock today may simply be the truth you are too afraid to face while it is still mercifully arriving as guidance rather than as consequence.

So one of the questions these ayahs ask us is uncomfortable and necessary: where do I use irony, casualness, or dismissive humor to keep from having to surrender to something I quietly know is true?

### Theme 2: Worldly Establishment Can Become a Spiritual Anesthetic

The second ayah is especially piercing for prosperous people, stable people, successful people, and communities with infrastructure, continuity, and resources.

Why? Because it names a common illusion: if we are thriving outwardly, perhaps we are safe inwardly.

[PAUSE]

Those earlier generations had establishment in the earth.
They had rain.
They had rivers.
They had continuity strong enough to feel durable.

And still they were destroyed for their sins.

That means prosperity cannot be read lazily. It may be mercy, test, respite, or evidence against us depending on what we do beneath it. A flowing life is not the same thing as an approved life.

This matters at both civilizational and personal scale. A person can have career, family, influence, and visible blessing while the deeper moral account is deteriorating. A community can have strength, institutions, and provision while becoming inwardly brittle through denial, injustice, arrogance, and trivialization of truth.

So the ayah is not anti-blessing. It is anti-delusion. It teaches us to receive abundance with fear, gratitude, and repentance rather than reading it as proof that ruin is impossible.

### Theme 3: Sins Do Not Merely Decorate a Life - They Can Become the Reason It Falls

The phrase *bi-dhunubihim* is short, but devastating.

We destroyed them for their sins.

[PAUSE]

That means sins are not always isolated stains sitting harmlessly at the edge of an otherwise flourishing life. They can become structurally causal. They can become part of why a life, a family, a community, or a civilization begins to collapse.

This is hard for modern sensibilities because we like to separate morality from consequence. We speak as though wrongdoing can be cordoned off privately while public strength remains untouched. The Qur'an does not let us believe that. It insists that moral corruption eventually enters the architecture of collective life.

And again, this is mercy. Not because the warning is pleasant, but because it teaches us before the full consequence arrives. The ayah is an invitation to read the past correctly so we do not become one more addition to that graveyard of generations.

## Closing Synthesis

These two ayahs move with stark force.

The truth comes.
It is denied.
It is mocked.
Then the Qur'an says that what is mocked will return as disclosed news.

And to make that warning concrete, it points to generations stronger than the present audience - people established in the earth, drenched in provision, crossed by rivers - who were still erased because their sins ripened into destruction.

[PAUSE]

That is the architecture: arrival, denial, mockery, disclosure, historical proof.

And the result is a deeply sobering correction. We are not safe because we can trivialize truth. We are not safe because life is currently flowing. We are not safe because others before us seemed more obviously vulnerable. If truth has come, then what matters is whether we receive it with surrender, not whether we have become skillful at laughing while postponing the day its reality reaches us in full.

### Questions to Carry

1. Where do I hide from truth by making it smaller through humor, irony, or casual dismissal?
2. Have I ever mistaken outward stability or provision for proof that my inner or communal state must be sound?
3. What sins have I been treating as manageable even though the Qur'an teaches that sins can become part of why a life collapses?
4. If the truth I am resisting returned today not as invitation but as consequence, what would I wish I had done differently?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs teach that truth denied and mocked does not vanish but returns as unavoidable reality, and that even generations more established and more blessed than us were destroyed when their sins ripened beneath an illusion of security.**

### Du'a

Allahumma, protect us from the arrogance that laughs while truth is arriving. Do not let abundance make us sleepy, do not let our sins become reasons for collapse, and make us of those who receive Your truth with humility before it returns to us as the news of what we once took lightly. Amin.

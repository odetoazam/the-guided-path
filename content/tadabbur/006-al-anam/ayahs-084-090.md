# Those Are the Ones Allah Guided

## Surah Al-Anam (6:84-90)

## Introduction

After the lonely clarity of Ibrahim standing under the night sky, the surah does something deeply strengthening.

It refuses to leave tawhid as an isolated breakthrough in one extraordinary man. It turns that breakthrough into a line. A family. A prophetic inheritance. A chain of guided, chosen, righteous, favored servants stretching across generations.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs are like a roll call of sacred continuity. Ishaq. Ya'qub. Nuh. Dawud. Sulayman. Ayyub. Yusuf. Musa. Harun. Zakariyya. Yahya. 'Isa. Ilyas. Isma'il. Al-Yasa'. Yunus. Lut. Fathers, descendants, brothers. Then the whole sequence is summarized: that is Allah's guidance. He guides by it whom He wills. Even for prophets, shirk would nullify deeds. To them were given scripture, judgment, and prophethood. And then Muhammad is told something weighty: those are the ones Allah guided, so follow their guidance.

This is not mere honorific listing. It is theological architecture. Guidance is one. Its carriers are many. Prophecy is not a set of disconnected inspirations; it is a single river of tawhid flowing through many human lives.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **Allah does not leave tawhid standing alone in history; He weaves it through a prophetic lineage so that guidance appears not as a private accident but as a continuous divine inheritance.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Passage Begins with Gift and Guidance

The opening says:

*Wa wahabna lahu Ishaqa wa Ya'qub.*

We gifted him Isaac and Jacob.

[PAUSE]

*Wahabna* matters. These are not merely biological continuations. They are gifts. Ibrahim's legacy is granted, not self-produced.

Then:

*Kullan hadayna.*

Each of them We guided.

This rhythm keeps returning: not merely named, but guided. The line is not sacred by blood alone. It is sacred by guidance.

Then Nuh is drawn in from before, and after him comes a wide sweep of names. The surah is deliberately broadening the horizon. Ibrahim's breakthrough belongs inside a much larger divine pattern.

### 2. The Prophetic Line Is Described Through Multiple Honors

Across these ayahs, several descriptions accumulate:

*Al-muhsinin.*
*Al-salihin.*
*Faddalna 'ala al-'alamin.*
*Ijtabaynahum.*
*Hadaynahum ila siratin mustaqim.*

[PAUSE]

This is important. The prophets are not collapsed into one flat compliment. They are described through ihsan, righteousness, preference, chosen-ness, and guidance.

*Ijtibā'* especially is precious. We chose them. Drew them out. Selected them. Guidance is not random movement toward Allah from below only; it is also divine selection from above.

Then the destination:

*Ila siratin mustaqim.*

To a straight path.

The line of prophets is many lives, one road.

### 3. Allah's Guidance Is the Real Unifying Principle

Then the surah makes its own summary:

*Dhalika huda Allah.*

That is the guidance of Allah.

[PAUSE]

Not the guidance of tribe.
Not the guidance of ancestry as such.
Not the guidance of a civilization's accumulated prestige.

Allah's guidance.

And then:

*Yahdi bihi man yasha'u min 'ibadih.*

He guides by it whom He wills among His servants.

So even after naming the most luminous people in sacred history, the ayah keeps returning the center to Allah. The point is not hero-worship. The point is the source of guidance.

### 4. Even the Greatest Deeds Would Collapse Under Shirk

Then comes a striking warning:

*Wa law ashraku la-habita 'anhum ma kanu ya'malun.*

If they had associated others with Allah, whatever they were doing would have become worthless.

[PAUSE]

This is not because the prophets were in danger of actual shirk here. It is because the surah is showing how absolute tawhid is. No spiritual rank, no history of obedience, no prophetic nearness creates an exemption clause. Shirk is so destructive that if it were imagined there, deeds themselves would collapse.

That gives us a scale for understanding the matter. Tawhid is not one virtue among others. It is the condition that keeps every other act alive.

### 5. Scripture, Judgment, Prophethood, and the Command to Follow

Then:

*Ula'ika alladhina ataynahumu al-kitaba wa al-hukma wa al-nubuwwah.*

Those are the ones to whom We gave the Book, judgment, and prophethood.

[PAUSE]

Three gifts gather here:

*Kitab* - revealed scripture.
*Hukm* - authority, sound judgment, wise decision.
*Nubuwwah* - prophethood itself.

Then the surah says that if these people reject, Allah has already entrusted this trust to a people who will not reject it.

And then the command:

*Fa-bi-hudahumu iqtadih.*

So by their guidance, follow.

The wording is powerful. Not merely "respect them," "study them," or "admire their memory." Follow their guidance.

Then the Prophet is told to say:

*La as'alukum 'alayhi ajran.*

I ask of you no payment for it.

The prophetic inheritance is not monetized. It is carried as trust.

And finally:

*In huwa illa dhikra li-al-'alamin.*

It is nothing but a reminder for the worlds.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Guidance Is a Line, Not a Lone Event

One of the most comforting things in this passage is that Ibrahim is not left alone.

His certainty becomes lineage.
His tawhid becomes inheritance.
His break with shirk becomes part of a river that keeps flowing through time.

[PAUSE]

That matters because many believers know what it is like to feel isolated in clarity. To feel like surrender to Allah puts you at odds with the mood of your age, your social world, even your family. These ayahs answer that isolation by placing the believer inside a much older company.

If you stand with tawhid, you are not inventing a new path. You are entering a very old procession.

And that procession is not ornamental. It is instructional. The command *fa-bi-hudahumu iqtadih* means that the way forward is not novelty, but alignment with the prophetic road.

### Theme 2: Tawhid Is the Condition of Meaningful Deeds

The warning about shirk nullifying deeds should sober the heart.

Because religious people can become fascinated by output: scholarship, activism, sacrifice, service, spiritual effort, visible righteousness. And all of that matters. But the ayah reminds us that no quantity of action can compensate for corruption at the root.

[PAUSE]

If tawhid is damaged, everything else is endangered.

That does not mean minor sins erase all good. The point here is far more fundamental. The worship of Allah alone is the condition under which every other act retains its meaning as worship. Without that center, deeds are spiritually detached from their rightful Lord.

So one of the hidden mercies of this passage is that it keeps calling us back to the axis before we get lost in branches.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs widen Ibrahim's story into the map of prophetic history.

Allah gifts him descendants.
He names prophet after prophet.
He describes them through guidance, righteousness, excellence, chosenness, and favor.
He gathers them onto one straight path.
He declares this all to be Allah's guidance.
He warns that even the greatest deeds would collapse under shirk.
He names scripture, judgment, and prophethood as the entrusted gifts.
Then He tells Muhammad to follow their guidance and to carry the message without asking payment for what is only a reminder to the worlds.

[PAUSE]

So the believer is meant to feel both humbled and steadied here. Humbled, because guidance is a gift and not a self-authored distinction. Steadied, because the road we are trying to walk is ancient, prophetic, and already lit by those whom Allah Himself guided.

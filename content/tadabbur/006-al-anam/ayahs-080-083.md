# Which of the Two Parties Has More Right to Security?

## Surah Al-Anam (6:80-83)

[ayah:6:80] وَحَآجَّهُۥ قَوْمُهُۥ ۚ قَالَ أَتُحَـٰٓجُّوٓنِّى فِى ٱللَّهِ وَقَدْ هَدَىٰنِ ۚ وَلَآ أَخَافُ مَا تُشْرِكُونَ بِهِۦٓ إِلَّآ أَن يَشَآءَ رَبِّى شَيْـًٔا ۗ وَسِعَ رَبِّى كُلَّ شَىْءٍ عِلْمًا ۗ أَفَلَا تَتَذَكَّرُونَ

**And his people argued with him. He said, "Do you argue with me concerning Allah while He has guided me? And I do not fear what you associate with Him except that my Lord should will something. My Lord encompasses all things in knowledge; then will you not remember? So which of the two parties has more right to security, if you should know? Those who believe and do not mix their belief with wrongdoing - those will have security, and they are rightly guided. And that was Our argument which We gave Abraham against his people. We raise by degrees whom We will. Indeed, your Lord is Wise and Knowing."**

## Introduction

Once you reject false gods, the next pressure usually arrives as fear.

People ask:
Are you not afraid of what you have insulted?
What if your break with the familiar costs you protection?
What if security lies in keeping all powers appeased?

[PAUSE]

That is the pressure Ibrahim faces here. His people argue with him after his open declaration of tawhid, and the argument appears to have included fear: fear of the idols, fear of offending what the community regards as sacred, fear of what happens to the person who steps outside inherited religion. Ibrahim does not merely resist that fear. He reverses it. Why should I fear what you associate, he asks, while you do not fear associating with Allah without any authority from Him? Then the question sharpens into one of the deepest in the surah: which of the two parties has more right to security? The answer becomes a principle for all believers: the people of iman whose faith is not clothed in zulm are the ones who truly have amn.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **the deepest human insecurity does not come from refusing false gods, but from wronging tawhid itself, and real security belongs only to a faith that is not mixed with zulm.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Conflict Moves from Observation to Debate

The opening is brief:

*Wa hajjahu qawmuhu.*

His people argued with him.

[PAUSE]

The root of *hajj* is argumentation, contest, pressing a claim. Ibrahim's inward clarity now meets public resistance.

He answers:

*A-tuhajjunni fi Allahi wa qad hadan?*

Do you argue with me concerning Allah while He has guided me?

This is not arrogance. It is proportion. Once guidance has been granted, debate that asks the guided to unsee what Allah made clear becomes spiritually absurd.

### 2. Ibrahim Refuses Creature-Fear Without Denying Divine Sovereignty

Then he says:

*Wa la akhafu ma tushrikuna bihi illa an yasha'a rabbi shay'an.*

I do not fear what you associate with Him, except if my Lord wills something.

[PAUSE]

This is a beautiful balance. Ibrahim does not fear the idols or associated beings as independent powers. But he also does not speak as though anything falls outside Allah's will. Fear of creatures is denied; sovereignty of the Creator is affirmed.

Then:

*Wasi'a rabbi kulla shay'in 'ilman.*

My Lord encompasses everything in knowledge.

His calm is not personality. It is theology. The One he trusts misses nothing.

### 3. The Question of Fear Is Reversed

Then the piercing reversal:

*Wa kayfa akhafu ma ashraktum wa la takhafuna annakum ashraktum bi-Allahi ma lam yunazzil bihi 'alaykum sultanan?*

How should I fear what you associate, while you do not fear that you have associated with Allah what He never sent down any authority for?

[PAUSE]

The argument is devastating because it exposes distorted fear. People are terrified of powerless rivals, but casual before the enormity of fabricating partners beside Allah.

Then the word:

*Sultanan.*

Authority, warrant, proof, authorization. Shirk has no divine warrant behind it. No descent of authority. No revealed legitimacy.

### 4. Security Belongs to Unmixed Faith

Then comes the central question:

*Fa-ayyu al-fariqayni ahaqqu bi-al-amn?*

Which of the two parties has more right to security?

[PAUSE]

Notice that Ibrahim does not ask simply, who feels safer? He asks who has more right to security.

Then the divine verdict:

*Alladhina amanu wa lam yalbisu imanahum bi-zulmin ula'ika lahumu al-amnu wa hum muhtadun.*

Those who believe and do not mix their faith with wrongdoing - they have security, and they are guided.

The verb *yalbisu* is important. To clothe, mix, confuse, dress one thing in another. The point is not a faith occasionally accompanied by human weakness in the broad sense, but faith corrupted by zulm at its root. The hadithically clarified meaning here is shirk as the great zulm, the darkness that stains tawhid itself.

So real *amn* is not available to every form of belief. It belongs to tawhid kept clear.

### 5. The Argument Itself Was Allah's Gift

Then the final sentence protects us from romanticizing Ibrahim as merely a self-made genius:

*Wa tilka hujjatuna ataynaha Ibrahima 'ala qawmihi.*

That was Our argument which We gave Ibrahim against his people.

[PAUSE]

Even the argument belongs to Allah. Hujjah is granted.

Then:

*Narfa'u darajatin man nasha'.*

We raise in degrees whom We will.

Clarity, argument, certainty, and rank are gifts under divine will.

And the closing names:

*Inna rabbaka hakimun 'alim.*

Your Lord is Wise and Knowing.

The distribution of guidance and raised rank is neither random nor blind. It is wise and knowing.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: False Religion Often Maintains Itself Through Misplaced Fear

These ayahs help explain why falsehood can endure even when its arguments are weak.

It survives because fear does work that proof cannot.

[PAUSE]

People remain tied to systems, idols, and inherited false loyalties because they are afraid of what happens if they step away. Afraid of social exile. Afraid of invisible retaliation. Afraid of loss. Afraid of displeasing the forces that their community taught them to dread.

Ibrahim's answer is liberating because he does not merely say, "Your fears are irrational." He says: your fear is aimed at the wrong object. Why fear what has no independent power, and not fear the enormity of attributing partners to Allah without warrant from Him?

This is still one of the great spiritual reversals. The cure for many false attachments is not becoming fearless in a general sense. It is learning to fear rightly.

### Theme 2: Security Is a Tawhid Word Before It Is an Emotional Word

Modern people often treat security as felt comfort, emotional stability, or reduced threat. But the Qur'an here roots it much deeper.

*Lahumu al-amn.*

They have security.

[PAUSE]

Security is not first the absence of difficulty. Ibrahim is standing against his people when this logic is unveiled. Outwardly, he is the vulnerable one. Yet he is the secure one.

Why? Because security comes from being aligned with reality, not from being buffered from immediate cost. A soul can be socially protected and metaphysically exposed. Another can be externally pressured and inwardly secure because it stands inside tawhid.

This also means that mixing faith with zulm is ultimately self-sabotage. The soul seeks safety by compromise, by appeasing rivals, by hedging devotion, by keeping doors open to other allegiances. But the ayah says the opposite. Security belongs to unmixed faith.

## Closing Synthesis

In this passage, Ibrahim wins the fear argument.

His people argue with him.
He refuses to fear their associated powers.
He keeps all sovereignty with Allah alone.
He exposes their failure to fear shirk itself.
He asks which party truly has more right to security.
Allah answers: those who believe and do not mix their faith with zulm are the people of security and guidance.
Then He declares that the argument itself was His gift to Ibrahim and that ranks are raised by His wise and knowing will.

[PAUSE]

So the believer's task is not merely to claim tawhid, but to keep tawhid clear enough that security can actually live inside it. Many of our fears become tyrannical because we no longer measure them against the one fear that should order all others: wronging Allah in the very matter of who He is.

### Questions to Carry

1. What fears in my life become smaller when I remember that they are creature-fears standing under the will and knowledge of Allah?
2. In what ways might I still be trying to secure myself by compromises that actually mix faith with forms of zulm rather than purify it?

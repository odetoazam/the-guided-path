# They Kept Others Away and Withdrew Themselves

## Surah Al-Anam (6:25-26)

[ayah:6:25] وَمِنْهُم مَّن يَسْتَمِعُ إِلَيْكَ ۖ وَجَعَلْنَا عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِهِمْ أَكِنَّةً أَن يَفْقَهُوهُ وَفِىٓ ءَاذَانِهِمْ وَقْرًا ۚ وَإِن يَرَوْا۟ كُلَّ ءَايَةٍ لَّا يُؤْمِنُوا۟ بِهَا ۚ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَا جَآءُوكَ يُجَـٰدِلُونَكَ يَقُولُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوٓا۟ إِنْ هَـٰذَآ إِلَّآ أَسَـٰطِيرُ ٱلْأَوَّلِينَ

[ayah:6:26] وَهُمْ يَنْهَوْنَ عَنْهُ وَيَنْـَٔوْنَ عَنْهُ ۖ وَإِن يُهْلِكُونَ إِلَّآ أَنفُسَهُمْ وَمَا يَشْعُرُونَ

*Wa minhum man yastami'u ilayk. Wa ja'alna 'ala qulubihim akinnatan an yafqahuhu wa fi adhanihim waqra. Wa in yaraw kulla ayatin la yu'minu biha. Hatta idha ja'uka yujadilunaka yaqulu alladhina kafaru in hadha illa asatiru al-awwalin.*

*Wa hum yanhawna 'anhu wa yan'awna 'anhu. Wa in yuhlikuna illa anfusahum wa ma yash'urun.*

**And among them are those who listen to you, but We have placed coverings over their hearts lest they understand it, and in their ears there is heaviness. And even if they were to see every sign, they would not believe in it. Until, when they come to you arguing with you, those who disbelieve say, "This is nothing but legends of the former peoples." And they keep others away from it and keep themselves far away from it, and they destroy none but themselves, though they do not realize it.**

## Introduction

There is a way of being near truth that is actually a form of distance.

You can sit close enough to hear the words.
You can enter the gathering.
You can listen with your ears.
You can even return again and again to argue.

And still remain inwardly untouched.

That is the tragedy of this passage.

The people described here are not absent from the Prophet's recitation. Some of them listen. But listening is not the same thing as receiving. Their hearts are covered, their ears are burdened, and even a multiplying of signs does not become faith for them. When they finally speak, they reduce revelation to inherited tales. Then the passage goes one step further: they do not only stay far from it themselves, they help keep others away as well. And all the while, they are destroying only themselves without even sensing the scale of what they are doing.

[PAUSE]

That is one of the most frightening spiritual conditions in the Qur'an. Not simple absence from truth, but exposure without penetration. Religious proximity without inward opening. Argument without surrender. Influence used not only to justify one's own distance, but to recruit others into it.

The hook of these ayahs is this: **the soul can remain near revelation physically while becoming profoundly distant from it inwardly, and once that distance hardens, it often begins pushing others away from the very truth that could have healed it.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. Listening Is Not Yet Understanding

The passage opens:

*Wa minhum man yastami'u ilayk.*

And among them are those who listen to you.

[PAUSE]

The verb *yastami'u* suggests real listening, not merely accidental hearing. These are not people who never encountered the recitation. They are close enough to the Prophet to listen.

And that is exactly what makes the next line so devastating:

*Wa ja'alna 'ala qulubihim akinnatan an yafqahuhu.*

We placed coverings over their hearts lest they understand it.

The word *akinnah* comes with the sense of coverings, sheaths, containers that keep something enclosed and inaccessible. The problem is not with the Qur'an's clarity. The problem is that the inner organ meant to receive it is shrouded.

Then:

*Wa fi adhanihim waqra.*

And in their ears there is heaviness.

So the passage names both centers of reception: the heart and the ear. One is covered. The other is burdened. Revelation is near, but the channels of real reception are obstructed.

And the goal denied to them is not merely to hear the sounds. It is:

*an yafqahuhu* - that they understand it deeply, grasp it, penetrate into its meaning.

That is a sobering distinction. A person can hear much Qur'an and yet never *fiqh* it in the sense this ayah points toward.

### 2. More Signs Do Not Heal a Refusing Heart

Then the ayah says:

*Wa in yaraw kulla ayatin la yu'minu biha.*

Even if they were to see every sign, they would not believe in it.

[PAUSE]

This continues the surah's repeated demolition of the fantasy that evidence alone is always the missing piece. Once the heart is bent away in a certain way, more signs do not necessarily open it. They can simply accumulate against it.

That is why the passage is so psychologically precise. It is not anti-sign. It is anti-self-deception about what the real barrier is. The issue is not that Allah failed to show enough. The issue is that the inward posture has become one of refusal.

Then the social result appears:

*Hatta idha ja'uka yujadilunaka...*

Until, when they come to you arguing with you...

They do not come emptied out and seeking light. They come armed for dispute.

And what do they say?

*In hadha illa asatiru al-awwalin.*

This is nothing but legends of the former peoples.

The word *asatir* carries the sense of written-down tales, old stories, inherited narratives without binding truth. This is how a defending ego trivializes revelation: not by answering it at its depth, but by reducing it to a category beneath obedience.

### 3. They Not Only Stay Away - They Keep Others Away

Then the next ayah sharpens the tragedy:

*Wa hum yanhawna 'anhu wa yan'awna 'anhu.*

They forbid from it, and they themselves keep far from it.

[PAUSE]

This is one of the most devastating pairings in the surah. The sounds even mirror one another: *yanhawna* and *yan'awna*. They warn others off and they withdraw themselves. They become both personally distant and socially obstructive.

That is a darker stage than private disbelief. It is missionary distance. A person alienated from truth begins helping produce alienation in others.

And then the Qur'an gives the verdict:

*Wa in yuhlikuna illa anfusahum wa ma yash'urun.*

They destroy none but themselves, and they do not realize it.

The irony is severe. They may feel powerful, persuasive, culturally influential, intellectually superior. But the real destruction is landing inwardly, against their own selves, while they remain unaware.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Religious Nearness Can Hide Spiritual Distance

One of the most uncomfortable lessons in these ayahs is that proximity is not safety.

You can sit in circles of knowledge.
You can hear recitation often.
You can know the language of religion.
You can even spend time debating its claims.

[PAUSE]

And still remain far if the heart is closed and the nafs is defending itself.

That should make us humble about our own forms of nearness. It is possible to mistake familiarity for receptivity. We assume that because the Qur'an is often around us, it must be entering us. But these ayahs say otherwise. The real question is not only whether revelation is near your ears. It is whether anything in your heart is still soft enough to understand it.

So a frightening but necessary self-audit emerges: am I truly receiving what I am regularly around, or am I becoming a person who can hear much while understanding little because the inner posture remains guarded?

### Theme 2: Argument Is Sometimes a Shield Against Transformation

The people come *yujadilunaka* - arguing with you.

Argument is not always blameworthy. Honest questions matter. Clarification matters. Careful reasoning matters. But there is a kind of argument whose purpose is not to reach truth; it is to keep truth from reaching me.

[PAUSE]

That kind of debate is spiritually dangerous because it produces the feeling of engagement without the vulnerability of surrender. I stay active, sharp, responsive, and mentally occupied, so I can tell myself I am taking the matter seriously. But the whole activity may actually be functioning as a shield.

That is why the endpoint of their disputation is so small: "old tales." A person can spend a lot of energy around revelation while using all of that energy to avoid being changed by it.

This matters deeply in our own lives. We may not use the same words, but we have our own modern versions of *asatir al-awwalin*: dismissive categories that help us keep a safe intellectual distance from what would otherwise demand repentance, humility, or obedience.

### Theme 3: Turning Others Away While Distant Yourself Is a Special Kind of Ruin

There is something especially tragic about the pairing *yanhawna 'anhu wa yan'awna 'anhu*.

It is one thing to be lost and isolated in that loss.
It is another to become an obstacle.

[PAUSE]

When a person discourages others from revelation, mocks sincerity, frames obedience as naivete, or makes closeness to Allah look socially embarrassing or intellectually unserious, he is multiplying darkness beyond himself. Yet the Qur'an still says: they destroy none but themselves.

Why? Because every time they do that, they are deepening the condition of their own soul. They are etching distance more permanently into themselves.

And the most frightening part is the ending:

*wa ma yash'urun*.

They do not realize it.

Not all destruction feels like collapse in the moment. Some of it feels like control, wit, social ease, or self-assurance while the inner world is being hollowed out.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs trace a chilling progression.

People listen to revelation but do not truly understand it.
They see signs but do not believe.
They come to argue rather than be transformed.
They reduce the Qur'an to inherited tales.
Then they move from private distance to actively keeping others away.
And all the while their destruction is landing back on themselves without their awareness.

[PAUSE]

That is the architecture: proximity, blockage, argument, dismissal, obstruction, self-ruin.

And the warning is painfully relevant. The soul does not always drift from truth by running far away from it at once. Sometimes it stays close enough to hear while training itself not to receive. Then one day it is no longer only distant; it has become an obstacle. The mercy is to notice that process early enough to break it before closeness to revelation becomes one more witness against us.

### Questions to Carry

1. Am I truly receiving the Qur'an, or have I become too familiar with its presence to notice that my heart is not opening?
2. Where might I be using argument, cleverness, or dismissive categories to keep truth from becoming costly in my life?
3. Have I ever, through words or posture, made it harder for someone else to move toward Allah?
4. What patterns of self-ruin in me still feel normal only because I have not yet learned how to sense them?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs teach that a person can remain near revelation while inwardly blocked from it, and if that distance hardens, it can begin pushing others away too, all while ruining the self without even realizing it.**

### Du'a

Allahumma, do not let nearness to Your Book become a witness against us. Remove the coverings from our hearts, the heaviness from our hearing, and every argumentative habit that keeps us from being changed. Protect us from pushing anyone away from You, and save us from the kind of self-destruction we cannot even feel. Amin.

# Ornamented Speech of Delusion

## Surah Al-Anam (6:112-113)

[ayah:6:112] وَكَذَٰلِكَ جَعَلْنَا لِكُلِّ نَبِىٍّ عَدُوًّا شَيَـٰطِينَ ٱلْإِنسِ وَٱلْجِنِّ يُوحِى بَعْضُهُمْ إِلَىٰ بَعْضٍ زُخْرُفَ ٱلْقَوْلِ غُرُورًا ۚ وَلَوْ شَآءَ رَبُّكَ مَا فَعَلُوهُ ۖ فَذَرْهُمْ وَمَا يَفْتَرُونَ

[ayah:6:113] وَلِتَصْغَىٰٓ إِلَيْهِ أَفْـِٔدَةُ ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱلْـَٔاخِرَةِ وَلِيَرْضَوْهُ وَلِيَقْتَرِفُوا۟ مَا هُم مُّقْتَرِفُونَ

*Wa kadhalika ja'alna li-kulli nabiyyin 'aduwwan shayatina al-insi wa al-jinni yuhi ba'duhum ila ba'din zukhrufa al-qawli ghururan. Wa law sha'a rabbuka ma fa'aluhu. Fa-dharhum wa ma yaftarun.*

*Wa li-tasgha ilayhi af'idatu alladhina la yu'minuna bi-al-akhirati wa li-yardawhu wa li-yaqtarifu ma hum muqtarifun.*

**And thus We have made for every prophet an enemy - devils of humankind and jinn, inspiring one another with ornamented speech of delusion. And if your Lord had willed, they would not have done it, so leave them and what they invent. And so that the hearts of those who do not believe in the Hereafter may incline toward it and be pleased with it and so that they may commit what they are committing.**

## Introduction

Falsehood rarely survives by ugliness alone.

If evil always appeared in its most naked form, fewer souls would follow it. What gives it social power is presentation. It is polished, decorated, emotionally resonant, plausibly phrased, dressed in intelligence, wrapped in beauty, sharpened for appetite. It arrives not only as argument, but as atmosphere.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs pull back the curtain on that atmosphere. Every prophet faces enemies, and those enemies are not only external opponents in the obvious sense. They include *shayatin al-insi wal-jinn* - devils from humanity and jinn - passing speech to one another. And what kind of speech? *Zukhrufa al-qawl*: ornamented, gilded, embellished speech. The point is not simply that it is false. The point is that it is attractive. Then the next ayah traces the full arc of seduction: the heart inclines, then it becomes pleased, then it commits.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **the path to open sin often begins much earlier, when the heart starts leaning toward beautified falsehood because the Hereafter has become too faint to govern what it loves.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. Enmity to Prophets Is Not an Anomaly

The passage begins:

*Wa kadhalika ja'alna li-kulli nabiyyin 'aduwwan...*

Thus We made for every prophet an enemy.

[PAUSE]

That line alone is a mercy to anyone carrying truth. Opposition is not proof that the prophetic path is failing. It is part of the terrain prophets themselves walked.

Then the enemy is specified:

*Shayatina al-insi wa al-jinn.*

Devils from humankind and jinn.

This is arresting because it tells us that "shaytanic" is not limited to the unseen realm. Human beings can participate in devilish work when they embody its rebellion, seduction, and distortion.

### 2. Falsehood Is Passed Along in Decorated Language

Then:

*Yuhi ba'duhum ila ba'din zukhrufa al-qawli ghururan.*

They inspire one another with ornamented speech as delusion.

[PAUSE]

The verb *yuhi* is striking. It is the same root as revelation, but here it is used for covert passing, suggestive casting, secretive conveying from one to another. This is not wahy in the noble prophetic sense, but it mimics the pattern of transmission. Falsehood has its own whisper-networks.

Then the phrase:

*Zukhrufa al-qawl.*

Gilded speech. Embellished speech. Speech plated with decorative surface.

*Zukhruf* is adornment, ornament, gold-like beautification. The point is not that the content is strong. The point is that the presentation glitters.

Then:

*Ghururan.*

As deception, delusion, beguilement.

The ornament is not innocent style. It is style functioning as spiritual misdirection.

### 3. Allah Still Governs the Field

Then the ayah restores the vertical frame:

*Wa law sha'a rabbuka ma fa'aluhu.*

If your Lord had willed, they would not have done it.

[PAUSE]

Again the surah refuses panic. The existence of hostile rhetoric does not place events outside Allah's sovereignty. Nothing slips beyond Him.

Then:

*Fa-dharhum wa ma yaftarun.*

So leave them and what they invent.

This is not passive approval. It is strategic release. Do not be swallowed by their fabrications. Do not treat every decorated lie as though it deserves your soul's full occupancy.

### 4. The Ayah Maps the Psychology of Seduction

Then comes the next verse:

*Wa li-tasgha ilayhi af'idatu alladhina la yu'minuna bi-al-akhirah.*

So that the hearts of those who do not believe in the Hereafter may incline toward it.

[PAUSE]

The verb *tasgha* means to incline, lean, bend toward. Sin rarely begins with action. It begins with an inward lean.

And notice that the ayah says *af'idah* - hearts in the sense of inward, burning centers of desire and perception - not merely minds. Seduction is not only intellectual.

Then:

*Wa li-yardawhu.*

So that they become pleased with it.

Leaning becomes liking. Exposure becomes comfort. What first felt questionable becomes emotionally acceptable.

Then:

*Wa li-yaqtarifu ma hum muqtarifun.*

So that they may commit what they are committing.

*Iqtiraf* is acquisition through commission, drawing something onto oneself through deed. The movement is complete: attraction, approval, action.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The Hereafter Is a Defense Against Beautiful Lies

The ayah ties susceptibility to beautified falsehood directly to not believing in the Hereafter.

That is profoundly important. It means attachment to akhirah is not only about hope for Jannah or fear of judgment later. It is an interpretive power now. The one who lives before the Hereafter does not evaluate speech only by how it sounds, flatters, thrills, or liberates in the moment. He asks where it leads, what it costs, what it normalizes, and what it will look like when brought before Allah.

[PAUSE]

When akhirah fades, ornament becomes stronger than consequence. Style beats truth. Emotional relief beats surrender. Present appetite beats final return. The heart becomes easy prey for *zukhrufa al-qawl* precisely because it has lost the gravitational pull of what comes after death.

### Theme 2: Sin Often Begins With Enjoying the Tone of Falsehood

This passage is useful because it identifies a stage people often ignore: *wa li-yardawhu*.

We usually notice the final outward act. But the Qur'an here points to prior emotional consent. There is a moment when the heart stops resisting the false thing and begins enjoying it. That interior pleasure is already spiritually consequential.

[PAUSE]

So one way to examine ourselves is not only to ask, what have I done? It is also to ask, what kinds of speech have I begun to enjoy? What rhetoric makes me lean? What presentation of disobedience now feels sophisticated, freeing, witty, or deep to me simply because it has been decorated well?

If we only fight the deed and ignore the seduction, we will keep losing upstream.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs teach us that prophetic struggle includes contesting language, aesthetics, persuasion, and atmosphere.

There are human and jinn devils. They collaborate. They pass ornamented speech. Hearts without a living Hereafter lean toward it, take pleasure in it, and eventually embody it.

[PAUSE]

But the passage also gives us steadiness. Allah governs the field. Opposition is not new. And not every shining sentence deserves our trust simply because it is well-made.

Perhaps one way to sit with this passage is to ask: what kind of speech has been quietly training my heart to lean before I ever notice what it is training me to do?

[PAUSE]

The architecture of the passage lands here: **falsehood becomes powerful not only when it is believed, but when it is beautified enough to be loved, and the heart most protected from that love is the heart still anchored in the Hereafter.**

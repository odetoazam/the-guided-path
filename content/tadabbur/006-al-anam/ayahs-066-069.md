# Every Report Has Its Resting Place

## Surah Al-Anam (6:66-69)

[ayah:6:66] وَكَذَّبَ بِهِۦ قَوْمُكَ وَهُوَ ٱلْحَقُّ ۚ قُل لَّسْتُ عَلَيْكُم بِوَكِيلٍ

[ayah:6:67] لِّكُلِّ نَبَإٍ مُّسْتَقَرٌّ ۚ وَسَوْفَ تَعْلَمُونَ

## Introduction

Not every distance from falsehood is cowardice.

Sometimes stepping back is obedience.
Sometimes refusing to stay seated is the moral act.
Sometimes the clearest witness to truth is not endless argument, but knowing when the room has turned into violation.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs come after warnings of punishment and after repeated clarification of the signs. Still, the people deny the truth. So the Prophet is told: say, I am not your wakil. I am not the manager of your outcomes. Then comes a sentence that should steady the believer for life: every report has its resting place, and you will come to know. After that, the surah teaches adab with revelation. When people begin to *yakhuduna fi ayatina* - wade into Allah's verses with mockery, distortion, or insolent speech - do not remain with them. Turn away until they enter another kind of conversation. And if forgetfulness overtakes you for a moment, then once you remember, do not continue sitting with the wrongdoing people. Yet the people of taqwa are also reminded that they are not charged with the mockers' account itself; their task is reminder.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **truth does not become weak because people deny it, and one part of fidelity to revelation is knowing when to stop sharing space with speech that is violating it.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. Denial Does Not Change the Nature of the Truth

The passage opens with a painful but clarifying statement:

*Wa kadhdhaba bihi qawmuka wa huwa al-haqq.*

Your people have denied it, while it is the truth.

[PAUSE]

The sentence does not allow denial to define reality. Their rejection is real, but so is the status of what they rejected: *wa huwa al-haqq*. It is the truth.

Then comes the Prophet's boundary:

*Qul lastu 'alaykum bi-wakil.*

Say: I am not over you a wakil.

*Wakil* here carries the sense of one entrusted with managing, guaranteeing, or controlling affairs on another's behalf. The Prophet delivers, warns, clarifies, and calls. He is not the owner of their choices, nor the manager of the outcome of every soul.

That is both a warning to the deniers and a mercy to every carrier of truth after him. We are not permitted to abandon reminder, but we are also not asked to carry divine agency on our shoulders.

### 2. Every Report Has a Resting Place

Then comes one of the shortest and strongest sentences in the surah:

*Li-kulli naba'in mustaqarr.*

For every report there is a settled place.

[PAUSE]

*Naba'* is not just any news. It is weighty report, consequential news, something with real gravity. And *mustaqarr* carries the sense of a place or time of settling, arriving, becoming fixed.

So the ayah means that every divine report, every warning, every promise, every truth eventually arrives at its point of manifestation. Nothing remains suspended forever in the realm of being merely said.

Then:

*Wa sawfa ta'lamun.*

And you will come to know.

Not through argument alone. Through arrival. Through reality catching up with what was denied.

### 3. Not Every Conversation Is Neutral Space

Then the passage becomes extremely practical:

*Wa idha ra'ayta alladhina yakhuduna fi ayatina fa-a'rid 'anhum.*

When you see those who engage in offensive discourse concerning Our verses, then turn away from them.

[PAUSE]

The verb *yakhuduna* is vivid. It is to wade in, plunge in, move about carelessly. In this context the classical tafsir frames it as mockery, false speech, distortion, and irreverent treatment of revelation.

The command is not "defeat them rhetorically in every sitting." It is:

*Fa-a'rid 'anhum.*

Turn away from them.

Until when?

*Hatta yakhudu fi hadithin ghayrih.*

Until they enter another conversation.

The space is not permanently abandoned as such. But the believer is not to remain seated in the moment of desecration, as if the violation were ordinary talk.

Then the ayah shows profound realism:

*Wa imma yunsiyannaka al-shaytanu...*

If Satan causes you to forget...

Forgetfulness is named. Human lapse is recognized. But once memory returns, so must obedience:

*Fa-la taq'ud ba'da al-dhikra ma'a al-qawmi al-zalimin.*

Do not remain seated after the reminder with the wrongdoing people.

### 4. You Are Not Accountable for Their Account, But You Are Responsible for Reminder

Then the final ayah protects the conscience from a different mistake:

*Wa ma 'ala alladhina yattaquna min hisabihim min shay'.*

Nothing of their account is upon those who have taqwa.

[PAUSE]

This matters. The believer might fear that any disengagement is moral surrender, or that he somehow carries the sin-account of everyone violating truth around him. The ayah relieves that burden. Their حساب is not yours.

But relief does not become indifference:

*Wa lakin dhikra la'allahum yattaqun.*

But rather reminder, so that perhaps they may develop taqwa.

So the believer neither absorbs another person's moral account nor abandons the duty to remind. He keeps the boundary and preserves the call.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: A Believer Must Learn the Difference Between Witness and Contamination

Many sincere people stay too long in corrupt spaces because they confuse endurance with principle.

They tell themselves:
If I leave, I am weak.
If I disengage, I am losing.
If I do not remain present, I am failing the truth.

[PAUSE]

But these ayahs teach something subtler. There is a way of staying that becomes quiet participation. There is a way of remaining seated that normalizes what should not feel normal. When Allah's verses are being treated as material for mockery or desecration, leaving is not betrayal of witness. It is part of witness.

This applies beyond formal religious argument. It applies to the soul's environments: what we laugh at, what we tolerate, what we keep streaming into ourselves, what kinds of company train us to become casual with what Allah made weighty.

The ayah does not ask us to be brittle or theatrically offended. It asks for moral clarity. Some rooms are spiritually expensive, and one form of taqwa is to know when not to keep sitting in them.

### Theme 2: The Truth Does Not Need Us to Control Outcomes

The other deep mercy here is in the phrase:

*Lastu 'alaykum bi-wakil.*

This liberates the bearer of truth from a subtle arrogance. We are responsible for honesty, reminder, good boundaries, and faithful delivery. We are not responsible for making every soul respond.

[PAUSE]

That means you can walk away from a violating conversation without feeling that you abandoned Allah's cause. You can keep reminding without imagining that another person's guidance sits in your hand. You can be grieved by denial without acting as if denial has injured the truth itself.

And the sentence *li-kulli naba'in mustaqarr* steadies the heart further. Every true report will arrive at its resting place. Revelation is not on a timer set by the mocker. Reality will eventually disclose what words only announced.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs teach composure in the presence of denial.

Your people may deny the truth, but the truth remains the truth.
The Messenger is not their wakil.
Every serious report has its settled arrival.
Those who wade mockingly into Allah's verses are not to be sat with in that moment.
If forgetfulness happens, obedience resumes as soon as remembrance returns.
The people of taqwa do not carry the mockers' account.
But they still carry reminder.

[PAUSE]

So the believer learns two difficult forms of maturity at once: not trying to control what only Allah controls, and not staying where revelation is being violated just to prove something to himself. Sometimes the holiest thing in the room is the person who gets up.

### Questions to Carry

1. Are there spaces, conversations, or media habits in my life where I have confused prolonged exposure with courage, even though they are eroding reverence?
2. Where do I still act as though another person's response to truth is mine to control rather than mine to faithfully address and then leave with Allah?

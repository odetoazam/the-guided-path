# Do Not Turn Back on Your Heels

## Surah Al-Anam (6:70-73)

## Introduction

One of the strangest tragedies of the soul is that it can receive guidance and still be tempted to walk backward.

Not because the path was unclear.
Not because the idols ever became capable.
Not because truth changed.

But because dunya can make seriousness feel heavy and triviality feel easy.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs gather that whole tragedy and answer it with stunning force.

The surah first says to leave the people who have turned religion into play and diversion and have been deceived by worldly life, but still keep reminding with the Qur'an before any soul becomes delivered over to what it earned. Then it portrays relapse after guidance as something humiliating and disorienting: turning back on one's heels, like someone lured off course by devils into bewildered wandering even while companions call him toward the road. Then the answer comes with final clarity: the guidance of Allah is the only guidance. We have been commanded to submit, establish prayer, fear Him, and remember that we will be gathered back to Him, the One who created the heavens and the earth in truth, whose command "Be" is reality itself, and whose dominion stands on the Day the Trumpet is blown.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **after Allah has guided you, there is nothing more irrational than walking back toward what cannot benefit or harm you, and the only way to stay steady is to live under the gravity of His guidance, His gathering, and His final dominion.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. Religion Can Be Turned into Play

The opening is severe:

*Wa dhari alladhina ittakhadhu dinahum la'iban wa lahwan.*

Leave those who have taken their religion as play and diversion.

[PAUSE]

This is not describing innocent lightness. *La'ib* is play, performance, unserious manipulation. *Lahw* is diversion, that which distracts and occupies away from what matters. Together they describe a soul that does not approach religion as reality, responsibility, and truth, but as something to toy with, reduce, weaponize, or use for passing amusement.

Then:

*Wa gharrathumu al-hayatu al-dunya.*

The worldly life deluded them.

Dunya here is not simply mentioned as a neutral setting. It has deceived them. The glitter of nearness and immediacy has made eternity feel remote and seriousness feel excessive.

Yet the command is not to abandon warning:

*Wa dhakkir bihi...*

Remind by it.

The Qur'an still addresses even these people before the final sealing.

### 2. A Soul Can Be Given Over to What It Earned

Then the warning phrase:

*An tubsala nafsun bima kasabat.*

Lest a soul be given over for what it earned.

[PAUSE]

The verb *tubsal* is heavy. Classical tafsir carries the sense of being detained, delivered over, held hostage, surrendered to the consequence of what one earned. The soul is no longer free-moving. It is handed to its deeds.

Then the stripping begins:

*Laysa laha min duni Allahi waliyyun wa la shafi'.*

It has besides Allah no protector and no intercessor.

Then:

*Wa in ta'dil kulla 'adlin la yu'khadh minha.*

Even if it offered every ransom, it would not be accepted.

Nothing can buy back the lost opportunity once the matter has crossed into finality. No substitute, no compensation, no exchange value remains.

### 3. Turning Back After Guidance Is Like Being Lured into Bewilderment

Then the surah asks one of the most devastating rhetorical questions in the passage:

*A-nad'u min duni Allahi ma la yanfa'una wa la yadurruna wa nuraddu 'ala a'qabina ba'da idh hadana Allah?*

Shall we call on besides Allah what cannot benefit us or harm us, and be turned back on our heels after Allah has guided us?

[PAUSE]

The phrase *nuraddu 'ala a'qabina* is not merely "go back." It is to be turned back on our heels. It carries humiliation, reversal, and regression. Guidance had moved you forward. Shirk pulls you backward.

Then comes the parable:

*Ka-alladhi istahwat-hu al-shayatin fi al-ardi hayran.*

Like one whom the devils have lured into the land, bewildered.

*Istahwat-hu* carries force. He was swept away by misleading pull. And *hayran* is bewildered, directionless, unable to orient himself.

Then the tragic tension:

*Lahu ashabun yad'unahu ila al-huda i'tina.*

He has companions calling him to guidance: come to us.

So this is not a person with no access to truth. The call is reaching him. Good company exists. Guidance is being voiced nearby. But he is still wandering because the misleading pull has seized him.

### 4. Allah's Guidance Alone Is Real Guidance

Then comes the corrective:

*Qul inna huda Allahi huwa al-huda.*

Indeed, the guidance of Allah is the guidance.

[PAUSE]

The construction is exclusive and emphatic. Not one guidance among many. Not one helpful option within a broader market of spiritual paths. The guidance.

Then the response demanded of the guided:

*Wa umirna li-nuslima li-rabbi al-'alamin.*

We have been commanded to submit to the Lord of the worlds.

And that submission immediately takes concrete form:

*Wa an aqimu al-salata wa ittaquhu.*

Establish prayer and fear Him.

Guidance is not vague inspiration. It becomes worship, uprightness, and taqwa.

Then the horizon of accountability returns:

*Wa huwa alladhi ilayhi tuhsharun.*

He is the One to whom you will be gathered.

### 5. The Passage Ends by Returning Everything to Creation, Command, and Dominion

Then the final ayah widens the sky:

*Wa huwa alladhi khalaqa al-samawati wa al-arda bi-al-haqq.*

He is the One who created the heavens and the earth in truth.

[PAUSE]

Truth is not only what He says. It is the very mode of His creation.

Then:

*Wa yawma yaqulu kun fa-yakun.*

The Day He says "Be," and it is.

The divine command is not process-dependent in the way creaturely speech is. His word does not struggle toward execution.

Then the ayah says it explicitly:

*Qawluhu al-haqq.*

His word is the truth.

Then:

*Wa lahu al-mulku yawma yunfakhu fi al-sur.*

His is the dominion the Day the Trumpet is blown.

This is not a temporary dominion revealed only at the end, but a dominion made undeniable on that Day.

And the closing names gather all of this:

*'Alimu al-ghaybi wa al-shahadah wa huwa al-hakimu al-khabir.*

Knower of the unseen and the witnessed, the Wise, the All-Aware.

So the One to whom we are told to submit is not merely powerful. He knows hidden and visible, acts with wisdom, and is intimately aware.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The Real Danger Is Not Ignorance Alone but Trivialization

These ayahs make a frightening diagnosis: a person can still speak the language of religion while having turned it into *la'ib* and *lahw*.

[PAUSE]

That means the opposite of seriousness is not always open atheism or formal rejection. Sometimes it is treating revelation as content, as theater, as identity-performance, as material for ego, as background noise. The self no longer meets it as judgment and rescue, only as something to play around with.

And dunya helps that happen. Not because created life is evil, but because when it deceives, it makes the urgent feel optional and the eternal feel abstract. The soul begins to prefer amusement over awakening.

So one of the deepest forms of piety is recovering weight. Let the Qur'an be heavy enough to interrupt you. Let prayer be serious enough to reorganize you. Let gathering before Allah be real enough to humble your distractions.

### Theme 2: Guidance Must Be Guarded from Relapse

The image of turning back on one's heels after guidance should stay with us.

Because relapse is rarely experienced from inside as, "I am now making an obviously absurd choice." It often feels more like drift, like confusion, like the slow pull of old attachments, like devils widening the distance while better voices still call from nearby.

[PAUSE]

That is why the ayah gives us both diagnosis and treatment.

Diagnosis:
There are forces that lure.
There is bewilderment.
There is the possibility of moving backward even after guidance.

Treatment:
*Inna huda Allahi huwa al-huda.*
Submission.
Prayer.
Taqwa.
Remembering the gathering.
Remembering creation in truth.
Remembering that His command is reality and His dominion is final.

People do not stay guided merely by admiring guidance. They stay guided by inhabiting its disciplines until the heart becomes less available for reversal.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs close the pre-Ibrahim movement with immense seriousness.

Some people turn religion into play and diversion and are deceived by dunya.
Still, the Qur'an keeps warning before a soul is given over to what it earned.
No protector, no intercessor, no ransom can help once the matter is sealed.
Turning back after guidance is like being lured into bewildered wandering while true companions still call you toward the road.
Allah's guidance alone is real guidance.
That guidance becomes submission, prayer, taqwa, and readiness for gathering.
And all of it stands beneath the Lord who created the heavens and the earth in truth, whose word is truth, whose command "Be" is effective, and whose dominion will be undeniable on the Day of the Trumpet.

[PAUSE]

So the believer must resist two temptations at once: treating religion lightly, and treating guidance as something that can survive without being guarded. The road forward is not mysterious. Allah has named it. The real danger is choosing to turn around.

1. In what ways has dunya made seriousness with revelation feel heavier to me than diversion?
2. What concrete practices of submission, prayer, and taqwa are actually guarding my guidance from slow reversal?

# I Only Follow What Is Revealed

## Surah Al-Anam (6:50-51)

[ayah:6:50] قُل لَّآ أَقُولُ لَكُمْ عِندِى خَزَآئِنُ ٱللَّهِ وَلَآ أَعْلَمُ ٱلْغَيْبَ وَلَآ أَقُولُ لَكُمْ إِنِّى مَلَكٌ ۖ إِنْ أَتَّبِعُ إِلَّا مَا يُوحَىٰٓ إِلَىَّ ۚ قُلْ هَلْ يَسْتَوِى ٱلْأَعْمَىٰ وَٱلْبَصِيرُ ۚ أَفَلَا تَتَفَكَّرُونَ

[ayah:6:51] وَأَنذِرْ بِهِ ٱلَّذِينَ يَخَافُونَ أَن يُحْشَرُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰ رَبِّهِمْ ۙ لَيْسَ لَهُم مِّن دُونِهِۦ وَلِىٌّ وَلَا شَفِيعٌ لَّعَلَّهُمْ يَتَّقُونَ

*Qul la aqulu lakum 'indi khaza'inu Allahi wa la a'lamu al-ghayba wa la aqulu lakum inni malak. In attabi'u illa ma yuha ilayya. Qul hal yastawi al-a'ma wa al-basir. Afala tatafakkarun.*

*Wa andhir bihi alladhina yakhafuna an yuhsharu ila rabbihim laysa lahum min dunihi waliyyun wa la shafi'un la'allahum yattaqun.*

**Say, "I do not tell you that I have the depositories of Allah or that I know the unseen, nor do I tell you that I am an angel. I only follow what is revealed to me." Say, "Is the blind equivalent to the seeing? Then will you not give thought?" And warn by it those who fear that they will be gathered before their Lord - for them besides Him will be no protector and no intercessor - that they might become righteous.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

People often want revelation on terms that protect their ego.

They want a messenger who can satisfy spectacle.
They want access to hidden knowledge on demand.
They want religion to come attached to worldly control and visible exceptionality.

[PAUSE]

But these ayahs strip the prophetic role back to its truthful center.

The Messenger is commanded to say plainly: I do not claim Allah's خزائن. I do not claim independent knowledge of the unseen. I do not claim to be an angel. I only follow what is revealed to me. Then the surah asks the question that exposes the whole matter: is the blind equal to the seeing? And from there it directs warning not toward the loudest mockers first, but toward those who still fear being gathered before their Lord, those who know there will be no wali and no intercessor besides Him. They are reachable because the fear is still alive in them.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **the Messenger's greatness lies not in possessing divine prerogatives but in perfectly following revelation, and the people most able to benefit from warning are those whose hearts still tremble at the thought of being gathered before Allah alone.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Ayah Begins by Denying Three False Expectations

The first ayah is built through successive negations:

*La aqulu lakum 'indi khaza'inu Allahi.*

I do not say to you that with me are the treasuries of Allah.

[PAUSE]

*Khaza'in* suggests stores, reserves, held provisions, the divine depositories from which outcomes, provision, and created distributions emerge. The Messenger is told to deny ownership or independent control over any of that.

Then:

*Wa la a'lamu al-ghayb.*

And I do not know the unseen.

This is crucial. The Prophet is not being introduced to us through inflated spiritual mystique. He is being protected from it. Unseen knowledge belongs to Allah unless specifically revealed.

Then:

*Wa la aqulu lakum inni malak.*

And I do not say to you that I am an angel.

Again the ayah clears away the false demand that messengership must appear in non-human form to be worthy of reception.

### 2. The Prophet's Identity Is Defined by Following Revelation

After the negations comes the positive center:

*In attabi'u illa ma yuha ilayya.*

I only follow what is revealed to me.

[PAUSE]

This is one of the purest definitions of prophetic dignity in the Qur'an. Not self-generated authority. Not autonomous mastery. Not theatrical exceptionality. Following revelation.

And the verb *attabi'u* matters. I follow. The Messenger is the most perfectly responsive servant. Revelation does not merely pass through him outwardly. He follows it completely.

That is why the ayah then asks:

*Hal yastawi al-a'ma wa al-basir?*

Is the blind equal to the seeing?

The contrast is not mainly about physical eyesight. It is about basirah, the capacity to perceive what revelation makes clear. One person receives the limits of prophetic truth and sees rightly. Another keeps demanding what was never promised and remains blind.

### 3. Warning Belongs Especially to Those Still Reachable

Then the next ayah says:

*Wa andhir bihi alladhina yakhafuna an yuhsharu ila rabbihim.*

Warn by it those who fear being gathered to their Lord.

[PAUSE]

This is not because others never need warning. It is because these are the people in whom warning can still do its work. Fear here is not mere panic. It is moral receptivity. The heart still believes that gathering is real and consequential.

Then the ayah deepens the scene:

*Laysa lahum min dunihi waliyyun wa la shafi'.*

They have besides Him no protector and no intercessor.

Every false support is removed. No one owns the standing. No one can provide the decisive shelter apart from Allah's permission and mercy.

Then:

*La'allahum yattaqun.*

So that they may develop taqwa.

The warning is not an end in itself. It is meant to mature into a guarded life, a heart that lives carefully before Allah.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: We Corrupt Religion When We Demand From Messengers What Belongs Only to Allah

These ayahs are deeply cleansing because they separate messengership from divinization.

[PAUSE]

The Messenger does not own Allah's خزائن.
He does not independently know the unseen.
He is not an angel.

Why is this so important? Because the soul loves to displace obedience into fascination. If the messenger can be turned into a figure of overwhelming exceptionality detached from servant-following, then people can admire religion without submitting to it.

But the ayah keeps the center where it belongs: the Messenger's honor is that he follows what is revealed better than anyone.

That means love for the Prophet must deepen our following, not replace it with vague reverence stripped of obedience.

### Theme 2: Real Sight Is Moral and Spiritual, Not Merely Informational

*Hal yastawi al-a'ma wa al-basir?*

The question sounds obvious, but the Qur'an is aiming it far deeper than appearances.

[PAUSE]

Two people may hear the same recitation.
Two people may witness the same prophet.
Two people may share the same outward world.

And yet one sees while the other remains blind.

Why? Because seeing in the Qur'an is not just data intake. It is rightly receiving, rightly situating, rightly responding. Basirah is when the soul allows revelation to tell it what matters, what does not, who the Messenger is, who Allah is, and where the human being stands.

### Theme 3: The Most Reachable Hearts Are Often the Ones Already Afraid

The ayah does not say, "Warn those who feel strongest, smartest, or most socially secure." It says warn those who fear the gathering.

[PAUSE]

That is a beautiful principle. A heart that still fears meeting Allah is a heart that has not completely died. It may be fragile. It may be burdened. It may feel small. But it is open.

And perhaps many of us need that reassurance. Feeling the weight of the gathering is not a sign of spiritual failure. It may be one of the last mercies keeping the heart alive and reachable. The danger is not trembling. The danger is becoming so armored that the gathering no longer disturbs us at all.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs do two powerful things at once.

They purify our understanding of the Messenger by stripping away claims that belong only to Allah: divine خزائن, independent unseen knowledge, and angelic nature.
Then they define prophetic nobility by one thing: following what is revealed.
After that, they separate blindness from sight and direct warning especially toward those whose fear of being gathered before their Lord has kept them inwardly reachable.

[PAUSE]

That leaves us with a very sober mercy. The truth does not need mythologized intermediaries; it needs obedient messengers and hearts willing to see. And one of the clearest signs that a heart may still benefit is that it has not become casual about the meeting with Allah. If that fear is still in us, then the door is still open. The right response is not to run from the warning, but to let it mature into taqwa while revelation is still reaching us.

### Questions to Carry

1. Have I ever demanded from revelation forms of spectacle, control, or exceptionality that actually belong only to Allah?
2. In what ways is my love for the Prophet increasing my following of revelation rather than remaining only admiration?
3. Do I treat fear of the gathering before Allah as a problem to suppress, or as a mercy that may still be keeping my heart alive?
4. Where am I still hearing the Qur'an with information-level contact but not with the seeing that leads to change?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs teach that the Messenger's honor is perfect following of revelation, not possession of divine prerogatives, and that the hearts most ready to benefit are the ones still unsettled by the coming gathering before Allah alone.**

### Du'a

Allahumma, let us know Your Messenger through truthful following rather than distorted expectation. Give us basirah instead of blindness, and keep the fear of being gathered before You alive in us until it ripens into taqwa, obedience, and sincere return. Amin.

# If He Took Your Hearing, Who Would Return It?

## Surah Al-Anam (6:46)

[ayah:6:46] قُلْ أَرَءَيْتُمْ إِنْ أَخَذَ ٱللَّهُ سَمْعَكُمْ وَأَبْصَـٰرَكُمْ وَخَتَمَ عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِكُم مَّنْ إِلَـٰهٌ غَيْرُ ٱللَّهِ يَأْتِيكُم بِهِ ۗ ٱنظُرْ كَيْفَ نُصَرِّفُ ٱلْـَٔايَـٰتِ ثُمَّ هُمْ يَصْدِفُونَ

*Qul a-ra'aytum in akhadha Allahu sam'akum wa absarakum wa khatama 'ala qulubikum man ilahun ghayru Allahi ya'tikum bihi. Unzur kayfa nusarrifu al-ayati thumma hum yasdifun.*

**Say, "Have you considered: if Allah should take away your hearing and your sight and set a seal upon your hearts, which deity other than Allah could bring it back to you?" Look how We diversify the signs; then still they turn away.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

We often move through life as if our basic capacities belong to us by default.

We hear and barely think about hearing.
We see and barely think about sight.
We process, judge, feel, and decide through the heart without pausing over how fragile that inner faculty really is.

[PAUSE]

This ayah tears that illusion open.

It asks the listener to imagine what would remain if Allah took hearing, took sight, and sealed the heart itself. Then it asks the only question that matters: who besides Allah could ever bring that back? It is an argument about tawhid, but it reaches us through vulnerability. The Qur'an is not only saying, "Allah alone created." It is saying, "Allah alone sustains the very channels by which you receive reality, and Allah alone could restore them if they were withdrawn."

[PAUSE]

The hook of the ayah is this: **the faculties by which we encounter truth are themselves divine trusts, and realizing how completely their return belongs to Allah should break the illusion of self-sufficiency and false reliance.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Ayah Begins with Forced Reflection

Again the ayah starts:

*A-ra'aytum.*

Have you considered?

[PAUSE]

This is the Qur'anic way of not letting the listener stay vague. It forces imagination into service of truth. Do not answer with slogans. See the scene. Feel the loss. Then answer honestly.

### 2. Hearing, Sight, and Heart Are Gathered Together

Then the ayah names three gifts:

*Sam'akum wa absarakum wa khatama 'ala qulubikum.*

Your hearing, your sights, and a seal upon your hearts.

[PAUSE]

The sequence is profound. Hearing and sight are the outer avenues of reception. The heart is the inner center where meaning lands, is processed, accepted, rejected, or sealed away from.

So the ayah is not merely asking about disability. It is asking about total epistemic dependence. What if the channels closed? What if the inner organ of reception itself were stamped shut?

And the verb *khatama* intensifies the warning. A seal is not mere weakness. It is closure. Access blocked. Entry stopped. The soul is being reminded that inner openness is not something it owns unconditionally.

### 3. The Question Destroys Every False God

Then:

*Man ilahun ghayru Allahi ya'tikum bihi?*

Which god other than Allah could bring it back to you?

[PAUSE]

The question is devastating because it forces practical theology again. Not: what do you claim in ritual language? But: who can actually restore the lost gift?

If hearing disappears, who returns it?
If sight is removed, who restores it?
If the heart itself is sealed, who can reopen it?

The answer is built into the question. No one besides Allah.

That means every form of shirk collapses here, because the supposed partners do not possess even the most basic restorative authority over the faculties by which we know the world and answer revelation.

### 4. The Signs Are Varied, but the Turning Away Persists

Then the ayah ends:

*Unzur kayfa nusarrifu al-ayati thumma hum yasdifun.*

Look how We diversify the signs; then still they turn away.

[PAUSE]

*Nusarrifu* suggests turning, varying, presenting from multiple angles, repeating in different forms. The Qur'an's proofs do not come in one flat register. They arrive through history, creation, argument, warning, mercy, inner states, worldly vulnerability, and scenes of the Hereafter.

And yet:

*Thumma hum yasdifun.*

Still they turn away.

The verb carries aversion, turning aside after exposure. The tragedy is not scarcity of signs. It is stubborn refusal despite their repeated diversification.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Our Capacity to Receive Truth Is Not Self-Owned

This ayah is deeply humbling because it does not only speak about revelation "out there." It speaks about the instruments by which we encounter revelation at all.

[PAUSE]

We often talk as though guidance were merely a matter of what is presented to us. But the ayah reminds us that even the ability to hear rightly, see rightly, and keep a heart open enough to process truth is already from Allah.

That should make a believer more grateful and more afraid in the healthiest way. Gratitude, because every act of hearing Qur'an and actually receiving it is a gift. Fear, because a person can arrogantly misuse the faculties he has until he forgets that their continued function is not guaranteed.

### Theme 2: Tawhid Becomes Clear When We Think in Terms of Restoration

Many people can verbally admit that Allah is the Creator while emotionally living as though other powers hold decisive control. This ayah cuts through that fog by shifting from origin to restoration.

Who can bring it back?

[PAUSE]

That question exposes dependency with unusual force. We may imagine ourselves buffered by means, medicine, institutions, or people. And of course means matter in the created order. But the heart of tawhid is remembering that no means operates independently and no secondary cause can restore what Allah does not permit to be restored.

This does not make us passive. It makes us truthful. We use means, but we do not worship them. We seek help, but we do not transfer lordship to the helpers.

### Theme 3: Turning Away After Repeated Signs Is a Moral, Not Informational, Problem

The end of the ayah matters because it saves us from a common lie: "I just haven't seen enough yet."

The signs are already being diversified.
They are already being turned from angle to angle.
They are already arriving through multiple doors.

[PAUSE]

So when a person keeps turning away, the issue is usually no longer lack of evidence. It is some mixture of desire, fear, pride, heedlessness, habit, and resistance at the level of the heart.

That is why the ayah earlier spoke of the seal upon hearts. A turning-away person is not only declining information. He is risking the instrument by which he could still receive it.

## Closing Synthesis

This ayah gathers vulnerability and tawhid into one piercing question.

If Allah took hearing, sight, and the open functioning of the heart, who besides Him could restore them?
No one.
Then the ayah tells us to look at how varied the signs already are.
And yet many still turn away.

[PAUSE]

That should leave us humbled. The very faculties by which we assess truth are already in Allah's hand. So the right response to revelation is not intellectual vanity but grateful dependence. We ask Allah not only to show us the signs, but to preserve in us the hearing, the seeing, and the living heart by which the signs can still be received before repeated turning-away becomes one more reason for sealing.

### Questions to Carry

1. Do I treat hearing, sight, and the heart's openness as everyday defaults instead of as ongoing gifts from Allah?
2. Where have I been leaning on secondary means in ways that quietly forget who alone can truly restore what is lost?
3. Have I mistaken my continuing exposure to signs for proof that my heart is still responding well to them?
4. What would change in my relationship with the Qur'an if I approached every encounter asking Allah to keep my faculties alive and unsealed?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**This ayah teaches that even the faculties by which we receive truth belong wholly to Allah, and no god besides Him could restore them once withdrawn, even while the signs keep arriving from every angle.**

### Du'a

Allahumma, preserve our hearing, our sight, and our hearts for Your remembrance. Do not let us rely on means in a way that forgets You, and do not let repeated exposure to Your signs become a witness against us. Keep our inner faculties alive, open, and grateful until we meet You. Amin.

# So the Path of the Criminal Becomes Clear

## Surah Al-Anam (6:55-58)

[ayah:6:55] وَكَذَٰلِكَ نُفَصِّلُ ٱلْـَٔايَـٰتِ وَلِتَسْتَبِينَ سَبِيلُ ٱلْمُجْرِمِينَ

## Introduction

One of the mercies of revelation is that it does not only tell us that truth exists. It draws the lines so clearly that falsehood can no longer pretend to be confusion.

That is what these ayahs do.

[PAUSE]

Allah says He details the signs so that the path of the criminals becomes clear. Then the Prophet is told to say, without negotiation, that he has been forbidden from worshipping what they call upon besides Allah. He is told to say that he will not follow their desires. He is told to say that he stands on a clear proof from his Lord. And he is told to say that the punishment they keep demanding is not in his hands, because judgment belongs to Allah alone.

This passage is doing more than winning an argument with Quraysh. It is exposing a permanent human disease. People want truth if it will submit to their appetites, their timelines, and their demands for control. But revelation comes to do the opposite. It unmasks the road of crime against Allah, refuses the rule of desire, and returns judgment to the One who actually owns it.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **Allah details His signs not merely so we can admire the truth in the abstract, but so we can recognize the path of rebellion, refuse to let desire become religion, and surrender judgment to Him alone.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Signs Are Detailed So a Road Becomes Visible

The opening line is brief, but it gives the purpose of all the clarification that has been unfolding:

*Wa kadhalika nufassilu al-ayati wa li-tastabina sabilu al-mujrimin.*

Thus We detail the signs so that the path of the criminals becomes clear.

[PAUSE]

*Nufassilu* comes from separation and distinction. The signs are not dropped into the world in a blurred heap. Allah lays them out, differentiates them, unfolds them with precision.

And for what end?

*Li-tastabina sabilu al-mujrimin.*

So that the path of the criminals may become evident.

That is striking. Revelation is not only here to make guidance visible. It is also here to expose the road of corruption so plainly that no one can hide inside vagueness forever. The Qur'an clarifies the right path, but in doing so it also strips camouflage away from the wrong one.

### 2. The Prophet Is Defined by Prohibition Before Preference

Then the Messenger is commanded:

*Qul inni nuhitu an a'buda alladhina tad'una min duni Allah.*

Say: I have been forbidden from worshipping those you call upon besides Allah.

[PAUSE]

The wording matters. *Nuhitu* is passive: I have been forbidden. The Prophet does not present creed as a personal taste or an independent conclusion he can revise under pressure. He stands under command.

And what is refused is the whole field of false devotion:

*Alladhina tad'una min duni Allah.*

Those you call upon besides Allah.

Then comes the deeper refusal:

*La attabi'u ahwa'akum.*

I do not follow your desires.

The ayah does not say only "I reject your arguments." It says "I do not follow your desires." That reaches beneath intellectual dispute into the engine that drives it. *Ahwa'* are the inclinations, cravings, and preferred pulls of the self. The issue is not lack of data. It is the refusal to let truth rule over want.

Then the verdict on what would happen if desire were obeyed:

*Qad dalaltu idhan wa ma ana mina al-muhtadin.*

I would then have gone astray, and I would not be of the rightly guided.

The sentence is uncompromising. Following desire in the place of revelation is not a small distortion. It is misguidance.

### 3. Clear Proof and Human Desire Stand on Opposite Sides

Then the Prophet is told to say:

*Inni 'ala bayyinatin min rabbi wa kadhdhabtum bihi.*

Indeed I am upon a clear proof from my Lord, and you have denied it.

[PAUSE]

*Bayyinah* carries the sense of something clarified, evident, open enough to stand upon. The phrase *'ala bayyinah* is powerful. I am upon clear proof. Not reaching for it. Not guessing toward it. Standing on it.

That is the contrast the ayahs have been building:

On one side, *ahwa'akum* - your desires.
On the other, *bayyinatin min rabbi* - a clear proof from my Lord.

This is the Qur'anic division again and again. Either a person is carried by revelation from above, or dragged by appetite from within.

### 4. Judgment Belongs to Allah, Not to the Messenger's Hand

Then the ayah addresses their demand for the punishment they keep rushing:

*Ma 'indi ma tasta'jiluna bihi.*

I do not have what you seek to hasten.

[PAUSE]

The Prophet does not control the punishment. He does not summon it on demand to satisfy a crowd's defiant timetable.

Then comes one of the most central declarations in the surah:

*Ini al-hukmu illa lillah.*

Judgment belongs to none but Allah.

This includes decision, authority, final ruling. The matter is not in the hands of mockers, nor even in the hands of the Messenger except as a servant receiving command.

Then:

*Yaqussu al-haqqa wa huwa khayru al-fasilin.*

He sets forth the truth, and He is the best of those who decide.

The One who owns judgment is also the One who tells the truth exactly and separates matters perfectly. That is why the passage begins with *nufassilu al-ayat* and ends with *khayru al-fasilin*. The surah is full of divine clarification because Allah is the One who truly distinguishes.

The next ayah deepens the point:

*Law anna 'indi ma tasta'jiluna bihi la-qudiya al-amru bayni wa baynakum.*

If I had what you seek to hasten, the matter would have been decided between me and you.

There is a strange honesty here. The Prophet is not pretending he owes softness to rebellion as such. If the matter were in his hand, it would be over. But it is not in his hand.

Then the closing sentence returns everything to Allah's knowledge:

*Wa Allahu a'lamu bi-al-zalimin.*

Allah is most knowing of the wrongdoers.

He knows who is merely confused, who is wavering, who is reachable, and who is committed to wrongdoing beneath the performance of argument.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: One of Revelation's Mercies Is That It Makes Falsehood Easier to Recognize

Many of us imagine mercy only as comfort.

But these ayahs show another form of mercy: clarification.

[PAUSE]

There is great mercy in a world where the road of rebellion can still be recognized before we walk too far down it. There is mercy in a Book that exposes the logic of the criminal soul, the way desire disguises itself as principle, the way arrogance turns impatience into a challenge against Allah.

And this means one of the uses of Qur'an is not only to give us pleasant spiritual feelings. It is to train moral sight. We read so that *sabil al-mujrimin* becomes visible not only in history, not only in other people, but in the hidden tendencies of our own souls. Where do we resist obedience? Where do we prefer blur over clarity because clarity would cost us something?

The ayah is not inviting us merely to point outward and say, "Now I know who the criminals are." It is inviting us to become less vulnerable to the criminal path in ourselves.

### Theme 2: The Soul Goes Astray When It Wants Truth on Its Own Terms

This passage names three demands that corrupt religion.

First: worship what people already desire.
Second: follow the appetites of the crowd.
Third: force Allah's judgment onto our preferred timetable.

[PAUSE]

The answer to all three is the same. No.

The Prophet says: I have been forbidden.
He says: I do not follow your desires.
He says: I am on clear proof.
He says: the judgment belongs to Allah.

That sequence is a complete map for spiritual survival.

So much confusion enters religion when we want revelation to baptize what we already wanted, justify what we already preferred, or act when we demand it act. But servanthood begins where that posture ends. The servant does not ask Allah to ratify his whims. The servant lets Allah expose them.

And there is something deeply healing in that. We do not have to carry the burden of controlling outcomes, avenging truth on our schedule, or forcing final resolution now. We need only stand on the *bayyinah*, refuse to follow *ahwa'*, and leave *hukm* where it belongs.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs move with severe mercy.

Allah details His signs so the path of the criminals becomes clear.
The Prophet refuses worship of anything besides Allah.
He refuses to follow human desires.
He stands on a clear proof from his Lord.
He refuses to pretend that punishment and final judgment are in his hand.
And the whole matter returns to Allah, who knows the wrongdoers best and decides better than anyone.

[PAUSE]

That leaves us with a searching question. When we want religion to bend, what exactly are we asking it to bend toward? Usually it is not truth. Usually it is desire, impatience, or the ego's demand to manage timing and outcome. But the Qur'an came to free us from that prison. It details the signs until the road becomes visible, and then it asks us to choose whether we will stand on clear proof or keep following ourselves.

### Questions to Carry

1. In what areas of my life am I most tempted to prefer my desires over what Allah has made clear?
2. When I feel impatient with Allah's timing, do I still remember that judgment belongs to Him alone and that He knows the wrongdoers better than I do?

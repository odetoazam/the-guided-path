# Do Not Drive Away the Ones Who Want His Face

## Surah Al-Anam (6:52-54)

[ayah:6:52] وَلَا تَطْرُدِ ٱلَّذِينَ يَدْعُونَ رَبَّهُم بِٱلْغَدَوٰةِ وَٱلْعَشِىِّ يُرِيدُونَ وَجْهَهُۥ ۖ مَا عَلَيْكَ مِنْ حِسَابِهِم مِّن شَىْءٍ وَمَا مِنْ حِسَابِكَ عَلَيْهِم مِّن شَىْءٍ فَتَطْرُدَهُمْ فَتَكُونَ مِنَ ٱلظَّـٰلِمِينَ

[ayah:6:53] وَكَذَٰلِكَ فَتَنَّا بَعْضَهُم بِبَعْضٍ لِّيَقُولُوٓا۟ أَهَـٰٓؤُلَآءِ مَنَّ ٱللَّهُ عَلَيْهِم مِّنۢ بَيْنِنَآ ۗ أَلَيْسَ ٱللَّهُ بِأَعْلَمَ بِٱلشَّـٰكِرِينَ

**And do not send away those who call upon their Lord morning and afternoon, seeking His Face. Not upon you is anything of their account, and not upon them is anything of your account, so were you to send them away, you would be among the wrongdoers. And thus We have tried some of them through others that they might say, "Is it these whom Allah has favored among us?" Is not Allah most knowing of those who are grateful? And when those who believe in Our signs come to you, say, "Peace be upon you. Your Lord has written mercy upon Himself: that any of you who does wrong in ignorance and then repents after that and reforms - indeed, He is Forgiving and Merciful."**

## Introduction

Religious communities are often tempted by the same ugly bargain:

Keep the sincere, but hide the unimpressive.
Keep the message, but curate the people.
Keep the truth, but make sure it is socially decorated by the right faces.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs shatter that instinct.

The Messenger is told not to drive away the people who call on their Lord morning and evening seeking only His Face. Their social status is irrelevant before the reality of their devotion. Their account is not his burden, nor his theirs. To cast them aside for the sake of optics would be ظلم. Then the surah exposes the social arrogance underneath the objection: "Are these the ones Allah has favored among us?" Yes, and Allah knows best who is truly grateful. Then the tone turns astonishingly gentle. When these believers come, the Messenger is told to greet them with peace and to announce that their Lord has written mercy upon Himself. Even if one of them sins ignorantly, if he repents and repairs, the door remains open.

[PAUSE]

The hook of this passage is this: **Allah measures people by sincerity, gratitude, and turning back to Him, not by the social metrics through which human beings try to filter worthiness at the door of faith.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Command Begins with a Refusal to Expel the Sincere

The first line is direct:

*Wa la tatrudi alladhina yad'una rabbahum...*

Do not drive away those who call upon their Lord.

[PAUSE]

*Tatrud* is strong. It is not merely "prefer others over them." It is to repel, dismiss, send off, push to the margins. The ayah is protecting a people from spiritual and social eviction.

And who are they?

*Alladhina yad'una rabbahum bi-al-ghadati wa al-'ashiyyi.*

Those who call upon their Lord morning and evening.

This is the language of constancy. Their day is bracketed by remembrance. Their relationship with Allah is not occasional or decorative.

Then the inner criterion:

*Yuriduna wajhahu.*

They seek His Face.

This is one of the purest summaries of ikhlas in the Qur'an. They are not gathering for prestige, leverage, social belonging, or visible status. They want Allah.

### 2. The Ayah Dismantles Superficial Social Accounting

Then comes a powerful clarification:

*Ma 'alayka min hisabihim min shay' wa ma min hisabika 'alayhim min shay'.*

Nothing of their account is upon you, and nothing of your account is upon them.

[PAUSE]

This cuts through the politics of status. The Messenger is not to judge their worth by whatever the elite imagine counts for something. The real account belongs to Allah.

Then the warning:

*Fa-tatrudahum fa-takuna mina al-zalimin.*

If you were to drive them away, you would be among the wrongdoers.

That is severe. Marginalizing sincere believers to satisfy pride is not etiquette failure only. It is ظلم, because it misplaces honor away from where Allah placed it.

### 3. Social Hierarchy Becomes a Test

Then the next ayah says:

*Wa kadhalika fatanna ba'dahum bi-ba'd.*

Thus We tested some of them by means of others.

[PAUSE]

This is crucial. Difference in social standing, material condition, background, or visible esteem becomes a fitnah - a test that reveals hidden arrogance or hidden gratitude.

And the objection is quoted:

*A-ha'ula'i manna Allahu 'alayhim min baynina?*

Are these the ones Allah has favored among us?

This is the voice of class arrogance before divine grace. It cannot deny the message directly, so it tries to discredit the recipients of mercy.

Then the answer:

*Alaysa Allahu bi-a'lama bi-al-shakirin?*

Is not Allah most knowing of the grateful?

The real qualification is shukr. Gratitude. Receptive recognition. The heart that knows favor when it comes from Allah.

### 4. The Believers Are Received with Salam and Mercy

Then the passage turns:

*Wa idha ja'aka alladhina yu'minuna bi-ayatina fa-qul salamun 'alaykum.*

When those who believe in Our signs come to you, say: peace be upon you.

[PAUSE]

What a contrast. The people others wished to marginalize are received with salam. Not tolerated coldly. Welcomed.

Then:

*Kataba rabbukum 'ala nafsihi al-rahmah.*

Your Lord has written mercy upon Himself.

This is astonishing tenderness. The same Lord who set boundaries around community honor now opens the horizon of mercy wide.

And that mercy is made morally serious, not vague:

*Man 'amila minkum su'an bi-jahalah thumma taba min ba'dihi wa aslaha...*

Whoever among you does wrong in ignorance, then repents after it and reforms...

The sequence matters. Wrongdoing does not close the door if it is followed by tawbah and islah.

Then the ending:

*Fa-annahu ghafurun rahim.*

Indeed He is Forgiving and Merciful.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The Sincere Are Often the Ones Human Prestige Finds Embarrassing

These ayahs expose a very old disease.

We do not always reject sincerity because it is false.
Sometimes we reject it because it is socially inconvenient.

[PAUSE]

The poor believer, the unimpressive believer, the believer without polished cultural capital, the believer whose sincerity is real but whose status is small - such people become a test for communities that still carry pride in their bloodstream.

Allah's command here teaches us that one of the easiest ways to lose the moral center of a religious project is to start curating nearness by worldly prestige rather than by who actually wants His Face.

### Theme 2: Gratitude, Not Status, Is the Real Qualification for Nearness

*Alaysa Allahu bi-a'lama bi-al-shakirin?*

That question overturns an entire social theology.

[PAUSE]

Who does Allah know to be grateful?
Who recognizes favor when revelation reaches them?
Who receives truth instead of demanding that it pass through the approval of rank?

The grateful person may be socially low and spiritually high. The entitled person may be outwardly elevated and inwardly disqualified. So these ayahs train the community to ask a different question about people: not "How impressive are they among us?" but "How grateful are they before Allah?"

### Theme 3: Mercy and Repentance Belong at the Center of a Community of Faith

The ending of the passage is profoundly healing.

The sincere are not only defended from exclusion.
They are welcomed with salam.
They are told about mercy written upon Allah Himself.
And they are told that even when they fall, repentance and reform reopen the way.

[PAUSE]

This matters because some communities know how to protect status but do not know how to hold people through tawbah. The Qur'an does both: it humiliates arrogance and opens mercy.

That is a very Muhammadan balance. No compromise with pride. No despair for the repentant.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs redraw the map of worth inside the community of revelation.

Those who call upon their Lord morning and evening seeking His Face must not be driven away.
Their account belongs to Allah, not to social vanity.
Class arrogance itself is exposed as a test.
Allah knows best who is truly grateful.
And the believers are received not with distance but with peace, mercy, and a repentance-door that remains open through tawbah and reform.

[PAUSE]

That is a mercy for every heart that ever felt too small to belong near revelation, and a warning to every heart that tries to measure others by the standards of rank, polish, or social usefulness. Allah's door is not guarded by worldly prestige. It is opened by sincerity, gratitude, and return.

### Questions to Carry

1. Have I ever subconsciously measured people's nearness to truth by how socially impressive they seem rather than by their sincerity?
2. Do I honor the ones who quietly seek Allah's Face, or am I more easily impressed by polished visibility?
3. In what ways has social arrogance disguised itself as discernment in my view of religious people?
4. Do I really live as though mercy written upon Allah Himself remains open through tawbah and islah, both for me and for others?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs teach that the ones most worthy of nearness are those who sincerely seek Allah's Face, while social pride is exposed as a test and the repentant believer is received beneath a mercy Allah has written upon Himself.**

### Du'a

Allahumma, protect us from the arrogance that measures people by status instead of gratitude and sincerity. Make us love the people who want Your Face, welcome them with peace, and live under the mercy You have written upon Yourself through repentance, reform, and return. Amin.

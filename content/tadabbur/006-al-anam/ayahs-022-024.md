# They Lied Against Themselves

## Surah Al-Anam (6:22-24)

[ayah:6:22] وَيَوْمَ نَحْشُرُهُمْ جَمِيعًا ثُمَّ نَقُولُ لِلَّذِينَ أَشْرَكُوٓا۟ أَيْنَ شُرَكَآؤُكُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كُنتُمْ تَزْعُمُونَ

[ayah:6:23] ثُمَّ لَمْ تَكُن فِتْنَتُهُمْ إِلَّآ أَن قَالُوا۟ وَٱللَّهِ رَبِّنَا مَا كُنَّا مُشْرِكِينَ

[ayah:6:24] ٱنظُرْ كَيْفَ كَذَبُوا۟ عَلَىٰٓ أَنفُسِهِمْ ۚ وَضَلَّ عَنْهُم مَّا كَانُوا۟ يَفْتَرُونَ

*Wa yawma nahshuruhum jami'an thumma naqulu lilladhina ashraku ayna shuraka'ukum alladhina kuntum taz'umun.*

*Thumma lam takun fitnatuhum illa an qalu wa Allahi rabbina ma kunna mushrikin.*

*Unzur kayfa kadhabu 'ala anfusihim wa dalla 'anhum ma kanu yaftarun.*

**And on the Day We gather them all together, then We will say to those who associated others with Allah, "Where are your partners whom you used to claim?" Then their only recourse will be to say, "By Allah, our Lord, we were not those who associated partners." Look how they lied against their own selves, and all that they used to invent will have vanished from them.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

Some lies survive only because the world still allows them room to breathe.

As long as consequences are delayed, as long as social reinforcement remains, as long as a person can keep moving between distraction and self-justification, a falsehood can feel strangely livable. It can even start to feel like identity. But the Day of Judgment is the day lies lose oxygen. Reality closes in from every side, and what a person spent years protecting can collapse in a single exposed moment.

That is the atmosphere of this passage.

The polytheists are not first shown arguing sophisticated theology. They are shown standing in a gathered exposure. Allah asks them one question: where are the partners you used to claim? And their response is astonishing. Faced with the ruin of what they built their lives around, they do not confess first. They deny again. They swear by Allah Himself that they were not mushrikin.

[PAUSE]

That is one of the most frightening things in the Qur'an: the possibility that falsehood can sink so deep into a person that even when the Day comes, his first instinct is not repentance but one last self-protective lie.

Then the verse speaks with chilling clarity: look how they lied against themselves. That is the real wound. The lie is not only spoken against truth in the abstract. It lands back upon the self. And the fabricated gods, claims, and structures they once used to steady themselves all vanish.

The hook of these ayahs is this: **when the Day strips away illusion, false partners disappear and the soul discovers that the lie it spent years defending was finally a lie against itself.**

[PAUSE]

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. The Scene Begins with Total Gathering

The first ayah opens:

*Wa yawma nahshuruhum jami'an.*

And on the Day We gather them all together.

[PAUSE]

The root *h-sh-r* carries gathering, mustering, driving together after dispersion. It is a word of public exposure. People who were once spread across times, places, social ranks, private narratives, and self-made worlds are brought into one undeniable assembly.

And the word *jami'an* deepens the atmosphere. Altogether. No fragment left outside the scene. No tribe, no ideology, no hidden chamber of self spared from appearing.

That alone begins dismantling shirk. False partners often rely on dispersion. A person can keep different loyalties in different rooms of life. But the Day gathers everything together. What was compartmentalized becomes answerable at once.

### 2. "Where Are Your Partners?" Exposes the Distance Between Claim and Reality

Then Allah says:

*Ayna shuraka'ukum alladhina kuntum taz'umun.*

Where are your partners whom you used to claim?

[PAUSE]

This is not a request for information. It is exposure through question.

The ayah does not simply say, "Your partners are false." It asks for them at the moment when their help would actually matter. Where are they now? The ones you assigned loyalty, fear, hope, mediation, reverence, or protection to - where are they?

And notice the wording:

*shuraka'ukum* - your partners.
*alladhina kuntum taz'umun* - whom you used to claim.

The root *z-'-m* carries claiming, alleging, asserting something that does not carry the stability of truth. So the verse is already hinting that these partners were always living in the realm of asserted fiction rather than established reality.

This is one of the Qur'an's most piercing methods. It lets the falsehood reach its hour of need and then asks it to perform. It never can.

### 3. Their Final Fitnah Is One More Denial

Then comes the shocking next line:

*Thumma lam takun fitnatuhum illa an qalu wa Allahi rabbina ma kunna mushrikin.*

Then their only fitnah was to say: by Allah, our Lord, we were not mushrikin.

[PAUSE]

The word *fitnatuhum* here is extraordinary and layered. In this context it points to what their response amounted to when tested, what came out of them under exposure, the final answer produced by their inward condition. And what comes out?

Not humble confession.
Not broken admission.
Not, "We were wrong."

One more lie.

And not a small lie. They swear by Allah Himself. *Wa Allahi rabbina.* By Allah, our Lord.

That makes the moment even more devastating. The tongue finally acknowledges Allah as Lord, but instead of that acknowledgment producing repentance, it is recruited into denial. This is what entrenched self-deception can do: even a true phrase can be used in the service of one last falsehood.

### 4. The Lie Lands Against the Self and the Fabrication Vanishes

Then the Qur'an commands:

*Unzur.*

Look.

See this carefully. Learn from it.

[PAUSE]

*Kayfa kadhabu 'ala anfusihim.*

How they lied against their own selves.

This is such a devastating formulation. The verse does not merely say they lied. It says the lie was *against themselves*. The self they thought they were protecting through denial was actually the first victim of that denial.

Then:

*Wa dalla 'anhum ma kanu yaftarun.*

And what they used to invent vanished from them.

The root *f-r-y* carries fabrication, forging, inventing what was not true. Those invented structures - false intercessors, false stories, false securities, false theological claims - do not stand with them in the final scene. They go missing exactly when the soul most needs something real.

That is the tragedy of shirk and, more broadly, of every false refuge. It promises support, but at the hour of truth it dissolves.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The Human Being Can Become Loyal to a Lie Deep Enough That Exposure Does Not Instantly Heal Him

Many of us imagine that if reality became undeniable, we would immediately become honest.

If the consequences were fully visible, surely I would stop lying.
If the truth were made plain, surely I would not resist it anymore.

But these ayahs teach something far more sobering.

[PAUSE]

The soul can train itself into falsehood so deeply that even at the moment of exposure, its first instinct is still self-protection rather than surrender.

That should make us very humble about our current patterns. Every time we excuse, blur, rationalize, or rename what we know to be wrong, we are not only making a local mistake. We are shaping what kind of person we are becoming under pressure. We are rehearsing what the self does when cornered by truth.

That is why repentance cannot be postponed casually. We are not static. The more a lie is repeated, the more it begins to feel like part of us. And then one day the person no longer only tells the lie. He inhabits it.

### Theme 2: False Refuges Fail Most Completely at the Moment They Are Most Needed

The question *Ayna shuraka'ukum?* does not only expose ancient idolatry. It reaches every false refuge human beings build.

Where is the status you trusted?
Where is the approval you bent yourself for?
Where is the desire you obeyed like a god?
Where is the crowd you feared more than Allah?
Where is the image that made you feel safe?

[PAUSE]

So much of life is spent arranging these secondary shelters. But the Qur'an keeps teaching us that false shelters fail precisely when the storm becomes ultimate.

That is why tawhid is mercy. Not because it makes life instantly easy, but because it saves us from spending our years pouring devotion into things guaranteed to disappear at the hour we most need reality.

### Theme 3: The Worst Lie Is the One That Injures the Self While Pretending to Protect It

The phrase *kadhabu 'ala anfusihim* should stay with the heart for a long time.

We often lie because we think the lie will protect us.
Protect our image.
Protect our comfort.
Protect our desires.
Protect our social standing.

[PAUSE]

But the Qur'an says the deepest truth is the opposite: the lie lands against the self.

That means every time we refuse truth, we are not merely committing an offense in the abstract. We are carving damage into our own inner reality. The harm returns inward.

This is especially important for spiritual self-deception. A person can imagine that by avoiding honest tawbah, by refusing to name his shirk-like attachments, by denying what his heart truly worships or fears, he is preserving peace. In reality he is lying against himself.

And the most merciful thing that can happen before the Last Day is that this becomes visible to us early enough to change.

## Closing Synthesis

These ayahs unfold with terrifying precision.

All are gathered.
The people of shirk are asked for the partners they once claimed.
Their final tested response is not confession but one more oath-backed denial.
Then the Qur'an names the truth: they lied against themselves, and the fabricated things they once relied on disappeared.

[PAUSE]

That is the architecture: gathering, exposure, denial, verdict, disappearance.

And the lesson is as urgent as it is severe. Every false partner, false refuge, and false story eventually reaches an hour in which it must prove itself. On that Day, invention will not survive contact with reality. The only mercy is to let the lie die now, before it becomes so deeply rooted in us that even exposure still finds us clinging to it.

### Questions to Carry

1. What false refuge in my life would be unable to answer if Allah asked me today, "Where is it now?"
2. Are there truths I keep denying or softening in ways that may be training my soul into deeper self-deception?
3. Where have I mistaken self-protection for honesty, when I may actually have been lying against my own soul?
4. What needs to be confessed and released now before it hardens into the kind of lie that survives all the way to the moment of exposure?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These ayahs teach that when Allah gathers creation and false partners vanish, the soul discovers that the lie it defended was never protection at all, but a lie against itself.**

### Du'a

Allahumma, do not let falsehood sink so deep into us that even exposure does not wake us. Break our false refuges before the Day they fail us completely, make us honest with ourselves before we stand before You, and save us from every lie that injures the very soul it pretends to protect. Amin.

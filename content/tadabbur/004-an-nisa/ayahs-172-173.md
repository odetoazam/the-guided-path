# The Messiah Would Never Be Too Proud

## Surah An-Nisa (4:172-173)

[ayah:4:172] لَّن يَسْتَنكِفَ ٱلْمَسِيحُ أَن يَكُونَ عَبْدًا لِّلَّهِ وَلَا ٱلْمَلَـٰٓئِكَةُ ٱلْمُقَرَّبُونَ ۚ وَمَن يَسْتَنكِفْ عَنْ عِبَادَتِهِۦ وَيَسْتَكْبِرْ فَسَيَحْشُرُهُمْ إِلَيْهِ جَمِيعًا

[ayah:4:173] فَأَمَّا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّـٰلِحَـٰتِ فَيُوَفِّيهِمْ أُجُورَهُمْ وَيَزِيدُهُم مِّن فَضْلِهِۦ ۖ وَأَمَّا ٱلَّذِينَ ٱسْتَنكَفُوا۟ وَٱسْتَكْبَرُوا۟ فَيُعَذِّبُهُمْ عَذَابًا أَلِيمًا وَلَا يَجِدُونَ لَهُم مِّن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ وَلِيًّا وَلَا نَصِيرًا

*Lan yastankifa al-masihu an yakuna 'abdan lillah wa la al-mala'ikatu al-muqarrabun. Wa man yastankif 'an 'ibadatihi wa yastakbir fa sayahshuruhum ilayhi jami'a.*

*Fa amma alladhina amanu wa 'amilu al-salihati fa yuwaffihim ujurahum wa yaziduhum min fadlihi. Wa amma alladhina istankafu wa istakbaru fa yu'adhdhibuhum 'adhaban alima wa la yajiduna lahum min duni Allahi waliyyan wa la nasira.*

**The Messiah would never be too proud to be a servant of Allah, nor would the angels nearest to Him. And whoever is too proud for His worship and acts arrogantly - He will gather them all to Himself. Then as for those who believed and did righteous deeds, He will give them their rewards in full and increase them from His bounty. But as for those who were too proud and arrogant, He will punish them with a painful punishment, and they will find for themselves besides Allah no protector and no helper.**

## Introduction

The Qur'an now answers excess with something more decisive than argument alone: it asks what Isa himself would refuse.

That is the brilliance of these verses. The previous ayah corrected false speech about Allah and restored Isa to his rightful place as messenger of Allah, His word cast to Maryam, and a spirit from Him. But now the surah presses the matter deeper. If the Messiah himself would never consider servanthood to Allah beneath him, then every theology that makes him rise above servanthood has already contradicted the one it claims to honor.

Then the verse widens the frame still further. Not even the angels nearest to Allah are above being His servants. So the issue is not only a correction about Isa. It is a correction about the whole spiritual imagination. Nearness to Allah does not cancel 'ubudiyyah. It perfects it. The more noble the creature, the less fitting arrogance becomes.

Then the passage turns from creatures to us. Whoever shrinks from Allah's worship and acts arrogantly will be gathered back to Him anyway. And when that gathering comes, the line is simple: those who believed and worked righteousness are given their due in full and then more besides. Those who were too proud find punishment and no one to shield them.

The hook of the passage is this: **the highest created beings do not see servanthood to Allah as humiliation, so when humans recoil from worship out of pride, the problem is not the dignity of 'ubudiyyah but the sickness of the ego that wants rank without surrender.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Lan yastankifa al-masihu**: The Verse Does Not Merely Deny Divinity; It Denies Proud Distance From Servanthood

The verse begins with a striking word:

lan yastankifa al-masihu.

The Messiah would never be too proud.

Yastankif is stronger than simple refusal. It carries the sense of shrinking away as though something were beneath one's dignity, drawing back in offended self-regard. The ayah is not only saying that Isa is in fact a servant. It is saying that he would never consider servanthood disgraceful.

[PAUSE]

Then the phrase:

an yakuna 'abdan lillah.

To be a servant of Allah.

The word 'abd is the key. A servant, slave, worshipper, one belonging in utter dependence and devotion. In the Qur'an, this is not a demotion. It is one of the highest creaturely honors. The problem is not 'ubudiyyah. The problem is the ego's inability to love it.

So the verse dismantles excess in an elegant way. It does not only tell us what Isa is not. It tells us what he positively does not disdain. And that is far more searching, because it turns the mirror on our own hearts. What do we consider beneath us?

### 2. **Wa la al-mala'ikatu al-muqarrabun**: Nearness to Allah Deepens Servanthood Rather Than Canceling It

Then the verse adds:

wa la al-mala'ikatu al-muqarrabun.

Nor the angels nearest to Him.

Muqarrabun are the brought-near ones, those granted proximity and honor. This addition is beautiful because it removes the last imagined escape route. If even the nearest angels do not stand above servanthood, then nearness itself cannot be used as an argument for transcendence beyond worship.

[PAUSE]

This corrects a subtle human fantasy. We often imagine that elevation means needing less surrender. More knowledge, more spiritual status, more closeness, more honor - and somehow we feel these should reduce the weight of worship. The Qur'an says the opposite. The nearest are not the least worshipful. They are the least allergic to worship.

That means pride toward 'ibadah is not a sign of greatness. It is a sign of distance.

### 3. **Yastankif ... yastakbir ... fa sayahshuruhum ilayhi jami'a**: Pride Is Named Twice Before the Gathering Is Named Once

Then the verse universalizes:

wa man yastankif 'an 'ibadatihi wa yastakbir.

Whoever recoils from His worship and acts arrogantly.

Notice the pair. First yastankif: treating worship as beneath oneself. Then yastakbir: actively magnifying oneself. The first is aversion to servanthood. The second is inflation of self. Together they form the anatomy of spiritual pride.

[PAUSE]

Then comes the response:

fa sayahshuruhum ilayhi jami'a.

He will gather them all to Himself.

Hashr is gathering, collecting for judgment, leaving no one outside the summons. Jami'a intensifies it: all together. The irony is piercing. The one who thought himself too elevated for worship is still not beyond gathering. He can refuse the سجود of willing servanthood now, but he cannot refuse the return.

Then verse 173 divides the gathered:

fa amma alladhina amanu wa 'amilu al-salihati fa yuwaffihim ujurahum wa yaziduhum min fadlihi.

Yuwaffihim is rich. He gives them their reward in full, complete, without deficiency. Then yaziduhum min fadlihi: and He increases them from His bounty. So the servant who accepted 'ubudiyyah is not diminished by it. He receives what is due and more than what is due.

By contrast:

wa amma alladhina istankafu wa istakbaru.

The same pride pair returns, now as a settled identity. And their end is painful punishment, with no wali and no nasir besides Allah. No intimate protector, no rescuer.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Many of Us Do Not Reject Worship Openly; We Quietly Treat It as Beneath the Self We Are Trying to Be

These verses reach beyond formal doctrine into the psychology of every worshipper.

Most people will not say, "I am too great to worship Allah." The sentence is too exposed. But we may live its subtler forms all the time. Worship feels interruptive. Dependence feels embarrassing. Neediness feels incompatible with the image we are curating. So obedience becomes something we negotiate around rather than surrender into.

[PAUSE]

That is one way to sit with yastankif. It is not always loud rebellion. Sometimes it is a refined recoil, an inward flinch before anything that places us clearly in the position of servant. We do not mind spiritual vocabulary. We mind the abasement of needing Allah in a way that dethrones our self-story.

The ayah heals that by showing us the noblest created beings. Isa does not recoil. The nearest angels do not recoil. The issue, then, is not that worship lowers us. The issue is that pride has miseducated our sense of dignity.

Real dignity is not found in escaping servanthood. It is found in entering it without resentment.

### Theme 2: Allah Answers Willing Servanthood With More Than Justice

There is another tenderness in verse 173. The believer who accepts faith and righteous action is not merely compensated.

The verse says Allah will give them their rewards in full, then increase them from His bounty. This is one of the most beautiful patterns in the surah: justice is never the whole story with Allah. He gives due measure, then فضل. More. Excess of generosity after exactness.

[PAUSE]

That matters because pride often whispers that worship is loss. If you bow, you become smaller. If you submit, you give up too much. If you accept being 'abd, you will be deprived of stature. The Qur'an answers that lie by showing the opposite. The servant who does not recoil from Allah receives both his due and something beyond his due.

Meanwhile the proud person, who tried to preserve himself through distance from worship, is left with what pride always finally reveals: exposure, punishment, and the collapse of every borrowed support. No wali. No nasir. Pride looks like self-protection until judgment arrives.

So the passage is not merely threatening arrogance. It is inviting us into a different understanding of honor. The most honored created beings are not allergic to servanthood. Why are we?

## Closing Synthesis

These verses begin with a decisive theological criterion: the Messiah would never consider being a servant of Allah beneath him, nor would the angels brought near.

Then they name the human pathology beneath refusal of worship: recoiling from 'ibadah and magnifying the self.

Then they answer that pride with universal gathering, followed by two divergent outcomes: full reward plus added bounty for the believing servants, and painful punishment without protector or helper for the proud.

[PAUSE]

That is the architecture: honored servanthood, exposed pride, inevitable gathering, then justice and bounty on one side and lonely punishment on the other.

### Questions to Carry

1. Where in my life do I still treat dependence on Allah as beneath the person I want to appear to be?
2. Do I actually see 'ubudiyyah as honor, or do I still associate it with diminishment?
3. What forms of subtle spiritual recoil have been keeping me from willing worship?
4. When I imagine greatness, do I imagine the humility of Isa and the nearest angels, or only the inflation of the ego?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These verses teach that true nearness to Allah never produces disdain for servanthood, so whoever recoils from worship out of pride is not preserving dignity but opposing the very honor that the noblest creatures embrace.**

### Du'a

Allahumma remove from our hearts every quiet recoil from being Your servants. Teach us to love 'ubudiyyah the way Your noblest creatures do, without resentment and without bargaining. Do not let pride make worship feel beneath us, and answer our imperfect but willing servanthood with full reward and more from Your bounty. Ameen.

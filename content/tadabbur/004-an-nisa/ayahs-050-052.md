# When Falsehood Starts Looking Guided

## Surah An-Nisa (4:50-52)

[ayah:4:50] ٱنظُرْ كَيْفَ يَفْتَرُونَ عَلَى ٱللَّهِ ٱلْكَذِبَ ۖ وَكَفَىٰ بِهِۦٓ إِثْمًا مُّبِينًا

[ayah:4:51] أَلَمْ تَرَ إِلَى ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ نَصِيبًا مِّنَ ٱلْكِتَـٰبِ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱلْجِبْتِ وَٱلطَّـٰغُوتِ وَيَقُولُونَ لِلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ هَـٰٓؤُلَآءِ أَهْدَىٰ مِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ سَبِيلًا

[ayah:4:52] أُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ لَعَنَهُمُ ٱللَّهُ ۖ وَمَن يَلْعَنِ ٱللَّهُ فَلَن تَجِدَ لَهُۥ نَصِيرًا

*Unzur kayfa yaftaruna 'ala Allahi al-kadhiba wa kafa bihi ithman mubina.*

*Alam tara ila alladhina utu nasiban min al-kitabi yu'minuna bil-jibti wa al-taghuti wa yaquluna lilladhina kafaru ha'ula'i ahda mina alladhina amanu sabila.*

*Ula'ika alladhina la'anahumu Allah wa man yal'ani Allahu fa lan tajida lahu nasira.*

**Look at how they fabricate lies against Allah, and that alone is enough as a plain sin. Have you not seen those who were given a share of the Book believing in false superstition and rebellious powers, and saying of those who disbelieve, "These are more guided in path than those who believe"? Those are the ones Allah has cursed, and whoever Allah curses, you will never find for them any helper.**

## Introduction

There are moments when a person's moral collapse becomes visible not only in what they do, but in what they start calling good.

That is a frightening stage. Before it, there is still inner conflict. A person may do wrong while still knowing it is wrong. He may compromise while still feeling the tear inside conscience. But after a certain point the damage ripens further. Falsehood is no longer merely practiced. It is defended, admired, and described as the better path.

These verses stand inside that moment of inversion.

The surah has already been exposing envy, self-display, and the corruption of revelation into status. Now it takes us one step deeper. Some people were given a share of scripture. They were not blank slates. They inherited language of truth, memory of revelation, and some real portion of knowledge. Yet that inherited share did not protect them from preferring distortion. Instead, the verses show a soul so bent by fabrication and rivalry that it can look at jibt and taghut, then look at disbelievers, and finally pronounce them more guided than believers.

This is not only a political miscalculation. It is a spiritual catastrophe. The scale has flipped.

The hook of the passage is this: **one of the surest signs that the heart has drifted far is that it no longer merely tolerates falsehood, but starts naming falsehood as guidance.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Unzur kayfa yaftaruna**: Revelation First Makes You Look

The passage opens with a command:

**unzur**.

Look.

The root **n-z-r** is sight, attentive beholding, not a passing glance. Revelation is telling the listener to inspect the ugliness carefully, because some forms of moral collapse have to be seen in order to be feared properly.

Then comes the verb:

**yaftaruna**.

They fabricate. They invent. They forge.

The root **f-r-y** carries cutting, splitting, producing something as though carved out. In Quranic usage, **iftira'** becomes fabricated attribution, making something up and fastening it onto Allah as though it belonged there. This is not ordinary mistake. It is falsification with religious pretension.

[PAUSE]

And what are they fabricating?

**'ala Allahi al-kadhiba**.

Against Allah, the lie.

That wording is heavy. The lie is not floating in abstraction. It is set "upon" Allah, attributed upward, pinned to the divine name. That is why the verse ends the sentence with **wa kafa bihi ithman mubina**. That alone is enough as a clear sin. No further amplification is needed. To lie about Allah is already self-exposing.

This beginning matters because the next verses are not describing random confusion. They are describing what grows after religious falsification has already been normalized. Once a person becomes comfortable placing lies on Allah, the moral scale underneath everything else begins to warp.

### 2. **Utu nasiban min al-kitab**: A Share of Revelation Is Not the Same as Faithfulness to Revelation

Then the Quran says:

**alladhina utu nasiban min al-kitab**.

Those who were given a share of the Book.

The passive verb **utu** from **a-t-y** matters. What they have was given. Revelation was not their achievement. It was a bestowed gift. Then comes **nasib**, a share, portion, allotted part. The wording is careful. It does not say they were empty of scripture. It says they had a portion.

[PAUSE]

That is one of the most sobering phrases in the Quran. A person can inherit some true language, some real access, some portion of sacred learning, and still not be protected from betrayal of that trust. Possessing a share of the Book is not identical to being reshaped by the Book.

This should humble anyone who lives near religious knowledge. Familiarity is not immunity. Vocabulary is not surrender. Citation is not fidelity. The verse is not only about another community. It is a permanent warning to anyone who can mistake inherited nearness to revelation for actual submission to it.

### 3. **Yu'minuna bil-jibti wa al-taghut**: Belief Can Be Misdirected

Then comes one of the sharpest shocks in the passage:

**yu'minuna bil-jibti wa al-taghut**.

They believe in jibt and taghut.

The verb **yu'minuna** from **a-m-n** is the ordinary Quranic verb of faith, trust, affirming security in what is true. That verb is usually dignified by noble objects: Allah, His signs, His messengers, the unseen. Here the verse shocks you by attaching it to **jibt** and **taghut**.

The word **jibt** is broad and dark. Across tafsir it gathers what is false, worthless, superstitious, spiritually empty, severed from divine truth. And **taghut** from **t-gh-y** carries overstepping, rebellion, a force that exceeds rightful bounds and demands a loyalty it has no right to claim.

[PAUSE]

The horror is not only that they respect falsehood. It is that they are described with the language of iman while fastening it to things unworthy of faith. Belief itself has been misdirected.

That is a warning worth lingering inside. Human beings do not cease to believe when they drift from Allah. They often keep the machinery of belief alive, but relocate it. Into power. tribe. ideology. superstition. personality. fear. prestige. The capacity for trust survives; the object is corrupted.

So the verse is not only naming an old theological failure. It is unveiling a permanent law of the soul: if faith is not anchored in Allah, it will still seek somewhere to land.

### 4. **Ha'ula'i ahda... sabila**: The Final Collapse Is Calling the Wrong Path More Guided

Then the passage reaches its most devastating statement:

**wa yaquluna lilladhina kafaru ha'ula'i ahda mina alladhina amanu sabila**.

They say of those who disbelieve: these are more guided in path than those who believe.

Notice the form. They do not merely cooperate politically. They do not merely tolerate the unbelievers strategically. They pronounce a judgment of guidance. And the comparison is explicit: **ahda**. More guided. The comparative of huda.

[PAUSE]

This is where the collapse becomes complete. Once falsehood has been admired long enough, the eye begins to lose its calibration. The one who should have recognized path now confuses road and ruin. What is crooked starts appearing intelligent, sophisticated, effective, realistic, or socially superior. Meanwhile faith starts looking naive, weak, inconvenient, or behind the times.

Then verse 52 lands:

**ula'ika alladhina la'anahumu Allah**.

Those are the ones Allah has cursed.

The root **l-'-n** carries expulsion, being pushed far away from mercy. That verdict fits the crime. They were entrusted with some share of revelation, yet moved so far from its light that they called darkness the better path. The punishment is named as distance because the sin itself was already a movement of inner distancing from truth.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Moral Inversion Often Begins Long Before the Final Verdict

Most people do not wake up one morning and suddenly call falsehood guidance. There is usually a slower process. A lie becomes convenient. Rivalry enters the heart. Envy clouds judgment. Some worldly alliance begins to matter more than truth. The ego discovers that admitting revelation would cost status, comfort, or belonging. So first it bends, then it edits, then it rationalizes, and eventually it starts speaking as though distortion were wisdom.

That is why the opening command to look is so important. The Quran wants us to study inversion before we become participants in it.

[PAUSE]

You can see this even in ordinary life. A person keeps excusing what he privately knows is crooked because it benefits his side. Over time the apology deepens into admiration. He stops saying, "I know it is wrong, but..." and starts saying, "Actually this is the realistic thing. This is the stronger thing. This is the better path." At that point the issue is no longer only weakness. Perception itself has become diseased.

These verses warn us against reaching that stage. The heart must be watched early, while it still knows how to feel the difference between truth and convenience. Once convenience acquires the aura of guidance, repentance becomes harder because the person no longer experiences himself as leaving the path.

### Theme 2: Religious Nearness Can Be Weaponized by the Ego

There is another sting in the phrase "a share of the Book." These were not people standing at an obvious distance from revelation. They had vocabulary, inheritance, remembered scripture, and some real religious proximity. Yet that very proximity could be turned into a weapon when not accompanied by humility.

This is one of the hardest lessons for religious people to learn. Nearness to sacred language can feed the ego just as easily as it can purify it. If the self remains hungry for superiority, revelation may be treated not as light to submit to but as capital to deploy. Then scripture becomes a status-marker, argument-stock, or identity shield while the heart itself drifts farther from Allah.

[PAUSE]

That is why these verses are so relevant beyond their first audience. We all face the temptation to let tribe, prestige, or resentment distort our recognition of truth. We may not use the exact categories of jibt and taghut in the same historical form, but the soul still knows how to relocate faith into powers that overstep Allah's bounds. It still knows how to praise what is useful rather than what is true. It still knows how to call a crooked road "more guided" because admitting otherwise would injure the ego.

The cure begins with honesty. What do I already know is false but keep dressing in sophisticated language? Where am I admiring a path because it wins, not because it is true? What alliance in my life is pressuring me to bend the scale by which guidance is recognized?

## Closing Synthesis

These three verses move with severe clarity.

First, they command us to look at religious fabrication directly.

Then they expose the deeper corruption that follows: a share of revelation without faithfulness, belief misdirected into jibt and taghut, and a public declaration that unbelief is better guided than belief.

Finally they give the verdict: distance from Allah's mercy, and no helper against that distance.

[PAUSE]

That is the architecture of the passage: fabricated lie, corrupted allegiance, inverted judgment, then curse.

The warning is not only against open unbelief. It is against the subtler disaster by which falsehood becomes respectable, then admirable, then "more guided" in our eyes.

### Questions to Carry

1. Where in my life am I tempted to praise what is effective or fashionable even when I know it bends away from truth?
2. Has religious familiarity ever made me more self-assured than surrendered?
3. What false loyalties am I at risk of investing with a kind of faith they do not deserve?
4. Do I still have the humility to let revelation correct my scale before I start calling the wrong path guided?

### One-Sentence Distillation

**These verses teach that when inherited religious nearness is corrupted by fabrication and rivalry, the heart can become so inverted that it calls falsehood guidance and earns distance from Allah's mercy.**

### Du'a

Allahumma, keep our scale straight. Do not let us inherit words of truth while our hearts drift toward false loyalties. Protect us from praising what You have not guided, from trusting what oversteps Your bounds, and from ever calling the crooked path better than the path of iman. Keep us near Your mercy and far from the blindness that mistakes falsehood for huda. Ameen.

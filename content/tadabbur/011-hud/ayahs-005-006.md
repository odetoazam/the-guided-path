# No Folded Chest and No Moving Creature Lies Outside His Knowledge or His Provision

## Surah Hud (11:5-6)

[ayah:11:5] أَلَآ إِنَّهُمْ يَثْنُونَ صُدُورَهُمْ لِيَسْتَخْفُوا۟ مِنْهُ ۚ أَلَا حِينَ يَسْتَغْشُونَ ثِيَابَهُمْ يَعْلَمُ مَا يُسِرُّونَ وَمَا يُعْلِنُونَ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ عَلِيمٌۢ بِذَاتِ ٱلصُّدُورِ

[ayah:11:6] ۞ وَمَا مِن دَآبَّةٍ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ إِلَّا عَلَى ٱللَّهِ رِزْقُهَا وَيَعْلَمُ مُسْتَقَرَّهَا وَمُسْتَوْدَعَهَا ۚ كُلٌّ فِى كِتَـٰبٍ مُّبِينٍ

*Ala innahum yathnuna sudurahum li-yastakhfu minhu. Ala hina yastaghshuna thiyabahum ya'lamu ma yusirruna wa ma yu'linun. Innahu 'alimun bi-dhati al-sudur.*

*Wa ma min dabbatin fi al-ardi illa 'ala Allahi rizquha wa ya'lamu mustaqarraha wa mustawda'aha. Kullun fi kitabin mubin.*

**Unquestionably, they fold their chests to hide from Him. Unquestionably, when they cover themselves with their garments, He knows what they conceal and what they declare. Indeed, He is Knowing of what is within the chests. And there is no creature on the earth except that its provision is upon Allah, and He knows its place of settlement and its place of deposit. All is in a clear Book.**

[PAUSE]

## Introduction

There is a particular kind of human foolishness that appears when the heart knows, at some level, that Allah sees it, but still tries to manufacture a private corner against Him.

We do this in different ways. Sometimes we physically withdraw. Sometimes we lower our heads, dim the screen, shut the door, change the browser tab, mute the conversation, or keep the thought unspoken and hope its silence makes it safer. Sometimes we do not even try to hide from other people first. We try to hide from Allah in our own imagination. We fold inward and act as though inwardness itself were opacity.

[PAUSE]

These two ayahs shatter that illusion. But they do something even more beautiful than that. They do not move only from hiddenness to exposure. They move from hiddenness to provision. From the folded chest to the moving creature. From the secrets people try to shield to the rizq Allah Himself guarantees. It is a remarkable pairing. The One who cannot be escaped is also the One from whom every creature is fed.

The hook of the passage is this: **Hud teaches that no inward fold, garment-covering, secret, or public display can hide a servant from Allah; and the same Lord whose knowledge reaches the deepest chest is the Lord who has taken responsibility for the provision and places of every creature on earth.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Yathnuna sudurahum**: The Body Tries to Act Out Concealment

The passage opens:

**ala innahum yathnuna sudurahum li-yastakhfu minhu**.

Unquestionably, they fold their chests to hide from Him.

[PAUSE]

The verb **yathnuna** comes from the root **ث ن ي**, the root of folding, bending, doubling back, curving something over itself. The image is physical before it is psychological. They are not merely "having private thoughts." They are enacting concealment in posture. The chest bends inward. The body itself performs the wish to disappear.

That is deeply human. Shame, rebellion, desire, and hypocrisy often do not remain in the abstract. They recruit the body. The shoulders round. The chest closes. The gaze drops or turns away. We instinctively act as though folding ourselves makes us less visible.

Then the verse says:

**li-yastakhfu minhu**.

In order to hide from Him.

The verb is Form X from **خ ف ي**: to seek hiddenness, to try to make oneself concealed. The wording is important. The ayah is exposing not successful hiddenness, but the *attempt* at it. Human beings try for opacity before the One from whom opacity is impossible.

[PAUSE]

That makes the verse painfully relevant. Much of sin is not only desire. It is the fantasy of managed secrecy. It is the heart saying: if I can just fold the matter far enough inward, perhaps it becomes morally smaller. Perhaps it becomes mine alone. Perhaps even before Allah it becomes dimmer.

But the verse begins with **ala** twice, an attention-grabbing interruption. Wake up. Look again. This whole premise is false.

### 2. **Hina yastaghshuna thiyabahum**: Even Cloth Cannot Create an Inside Beyond Him

Then the ayah deepens the image:

**ala hina yastaghshuna thiyabahum**.

Unquestionably, when they cover themselves with their garments.

[PAUSE]

The verb **yastaghshuna** comes from **غ ش و**, covering over, veiling, draping something upon something else. It is a stronger image than mere clothing. The person pulls covering over themselves. The garment becomes part of the performance of retreat.

And then:

**ya'lamu ma yusirruna wa ma yu'linun**.

He knows what they conceal and what they declare.

The pair is comprehensive. **Yusirruna** from **س ر ر** names what is kept inward, hidden, carried in secrecy. **Yu'linun** from **ع ل ن** names what is made outward, open, public, announced. Between the two, the whole human field is covered. Inner and outer. Quiet and visible. Private and performed.

[PAUSE]

This is more than surveillance. It is the collapse of the false wall between the secret self and the public self before Allah. We live as though those are two different lives. The ayah says both are equally present to Him.

Then it lands with one of the most searching phrases in the Qur'an:

**innahu 'alimun bi-dhati al-sudur**.

Indeed He is Knowing of what is within the chests.

Not just of spoken thoughts. Not just of declared intentions. **Bi-dhati al-sudur** reaches the inward possession of the chest itself, what belongs to it, what sits at its core, what it carries as its interior substance. The ayah does not say merely that Allah knows what passes through you. It says He knows the very interiority you treat as your last private zone.

### 3. **Wa ma min dabbatin fi al-ard**: The Surah Moves From Hidden Humans to Every Moving Creature

Then, suddenly and beautifully, the next ayah widens:

**wa ma min dabbatin fi al-ard**.

There is no creature moving on the earth...

[PAUSE]

The word **dabbah** comes from **د ب ب**, the root of moving lightly, creeping, stepping along the ground. It is earthy language. Not grand beings only. Not heroic creatures only. Anything that moves along the earth as a living dependent being.

Why this shift? Because the surah is showing that Allah's relation to creation is total. He is not only the Knower of hidden rebellion in human chests. He is also the Sustainer of all that moves.

Then comes the astonishing phrase:

**illa 'ala Allahi rizquha**.

Except that its provision is upon Allah.

The wording is heavy with reassurance. Rizq is not described here as accidental flow in a cold universe. It is placed **upon Allah** in the language of divine commitment and guarantee. Not because anything obligates Him from outside Himself, but because He has taken this sustaining care upon Himself by His generosity.

[PAUSE]

So the same Lord from whom the hypocrite cannot hide is the Lord who is feeding the worm in the dark soil, the bird between migrations, the animal in its den, the infant in its weakness, the human who imagines they are self-sustaining, and the servant who fears tomorrow's provision. His knowledge is not detached from His care.

### 4. **Mustaqarraha wa mustawda'aha**: Every Settled Place and Entrusted Place Is Known

The ayah continues:

**wa ya'lamu mustaqarraha wa mustawda'aha**.

And He knows its place of settlement and its place of deposit.

[PAUSE]

These two words are part of the ayah's depth.

**Mustaqar** carries the sense of settling, abiding, coming to rest, remaining somewhere with a kind of stability.

**Mustawda'** carries the sense of being placed somewhere in deposit, entrusted somewhere, stored for a time, put down in a place not owned by itself.

The classical tafasir mention multiple applications here: a creature's present dwelling and future resting place, its place in life and place after death, the places it inhabits and the places it is held. The wording is wide enough to carry that breadth.

[PAUSE]

That breadth matters spiritually. We are always in one of these two conditions. Either we are in a place of apparent settlement, or we are in some kind of temporary deposit we wrongly imagine to be permanent. Allah knows both.

How much of our anxiety comes from forgetting that? We act as though our location, our season, our house, our job, our body, our social standing, even our emotional state, is an ungoverned accident. The ayah says your settled places are known. Your deposited places are known. You have never once landed outside divine knowledge.

Then the ayah closes:

**kullun fi kitabin mubin**.

All is in a clear Book.

The movement is complete: hidden chest, concealed thought, public declaration, creature, provision, settlement, deposit, all of it gathered into record. Nothing drifts loose.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: The Fantasy of Private Sin Survives by Treating Inwardness as Secrecy

These ayahs are not only about outward hypocrites in an ancient setting. They are about a permanent strategy of the nafs.

We often know better than to deny Allah openly. So we choose a subtler rebellion. We keep the act, thought, attachment, resentment, indulgence, or private loyalty folded inward. We do not bring it into speech, and then we start treating its silence as innocence. Because it is unannounced, we begin to feel it is unaccountable.

[PAUSE]

That is why the Qur'an uses the chest-image here. The chest is where emotion tightens, intention gathers, and secrecy curls up to live. The ayah is saying: do not confuse inwardness with invisibility.

This is actually a mercy. If Allah only judged what became public, the inner life would rot without interruption. But because He knows **ma yusirruna wa ma yu'linun**, the concealed and the declared, the unseen heart is still inside the field of Lordship. That means tawbah is possible before the hidden thing becomes a public ruin.

The verse does not simply expose us. It rescues us from the lie that secrecy can heal what only repentance can heal.

### Theme 2: The One Who Knows Everything About You Is Also the One Sustaining Everything Around You

The pairing of these ayahs is incredibly tender if we let it be.

We might expect the passage on hidden chests to be followed only by threat. But instead it is followed by rizq. The Lord who knows what is in the chest is the Lord on whom every creature's provision rests.

[PAUSE]

That changes the emotional weather of the passage. Allah is not saying: I see you, therefore despair. He is saying: I see what you hide, and I am still the One sustaining you, knowing your place, your transitions, your needs, your movements, and your record.

For the anxious believer, this matters immensely. Sometimes people hear Allah's total knowledge and feel only fear. But Hud makes us hold a fuller truth. His knowledge is not sterile omniscience. It is the knowledge of the Sustainer.

He knows the folded chest and does not lose track of its rizq.

He knows the hidden secret and does not cease to feed the one carrying it.

He knows the place where the creature rests and the place where it is deposited, and all of that remains within His Book.

[PAUSE]

So these ayahs call for two responses at once: honesty and trust. Honesty, because hiddenness is impossible. Trust, because the One from whom hiddenness is impossible is also the One upon whom provision rests.

## Closing Synthesis

Hud first exposes the illusion of concealment: people bend their chests, seek hiddenness, pull garments over themselves, and imagine they have created some inward room beyond Allah's gaze. But He knows what they conceal and what they announce, because He knows the very interior of the chests.

[PAUSE]

Then the surah widens from the inwardly folded human being to every moving creature on earth. Their rizq is upon Allah. Their settled place and entrusted place are known. All of it is written in a clear Book. The movement is breathtaking: chest, garment, secret, declaration, creature, provision, place, record. Nothing escapes the map of His knowledge.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with these ayahs is to ask: what am I still trying to keep folded inward as though silence made it safe? And at the same time, where have I forgotten that my provision, my place, and even the transitions I do not control are already known by the One who sustains all that moves on earth?

[PAUSE]

Hud leaves us with this integrated truth: **you cannot hide from the Lord who knows the deepest chest, and you do not need to panic before the Lord upon whom the provision of every creature already rests.**

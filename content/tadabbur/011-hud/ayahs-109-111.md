# Inherited Worship, Deferred Judgment, and the Full Repayment of Deeds

## Surah Hud (11:109-111)

[ayah:11:109] فَلَا تَكُ فِى مِرْيَةٍ مِّمَّا يَعْبُدُ هَـٰٓؤُلَآءِ ۚ مَا يَعْبُدُونَ إِلَّا كَمَا يَعْبُدُ ءَابَآؤُهُم مِّن قَبْلُ ۚ وَإِنَّا لَمُوَفُّوهُمْ نَصِيبَهُمْ غَيْرَ مَنقُوصٍ

## Introduction

After the passage that carried us to the gathered Day, Surah Hud returns to the people still walking around in this world and tells the Prophet something searching: do not be in doubt about what these people worship.

That is striking. The surah does not begin here by arguing in detail against their idols. It begins by removing hesitation about the entire phenomenon. What they worship is not intellectually impressive, spiritually deep, or historically weighty. It is inherited repetition.

[PAUSE]

Then the passage moves quickly. Musa also received a Book, and people differed about it. So the Prophet is not being singled out for rejection. A prior word from Allah delayed decisive judgment, but that delay is not acquittal. And the whole section closes with full repayment: every deed will be answered, because Allah is fully aware of what they do.

The hook of the passage is this: **Hud teaches that false worship does not become dignified by age, that disagreement around revelation is an old human pattern, and that delayed judgment still ends in complete repayment under the Lord who misses nothing.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **Fi miryah**: The Prophet Is Told Not to Let Falsehood Borrow the Weight of Mystery

The ayah begins:

**fa-la taku fi miryatin mimma ya'budu ha'ula'**.

So do not be in doubt about what these people worship.

[PAUSE]

The word **miryah** is not just a light question. It carries the feel of unsettled, troubling doubt. The surah is saying: do not let their worship create that disturbance in you. Do not grant it the dignity of ambiguity.

Then the Qur'an strips their practice down to its real content:

**ma ya'buduna illa kama ya'budu aba'uhum min qabl**.

They worship only as their fathers worshipped before.

That is devastating because it removes every claim of insight. Their religion is not built on proof. It is continuity without truth, inheritance without verification. Ancientness here is not an argument. It is merely age.

### 2. The Frame of **Waffa**: Full Share Now, Full Recompense Then

At the end of ayah 109 Allah says:

**wa inna la-muwaffuhum nasibahum ghayra manqus**.

We will surely give them their portion undiminished.

[PAUSE]

Then in ayah 111 the passage closes:

**la-yuwaffiyannahum rabbuka a'malahum**.

Your Lord will surely fully repay them for their deeds.

The same root, **w-f-y**, frames the section. In Arabic it carries the sense of giving something in full, not clipped, not shorted, not partly withheld.

That frame is heavy. Whatever portion has been appointed to them - whether worldly allotment, deferred punishment, or final recompense as the tafsir explains - it will arrive complete. And then the closing makes it even more explicit: their deeds themselves will be fully paid back.

The surah is teaching us that divine delay is never divine forgetfulness. The account is still exact.

### 3. **Fa-khtulifa fih ... kalimatun sabaqat**: Revelation Meets the Old Human Reflex of Disagreement

Then the surah turns to Musa:

**wa la-qad atayna Musa al-kitaba fa-khtulifa fih**.

We gave Musa the Book, and it was differed about.

[PAUSE]

This is consolation, but it is also exposure. The problem is not that this ummah received an unusually controversial revelation. The problem is that human beings repeatedly meet divine guidance with division.

Then:

**wa lawla kalimatun sabaqat min rabbika la-qudiya baynahum**.

Were it not for a word that had already preceded from your Lord, the matter would have been judged between them.

The surah names something quiet but immense: judgment is delayed because of a prior divine word. Allah is not struggling to decide. He has already spoken a principle into history by which people are granted term, space, and unfolding before the final decision manifests.

Then the ayah says:

**wa innahum la-fi shakkin minhu murib**.

They are in a doubt about it that is itself unsettling and suspicious.

This is not the doubt of a sincere seeker still on the road. It is doubt that breeds further disturbance, doubt that keeps producing resistance instead of humility.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: Inherited Religion Can Feel Stable While Being Empty

One of the hardest things for the heart to learn is that repetition does not prove truth. If something has been done for generations, it begins to feel weighty simply because it has outlived the people who began it. Tradition can borrow the emotional authority of age.

[PAUSE]

Hud cuts through that intoxication. **They worship only as their fathers worshipped before.** The Qur'an does not deny continuity. It denies that continuity, by itself, means guidance. Whole communities can hand down error with perfect sincerity. A child can inherit a vocabulary, a shrine, a story, and a social reflex without inheriting a proof.

That warning reaches beyond ancient idols. Any belief, habit, or communal loyalty can hide inside the sentence, "This is how our people have always done it." Revelation comes to ask a more frightening question: yes, but is it true before Allah?

### Theme 2: Delay Is Part of the Test, Not Cancellation of the Verdict

The passage keeps returning to deferral. A prior word delayed decisive judgment. Their portion will still be fully given. Their deeds will still be fully repaid.

[PAUSE]

This matters because delay changes human behavior. When judgment does not arrive quickly, people begin to reinterpret reality. They call patience weakness, suspension approval, and deferred consequence a sign that no reckoning is coming.

But Hud teaches a harder discipline. The absence of immediate collapse does not mean the matter is unresolved. It may mean the matter is being allowed to ripen under the exact word Allah has already spoken. And when the repayment finally comes, it will not be rough or approximate. It will be **wafa'** - full, measured, complete.

## Closing Synthesis

The Prophet is told not to be disturbed by the worship of the idolaters, because what they worship is only inherited imitation. Musa also received a Book and that Book too became a site of human disagreement. A prior word from Allah delayed the decisive unveiling, but that delay never meant the matter would vanish. Their portion will be given in full, and every deed will finally be repaid in full, because the Lord who judges is fully aware of everything they do.

[PAUSE]

These ayahs are clarifying in a way that can feel severe. They remove the false romance of ancestral error. They remove the shock of being opposed when carrying revelation. And they remove the illusion that delayed judgment means moral ambiguity.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with this passage is to ask: where in my life have I granted an inherited pattern more authority than a revealed truth? When I meet confusion or disagreement around the Qur'an, do I act as though something strange is happening, or as though I am watching an old human reflex repeat itself? And when consequence is delayed, do I quietly translate that into safety?

[PAUSE]

Hud leaves us with a bracing certainty: **falsehood does not become true by surviving generations, and no deed disappears into history because your Lord will repay every person in full.**

# Nuh Refuses to Sell the Call, Refuses to Expel the Believers, and Refuses Claims He Was Not Given

## Surah Hud (11:29-31)

[ayah:11:29] وَيَـٰقَوْمِ لَآ أَسْـَٔلُكُمْ عَلَيْهِ مَالًا ۖ إِنْ أَجْرِىَ إِلَّا عَلَى ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَمَآ أَنَا۠ بِطَارِدِ ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓا۟ ۚ إِنَّهُم مُّلَـٰقُوا۟ رَبِّهِمْ وَلَـٰكِنِّىٓ أَرَىٰكُمْ قَوْمًا تَجْهَلُونَ

[ayah:11:30] وَيَـٰقَوْمِ مَن يَنصُرُنِى مِنَ ٱللَّهِ إِن طَرَدتُّهُمْ ۚ أَفَلَا تَذَكَّرُونَ

[ayah:11:31] وَلَآ أَقُولُ لَكُمْ عِندِى خَزَآئِنُ ٱللَّهِ وَلَآ أَعْلَمُ ٱلْغَيْبَ وَلَآ أَقُولُ إِنِّى مَلَكٌ وَلَآ أَقُولُ لِلَّذِينَ تَزْدَرِىٓ أَعْيُنُكُمْ لَن يُؤْتِيَهُمُ ٱللَّهُ خَيْرًا ۖ ٱللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ بِمَا فِىٓ أَنفُسِهِمْ ۖ إِنِّىٓ إِذًا لَّمِنَ ٱلظَّـٰلِمِينَ

**And O my people, I do not ask you for wealth in return for it. My reward is only upon Allah. And I am not one to drive away those who believe. Indeed, they will meet their Lord, but I see you to be a people who are ignorant. And O my people, who would protect me from Allah if I drove them away? Will you not then remember? And I do not say to you that with me are the treasuries of Allah, nor do I know the unseen, nor do I say that I am an angel, nor do I say of those whom your eyes despise that Allah will never give them any good. Allah is most knowing of what is within their souls. Indeed, if I did that, I would surely be among the wrongdoers.**

## Introduction

The proud often try to negotiate truth through terms of access.

If you want our attention, what are you charging? If you want our respect, why keep those people near you? If you want our approval, raise your profile. Claim more power. Speak with more spectacle. Distance yourself from the unimpressive and align yourself with those who enhance your status.

[PAUSE]

Nuh refuses every one of those terms. He will not take money for the message. He will not expel the believers to make the gathering more elite. He will not inflate his claims about what he possesses or knows. And most beautifully, he will not pass a verdict of inner worth on the people whom arrogant eyes despise.

The hook of the passage is this: **Hud teaches that prophetic integrity refuses monetized da'wah, refuses class-cleansing the believing community, and refuses false spiritual claims, because the messenger stands before Allah rather than before the approval economy of the powerful.**

## Part 1: The Linguistic Journey

### 1. **La as'alukum 'alayhi malan... in ajriya illa 'ala Allah**: The Message Is Not for Sale

Ayah 29 opens:

**la as'alukum 'alayhi malan**.

I do not ask you for wealth in return for it.

[PAUSE]

The sentence is simple, but it cuts through one of the oldest suspicions against religious calling: "What is he getting out of this?" Nuh removes that calculation immediately. No money for the message.

Then:

**in ajriya illa 'ala Allah**.

My reward is only upon Allah.

The word **ajr** is powerful here. He is not claiming to be above reward as such. He is relocating the source of reward. Not from the audience. Not from patrons. Not from social return. From Allah.

This matters because the source of reward often determines the shape of speech. If your reward comes from people, their comfort will control your message. If your reward comes from Allah, you can stay truthful even when truth costs.

### 2. **Wa ma ana bi-taridi alladhina amanu**: The Messenger Will Not Purify the Gathering for Elite Taste

Then Nuh says:

**wa ma ana bi-taridi alladhina amanu**.

And I am not one to drive away those who believe.

[PAUSE]

The phrase is morally beautiful. The issue is not logistical only. It is ethical and theological. The elite apparently want distance from the lowly believers. Nuh refuses.

**Tarid** from the field of expelling and driving away carries a painful force. He will not clear out the sincere in order to make the room more acceptable to the proud.

Then he says:

**innahum mulaqu rabbihim**.

Indeed, they will meet their Lord.

This is an extraordinary reframing. These believers are not to be valued by your social scales. They are on their way to a meeting with Allah. That alone overturns every shallow metric.

Then Nuh diagnoses the real problem:

**wa lakinni arakum qawman tajhalun**.

But I see you to be a people who are ignorant.

[PAUSE]

Their ignorance is not mere lack of data. It is ignorance of value, ignorance of who matters, ignorance of what the believer's dignity is before Allah.

### 3. **Man yansuruni min Allah in taradtuhum**: Pleasing the Proud Can Mean Standing Defenseless Before Allah

Ayah 30 sharpens the matter:

**man yansuruni min Allahi in taradtuhum**.

Who would protect me from Allah if I drove them away?

[PAUSE]

This question shifts the axis completely. The proud are pressuring him horizontally: choose us over them. Nuh answers vertically: if I obey your arrogance at the expense of believers, who will protect me from Allah?

That is one of the great cleansings in the Qur'an. Many moral compromises become possible only because we fear people more immediately than we fear standing exposed before Allah.

Then:

**a-fa-la tadhakkarun**.

Will you not then remember?

Meaning: have you forgotten who actually judges this matter?

### 4. **La aqulu...**: Humility Includes Refusing Claims Allah Did Not Give

Ayah 31 is built by repeated negation:

**wa la aqulu lakum 'indi khaza'inu Allah**.

I do not say to you that with me are the treasuries of Allah.

**wa la a'lamu al-ghayb**.

Nor do I know the unseen.

**wa la aqulu inni malak**.

Nor do I say that I am an angel.

[PAUSE]

This is profound prophetic humility. Nuh does not exaggerate his station to win credibility. He tells the truth about the limits of what was given to him. Real messengership does not need inflation.

Then comes a line of extraordinary tenderness:

**wa la aqulu lilladhina tazdari a'yunukum lan yu'tiyahumu Allahu khayran**.

And I do not say of those whom your eyes despise that Allah will never give them any good.

The verb **tazdari** captures contempt, belittling, looking down with scorn. And notice the wording: **a'yunukum** - your eyes despise them. The problem begins in a certain kind of gaze.

But Nuh refuses to turn elite contempt into spiritual verdict. He will not translate social disdain into a claim that Allah has denied them good.

Then:

**Allahu a'lamu bima fi anfusihim**.

Allah knows best what is within their souls.

[PAUSE]

Here the whole moral field becomes clear. The inner reality of these believers is not open to arrogant eyes. It is known to Allah.

And Nuh closes:

**inni idhan la-mina al-zalimin**.

Indeed, if I did that, I would surely be among the wrongdoers.

So to expel the believers, or to deny their possible good, or to speak beyond what Allah gave, would not be sophistication. It would be **zulm**.

## Part 2: The Thematic Depths

### Theme 1: One of the Earliest Corruptions of Sacred Work Is Letting the Audience Purchase the Shape of the Message

These ayahs are especially sharp for anyone involved in teaching, leadership, writing, community work, or visible service.

The temptation is not always crude greed. Sometimes it is subtler. If people are effectively rewarding me, will I still speak in a way they dislike? If access, prestige, donations, invitations, attention, or safety come from a certain class, will I gradually shape the message to keep those channels open?

[PAUSE]

Nuh's answer is cleansing: **in ajriya illa 'ala Allah**. My reward is only upon Allah.

This does not mean taking money is always inherently corrupt in every setting. It means the heart must know who pays it. If the soul begins to depend on human reward for courage, the truth will slowly be resized to fit the audience.

And the same principle applies inwardly even outside formal da'wah. We all perform for some imagined audience. The question is whether that audience is now setting the limits of our honesty with Allah.

### Theme 2: Looking Down on the Lowly Is Spiritually Dangerous Because It Pretends to Read Souls Through Eyes

The line about those whom your eyes despise is one of the Qur'an's most searching indictments of social contempt.

There are people the eye dismisses quickly. The unimpressive, unpolished, poor, awkward, unconnected, culturally unglamorous. The proud eye sees them and assumes spiritual smallness. But Nuh refuses that entire move.

[PAUSE]

Why? Because the eye is not qualified to judge inner nearness to Allah.

This is a hard medicine for the nafs. We instinctively attach value to surface signals. Who speaks well. who carries themselves well. who has institutional legitimacy. who knows the codes. But the Qur'an keeps unsettling that instinct by honoring hearts the world misreads.

And this reaches beyond how we view others. Sometimes we think our own lack of polish means Allah has little good for us too. Nuh's sentence protects against that despair as well. The eye's disdain is not the same thing as Allah's decree.

The only safe place is humility: Allah knows what is in souls. We do not.

## Closing Synthesis

Nuh answers the pressure of the elite by clearing the field completely. He does not ask for wealth. His reward is with Allah. He will not drive away the believers. They are going to meet their Lord. He will not sacrifice them to elite preference, because who would defend him from Allah if he did?

[PAUSE]

Then he goes further and refuses every inflated claim: no treasuries of Allah in his control, no independent knowledge of the unseen, no pretense of angelic nature, and no verdict that those scorned by proud eyes are deprived of divine good. Allah alone knows what is in souls.

[PAUSE]

One way to sit with these ayahs is to ask: who is really setting the terms of my integrity? Do I still need human reward to remain honest? Have I let my eyes make arrogant assumptions about who matters before Allah? And where am I tempted to claim, imply, or perform more than Allah has actually given me?

[PAUSE]

Hud leaves us with this model of prophetic dignity: **the truthful caller neither sells the message, nor trims away the weak to impress the powerful, nor inflates his own station, because he stands under Allah's gaze and knows that inner worth is known by Him alone.**

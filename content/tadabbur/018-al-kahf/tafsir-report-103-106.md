## 18:103-106

### Ibn Kathir (English)

Ibn Kathir treats 18:103-106 as one unit under the heading of the greatest losers in respect of deeds. He cites early discussions about whether the ayah applies to specific groups, then explicitly broadens the meaning: the ayah is general for anyone who worships or acts in an unacceptable way while assuming that he is right and that his deeds will be received. He explains 18:104 as deeds not in accordance with the prescribed, accepted way; 18:105 as denial of Allah's signs and the meeting with Him causing deeds to become void and weightless; and 18:106 as Hell being recompense for disbelief and mockery of Allah's signs and messengers.

### al-Tabari (Arabic)

Al-Tabari explains that Allah commands the Prophet to ask whether he should inform people of those who are most losing in deeds. He glosses the loss through a trade image: people exhaust themselves seeking profit and advantage, but what they gain is ruin and destruction rather than what they sought. He records early views applying the ayah to monks, priests, People of the Scripture, and other groups, while the wording remains capacious. His treatment of the passage supports the reflection's emphasis on effort that appears meaningful to the doer but fails when measured by Allah.

### al-Muyassar (Arabic)

Al-Muyassar summarizes the passage as a warning: the Prophet is commanded to inform people about the greatest losers in deeds, those whose efforts went astray in worldly life while they thought they were doing good. It explains that these are people who disbelieved in the signs of their Lord and in meeting Him, so their deeds became void, no weight is established for them on the Day of Resurrection, and their recompense is Jahannam because of disbelief and mockery of Allah's signs and messengers.

The reflection aligns with the classical frame: the passage is about deeds and striving that fail because they are disconnected from accepted guidance, belief in Allah's signs, belief in meeting Him, and reverence for His messengers.
